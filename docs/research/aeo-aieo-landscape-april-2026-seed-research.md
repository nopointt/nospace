## 1. Terminology Consolidation

### State of the Field

As of April 2026, the terminology landscape remains fragmented. No single acronym has "won." The most-used terms by category:

**Industry/agency usage:**
- **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)** — most frequently cited in agency and SEO influencer writing; approximately 59% of SEO influencers use this term per neelnetworks.com survey (date: early 2026, confidence: medium). Coined in academic context by Princeton/IIT Delhi paper (KDD 2024).
- **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)** — strong in tool/platform naming (Frase, Contently, Airefs all use "AEO"). Predates LLM era; originally referred to voice search / Alexa-type optimization.
- **LLMO (Large Language Model Optimization)** — used when the explicit target is training-data presence (what the model "knows"), not retrieval. Overlaps with LLMSEO.
- **AIEO (AI Engine Optimization)** — promoted specifically by Kalicube (Jason Barnard), positioned as umbrella term encompassing all AI touchpoints. Niche usage.
- **GAIO / AISO** — minor variants, essentially unused in mainstream sources.
- **LLM SEO / AI SEO** — informal catch-alls used in casual writing.

**Academic usage:**
- **GEO** — defined term in peer-reviewed literature. Princeton paper (Aggarwal et al., KDD 2024, arXiv:2311.09735) is the canonical academic source. Term defined as "optimizing content to improve visibility in generative engine responses."

**Analyst firm usage:**
- Gartner uses "AI search" and "generative AI search." No dedicated acronym in Gartner/Forrester analyst notes as of early 2026.

**TowardsAI taxonomy (2025, confidence: medium):**
- AEO = umbrella
  - LLMO = training-data presence
  - GEO = generative response retrieval
  - AAIO = AI-assisted information organization (minor variant)

**Conclusion for observation:** The field is operating with overlapping, partially-synonymous terms. "GEO" dominates academic and forward-looking SEO commentary. "AEO" dominates tool/service naming. Both are in active use. Practitioners often use them interchangeably. No industry standards body has codified a definition.

### 2.2 Perplexity

**Crawlers:**
- Indexing/retrieval: PerplexityBot (`Mozilla/5.0 ... PerplexityBot/1.0`), IP range at `/perplexitybot.json`
- User-triggered: Perplexity-User (`Mozilla/5.0 ... Perplexity-User/1.0`), IP range at `/perplexity-user.json`

**Retrieval logic — 3-layer system (authoritytech.io, 2026):**
1. Layer 1: Initial relevance-based document retrieval (standard IR)
2. Layer 2: Authority + relevance signals → ordered candidate list
3. Layer 3: XGBoost Reranker with BERT-based entity linking (`l3_reranker_drop_threshold`) — drops entire result set if quality threshold not met

**Citation patterns:** Inline numbered citations on every claim — most transparent citation system among AI engines (PCMag 2026 comparison). Uses RAG architecture enabling real-time information.

**Source biases:**
- Maintains curated manual domain lists: Amazon, GitHub, LinkedIn, Coursera, Reddit receive algorithmic boosts
- Top sources overall: Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, Wikipedia, G2
- Strong B2B-commercial skew (G2 prominent for software queries)
- News content heavily concentrated among small number of outlets (arXiv study July 2025, 366K citations across 65K responses)
- Freshness: ~30-day decay rate; new content gets `new_post_ctr` launch window boost
- Topic multipliers: AI/science/technology/business strategy topics get ~3x visibility boost vs neutral topics
- Formalized publisher payment program for news outlets (Forbes, June 2025)

**Notable controversy:** Cloudflare documented Perplexity bypassing robots.txt (2025). Behavior inconsistent with stated policy.

## 3. llms.txt Ecosystem

### Specification Origin

Proposed by Jeremy Howard (Answer.AI / fast.ai), published September 3, 2024. Original post: https://www.answer.ai/posts/2024-09-03-llmstxt.html

Official spec: https://llmstxt.org/

**Format (from llmstxt.org spec):**
- H1 heading (only mandatory element): project/site name
- Blockquote summary: essential context about the site
- Optional prose sections
- H2-delimited file lists with markdown bullet items: `[name](url): optional notes`
- "Optional" section marker = content skippable when context window is tight

**Two-file convention:**
- `llms.txt` — curated navigation file: structure + priority content highlights
- `llms-full.txt` — comprehensive: entire documentation in single consumable format
(Note: the official spec names these `llms-ctx.txt` and `llms-ctx-full.txt`; common convention uses `llms.txt` / `llms-full.txt`)

**Companion convention:** `.md` suffix on HTML URLs to serve markdown versions (e.g., `docs/page.html.md`)

### Adoption Rate (April 2026)

Conflicting data points (confidence: medium for all figures):
- SE Ranking analysis of 300K domains: 10.13% adoption
- BuiltWith tracking (Oct 25, 2025): 844,473 websites implemented
- Top 1M sites (May 2025): 105 valid files (0.011%) → 844,473 by Oct 2025 — extraordinary growth
- Higher adoption rate among medium/low-traffic sites; major authoritative sites have lower adoption

**Notable adopters:** Anthropic (Claude docs), OpenAI, Cloudflare, Stripe, Vercel, Perplexity

### Which AI Engines Parse It — Verified vs Claimed

**Google:** Explicitly does NOT use it. John Mueller (Google): "AFAIK, none of the AI services have said they're using LLMs.TXT (and you can tell when you look at your server logs that they don't even check for it)." (nohacks.co, 2026 — quoting Mueller)

**OpenAI, Microsoft, others:** Profound (AEO tracking platform) claims data showing these companies are crawling and indexing both llms.txt and llms-full.txt. No official public commitment from OpenAI or Anthropic.

**Claude:** Cannot browse web in base mode; llms-full.txt is useful as manual context upload (Cursor integration via @Docs).

**Practical status (April 2026):** No verified automated consumption by any major AI platform. Primary current value is as developer documentation navigation aid and for tools like Cursor/IDE context loading.

### Tool Support

VitePress plugin, Docusaurus plugin, PHP and JavaScript libraries, VS Code extension for auto-loading external documentation context.

### Adoption Statistics

Tools generating llms.txt are proliferating (multiple npm packages, CMS plugins). Adoption grew ~8000x in 5 months (May→Oct 2025) primarily driven by plug-and-play generators requiring minimal effort.

### Counter-arguments

- No correlation found between having llms.txt and receiving AI citations (Goodie/ALLMO data, 2026)
- Google's explicit non-support is significant given AI Overviews' dominance
- Adoption may be cargo-culting (low-effort, unclear benefit)
- Counter-position from Profound: enterprise AI orchestration tools (Cursor, Claude Code, GitHub Copilot) DO use llms.txt for documentation ingestion — not the public-facing AI search engines

## 5. Content Patterns AI Engines Prefer (Empirically Observed)

### Academic Baseline: Princeton/IIT Delhi GEO Paper (KDD 2024)

Paper: "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization" — Aggarwal et al., arXiv:2311.09735, published KDD August 2024.
Tested on GEO-bench (large-scale benchmark, diverse user queries, multiple domains).
Key finding: Adding citations and quotations significantly improves visibility. Visibility boost up to 40% possible. Efficacy varies by domain — domain-specific optimization is necessary.

### Structured Data

**JSON-LD is required.** Every AI engine tested prefers JSON-LD because it is cleanly separated from HTML and easier to parse programmatically. Google's official guidance (May 2025) explicitly recommends JSON-LD.

**Critical constraint: Server-side rendering.** Schema must be server-side rendered — not JavaScript-injected. GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot do NOT execute JavaScript (analysis of 500M+ GPTBot fetches: zero evidence of JS execution). SSR/SSG/ISR is mandatory for AI crawler visibility.

**Schema types ranked by AI citation value:**
1. FAQPage — pages with FAQPage markup are 3.2x more likely to appear in Google AI Overviews (BrightEdge study); direct Q&A pairs match AI output format
2. HowTo — procedural queries, step structure AI can navigate
3. Organization + sameAs — entity disambiguation (critical for brand recognition across models)
4. Article + Author + datePublished + dateModified — credibility signals
5. SoftwareApplication — relevant for developer tools and SaaS
6. QAPage, ClaimReview, Product, Review/AggregateRating

Sites with structured data: up to 30% higher visibility in AI Overviews (multiple sources, confidence: medium — exact figure varies by study). BrightEdge study: 44% increase in AI citations with structured data + FAQ blocks.

### Content Format Signals

**Q&A structure:** Question as heading (H2/H3) + direct answer in first 1-2 sentences. This matches AI output format and enables direct extraction.

**Factual density:** Statistics, specific dates, named entities, precise numbers. AI systems prefer citable declarative statements ("X does Y at Z rate") over narrative prose.

**Structured lists:** Numbered and bulleted lists are directly extractable by AI parsers.

**Code blocks:** For technical content, fenced code blocks improve citation probability — Perplexity in particular indexes technical content with syntax highlighting.

**Chunkability:** Clear H-tag hierarchy, semantic section boundaries, consistent heading depth. AI retrieval systems chunk pages for embedding — poor structure = poor chunk quality.

**Citation hooks:** Declarative standalone statements readable as standalone quotes. Short declarative paragraphs outperform embedded-in-flow prose.

**Author credentials + E-E-A-T parallel:** Visible author bio, credentials, publication date, outbound links to primary sources — all treated as trust signals.

**Freshness:** Perplexity has documented 30-day decay function. Google AI Overviews prefer recently-updated content for non-evergreen queries.

**Length:** No definitive sweet spot found in sources reviewed. GEO paper suggests comprehensiveness over brevity for visibility. Conflicting evidence — specific short Q&A formats also perform well.

**Markdown vs HTML:** For AI crawlers parsing raw HTML, markdown formatting within HTML (using proper tags) performs equivalently to markdown served as `.md` files, as long as content is SSR'd. For llms.txt/llms-full.txt: markdown is native format.

## 7. Process — What to Do, How

### Audit Phase

1. **Define target queries:** Start with 50 core buyer/user questions representing intended discovery scenarios. Include product-category questions, comparison queries, problem-solution queries.
2. **Baseline establishment:** Run all queries manually on ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode (minimum). Document: cited or not cited, citation position, brand name mentioned or not, sentiment.
3. **Competitive benchmarking:** Same query set, observe which competitors appear in citations.
4. **Platform differentiation:** Different engines cite different sources — audit needs to be per-engine, not aggregated.

### Content Production Cycle for AEO

1. **Gap identification:** Which queries trigger no citation for your brand?
2. **Content asset creation:** Per-query Q&A assets with direct answer opening, FAQPage schema, HowTo where applicable
3. **Entity profile strengthening:** Wikipedia page (if eligible), Google Knowledge Panel claim, organization schema with sameAs links to all authority sources
4. **Structured data deployment:** JSON-LD server-side rendered on all target pages
5. **Sprint cadence:** Typical sprint = 10-30 optimized assets; 30-90 day post-deployment monitoring before next sprint. Citation rates can decline in 3-6 months without ongoing reinforcement.

### On-Page Optimization Checklist

- H-tag hierarchy reflects semantic structure
- FAQPage schema on FAQ sections
- HowTo schema on instructional content
- Organization schema with sameAs on all key pages
- Article schema with Author, datePublished, dateModified on blog/documentation
- All schema in JSON-LD, server-side rendered
- Direct answer in first 1-2 sentences of each section
- Statistical claims with inline source attribution

### Off-Page: Where AI Engines Mine

**Primary third-party sources AI engines draw from:**
- Reddit (40.1% of LLM data training sources per completeaitraining.com 2025 data) — authentic discussion, Q&A threads
- Wikipedia — 26.3% citation rate; essential for entity recognition
- YouTube — 23.5%; video transcript content indexed
- GitHub — code documentation, README files, technical content; Perplexity manual domain boost
- LinkedIn — professional/B2B credibility signal across all engines
- Stack Overflow — technical Q&A; enterprise data licensing with AI labs
- G2, Capterra — software reviews; heavily weighted by Perplexity for SaaS
- Medium — thought leadership articles
- News media — Forbes, TechRadar, niche publications; earned media placements

**Publishing distribution strategy for AI training-data inclusion:**
- Guest posts on news/tech publications (earned media = Perplexity's most-cited category)
- Community participation (Reddit AMAs, Stack Overflow answers for relevant technical questions)
- Wikipedia contribution where eligible
- GitHub documentation quality (README, wiki, code comments)
- Press release distribution to indexed news aggregators

### Measurement

**Core KPIs (from llmpulse.ai, averi.ai, airops.com):**
- Citation Frequency: % of target queries where brand/product is cited
- AI Share of Voice: brand citations / (brand + competitor citations) across query set
- Brand Visibility Score: weighted citation frequency by query volume
- Sentiment Analysis: positive/neutral/negative framing in citations
- LLM Conversion Rate: AI citation → referral traffic → conversion (hardest to measure; requires UTM tracking of AI referral sessions)

**Monitoring cadence:**
- Monthly lightweight: re-run highest-priority 10-20 queries, check citation frequency and referral traffic trends
- Quarterly full audit: re-score all content against entity clarity, E-E-A-T; validate schema
- Annual: competitive benchmarking, full schema validation

## 8. Dependencies — Technical

### Schema.org Types — Priority for AI

| Schema Type | AI Value | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| FAQPage | CRITICAL | Q&A pairs extracted directly into AI answers; 3.2x AI Overview probability |
| HowTo | HIGH | Procedural/instructional content; step structure AI can navigate |
| Organization + sameAs | HIGH | Entity disambiguation across models; required for brand recognition |
| Article + Author + dates | HIGH | Credibility signals; freshness indicators |
| SoftwareApplication | HIGH for dev tools | Software product pages; relevant for Contexter/Vault |
| QAPage | MEDIUM | Similar to FAQPage but for single-question pages |
| ClaimReview | MEDIUM | Fact-check content; trust signal |
| Product + Review/AggregateRating | MEDIUM | E-commerce; user review aggregation |

### Implementation Format

**JSON-LD is mandatory** — microdata and RDFa are not preferred by AI parsers. Google explicitly recommends JSON-LD as of May 2025. All schema must be in `<script type="application/ld+json">` blocks in `<head>` or early `<body>`.

**Server-side rendered — mandatory.** AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot) do NOT execute JavaScript. This was confirmed by analysis of 500M+ GPTBot fetches showing zero JS execution. Implications:
- React/Vue/Angular SPA without SSR = invisible to AI crawlers
- Next.js SSR, Nuxt SSR, Astro, SvelteKit SSR/SSG, or prerendering required
- **SolidJS (tLOS stack):** SolidStart with SSR mode required for AI crawler visibility
- **Cloudflare Workers (Harkly/Contexter stack):** SSR is default for worker-rendered responses — generally compatible

### Open Graph + Twitter Cards

Relevant for social platform citations (Facebook, LinkedIn appear in AI top-cited domains). OG tags support entity recognition. Twitter Cards less relevant for AI citation mechanics directly.

### Sitemap

Standard XML sitemap with `<lastmod>` dates helps freshness signals for Googlebot-fed AI Overviews. PerplexityBot does fetch sitemaps. GPTBot sitemap behavior not documented.

### JavaScript Rendering

**AI crawlers do NOT render JavaScript.** Confirmed across GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot.
Workaround options:
1. SSR/SSG (preferred — content is in initial HTML)
2. Dynamic rendering / prerendering service (detect AI UA → serve pre-rendered HTML snapshot)
3. For Contexter Vault npm package documentation: static site generation (VitePress, Docusaurus) with SSR is optimal

### HTTP Versions

No documented evidence that HTTP/2 or HTTP/3 affects AI crawler behavior. Standard HTTPS required. Cloudflare-fronted sites function normally with AI crawlers.

### robots.txt Syntax Extensions

No standardized extensions for AI specifically. The `User-agent: Google-Extended` pattern (to control training vs retrieval separately) is now the de facto pattern for multi-purpose differentiation. IETF Web Bot Auth working group chartered early 2026 — standards-track spec for cryptographic bot verification using Ed25519/HTTP Message Signatures (RFC 9421). Status: draft.

## 9. Controversies / Debated

### 9.1 Does AEO Drive Traffic? (Zero-Click Data)

**The core tension:**

Zero-click rate is rising — and AI Overviews accelerate it:
- Overall: 58-64.82% of Google searches end without a click (various studies, Nov 2025 / 2026)
- When AI Overviews present: 80-83% zero-click rate (median)
- Google AI Mode: 93% of queries end with zero external clicks
- CTR for top-ranking page when AI Overview present: -47% to -61% (studies vary; Seer Interactive Sept 2025: -61%; Ahrefs Dec 2025: -58%)
- Major publisher traffic collapses: HubSpot -70-80%, Business Insider -55% (April 2022–April 2025)

**But being cited within AI Overviews reverses the trend:**
- Brands cited in AI Overviews earn 35% more organic clicks and 91% more paid clicks (multiple sources citing same dataset — confidence: medium)

**Conclusion:** AEO may not drive traffic broadly; it drives traffic to the specific sources cited. For uncited brands, AI search is pure zero-click. For cited brands, it amplifies existing traffic.

**LLM referral traffic converts better:** VentureBeat (cited in semrush compilation): LLM-referred traffic converts at 30-40% — higher than typical organic search conversion rates. Small volume but high quality.

### 9.2 Is AEO Just Good SEO Rebranded?

**The correlation evidence:**
AirOps April 2026 study (30M citations): position 1 in underlying search retrieval = 58% citation rate vs 14% at position 10. Four times more citations at the top. The study's conclusion: "The retrieval layer is SEO. The heading match is SEO. The schema is SEO." (freeagency.ai summary)

**The counter-argument:**
- Domain authority and backlink count show *no positive correlation* with AI citation rate — in some cases, slightly negative (multiple sources)
- Q&A structure, entity clarity, schema completeness, and UGC/community presence are AEO-specific signals that traditional SEO does not fully address
- Perplexity's topic multipliers (3x for AI/science/tech) and entity disambiguation requirements are genuinely different from PageRank signals
- Claude's strong UGC/review preference has no direct SEO equivalent

**Observation:** AEO requires the same foundation as good SEO (technical health, authority signals) but adds distinct layers: schema completeness, entity disambiguation, Q&A formatting, and community/third-party source presence. Neither "pure rebranding" nor "completely different discipline" — it is SEO-as-prerequisite with AEO-specific optimization layer on top.

### 9.3 Are LLM Citations Gameable?

The field is debating whether early AEO techniques will follow the trajectory of 2010-era link building (gameable → gamed → penalized).

**Evidence of early manipulation:**
- Up to 45% of AI responses cite a brand's content without mentioning the brand — attribution confusion
- Citation presence is highly variable across runs (only 20% of brands maintain presence across 5 consecutive identical queries — AirOps data)
- Perplexity's manual domain lists and XGBoost reranker create specific manipulation targets

**No evidence yet of large-scale manipulation penalties** — the field is too new (April 2026).

### 9.4 Trust Collapse Risk (Hallucinations + Misattribution)

AI engines can cite a source for claims the source does not actually make. Claude's high UGC citation rate combined with hallucination risk creates brand reputation vulnerability — a brand can be cited in context of inaccurate claims derived from user-generated content about them.

No standardized recourse mechanism exists. Brand monitoring (Meltwater, Brand24 with AI extensions) is the current response.

### 9.5 IP / Copyright Issues

**NYT v. OpenAI (filed Dec 2023, ongoing April 2026):**
- Case cleared to proceed (March 2026 judge ruling)
- OpenAI required to produce 20M ChatGPT logs (ordered Nov 2025)
- Plaintiffs' expert reports due Nov 14, 2026; Summary Judgment concluding April 2026
- Two separate federal judges have found AI training "highly transformative" and potentially fair use — but cases not yet resolved

**Broader pattern:**
- Reddit: $60M API licensing with Google (2024); 40.1% of LLM training data source
- Stack Overflow: enterprise content licensing deals with AI labs
- GitHub Copilot: from April 24, 2026, interaction data used for model training unless opted out

**Implication for content creators:** Contributing to Reddit/GitHub/Stack Overflow puts content in AI training pipelines. No individual compensation mechanism. Opt-out = loss of training-data inclusion = potential citation surface reduction over model update cycles.
