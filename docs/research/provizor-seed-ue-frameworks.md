## Key Frameworks Found

### Framework 1: CIS Pharmacy Profitability Model (FarmBazis)
**Origin:** Russian pharmacy management practice
**What it covers:** Relationship between markup percentage, inventory turnover, receivables/payables balance, and resulting profitability. Shows non-linear interaction: same markup = very different profitability depending on turnover.
**Core formula:**
```
Рентабельность (%) = ReализНац(%) × (S / ТЗ) × (1 + (Кред - Деб) / (ТЗ - (Кред - Деб)))
```
Where: S = monthly sales, ТЗ = average inventory value, Кред = payables, Деб = receivables.
**Key insight:** At 30-day turnover with balanced AP/AR, profitability = markup %. At 24-day turnover (+25% improvement), profitability grows 50%.
**Applicability to Provizor:** Direct. Monthly revision cadence should feed into this formula. Faster inventory turn is more valuable than trying to raise markup above regulatory ceilings.

### Framework 2: NCPA Independent Pharmacy Benchmarking Model
**Origin:** National Community Pharmacists Association (USA), annual Digest
**What it covers:** Full P&L benchmark by size tier. Revenue structure (96% Rx / 4% OTC in US vs. inverse in KZ). Gross margin compression trajectory. Cost structure as % of revenue.
**Core benchmarks (2024):**
- Gross profit margin: 19.7% (all prescriptions)
- Total Rx revenue: $94.9B across 18,984 pharmacies
- Avg prescriptions per store/year: 59,644
**Applicability to Provizor:** Methodology transferable. US gross margin (19.7%) is a floor reference; KZ OTC-heavy model with 30% markup and no PBM clawback likely yields higher gross margins. NCPA's KPI list (14 KPIs) is a practical template.

### Framework 3: Drug Category Margin Segmentation (ASPE 2024)
**Origin:** US Department of Health & Human Services, ASPE
**What it covers:** Gross margin decomposition by drug type: generics vs. branded. Pharmacies make money on generics, break even on branded. Quantified: generics 42.7%, branded 3.5%.
**Applicability to Provizor:** KZ equivalent: high-margin = generics + OTC supplements/cosmetics. Low-margin = OSMS-reimbursed branded drugs at regulated ceilings. Provizor's profitability driven by mix management toward high-margin categories.

### Framework 4: ABC-XYZ + VEN Matrix for Pharmacy Assortment
**Origin:** Pharmaceutical management science (global standard)
**What it covers:**
- ABC = value tier (A: 20% SKUs = 80% revenue)
- XYZ = demand stability (X = predictable, Z = erratic)
- VEN = criticality (Vital, Essential, Non-essential)
- Combined matrix = 9-27 cell decision framework
**Applicability to Provizor:** 6-7K SKUs per pharmacy is standard territory for ABC-XYZ. Justifies monthly inventory revision (standard recommendation: weekly for A-class, monthly for C-class).

### Framework 5: Kazakhstan Regressive Markup Model
**Origin:** Ministry of Health RK, Order December 2020, updated February 2025
**What it covers:** State-mandated maximum retail markup on registered drugs within GOMBP/OSMS system.
**Regressive scale (retail, special order mode):**
| Drug cost (tenge) | Max retail markup |
|---|---|
| ≤350 | 15% |
| 350–1,000 | 14.5% |
| 1,000–3,000 | 13.75% |
| 3,000–5,000 | 12.5% |
| 5,000–7,500 | 11.25% |
| 7,500–10,000 | 10% |
| 10,000–13,500 | 9% |
| 13,500–20,000 | 8% |
| 20,000–40,000 | 7% |
| 40,000–100,000 | 6% |
| >100,000 | 5% |
**Wholesale regressive scale:** 25% (≤350 tenge) down to 10% (>500K tenge)
**Applicability to Provizor:** CRITICAL. Alimkhan's "+30% over cost price" rule applies to NON-regulated drugs. For 5,936 SKUs in state registry, the above table is the binding constraint. Mix of regulated vs. free-price drugs determines achievable blended margin.

### Framework 6: Lost Sales / Дефектура Quantification
**Origin:** CIS pharmacy management practice + universal retail methodology
**What it covers:** Measuring revenue loss from stockouts. Two sub-methods:
1. Demand capture: "спросили — не было" logging (manual or POS-flagged)
2. Retrospective: Compare avg sales when in-stock vs. out-of-stock period × margin
**Formula:** Lost revenue = Σ (days_OOS × avg_daily_sales × price)
**Lost margin** = Lost revenue × gross margin %
**Applicability to Provizor:** WhatsApp inquiries (~30/day) are a natural defectura signal. Many inquiries likely result in "нет в наличии" → lost sale. Instrumenting this is high-value.

### Framework 7: Multi-Channel Margin Waterfall
**Origin:** Retail/e-commerce standard, applied to pharmacy
**What it covers:** True margin calculation when same SKU sells across different channels at different net prices.
**Formula per channel:**
```
Net margin per channel = Gross margin % − Channel cost %
- Offline: GM% − 0% (no commission) − labor % − overhead %
- Halyk Market: GM% − 5-19% commission − fulfillment %
- Wolt Apteka: GM% − Wolt commission % − fulfillment %
- WhatsApp/iTeka: GM% − 0% commission + inquiry handling cost
```
**Blended margin** = Σ (channel revenue share × channel net margin)
**Applicability to Provizor:** High. With 5-19% Halyk Market commission and 30% markup, some SKUs may have near-zero or negative contribution margin on Halyk. This framework identifies which SKUs to de-list from marketplace.

## Pharmacy P&L Structure (Standard Formula)

```
Revenue (Товарооборот)
  − COGS / Cost of Goods Dispensed (Себестоимость)
  = Gross Profit (Валовой доход)
    − Payroll & Benefits (~9-12% of revenue)
    − Rent & Occupancy (~5-8% of revenue)
    − Utilities & Supplies (~1-3% of revenue)
    − Marketing & Marketplace Commissions (variable: 0-19%)
    − Shrinkage & Write-offs (target: <2% of revenue)
    − Administrative & Software Costs (~1-2%)
  = EBITDA (target: 5-10% for independent pharmacy in CIS)
    − Depreciation & Amortization
    − Interest Expense (if debt-financed inventory)
    − Taxes
  = Net Profit (target: 3-6% for CIS independent pharmacy; 2-4% US)
```

**Key insight for Provizor:** Blended gross margin target depends on:
1. Share of regulated (OSMS/GOMBP) drugs in revenue → ceiling ~10-15%
2. Share of free-price OTC/supplements → Alimkhan's 30% markup applies
3. Channel mix: offline (0 commission) vs. Halyk (5-19%) vs. Wolt

### KZ Price Regulation Architecture
1. **GOMBP/OSMS drugs** (госреестр): State ceiling price + regressive markup scale. Updated 2× per year. 5,936 SKUs as of 2025.
2. **Free-market drugs**: No price ceiling. Alimkhan's +30% markup rule applies here.
3. **Serialization**: All Rx drugs must be serialized from Jan 1, 2025 (track & trace requirement → impacts 1С integration).
4. **New 2025 rule** (effective July 2025): Maximum retail price = average price in pharmacy chains from IS MPT cash receipt data. Dynamic ceiling rather than fixed — compresses margins further.
5. **FX risk**: 86.6% imported drugs priced in foreign currency; retail prices in tenge. Tenge depreciation (8.6% inflation, 519.7 USD/KZT 2024) creates margin compression pressure on imported SKUs.

### CIS-Specific Metrics Ecosystem
- **УТН (Уровень торговой наценки)**: Realized markup % — core profitability driver in CIS accounting
- **УВД (Уровень валового дохода)**: Gross profit as % of revenue — same concept, alternative term
- **Оборачиваемость в днях**: Turnover in days (ТЗ/S × 30) — more intuitive than annual turns in CIS practice
- **Дефектура**: Stockout/unfulfilled demand — uniquely tracked metric in CIS pharmacy (no English equivalent with same precision)
- **Баланс-прогноз**: CIS-specific tool showing excess/deficit vs. calculated norms in 6 inventory groups
- **1С:Управление аптечной сетью**: Standard ERP for CIS pharmacy chains. Tracks: валовая прибыль, рентабельность продаж, себестоимость, Intercompany схемы, ABC analysis. Directly integrates with госреестр prices.
