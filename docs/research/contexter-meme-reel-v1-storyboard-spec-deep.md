## Layer 1 — Current State: Short Promo Reel Storyboarding Best Practices [CRITICAL]

[PROGRESS] 00:05 UTC — Layer 1 complete.

### 1.1 The 30-Second Format — Empirical Baseline

The 30-second spot remains the agency standard for brand storytelling. Research from the World Advertising Research Center confirms that 30-second spots "effectively tap all three components of a good brand-building television commercial" — problem, solution, emotion. For our 40-second reel (5 × 8 sec), this is the closest empirical anchor.

Key attention benchmarks (sourced from RetentionRabbit 2025 + influencermarketinghub.com 2025):
- **First 2 seconds**: Decision window. Viewers decide to stay or leave.
- **8-15 seconds**: Stanford neuroscience finding — attention allocation is set by second 15.
- **70%+** viewer retention at 30 seconds = benchmark for strong hooks.
- **55%** of viewers lost by the 60-second mark.
- Red Bull's top videos achieve 97% retention at 15 seconds via "hook pattern" — immediate context establishment + pattern interruption.

For our 40-second reel: the hook must fire by second 2, not second 7. The first clip (seconds 0-8) carries disproportionate weight.

### 1.2 Tested 5-Shot Patterns from Agency Practice

The dominant 5-beat framework in agency practice (confirmed across sovran.ai, typeshift.in, and startmotionmedia.com):

**Hook-Body-CTA** (direct response):
1. Hook — attention disruptor (0-8s)
2. Problem context — viewer identifies with situation (8-16s)
3. Solution reveal — product/mascot enters (16-24s)
4. Proof / emotional peak — trust signal (24-32s)
5. CTA — clear invitation (32-40s)

**Curiosity Arc** (brand awareness):
1. Atmospheric tease — environment, world-building (0-8s)
2. Subject approach — camera or character moves toward focal point (8-16s)
3. Reveal — close-up, character identity established (16-24s)
4. Action peak — character performs defining behavior (24-32s)
5. Resolution + CTA — pull back, brand name, soft invite (32-40s)

**Cinematic Teaser** (awareness, festival):
1. Texture/detail close — material, surface, depth (0-8s)
2. Pull back to subject — scale reveal (8-16s)
3. Environment in context — world expands (16-24s)
4. Emotional peak — character's defining moment (24-32s)
5. Brand resolution — wide, quiet, logo (32-40s)

For Meme specifically, the **Curiosity Arc** maps best to the "Quietly Authoritative" TOV and the "memory belongs to you" thesis. The Cinematic Teaser is the strongest alternative for a pure awareness play.

Sources:
- [sovran.ai Hook-Body-CTA framework 2025](https://sovran.ai/blog/hook-body-cta-video-editor-the-ultimate-guide-to-creating-high-converting-video-ads-in-2025)
- [StartMotionMedia — top brand videos 2024](https://www.startmotionmedia.com/top-brand-videos-of-2024-inspiring-cases/)
- [Advids — 30 second advertisement examples](https://advids.co/blog/30-30-second-advertisement-video-examples-to-create-lasting-impression)

### 1.3 Veo-Specific Storyboard Constraints That Override Agency Defaults

The Veo 3.1 production constraint is the 8-second hard ceiling per clip (SEED S-39). This forces the storyboard to treat each clip as an atomic unit with:
- One primary action per clip (SEED S-38 studio distinguisher #2)
- 60% background pixel match between consecutive clips (SEED S-13)
- Copy-paste identity block in every prompt (SEED S-27 continuity table)
- Timestamp-granular audio cues per 2-second window (SEED S-07)

The implication: the 5-beat structure cannot have beats "bleed" between clips. Each beat must complete and resolve within its 8-second clip. This is a constraint that traditional 30-second spots do not have — they allow continuous action across the entire duration.

### 4.1 Traditional 30-Second TV Spot Structure (Most Direct Analog)

Agency-standard formula for 30-second spots (confirmed by Voices.com, Advids, CloudAnimations):
- **0-5s**: Hook — problem or disruption that grabs attention
- **5-15s**: Context — establish world, character, stakes
- **15-25s**: Solution/proof — product or character demonstrates value
- **25-30s**: CTA — logo + single action

For a 40-second reel, this stretches to:
- **0-8s**: Hook / atmosphere
- **8-20s**: Context / approach / reveal
- **20-32s**: Solution / action peak / emotional proof
- **32-40s**: CTA / brand resolution

This maps almost exactly to the Curiosity Arc structure (SEED S-40 Structure A). Validation: the Curiosity Arc IS the cinematic adaptation of the standard 30-second TV spot formula.

### 4.2 Music Video Structure — Scale Contrast as Emotional Rhythm

Music video editing (studied via artlist.io BPM guide + Frontiers neuroscience paper):
- Scale alternation as standard technique: close-up → wide → medium → extreme close → wide
- Cuts on musical beats create "satisfying and memorable" viewer experience (Facebook study: 40% higher completion rates with audio-visual sync)
- 3-5 second shot duration is "the invisible rhythm that holds attention in modern editing"
- BPM math: at 70 BPM with 24fps = 1 beat per 2.06 seconds; at 85 BPM = 1 beat per 1.69 seconds

For our 8-second clips at 70 BPM: approximately 3-4 beat markers per clip. The primary action should occur on beat 2 of the clip (second 2-3), with resolution on beats 3-4 (seconds 4-6), and a trailing "settle" beat at the end (seconds 7-8).

### 4.3 Kinetic Typography — Text as Character

Motion graphics reels (Tendril, Block & Tackle approaches):
- Text overlays used as transition devices, not interruptions
- "Bold, moving typography brings messaging to life by adding energy and rhythm"
- 15-30 seconds is the ideal kinetic typography duration
- Text appears on a visual "rest" moment, not on action (never compete with primary visual event)

For clip 5 (CTA beat): product name "Contexter" should appear on a visual settle moment, not on Meme's action peak. Text fades in after Meme's gesture completes.

### 4.4 Pixar Short Film Grammar — Non-Verbal Mascot

The Luxo Jr. (1986) principles translated directly (SEED S-31-32):
- **Anticipation before action**: every significant Meme movement must have a 0.5-1s setup gesture
- **Follow-through after stop**: eye-dots settle 0.5s after body stops
- **Arc motion over straight lines**: bracket floats in curves, never machines in straight lines
- **Scale as emotion**: Meme can appear larger/smaller relative to archive drawers to signal confidence vs. curiosity

For Veo 3.1 prompting, these translate to exact language constructions — see Layer 6 prompts.

### 4.5 Museum / Gallery Film Language — The Quiet Reveal

Museum exhibit films (seen in MoMA, V&A, Science Museum formats) use:
- Long holds on objects (4+ seconds) at single framing — confidence through stillness
- Atmospheric room tone as primary audio — no music competition with the object
- Slow camera movements to allow viewer eyes to explore (orbital, pedestal, drift)
- No dialogue about what you're seeing — let the object speak

For Meme: clips 1 and 3 can use museum film grammar (slow orbital camera, quiet bioluminescent glow, atmospheric archive hum). This matches the "Quietly Authoritative" TOV pillar.

### 5.2 BPM-to-Cut Math for 70 BPM Score

At 70 BPM (our selected tempo), 24 fps:
- 1 beat = 60/70 = 0.857 seconds
- 1 beat = 0.857 × 24 = 20.57 frames ≈ 20 frames per beat
- 4 beats = 3.43 seconds (half-bar in 4/4)
- 8 beats = 6.86 seconds (full bar in 4/4) ≈ one Veo clip

This means each 8-second Veo clip contains approximately **9.3 beats** = 2.3 bars of 4/4 at 70 BPM. The primary action should align with the first downbeat of bar 2 (beat 5 of the clip, approximately second 4.3).

In practice for DaVinci assembly: import Lyria score → set timeline to 24fps → cut on bar downbeats. At 70 BPM, bar downbeats occur every 34 frames (≈ every 1.43 seconds at 24fps). Mark 9 downbeat markers across 40 seconds.

### 5.3 Audio-Visual Sync Windows

Facebook research (cited in artlist.io + producersociety.com): audio-visual sync improves completion rates by 40% when cuts hit on musical beats.

For Veo 3.1: the primary action within each clip should complete by frame 144 of 192 (second 6 of the 8-second clip), leaving the final 2 seconds for the action to settle and the next clip's continuity frame to be set up. This "settle window" is critical for smooth transitions.

Practical rule: every Veo clip prompt should specify:
- Action starts: second 1-2 of clip (after brief anticipation)
- Action peak: second 3-5
- Action resolution: second 6-7
- Hold/settle: second 7-8 (overlap with next clip's start via J-cut)

### 6.1 Reel Structure Decision

**Decision: Confirm Curiosity Arc (SEED S-40 Structure A) with modifications.**

Justification:
1. **Attention math alignment** (Layer 5): The Curiosity Arc places atmospheric hook at 0-8s (correct for attention curve), reveal at 16-24s (correct for midpoint attention counteraction), and CTA at 32-40s (correct for CTA survival zone).
2. **Traditional TV spot validation** (Layer 4.1): The Curiosity Arc is structurally identical to the agency-standard 30-second formula expanded to 40 seconds. This is not a coincidence — it is the empirically validated structure for short brand films.
3. **TOV alignment**: "Quietly Authoritative" does not lead with a problem/disruption (Hook-Body-CTA). It leads with atmosphere and earns authority through visual quality. The Curiosity Arc's slow build is the correct structure for this voice.
4. **Meme's mascot spec alignment**: The 5-state emotional machine (curiosity → searching → discovery → satisfaction → invitation) from SEED S-33 maps 1:1 onto the Curiosity Arc beats.

**Modification from base S-40 structure**: Clip 1 adds a **pattern interrupt** by second 2 (per Layer 5 hook window). Rather than purely atmospheric wide shot, Meme's bioluminescent glow pulses ONCE in the first 2 seconds, visible even in silhouette. This gives the viewer a visual "hook" while maintaining atmospheric quality.

**Alternative rejected**: Hook-Body-CTA (Structure B) was rejected because: (a) it leads with a problem, which is more aggressive than the TOV calls for; (b) it requires implying user pain (context drift between Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini), which risks feeling like a complaint rather than an invitation; (c) it does not allow the atmospheric archive world to establish itself.

**Cinematic Teaser (Structure C) rejected**: This structure works for pure brand awareness without product context. Our brief requires seeding the "memory belongs to you" thesis, which needs the action beat (Meme interacting with drawers) to be present. A pure texture-reveal structure leaves no room for the thesis beat.

### 6.3 Lyria 3 Score Prompt

**Decision**: Use Lyria 3 Pro (single 40-second track) — confirmed available as of March 25 2026.

**Rationale**: Lyria 3 Clip maximum = 30 seconds. Stitching two 30-second clips requires crossfade management in DaVinci, which introduces a mix seam risk at the critical discovery/action transition (seconds 28-32). Lyria 3 Pro at a single 40-second duration eliminates this risk entirely.

**Access**: Lyria 3 Pro is available via Gemini app (paid subscribers) and Google AI Studio API. Google AI Pro subscription ($19.99/mo) grants access.

**Full Lyria 3 Pro Prompt**

```
Cinematic dark electronic ambient score. Instrumental — no vocals, no voice, no singing. Slow, meditative 70 BPM. Deep archive atmosphere — the mood of a vast ancient library at night.

Instrumentation: sustained synthesizer pads (warm, analog-textured), sparse metallic percussion (sparse, deliberate — like distant metal drawers opening and closing), one sustained cello motif (occasional, not dominant), sub-bass drone throughout, faint resonant bell tones interspersed at key moments.

Mood arc: begins with spacious stillness and atmospheric mystery, builds with growing presence and curiosity, peaks with a warm melodic lift (discovery), resolves into authoritative warmth (satisfaction), ends with an open hopeful single-note resolution (invitation).

[00:00] Begin with sub-bass drone and single sustained synthesizer pad — very quiet, vast, spacious. No melody yet. Atmospheric.
[00:08] Sparse metallic percussion enters — one or two deliberate hits per bar. A sparse synthesizer melodic fragment, 3 notes, descending. Searching feeling.
[00:16] Energy builds — percussion becomes slightly more present, second synthesizer layer enters, cello motif appears briefly. Growing toward lift.
[00:20] Melodic lift moment — full melodic phrase arrives, warm and clear. The discovery. This is the emotional peak. Maximum instrumentation (still sparse by design).
[00:28] Warm resolution — melody resolves downward. Instrumentation reduces. Warm satisfied feeling. Sub-bass remains anchoring.
[00:32] CTA beat — a single clear sustained note, warm and open. Invitation. Very quiet. Lets the visual breathe.
[00:38] Fade — music trails to near-silence, leaving only the sub-bass drone fading.
[00:40] End.

Output: single 40-second instrumental track. WAV format preferred. No loops, no repetitive patterns — this is a linear score that progresses from start to finish.
```

**Selection Rubric (for 5 generated variants)**

Generate 5 variants using the above prompt. Select the variant that best satisfies:

1. **Sub-bass present from second 0** — the archive needs physical presence in the low end
2. **Metallic percussion is SPARSE** — 1-2 hits per bar max, not a driving beat
3. **Melodic lift at second 20 is clearly audible** — this is the musical "discovery" anchor
4. **The bell tones (if present) do not dominate** — they should be incidental, not melodic lead
5. **The [00:32] CTA note is quiet and warm, not triumphant** — avoid any "fanfare" feeling
6. **Overall level balance**: sub-bass -24 to -20dBFS, mid instrumentation -18dBFS, melody peaks at -12dBFS

Disqualify any variant with: audible vocals or voice sounds, dominant percussive drive (sounds like a dance track), reverb so heavy the track sounds "washed out," or melodic lift that sounds triumphant rather than warm-and-clear.

**Fallback if Lyria 3 Pro unavailable**: Use two Lyria 3 Clip generations (30 seconds each), crossfaded at the 28-second mark in DaVinci. Second track prompt: same style, begins mid-phrase (matching energy level of the first track's second 28), continues to resolution and open note at 10 seconds, then fade.

Source for Lyria 3 Pro prompt structure: [Google Cloud Ultimate Prompting Guide for Lyria 3 Pro](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/ultimate-prompting-guide-for-lyria-3-pro) | [Lyria 3 Pro launch announcement](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/ai/lyria-3-pro/)

### 6.5 ElevenLabs Narration Decision

**Decision: NO voiceover narration for the v1 reel.**

**Justification**:

1. **Meme has no mouth** — voiceover narration would create a disembodied voice attributed to a character that visually has no mouth. This creates a logical gap that undermines Meme's design language (bracket X-axis separation = speech, not audio narration).

2. **TOV "Quietly Authoritative" actively opposes narration** — the TOV brief specifies "Sparing / Trustworthy / Spatial." Narration adds words where visual confidence should suffice. Apple's most iconic product reveals use voiceover minimally or not at all in the 30-second format.

3. **Lyria score would compete** — adding narration to a carefully tuned ambient instrumental score creates a mix hierarchy conflict. For a 40-second reel, you can have atmosphere (Lyria) or narration (ElevenLabs), but not both without professional audio mastering that the free DaVinci tier can approximate but not guarantee.

4. **Audience (AI power users) does not need product explanation** — the "memory belongs to you" thesis is communicated via visual metaphor (Meme → archive → open drawer = memory retrieved). The target audience (3+ AI models/day, context-drift aware) will make the connection from visual language alone. Narration is a signal of low trust in the audience's intelligence.

5. **Social media auto-mute behavior** — approximately 85% of social media video is watched without sound initially. A narration-dependent reel fails this test. An atmospheric reel with visual storytelling succeeds mute.

**What to reserve ElevenLabs for**: Optional text-overlay version (captions for muted playback), or a separate 15-second cut with voiceover for a performance ad variant (v2).

**If narration is needed for v1** (per operator override):
- Voice profile: "Adam" (ElevenLabs, male, authoritative, calm) or custom clone
- Script (Russian): *"Контекстер. Ваша память — ваша."* (2.1 seconds, 24 characters)
- Script (English): *"Contexter. Your memory. Yours."* (2.0 seconds)
- Placement: Clip 5, seconds 33-36 (after Meme's invitation gesture, before music fade)
- Duration: 2-3 seconds maximum, then silence

### 6.6 DaVinci Resolve Assembly + Grade Plan

#### Timeline Setup

**Resolution**: 1920×1080 (1080p) — not 4K.
**Justification**: Veo 3.1 Standard outputs 1080p. Attempting to upscale to 4K adds no genuine resolution and increases export file size. Social platforms (Instagram, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn) are optimized for 1080p delivery. Use 1080p natively.

**Frame rate**: 24fps.
**Justification**: 24fps is the cinematic standard. It matches Veo 3.1's output and gives the reel an intentionally filmic quality distinct from 30fps "video" aesthetics. Lyria 3 Pro score generated independently of frame rate — no sync issue.

**Timeline settings**:
- Name: `contexter-meme-reel-v1`
- Resolution: 1920×1080
- Frame rate: 24fps
- Color science: DaVinci Wide Gamut / DaVinci Color Managed
- Output color space: Rec.709 (for social delivery)
- Monitoring: Enable broadcast safe (IRE 0-100)

#### Import and Sync Sequence

1. Import 5 Veo clips (ProRes 422 recommended for lossless quality during grade)
2. Import Lyria 3 Pro WAV score
3. Import ElevenLabs WAV (if used)
4. Create new timeline with above settings
5. Place 5 clips in sequence (C1 → C2 → C3 → C4 → C5) — no overlap yet
6. Place Lyria score on audio Track 3 aligned to frame 0
7. Review: does Lyria's metallic percussion entry (at 8 seconds) align with C2 start? If not, adjust Lyria trim point by ±0.5 seconds

#### Cut Points and Transitions

| Transition | Type | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 → C2 | L-cut (audio bleeds) | Audio: 0.5s overlap; Video: hard cut | Lyria percussion enters before C1 ends — audio continuity bridges the cut |
| C2 → C3 | Hard cut on beat | 0 frames | Cut exactly on Lyria's melodic lift beat. This is the most important timing cut. |
| C3 → C4 | L-cut | Audio: 0.5s overlap; Video: hard cut | Audio match cut to satisfaction beat |
| C4 → C5 | Dissolve | Video: 12 frames (0.5s) | Soft dissolve for pull-back transition — harder cut would interrupt the spatial expansion |
| C5 end | Fade to black | 12 frames | Final resolution fade |

**CTA Text Overlay (Clip 5)**:
- Font: Clean sans-serif (recommend DM Mono or Inter — available in DaVinci)
- Text: `contexter.cc`
- Color: Pure white (#FFFFFF)
- Position: Center-bottom, approximately 20% from frame bottom
- Fade-in: 18 frames (0.75s)
- Hold: 36 frames (1.5s)
- Fade-out: 12 frames (0.5s)
- Do NOT add logo graphic in v1 — URL only

#### Grade Pass (8 Nodes Maximum)

Node tree structure:

```
[Input] → Node 1 → Node 2 → Node 3 → Node 4 → Node 5 → Node 6 → Node 7 → Node 8 → [Output]
```

| Node | Name | Function | Settings |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Base Correction | White balance, exposure normalize | Lift shadows to -0.08, reduce highlights if blown. Each clip may need individual adjustment. |
| 2 | Bronze Warmth | Warm the overall palette | Shadows: R+0.03 G+0.02 B-0.04. Midtones: R+0.02 G+0.01. Highlights: amber-gold push. |
| 3 | HSL Qualifier — Highlights | Pull bronze surfaces to amber-gold | Qualifier: isolate high-lum warm range. Hue: 25-45°. Sat: 40-80%. Push Hue 5° warmer, Sat +10. |
| 4 | Meme Glow Window | Power Window around Meme figure — isolate for glow treatment | Draw Power Window around Meme figure. Apply: Glow effect, Glow Color H185° S80% (cyan-teal). Glow Size: 1.2. Radius: 0.3. This is clip-specific — window must be redrawn or motion-tracked per clip. |
| 5 | Background Depth | Pull background down, increase atmospheric depth | Vignette shape: graduated, bottom-right to upper-center. Darken: lift -0.15. Blue push: B+0.02 in vignette zone only. |
| 6 | Teal-Orange LUT | Apply Teal & Orange style LUT | Import Teal_Orange_CineLook LUT (free from Pixeltoolspost or similar). Apply at 30% intensity via Key > Key Output Gain. Do not over-apply. |
| 7 | Final Saturation + Contrast | Overall image finesse | Saturation: 1.05 (slight boost). Contrast: +0.05. Pivot: 0.45 (protect shadows from harsh contrast). |
| 8 | Broadcast Safe | IRE 0-100 | Enable Legal Range Limit on Output. Clamp: 16-235 (Rec.709). Use Waveform scope to confirm no clipping. |

**Per-clip variation**: Node 4 (Meme Glow Window) must be adjusted per clip because Meme's position changes. Use DaVinci's Magic Mask or manual Power Window keyframing. Clips 3 and 4 (ECU shots) require the window to encompass the larger frame area. Clip 5 (wide pull-back) requires a smaller window.

**Grade order discipline**: Always balance (Node 1) before creative (Nodes 2-7). Never apply LUT (Node 6) before primary correction (Nodes 1-2) or it will fight the base image.

#### Export Settings

**ProRes 422 master** (for archive):
- Format: QuickTime
- Codec: Apple ProRes 422
- Resolution: 1920×1080
- Frame rate: 24fps
- Audio: PCM, 48kHz, stereo

**H.264 delivery** (for social upload):
- Format: MP4
- Codec: H.264
- Bitrate: 20 Mbps CBR (high quality for upload)
- Audio: AAC, 320 kbps, 48kHz stereo
- Resolution: 1920×1080

## Adjacent Findings

**Finding A: Google Vids integration may offer alternative assembly surface**
Google Vids (April 2026) integrates Veo 3.1 + Lyria 3 in a browser-based tool. For operators who find DaVinci Resolve intimidating, Vids offers a simpler assembly surface. However, Vids does not offer node-based color grading — DaVinci remains the only free tool capable of the 8-node bronze grade specified in Section 6.6.

**Finding B: The 5×8 second structure creates a natural vertical/shorts edit**
Each 8-second clip is individually Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts compliant. The 5 clips can be published as individual teasers before the full 40-second reel, each representing one emotional beat. This extends the content footprint without additional production cost.

**Finding C: The CTA text placement decision has downstream brand risk**
The current spec places `contexter.cc` text in Clip 5 at seconds 33-36. This is early enough that viewers who drop off at second 32 (CTA survival rate drop) will still see the URL. However, placing the URL in video frame (not as description text) risks the reel feeling like an ad rather than a brand film. Alternative: no URL in video frame; place CTA entirely in video description and caption. This preserves the cinematic quality of the reel.

**Finding D: Researcher freedom deviation — Lyria 3 Pro is strictly superior to stitched Lyria 3 Clips**
The SEED brief suggested "Lyria 3 Clip (30 sec) for score body + cross-fade to Lyria 3 second clip for CTA." This approach was superseded by Lyria 3 Pro (March 25 2026, launched after the SEED was written). The DEEP investigation found that Lyria 3 Pro's 40-second capability with timestamp control eliminates the cross-fade stitching problem entirely. The storyboard spec uses Lyria 3 Pro throughout. This is a material deviation from the SEED suggestion, justified by newer capabilities.
