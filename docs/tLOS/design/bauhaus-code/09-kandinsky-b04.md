## Die freien Geraden (gleichgewichtslosen) — Continued

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The free (non-schematic) straight lines — diagonal and azentral — carry a special quality: they are neither fully warm nor fully cold, and cannot arrive at equilibrium between warmth and coldness.
**Evidence**: The diagonal is already distinguished from horizontal and vertical by its Temperatur difference. The azentral free lines go furthest — they are most distant from the plane's center, and thus most fully departed from the element of rest (Ruhe). They are "scattered" lines with the loosest relationship to the plane (abgegrenzte Fläche).
**Conclusion**: The azentral lines, being the most remote from the schematic, carry mixed color energies (Gelb and Blau) and exist in a state of tension without resolution. Their behavior on an abgegrenzte Fläche can only be discussed when the Grundfläche section is reached.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Die azentralen freien Geraden / The azentral free straight lines
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The azentral free straight lines possess a special capacity — they bring "bunte" (saturated) colors into a kind of parallel with the "unbunte" (achromatic) colors Schwarz and Weiß. They carry within themselves the tensions of both Vor- and Zurücktreten (advancing and receding). The schematic lines (Horizontal, Vertical, Diagonal) develop their Spannung on the plane and show no tendency to leave it; at the free lines, and especially at the azentral ones, we encounter a looser Verhältnis (relationship) to the plane — they seem sometimes to dissolve through it (durchscheinen).
- **Rule**: Use azentral free lines when the composition requires a quality that is neither advancing nor receding, neither purely warm nor purely cold — the maximum tension of unresolved spatial ambiguity.
- **Source**: p.56
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die azentralen freien Geraden sind die ersten Geraden, die eine spezielle Fähigkeit haben — eine Fähigkeit, die sie in eine gewisse Parallele mit den 'bunten' Farben bringt, die sie von Schwarz und Weiß unterscheidet."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The azentral free straight lines are the first lines that possess a special capacity — a capacity that brings them into a certain parallel with the 'saturated' colors, distinguishing them from Black and White."
- **Category**: line | tension | color

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Azentrale Geraden und die abgegrenzte Fläche / Azentral lines and the bounded plane
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: On the abgegrenzte Fläche (bounded surface), the full behavior of azentral free lines can only be discussed later under Grundfläche. The loose cohesion is only possible when the line, if freed at its borders, does not touch its outer boundaries. The natural Spannungen of the azentral free lines reside in the "bunten" Farben-Elementen and in the connections between the "lyrical" and "dramatic" — which are, to the future Kompositionslehre (theory of composition), of immeasurable importance.
- **Rule**: Reserve analysis of azentral lines on a bounded plane for the Grundfläche section; do not attempt to resolve their behavior prematurely.
- **Source**: p.56
- **Category**: line | plane | composition

## Schwarz und Weiß — Color Properties of Horizontal and Vertical

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: When the schematic lines — Horizontal and Vertical — are tested for their color properties, logic compels a comparison with Schwarz and Weiß.
**Evidence**: Just as these two colors are "Nichtfarben" (non-colors) — silent colors that are dismissed today as "un-bunte" — so too are Horizontal and Vertical the silent lines. Their Klang is reduced to the minimum: silence (Schweigen) or barely audible whispering (Flüstern) and rest (Ruhe). Schwarz and Weiß lie outside the Farbenkreis (color circle). Horizontal and Vertical also occupy a special, isolated position: they are the most cold and most warm lines respectively, but in the extreme — unwiederholbar (irrepeatable), alone and solitary. Together they produce a strong Klang that nothing can fully surpass, and they thus form the Urklang der Geraden (primordial resonance of the straight lines).
**Conclusion**: Schwarz and Weiß are the chromatic equivalents of Horizontal and Vertical. The axis Schwarz ↔ Weiß corresponds to the axis cold ↔ warm in the line domain.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Schwarz = Horizontal / Weiß = Vertikal (Farb-Linien-Entsprechung)
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Horizontal corresponds to Schwarz: both are cold, silent, non-color/non-dynamic. The Vertikal corresponds to Weiß: both are warm, whispering, non-color but active. The two together form the Urklang — the irreducible primordial resonance that no other combination of lines can fully surpass.
- **Rule**: When the maximum cold-warm polarity is required without color, use Horizontal and Vertical together — they form the Urklang der Geraden.
- **Source**: p.56–57
- **Quote (DE)**: "Schwarz und Weiß liegen außerhalb des Farbenkreises. Horizontal und Vertikal nehmen auch einen Sonderplatz unter den Linien ein, da sie in der zentralen Lage unwiederholbar und dadurch einsam sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Black and White lie outside the color circle. Horizontal and Vertical also occupy a special position among the lines, since in their central position they are irrepeatable and thereby solitary."
- **Category**: line | color | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Die horizontale Farbskala / The horizontal color scale
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The horizontal color scale runs from Weiß through Gelb, Rot, Blau to Schwarz (Fig. 21). This is a slow, natural descent from above downward. Weiß and Schwarz are thus the elements of Höhe (height) and Tiefe (depth), enabling a Mit-gehen (accompanying) with Vertikal and Horizontal. The inner meaning: "Heute" (today), the modern human is claimed restlessly from outside and is dead to the inside — this is the last step in the Sackgasse (dead end).
- **Rule**: Map the horizontal color scale (Weiß → Gelb → Rot → Blau → Schwarz) as a compositional axis of descent, not merely as a chromatic sequence.
- **Source**: p.57
- **Category**: color | composition

## Rot — The Diagonal and Primary Color

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Diagonal corresponds to Rot (red) — this is asserted as a claim whose full proof exceeds the scope of this book.
**Evidence**: Rot, combined with Gelb and Blau through its property of lying between Schwarz and Weiß on the surface, forms an intensive, inner Spannung (tension). The Diagonal shows, versus the free lines, a firm Liegen (lying on) the plane; versus Horizontal and Vertical, it shows a greater inner Spannung.
**Conclusion**: The Diagonal is the most internally tense of the schematic lines, and this corresponds to Rot's position as the most intense, most internally concentrated of the primary colors.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Diagonal = Rot (Linien-Farb-Entsprechung)
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Diagonal is parallel to Rot. Rot, through its Eigenschaft of lying between Schwarz and Weiß on the surface, forms an intensive, inner Kochen (boiling) — an internal tension that belongs neither fully to cold nor fully to warm. As the Diagonal is the Unterschied from the free lines (firm on the plane) and the Unterschied from Horizontal/Vertical (greater inner Spannung), so Rot is the intermediate color with the greatest inner pressure.
- **Rule**: Use the Diagonal and Rot when the design requires maximum internal tension without the extremes of hot or cold — an unresolved, active middle state.
- **Source**: p.59
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Parallele Diagonale — Rot ist hier als Behauptung aufgestellt, deren ausführliche Beweise zu weit über das Thema dieses Buches hinausführen würden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The parallel Diagonal — Red is here asserted as a claim whose detailed proofs would lead far beyond the subject of this book."
- **Category**: line | color | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Urklang der Geraden / Primordial resonance of the straight lines
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A resting Punkt (point) placed at the center of a quadratic Fläche (plane) defines the Einklang (unison) of the point and the plane, and the whole image is thus the Urbild des malerischen Ausdrucks (primordial image of painterly expression). The complication of Horizontal and Vertical in central position would produce — as already said — living lines. These two lines, alone and solitary, develop a strong Klang that nothing can fully surpass. They form the Urklang der Geraden.
- **Rule**: The centered point on a quadratic plane is the Urbild — the irreducible zero-point of composition; every addition complicates from this primal unity.
- **Source**: p.59
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der im Zentrum einer quadratischen Fläche ruhende Punkt wurde oben als Einklang des Punktes mit der Fläche definiert, und das gesamte Bild als das Urbild des malerischen Ausdrucks bezeichnet."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The point resting at the center of a quadratic plane was defined above as the unison of the point with the plane, and the whole image was designated as the primordial image of painterly expression."
- **Category**: point | plane | composition | tension

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-23]**: Urchild des linearen Ausdrucks / Primordial image of linear expression
- **Shows**: A square divided into four equal sub-squares by a centered cross of two lines — one Horizontal and one Vertical meeting at the exact center.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: This is the most primitive subdivision of a schematic plane — the Urform (primordial form) of linear composition. The sum of Spannungen: 6 elements of cold Ruhe + 6 elements of warm Ruhe ≈ 12 Klänge. From this comes 12 Klänge der Fläche + 2 Klänge der Linie = 6. The simple tension is doubled.
- **Text reference**: "Diese Konstruktion ist also das Urbild des linearen Ausdrucks oder der linearen Komposition (Fig. 23)." / "This construction is thus the primordial image of linear expression or linear composition."
- **Source**: p.60

## Lyrik und Dramatik in der Linie / Lyric and Dramatic in the Line

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The entire domain of straight lines is lyrical — explicable by the action of a single force from outside. The dramatic arises only when at least two forces act simultaneously.
**Evidence**: The gradual transition from Horizontal (azentralen lines) transforms the cold lyric into an ever-warmer lyric that acquires at its end a certain Beigeschmack des Dramatischen (flavor of the dramatic). The lyrical remains even so — the entire Geradenbereich (domain of the straight line) is lyrical, explicable by the action of a single external force. The dramatic carries, besides the Klang of displacement (in the mentioned cases of azentral lines), also the Klingen of Zusammensein (being together), for which at least two forces are necessary. The effect of two forces on the line domain can go in two ways: (1) the two forces alternate = alternating effect; (2) the two forces act simultaneously = simultaneous effect.
**Conclusion**: The second process is more temperamental and thereby "hotter" — especially when viewed as the result of many releasing forces. Accordingly the purely dramatic line comes into being.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Lyrisch = einzige Kraft / Dramatisch = zwei Kräfte (Linientypologie)
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The lyrical line arises from the action of a single external force. The dramatic line arises from the simultaneous or alternating action of at least two forces. Every straight line is lyrical — even the most "warm" diagonal with its flavor of the dramatic. True dramatic lines require two forces.
- **Rule**: When designing a lyrical element, use straight lines (any type) — they all record the action of one force. When dramatic tension is required, use lines that show the record of two forces — angular lines (Winkellinien) or curves produced by simultaneous forces.
- **Source**: p.61
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Lyrische bleibt aber trotzdem überwiegend — das ganze Gebiet der Geraden ist lyrisch, was sich durch das Einwirken einer einzigen Kraft von außen erklären läßt. Das Dramatische trägt außer dem Klang der Verschiebung noch das Klingen des Zusammenseins, wozu mindestens zwei Kräfte notwendig sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The lyrical nevertheless predominates throughout — the entire domain of straight lines is lyrical, which can be explained by the action of a single force from outside. The dramatic carries, beyond the resonance of displacement, also the resonance of togetherness, for which at least two forces are necessary."
- **Category**: line | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Lineare Übersetzung / Linear translation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every phenomenon of the outer and inner world can receive a linear expression — a kind of Übersetzung (translation). It is advisable to seek the form appropriate to the given lyrical or dramatic case before performing the Übersetzung. The mapping of forces to line types:
  - Punkt → two alternating forces → Eckige Linien (angular lines)
  - Punkt → two simultaneous forces → Gebogene Linien (curved lines)
- **Rule**: Before translating any external phenomenon into linear form, first identify whether its character is lyrical (single force → use straight line) or dramatic (two forces → use angular or curved line).
- **Source**: p.62
- **Quote (DE)**: "Selbstverständlich kann also jede Erscheinung der äußeren und der inneren Welt einen linearen Ausdruck erhalten — eine Art Übersetzung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Of course every phenomenon of the outer and inner world can receive a linear expression — a kind of translation."
- **Category**: line | tension | pedagogy

## I B — Eckige Linien oder Winkellinien / Angular Lines or Angle-Lines

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Angular lines (Winkellinien) are composed of straight lines and are the results of two forces. They belong to Class I (straight-line family) and are placed in subclass B.
**Evidence**: The angular line arises when a Punkt is driven by two forces that have brought their action to a halt after a single impact (Stoß). This produces the essential difference between the straight line and the angular line: at the angle, a much greater Fühlung (feeling/contact) with the Fläche develops, and the angular line already carries something plane-like (Flächenhaftes) within itself. At its formation (Entstehen), the Fläche becomes a bridge (Brücke). The differences between the innumerable angle-lines depend exclusively on the Winkelgröße (angle magnitude), which allows them to be divided into three schematic types plus one free type.
**Conclusion**: The taxonomy of angular lines by angle magnitude yields three schematic and one free type, with the right angle as the coldest, the acute as the hottest, and the obtuse as the most passive.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Winkel-Taxonomie / Angle taxonomy
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Angular lines are classified by the Winkelgröße (angle magnitude) into four types:
  - a) spitzer Winkel (acute angle) — 45°
  - b) rechter Winkel (right angle) — 90°
  - c) stumpfer Winkel (obtuse angle) — 135°
  - d) freier Winkel (free angle) — atypical, either sharper or blunter than the above; a fourth category: die unschematische Eckige (the non-schematic angular line)
- **Rule**: Classify every angular line by its Winkelgröße before assigning it a compositional role — the angle determines the temperature, color correspondence, and Flächenbeziehung (plane relationship).
- **Source**: p.63
- **Category**: line | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Rechter Winkel — kälteste Eckige / Right angle — coldest angular line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The right angle (90°) stands alone in its magnitude and changes only its direction. From touching right angles one can get only 4 — they either touch at their tips and form a cross, or by touching of the diverging sides form right-angled planes — in the most regular case the Quadrat (square). The horizontal-vertical cross consists of a warm and a cold — it is nothing more than the central position of Horizontal and Vertical, and carries therefore the cold-warm Temperatur of the right angle — neither cold nor warm. Hence the cold-warm Temperatur of the right angle depends on its central position of Horizontal and Vertical.
- **Rule**: The right angle produces quadratic planes and the coldest-warmest combination without resolution — use it for compositions requiring balanced thermal stasis.
- **Source**: p.64
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der rechte Winkel steht in seiner Größe einsam und ändert nur seine Richtung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The right angle stands alone in its magnitude and changes only its direction."
- **Category**: line | tension | color

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Absoluter Klang der Eckigen / Absolute resonance of angular lines
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The absolute Klang of a given angular form depends on three conditions:
  1. Klang of the lines with the mentioned changes (Fig. 25)
  2. Klang of the inclination toward a more or less acute Spannung (Fig. 26)
  3. Klang of the inclination toward smaller or larger conquest of the Fläche (Fig. 27)
- **Rule**: To determine the absolute Klang of any angular line, analyze all three factors simultaneously — arm lengths, angle acuity, and plane-conquest tendency.
- **Source**: p.64
- **Category**: line | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Drei Klänge der drei Winkel / Three resonances of the three angles
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: These three Klänge of the three schematic angles correspond (korrespondieren) with three different Klänge of three forms:
  1. das Kalte und Beherrschte (the cold and controlled) — right angle
  2. das Scharfe und Höchstaktive (the sharp and maximally active) — acute angle
  3. das Unbeholfene, Schwache und Passive (the clumsy, weak, and passive) — obtuse angle
  These three Klänge translate into three Klänge of artistic creation:
  1. das Scharfe und Höchstaktive des inneren Gedankens (Vision)
  2. das Kühle und Beherrschte der meisterhaften Ausführung (Verwirklichung)
  3. das unbefriedigte Gefühl und die Empfindung eigener Schwäche nach vollendeter Arbeit (bei Künstlern "Kater" genannt)
- **Rule**: Map the three angles to the three phases of artistic work — the acute to vision/idea, the right to controlled execution, the obtuse to the post-completion Kater (hangover).
- **Source**: p.66
- **Category**: line | tension | pedagogy

## Eckige und Farbe / Angular Lines and Color — The Angle-Color System

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The three schematic angular lines correspond to the three primary colors: acute angle → Gelb; right angle → Rot; obtuse angle → Blau.
**Evidence**: The Kältewärme (cold-warmth) character of the right angle corresponds to Rot, which combines Gelb and Blau — a Mittelstufe (intermediate level) between the two. The Gelb and Blau of the angles are Wegweiser (signposts) — the more a line approaches acuity, the more it approaches Gelb's warmth; the more it approaches obtuseness (150°), a typically blue obtuse angle emerges, and the passive quality of the stumpfen Winkels, its almost absent Spannung, gives this Winkel a light blue coloring. The acute angle (60°) with two such openings placed together creates an equilateral triangle — the sharpest, most active angles → Gelb.
**Conclusion**: The angle-color system forms a schematic mapping: acute → Gelb, right → Rot, obtuse → Blau. This connects directly to the form-color system: Triangle → Gelb, Square → Rot, Circle → Blau.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Winkel-Farb-Entsprechung / Angle-color correspondence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The angle-color system maps as follows:
  - Spitzer Winkel (acute 45°) → Gelb (yellow): aggressive, warm, sharp
  - Rechter Winkel (right 90°) → Rot (red): balanced, intermediate, controlled
  - Stumpfer Winkel (obtuse 135°) → Blau (blue): passive, cool, weak
  The temperature gradient: the more acute → the warmer (→ Gelb); the more obtuse (→ 180°) → the cooler (→ Blau). At 180° the obtuse angle becomes a straight line (Gerade), which is the precursor of the Gebogenen (curves) and will, in the further course, lead to the Circle.
- **Rule**: Assign color to angular elements by angle magnitude: acute = yellow, right = red, obtuse = blue. For intermediate angles, interpolate accordingly.
- **Source**: p.66–67, 68
- **Quote (DE)**: "So ist der spitze Winkel innerlich gelb gefärbt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Thus the acute angle is internally colored yellow."
- **Category**: line | color | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Fläche-Form-Farbe Entsprechung / Plane-form-color correspondence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The schematic mapping between line angles, primary forms (Flächen), and primary colors:
  - Fläche und Farbe / Spitzer Winkel → Dreieck (triangle) → Gelb
  - Rechter Winkel → Quadrat (square) → Rot
  - Stumpfer Winkel → Kreis (circle) → Blau
  These three typical angles develop, in their further evolution as forms (Flächen), into the three primary forms. Thus the schematic significance of linear-Fläche-color correspondences can be set up in a table.
- **Rule**: In any composition, treat Triangle/Yellow, Square/Red, and Circle/Blue as formal-chromatic unities — their combination effects multiply their individual Klänge.
- **Source**: p.68
- **Category**: line | plane | color | composition

## Linie und Farbe — Die Komponenten-Tabelle / Line and Color — The Components Table

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: When the full line-color-temperature correspondences are assembled, a table emerges that reveals organic (non-additive) relationships between components.
**Evidence**: The Klänge and properties of the components yield, in individual cases, Summen (sums) from the Fläche — which are in some cases not covered by the individual components. Similar facts are known in chemistry, where a sum of components sometimes yields properties not derivable from the components alone. The table:
- Linie / Farbe / in bezug auf Temperatur und Licht:
  - Horizontale = Schwarz = Blau
  - Vertikale = Weiß = Gelb
  - Diagonale = Grau, Grün = Rot
- Fläche (Komponenten / dritte Primärfarbe):
  - Dreieck: Horiz. = Schwarz/Blau, Diagon. = Rot → Gelb (Summe)
  - Quadrat: Horiz. = Schwarz/Blau, Vert. = Weiß/Gelb → Rot (Summe)
  - Kreis²: Spannungen (aktive = Gelb, passive = Rot) → Blau (Summe)
**Conclusion**: The "organic" (non-arithmetic) correspondences in the elements of painting are difficult to prove — the Unmöglichkeit of proving identical identities — but these signs (→) indicate inner parallels, not equations.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Linie-Fläche-Farbe Tabelle / Line-Plane-Color Table
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The full system of line-form-color organic correspondences:
  - Horizontal ↔ Schwarz ↔ Blau (temperature: cold)
  - Vertikal ↔ Weiß ↔ Gelb (temperature: warm)
  - Diagonale ↔ Grau/Grün ↔ Rot (temperature: intermediate)
  - Dreieck (Horiz. + Diag.) → Gelb (third primary, sum)
  - Quadrat (Horiz. + Vert.) → Rot (third primary, sum)
  - Kreis (aktive Spannungen = Gelb, passive = Rot) → Blau (third primary, sum)
  These are not equations but inner parallels (Parallelen) — the signs → indicate organic correspondence, not identity.
- **Rule**: Use the full correspondence table as a compositional guide, not a mechanical formula — the relationships are organic, not additive. The sum of components can exceed their parts.
- **Source**: p.69–70
- **Quote (DE)**: "'Organische' Zusammenhänge in den Elementen der Malerei zu deuten, ist meine Aufgabe. Auch in Fällen der Unmöglichkeit Identitäten zu beweisen, d. h. sie restlos zu begründen, will ich mit Aufstellungen von zwei Wegweisern die inneren Beziehungen andeuten →."
- **Quote (EN)**: "'Organic' correspondences in the elements of painting to indicate — that is my task. Even in cases where it is impossible to prove identities, i.e. to ground them completely, I will indicate inner relationships with two signposts →."
- **Category**: line | plane | color | composition | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Gleichgewicht der Komponenten / Equilibrium of components
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The sum lacking for Gleichgewicht (equilibrium) would be supplied by the components. Artistic practice supports this alleged rule: black-and-white painting uses lines and points (Linien und Punkte) from the Fläche (i.e., from the plane's components) — adding a more eye-catching Gleichgewicht — heavier elements placed against lighter ones. In colorful painting this Notwendigkeit is observed to an even greater degree. The missing piece (fehlendes Glied) in the component equilibrium is what drives the eye to supply it.
- **Rule**: Calculate the color-balance of any composition through its components — where a Gleichgewicht is missing, the eye will supply it; design consciously toward or against this tendency.
- **Source**: p.70
- **Category**: color | composition | tension

## Methode / Method — International Art Institutes

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The goal of the analytical observations above is not to establish rigid rules but to find a more exact, more objective path — one where collective work in art science becomes possible.
**Evidence**: The methods of Kunstanalyse (art analysis) are until now too arbitrary and personal. The coming era pushes toward a more exact and objective path, on which collective work is made possible. Talents and gifts remain, as everywhere, different — but each can work to the extent of his/her forces, and this is therefore one of several acknowledged work areas requiring special importance. Here the idea of internationally organized art institutes surfaces — an idea being realized in various countries. Without excessive generalization one can claim that a broadly based art science with international character must be: interesting but certainly not sufficient — a European art theory is not enough. Not only geographical and other external conditions are important, but the differences in the inner content of "Nations" are on the art domain primarily decisive.
**Conclusion**: A Konsonanz (consonance), not a Dissonanz (dissonance), arises from Nations' different inner contents — provided the investigation is planned and organized. The realization of internationally organized art institutes can serve as an introduction to this.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Objektive Kunstwissenschaft / Objective art science
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The methods of Kunstanalyse are until now too arbitrary and personal. The goal of these analytical considerations is to find a more exact, objective path, on which collective work in art science is possible. Planned, exact experiments in the construction of works can be made on this path — the "böse Nebel" (evil fog) in which we wander today in laboratory art work will unconditionally become somewhat more transparent and less suffocating.
- **Rule**: Approach design analysis as a science: use planned experiments, seek collective validation, treat personal responses as data points rather than final authorities.
- **Source**: p.70
- **Quote (DE)**: "Nur auf diesem Wege können planmäßige, exakte Experimente in der Konstruktion von Werken gemacht werden, und der böse Nebel, in dem wir wandern wie heute bei den laboratorischen Kunstarbeiten, wird unbedingt etwas durchsichtiger und weniger erstickend wirken."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Only in this way can systematic, exact experiments in the construction of works be made, and the evil fog in which we wander today in laboratory art work will unconditionally become somewhat more transparent and less suffocating."
- **Category**: pedagogy | inner-necessity

## Kompliziertere Eckigen / More Complex Angular Lines

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Complex angular lines arise when simple Eckigen are multiplied (through Kombinationen of acute, right, obtuse, and free angles) and multiple Bruchteile (segments) of varying lengths join in multiple directions.
**Evidence**: A complex (mehreckige) line consists of multiple segments of equal length with right angles between them — forming a staircase or zigzag. The Linien werden auch Zickzacklinien genannt (are also called zigzag lines) and form with equal segments a moving Gerade (moving straight line). Pointed segments point to the Vertikale, obtuse ones lean to the Horizontale — stumpy angles always keep a tilt to the Horizontale. When the power of the obtuse angle consistently increases and the angle grows larger, this form gains a drive toward the Fläche and especially toward the Kreis.
**Conclusion**: The Verwandtschaft (kinship) of the obtuse line to the Gebogenen (curved lines) and to the Kreis is not external but internal: the passivity of the stumpfen Winkels, his non-combative relationship to the surroundings, his tendency toward deeper self-intensification — in the highest Selbstvertiefung (self-deepening) of the circle ends.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Zickzacklinie als bewegte Gerade / Zigzag line as moving straight line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Complex angular lines with equal segments and equal angles form a Zickzacklinie (zigzag line) and are also called Zickzacklinien. They form with equal segments a moving Gerade — spitze segments point to the Vertikale, stumpfe segments lean to the Horizontale. These lines are thus a moving straight line with an oscillating character.
- **Rule**: Treat zigzag lines as moving-straight-line forms, not as purely angular forms — their character is determined by the dominant angle type (acute → vertical tendency, obtuse → horizontal tendency).
- **Source**: p.72–73
- **Category**: line | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Verwandtschaft von Stumpfem Winkel und Kreis / Kinship of obtuse angle and circle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: When the power of the stumpfen Winkels consistently increases and the angle grows larger, this form gains a drive toward the Fläche and especially toward the Kreis. The Verwandtschaft of the stumpfen Linie to the Gebogenen and the Kreis is not external but internal: the passivity of the stumpfen Winkels, his non-combative Verhältnis (relationship) to the surroundings, his drive toward deeper Vertiefungen (intensifications) — in the highest Selbstvertiefung (self-deepening) of the Kreis ends.
- **Rule**: When composing with obtuse angles, recognize their inherent tendency toward the circle — they are formal precursors of circular energy, not independent forms.
- **Source**: p.73
- **Category**: line | plane | tension

## II — Gebogene Linien / Curved Lines

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: When two forces act simultaneously on a point — one continuously pushing, one continuously deflecting — the result is a curved line. This is the fundamental type (Grundtyp) of the curved line.
**Evidence**: The curved line (Gebogene) is essentially a straight line (Gerade) that has been deflected by a continuous lateral pressure (ständigen Seitendruck) from its path — the greater the Seitendruck, the further it deviates from the straight, and the greater the Spannung grows toward the outside (nach außen), until at the end it tends toward Sichselbstabschließen (self-enclosure). The inner difference between Gerade and Gebogene consists in the number and type of Spannungen: the Gerade has two clear primitive Spannungen that play an inessential role in the Gebogenen — the main Spannung (third Spannung) lies in the arc (Bogen). While the Winkel steckt etwas Unüberlegtes Jugendliches im Bogen (the angle contains something unreflecting/youthful in the arc) — a ripeness, with Rot self-consciously confident. At the ripeness and the elastic full-Klang of the Gebogenen we are prompted to find not in Eckigen but in determined Gebogenen.
**Conclusion**: Die Gerade und die Gebogene form the originally-opposed line pair (ursprünglich-gegensätzliches Linienpaar) — the fundamental polarity of the entire line domain.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-20]**: Definition der Gebogenen / Definition of the curved line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The curved line arises when two forces act simultaneously on a point: one continuously propelling force and one continuously deflecting lateral force. The Gebogene is the simplest curved line (einfache Gebogene). The inner difference from the Gerade: in the Gerade, two clear primitive Spannungen play a role that the Gebogene renders inessential — the main Spannung of the Gebogene is the arc itself (third Spannung), which while the angle contains something unreflecting-youthful, the arc contains a ripeness with self-confident Rot energy.
- **Rule**: Use curved lines when the design calls for a force that is simultaneously propelled and deflected — not the single force of a straight line, nor the alternating two forces of an angular line, but two truly simultaneous forces.
- **Source**: p.73
- **Quote (DE)**: "Wenn zwei Kräfte gleichzeitig ihre Wirkungen auf den Punkt ausüben, und zwar so, daß eine Kraft fortlaufend und stets in demselben Maße die andere an Druck überbietet, so entsteht eine gebogene Linie, mit ihrem Grundtyp der einfachen Gebogenen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "When two forces simultaneously exert their effects on a point, in such a way that one force continuously and always in the same measure surpasses the other in pressure, there arises a curved line, with its fundamental type: the simple curved line."
- **Category**: line | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-21]**: Gegensatz Gerade / Gebogene — das ursprünglich-gegensätzliche Linienpaar
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Die Gerade und die Gebogene are the originally-opposed line pair (ursprünglich-gegensätzliches Linienpaar). In the Gebogenen, the character of the Geraden — the full absence of the Geraden — compels submission (Beugung). The Gerade and the Gebogene form the most fundamental polarity in the domain of lines. Their pairing (Fig. 35) is the basis from which all line complexity develops.
- **Rule**: In any compositional analysis, identify whether the dominant line energy is Gerade or Gebogene — this is the primary polarity from which all other line relationships are derived.
- **Source**: p.74
- **Quote (DE)**: "die Gerade und die Gebogene bilden das ursprünglich-gegensätzliche Linienpaar."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the straight line and the curved line form the originally-opposed line pair."
- **Category**: line | tension | composition
