## Oben und Unten — Top and Bottom of the Basic Plane

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The top and bottom of the Grundfläche are not equivalent positions; "above" (oben) carries the quality of becoming and freedom, while "below" (unten) carries the quality of effort and resistance.
**Evidence**: Analysis of a curved form (Figs. 89–90) shows that placing the same form with its heavier mass toward the top yields a "stubborn" (hartnäckig) character with loose bends and weak resistance from the right, while placing the denser layer on the upper side under greater tension produces tighter bends and strong resistance from the right with a looser "air" below.
**Conclusion**: Every element placed on the GF acquires a different inner Klang depending on its vertical position; this is not metaphor but a measurable compositional law.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Oben / Unten — above / below on the Grundfläche
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The upper region of the GF is associated with Werdende ("becoming"), movement, and looseness; the lower region is associated with Mühsame ("the laborious"), resistance, and compression. A form placed in the upper zone enters the zone of freedom; a form placed in the lower zone meets thickening resistance.
- **Rule**: When placing a form on the GF, treat vertical position as an active compositional variable — the same form reads differently at top vs. bottom; never assume the plane is uniform.
- **Source**: p.129
- **Quote (DE)**: "In der Wendung nach links kommt mehr das Werdende zur Geltung, nach rechts — mehr das Mühsame."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Turning to the left brings more of the becoming into play; turning to the right — more of the laborious."
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Verdichtete Schicht / Konzentriertes Klingen — dense layer / concentrated resonance
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: As a form approaches the upper border of the GF (from below), it gains increasing tension (Spannung) until the moment of actual contact, at which point the tension abruptly ceases. Concentration disappears and everything becomes flat (flach).
- **Rule**: Use nearness to the GF border to build tension; allow contact or crossing only when the abrupt release of tension is the desired compositional effect.
- **Source**: p.129
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

## Fläche auf Fläche — Plane-Form on the Basic Plane

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: When a plane-form (Fläche) is placed on the GF, it now carries the inner Spannung of the GF rather than acting as a mere line boundary — its role is qualitatively different from a line on the GF.
**Evidence**: The example of a normally shifted (verschobenes) square on the GF is examined. Two variants are shown: a square shifted so that its sides run parallel to the lyrical inner quality of the GF (Fig. 91) vs. a square shifted so its sides become the inner parallel of the dramatic Klang (Fig. 92).
**Conclusion**: The relationship between a plane-form and the GF is governed by the parallel or non-parallel alignment of the form's sides to the GF's borders — harmonic alignment → lyrical Klang; counter-alignment → dramatic Klang.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Innere Parallele — inner parallel alignment between form and GF
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A plane-form whose sides run parallel to the GF borders participates in the lyrical ("harmonischen") inner Spannung of the GF. A plane-form whose sides run counter to or diverge from the GF borders produces dramatic ("disharmonischen") Spannung — it sets itself against the GF rather than merging with it.
- **Rule**: Use parallel alignment of form edges to GF borders for lyrical, calm composition; use tilted, divergent alignment for dramatic, tense composition.
- **Source**: p.130
- **Quote (DE)**: "Innere Parallele lyrischen Klanges. Mitgehen mit der inneren 'disharmonischen' Spannung." / "Innere Parallele dramatischen Klanges. Gegensatz zu der inneren 'harmonischen' Spannung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Inner parallel of lyrical resonance. Going along with the inner 'disharmonic' tension." / "Inner parallel of dramatic resonance. Opposition to the inner 'harmonic' tension."
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Entfernung von den GF-Grenzen — distance from the GF borders
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The distance of a form from the GF border is a special and very important role. A simple straight line of constant length placed near or far from the border creates two different types of effect on the GF (Figs. 93–94). As a form approaches the border, it gains increasing dramatic tension (Spannung nach oben) while the lower tension decreases. Upon touching the border, the tension snaps violently upward and becomes enlarged — even something sickly or desperate (Verzweifeltes) in expression.
- **Rule**: Proximity to the border amplifies tension dramatically; reserve border contact for maximum dramatic effect and use it as a last resort rather than a default position.
- **Source**: p.130–131
- **Quote (DE)**: "Im zweiten Falle stößt sie an die Grenze und verliert sofort dadurch ihre Spannung nach oben, wobei sich die Spannung nach unten vergrößert und etwas Krankhaftes, fast Verzweifeltes zum Ausdruck bringt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the second case it collides with the border and immediately loses its upward tension, while the tension below grows and brings something morbid, almost desperate, to expression."
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Lyrik und Dramatik der Komposition — lyrical and dramatic qualities
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The further a form lies from the GF border, the more it takes on the tension from the GF center — the nearer to the border, the more it takes on GF border tension. Forms near the border heighten the dramatic Klang of the construction; forms near the center bestow a lyrical Klang. These are schematic rules but always operative, even when dampened to near-inaudibility by other means.
- **Rule**: Map center-proximity → lyrical register; border-proximity → dramatic register. Use this axis consciously in every compositional placement decision.
- **Source**: p.131
- **Quote (DE)**: "die nahe an den Grenzen der GF liegenden Formen erhöhen den 'dramatischen' Klang der Konstruktion, wogegen die entfernt von der Grenze liegenden, sich mehr um das Zentrum sammelnden Formen der Konstruktion einen 'lyrischen' Klang verleihen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "forms lying close to the GF borders heighten the 'dramatic' resonance of the construction, whereas forms lying far from the border, gathering more around the center, lend the construction a 'lyrical' resonance."
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition | inner-necessity

## Verwendung der Exzentrik — Use of Eccentricity

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Eccentric (azentral) arrangement of elements is a specific compositional tool for amplifying dramatic quality.
**Evidence**: Four diagrams illustrate eccentricity and centricity in combination with horizontal/vertical vs. diagonal axial structure (Figs. 95–98). Schweigendes Lyrik ("silent lyricism") uses only a few elementary lines in stiffened expression (Fig. 95). Dramatization of the same elements through complication amplifies polarizing expression (Fig. 96). Diagonal + central axes under maximum Spannung (Fig. 97). All-eccentric arrangement where diagonals are reinforced by repetition and dramatic border-contact points appear at top (Fig. 98).
**Conclusion**: The azentral (eccentric) arrangement with diagonal axis maximizes dramatic Klang; the zentral (concentric) arrangement with horizontal/vertical axes maximizes lyrical Klang.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Schweigendes Lyrik / Dramatisierung — silent lyricism vs. dramatization
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The same elementary lines can produce either schweigendes Lyrik (silent/frozen lyricism) or full dramatization depending on the number of elements and their organizational principle. Lyrical = few, elementary, central-axial. Dramatic = complex, multiplied, polarized.
- **Rule**: To shift a composition from lyrical to dramatic: multiply elements, add diagonals, move forms toward borders, and break central symmetry. To shift from dramatic to lyrical: reduce, centralize, regularize.
- **Source**: p.132
- **Category**: composition | tension | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Diagonale + Azentrismus — diagonal axis + eccentricity
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The diagonal axis and azentral structure are paired tools: diagonal axis provides the directional tension (neither cold-horizontal nor warm-vertical), and azentral arrangement removes equilibrium. Together they produce maximum dramatic resonance. In Fig. 97: diagonal central, horizontal/vertical axial tensions exhausted (Abgewogene Spannungen) = high Spannung. In Fig. 98: all elements eccentric, diagonal reinforced by repetition, dramatic border-contact at top.
- **Rule**: When maximum dramatic effect is required, combine diagonal axial organization with eccentric (off-center) placement of all elements.
- **Source**: p.132
- **Category**: composition | tension | plane

## Vermehrung der Klänge — Multiplication of Resonances

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Replacing straight elements with curved ones multiplies the sum of tensions by the factor of a triangle (Dreifache), since each curve contains two tensions instead of one (as two lines meeting at a point).
**Evidence**: If simple curves replace the straight lines in the previous examples, the sum of Klänge increases as described — each curved element generates the tension equivalent of two meeting straight lines. Further addition of simple curves with their three tensions multiplies the total further. The GF's relationships at its borders become louder (lauter) or weaker (schwächer) depending on the interval arrangements chosen.
**Conclusion**: Curve density is a direct amplifier of total compositional tension; the formula is not aesthetic but arithmetic.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Kurve als Spannungsmultiplikator — curve as tension multiplier
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A simple curve carries two tensions simultaneously (as if at an imaginary meeting point of two straight lines). Replacing a straight element with a curve therefore increases the sum of tensions by the factor of a triangle — three tensions instead of one. Further curves multiply again. This is not a matter of "expressiveness" but of formal arithmetic.
- **Rule**: Use curves when the overall tension budget of a composition needs to be increased without adding new elements; substituting even one straight line with a curve triples the tension load at that position.
- **Source**: p.133
- **Category**: line | tension | composition

## Gesetzmäßigkeit des Verhaltens der Flächen zur GF — Laws Governing Plane-Forms on the GF

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The behavior of plane-forms on the GF follows lawful regularities (Gesetzmäßigkeiten) that can be formulated and tested.
**Evidence**: So far only rectangular GF have been discussed. The results of the horizontal equilibrium (cold ruling dominance of the upper border, or the warm vertical ruling) are determined by the GF's fundamental temperament before any element is placed. The square GF is taken as the reference case: in one orientation the cold dominates (Hochformat), in the other the warm balance is reversed (Querformat). The square Grundfläche contains two Strebende (upward-aspiring) and two Langestreckte (extended horizontal) as antipodes.
**Conclusion**: The GF's own format (Hochformat vs. Querformat, square vs. rectangular) is not a neutral container but a pre-compositional determination of temperature and Spannung distribution.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Hochformat vs. Querformat — portrait vs. landscape format
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In the Hochformat (portrait/tall format), the upper border is longer than the side borders, so the elements gain more freedom toward "Freiheit" ("freedom"), but this is dampened again by the shortness of the side borders. In the Querformat (landscape/wide format), the reverse holds. The Quadrat (square format) balances these forces most completely but also subordinates left and right to the horizontal equilibrium of top and bottom in the least differentiated way.
- **Rule**: Choose format before composition — Hochformat gives vertical freedom tempered by horizontal constraint; Querformat gives horizontal extension; square gives maximum balance but also maximum compression of all four directional tensions.
- **Source**: p.133–134
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Quadratische GF — the square basic plane
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The square GF gives the clearest (reinsten) and most self-sufficient case of the overweighing of the horizontal balance, or of the vertical one. The dominant drive "nach oben" (upward) of the square disappears and is replaced by a one-sided tension across the whole plane — the Objektive des Quadrates. Left and right become much more dependent than in the rectangle. The GF's elements all influence each other — more or less audibly.
- **Rule**: Use the square format when the goal is maximum compositional autonomy and self-referentiality — the square GF makes the least appeal to external coordinates.
- **Source**: p.133
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

## Weitere GF-Formen — Further GF Forms: Angle Variations

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Varying the angles of the GF (using acute or obtuse corners, or combining promoting and retarding elements) expands the compositional vocabulary of the GF itself.
**Evidence**: Further variations of the GF are possible using acute and obtuse angles in various combinations (Fig. 99: a mixed GF with both promoting/fördernd and retarding elements, shown as a dotted/irregular outline; Fig. 100: a complex polygonal GF).
**Conclusion**: The GF need not be rectangular; angle variation in the GF itself introduces the same Spannung laws that operate for elements within the GF.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Verschiedene Winkel auf der GF — angle variation in the GF border
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: By varying the angles of the GF itself — using acute angles that are "promoting" (fördernd) vs. obtuse angles that are "retarding" (hemmend) — the designer can embed directional tensions directly into the container before any element is placed. A right angle placed on the GF acts as either promoting or retarding depending on whether its angle is presented as confronting the element or yielding.
- **Rule**: Treat the format shape itself as a compositional decision — non-rectangular formats pre-code directional tensions that interact with every element placed inside.
- **Source**: p.134
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

## Kreisform — The Circular GF

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The circle is the most complex and at the same time the simplest of the three primary GF forms (triangle, square, circle), because it has no angles and therefore the concept of top/bottom/left/right loses its grip entirely.
**Evidence**: As the angles of a polygon multiply and become progressively more obtuse, the form becomes simpler (einfacher) and at the same time more complex (komplizierter). The circle is the final result of this process: it lacks all angles and therefore lacks the clear top/bottom/left/right of the square — all four distinctions (1:4, 2:3 in Kandinsky's numbering scheme via Puschkin's number theory diagram, Fig. 101) are equalized. The circle's tension is concentric (konzentrisch), pressing toward its own center; the square's tension is eccentric (exzentrisch), pressing outward to its corners.
**Conclusion**: The three primary GF forms — Dreieck, Quadrat, Kreis — are natural products of the regularly moving Punkt, geometrically derivable from the movement of a point through the two diagonals and two axes of the circle (Fig. 102, referencing A.S. Puschkin).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Kreisform — the circle as GF
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The circular GF is the most extreme case: angles vanish completely, top/bottom/left/right differences are nearly erased, and the concentric tension (konzentrische Spannung) replaces all directional tensions. The circle's composition is self-referring — elements placed inside a circular GF are drawn toward the center rather than pushed toward corners or borders.
- **Rule**: Use circular format when concentric, centripetal tension is compositionally intended — the circle removes directional hierarchy and centers all resonance inward.
- **Source**: p.135
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Dreieck, Quadrat, Kreis als Ur-GF — triangle, square, circle as primary GF forms
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The three primary GF forms are natural (natürliche Erzeugnisse) products of the regularly moving Punkt. When two diagonals of a circle are drawn from its center to its circumference, a square results at their intersections (ABCD); including the intersections with horizontal and vertical axes adds more forms. From this geometric derivation, Kandinsky connects these forms to the roots of number systems (Arabic and Roman, referencing A.S. Puschkin, Petersburg 1855) — visual forms and number systems share a common geometric root.
- **Rule**: Treat triangle, square, and circle as the three irreducible formal categories; every other form can be analyzed as a modification or combination of these three.
- **Source**: p.136
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die drei Grundflächen — Dreieck, Quadrat, Kreis — sind natürliche Erzeugnisse des sich planmäßig bewegenden Punktes."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The three basic planes — triangle, square, circle — are natural products of the regularly moving point."
- **Category**: plane | point | composition

## Ovalform und Freie Formen — Oval and Free Forms

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Beyond the three primary GF forms, oval and free (irregular) forms can serve as GF; they are merely more complex cases of the same Spannung laws, and the principles already derived remain valid.
**Evidence**: The oval and other angle-free forms are noted as being "winkellos" (without angles) like the circle but without its regularity. The principles governing them do not change — they remain subject to the same Spannung laws. The move to free forms is described as offering even more possibilities of "Freiheit" (freedom) for elements while still following the general laws.
**Conclusion**: Mastery of the three primary forms enables understanding of all derived forms, since the laws remain unchanged.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Freie GF-Formen — free GF forms follow the same laws
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Oval and free (irregular) forms as GF are extensions of the same Spannung system. The absence of clear angles means the directional distinctions of the rectangular GF are diffused, but not eliminated. The same laws governing top/bottom, border proximity, and center tension continue to operate, merely in more complex and less schematic form.
- **Rule**: Do not abandon the analytical framework when working with irregular formats — apply the same tension analysis, recognizing that the results will be more graduated and less clearly bipolar.
- **Source**: p.137
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

## Faktur — Texture/Facture of the GF

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The GF is material and emerges from a purely material process of manufacture (Herstellung); the Faktur (texture/facture) of its surface — smooth, rough, grainy, shiny, matte — is not a neutral background but an active compositional force that either runs parallel to the inner tensions of the elements or stands in opposition to them.
**Evidence**: The available Faktur-types are enumerated: glatt (smooth), rau (rough), körnig (grainy), glänzend (shiny), matt (matte), and plastische Oberfläche (relief/plastic surface). The Faktur acts on the GF's inner workings in two ways: (1) it strikes a parallel path with the elements, supporting their Klang, or (2) it is used as contrast, standing in external contradiction with the elements and supporting them in an inner way.
**Conclusion**: Faktur is a compositional medium, not a technical irrelevance. Its effects appear in the GF both in isolation and in combination with elements.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Faktur als Kompositionsmittel — facture as a compositional medium
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Faktur of the GF is not merely a consequence of material selection — it is an active compositional variable. It operates in two modes: (1) parallel to the elements — runs with them, supporting their inner Klang; (2) as Gegensatz (contrast) — used in external contradiction to the elements but supporting them inwardly. Between these two poles lie the Variationsmöglichkeiten (variation possibilities).
- **Rule**: Explicitly decide the Faktur relationship to the elements: parallel (harmonizing) or contrastive (polarizing). Never treat Faktur as a default or as neutral.
- **Source**: p.137–138
- **Quote (DE)**: "die Faktur schlägt mit den Elementen einen parallelen Weg ein und unterstützt sie dadurch überwiegend äußerlich, oder sie wird im Prinzip des Gegensatzes verwendet, d. h. sie steht mit den Elementen in einem äußeren Widerspruch und unterstützt sie innerlich."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The facture strikes a parallel path with the elements and thereby supports them predominantly externally, or it is used on the principle of contrast, i.e., it stands in external contradiction with the elements and supports them internally."
- **Category**: plane | composition | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Faktur und Material — facture and material relationship
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Faktur properties of the GF surface are naturally and necessarily derived from the material and the means of its treatment (canvas, paper, stone, glass, etc.) and from the tools connected with the material. Faktur is thus a precise, elastic, flexible means — schematically, it leans in two directions: (1) it runs a parallel path with the elements, or (2) as Gegensatz. Variation possibilities lie between these poles.
- **Rule**: Select GF material with awareness of its Faktur properties and their directional relation to the planned elements — do not select material for practical convenience alone.
- **Source**: p.138
- **Category**: plane | composition

## Dematerialisierte Fläche — The Dematerialized Plane

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The fixed (material) position of elements on the more or less hard GF, and the contrasting "floating" elements in the undefined (immaterial) space, are fundamentally, antipodally different appearances. The generally materialist standpoint — which naturally extends to the arts — has had the natural consequence of undervaluing the immaterial Fläche (plane), with all its further consequences for art history.
**Evidence**: The "Schwebeempfindungen" (floating sensations) the viewer perceives depend not on the stated conditions alone but on the inner setting of the viewer — a person with a poorly developed eye (organically connected to the psyche) cannot feel the depth, and therefore cannot emancipate themselves from the material plane. A trained eye can treat the GF elements as belonging to one of two types: either embedded in the plane or free in space.
**Conclusion**: The goal of theoretical investigation is (1) to make the pulsation of the Lebendige (living) accessible; (2) to find the laws of the Lebendige in their connection; (3) to derive Schlussfolgerungen (conclusions) from this material — which is synthetic work in the highest sense. This work leads to Offenbarungen des Inneren (revelations of the inner) — as much as any epoch can achieve.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Dematerialisierte Fläche — the immaterial/dematerialized plane
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The GF in composition can be treated in one of two ways: as a fixed material surface on which elements sit, or as a dematerialized, undefined (immaterieller) space through which elements float. These two modes are fundamentally antipodal (grundverschiedene, antipodisch). The dematerialized mode allows the elements to enter the undefined space — their "depth" (Tiefe) is illusory but has real compositional consequences.
- **Rule**: Consciously choose between materialized GF (elements on surface) and dematerialized GF (elements floating in undefined space) — these are not rendering choices but fundamental compositional decisions that alter the entire Klang structure.
- **Source**: p.138–139
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das feste (materielle) Liegen der Elemente auf einer festen, mehr oder weniger harten und für das Auge tastbaren GF, und das entgegengesetzte 'Schweben' dieser nicht materiell wiegenden Elemente in einem undefinier­baren (immateriellen) Raum, sind grundverschiedene, antipodisch zueinander stehende Erscheinungen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The fixed (material) lying of elements on a firm, more or less hard, eye-tangible GF, and the contrasting 'floating' of these non-materially weighted elements in an indefinable (immaterial) space, are fundamentally different, antipodally opposed phenomena."
- **Category**: plane | composition | inner-necessity

## Beschauer — The Viewer

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The "floating sensations" (Schwebeempfindungen) depend not only on the stated formal conditions but on the inner capacity of the viewer (Beschauer). A poorly developed eye cannot perceive the depth dimension and therefore cannot emancipate itself from the material surface.
**Evidence**: If the eye (organically connected to the psyche) is insufficiently developed, it cannot feel the depth — it is unable to emancipate from the material Fläche to treat it as immaterial space. The trained eye can see the GF elements as either embedded in the plane or as free in space. A line complex can ultimately be treated in two ways: either the viewer is absorbed into the plane and becomes free in space, or they remain external to it and remain on the material surface. If the starting point is right and the right direction is chosen, the goal will not be missed.
**Conclusion**: Theoretical study is necessary precisely because the goal of compositional work — to find the living — depends on a trained viewer capable of perceiving both levels (material and immaterial) of the composition.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Innere Einstellung des Beschauers — inner orientation of the viewer
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Schwebeempfindungen (floating sensations) that a composition can produce depend on the viewer's inner orientation — their Beschauer-capacity. A trained eye can simultaneously perceive a composition as fixed material surface and as floating spatial arrangement. A poorly developed eye perceives only the surface. The theoretical analysis of formal laws is therefore not merely academic — it trains the eye.
- **Rule**: Design for the trained eye, but understand that the theoretical articulation of formal laws is itself a pedagogical tool for training viewers to perceive the full Klang range of a composition.
- **Source**: p.139–140
- **Category**: composition | pedagogy | inner-necessity

## Ziel der Theorie — Goal of the Theory

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The goal of the theoretical investigation (of which this book is a part) is: (1) to make the pulsation of the Lebendige accessible; (2) to find the laws of the Lebendige in their connections; (3) to derive conclusions from this material — which is in the highest sense synthetic work.
**Evidence**: This task is the task of philosophy. It leads to Offenbarungen des Inneren (revelations of the inner) — as much as a given epoch can provide.
**Conclusion**: Formal theory is not an end in itself but a means toward revealing inner laws — and the work of finding, articulating, and connecting those laws is the only valid goal of any art-theoretical enterprise.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Ziel der theoretischen Untersuchung — goal of theoretical investigation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The goal of the theory is threefold: (1) das Lebende zu finden (to find the living); (2) seine Pulsierung wahrnehmbar zu machen (to make its pulsation perceptible); (3) die Gesetzmäßigkeiten im Lebenden in ihrem Zusammenhang zu fassen und daraus Schlußfolgerungen zu ziehen (to grasp the laws of the living in their connections and to draw conclusions from them). This is in the highest sense a synthetic work.
- **Rule**: Every formal analysis should aim not at cataloguing properties but at revealing the inner Gesetzmäßigkeit — the lawful connections that make formal decisions non-arbitrary and non-conventional.
- **Source**: p.139–140
- **Quote (DE)**: "Und das Ziel einer theoretischen Untersuchung ist: 1. das Lebende zu finden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "And the goal of a theoretical investigation is: 1. to find the living."
- **Category**: inner-necessity | pedagogy | composition

## ANHANG Tafeln (Plates 1–4) — Appendix Plates

**FIGURE [Tab-1]**: Tafel 1 — Punkt: Kühle Spannung zum Zentrum (p.142 / plate verso p.141)
- **Shows**: A rectangular framed field (Grundfläche) containing numerous black dots (Punkte) of varying sizes, distributed loosely across the GF with no obvious axis. The dots cluster loosely in the center-diagonal area but are scattered throughout, with some isolated points near the edges. The distribution is random-appearing but slightly denser toward the center-diagonal zone. No dots touch the border.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The principle of "cool tension toward the center" (Kühle Spannung zum Zentrum) — multiple Punkte of varying size on the GF, each individually establishing its position-relation to the GF, collectively creating a cool centripetal (concentric) tension rather than a dramatic eccentric one. Demonstrates P-01 (positional tension), P-12 (concentric tension principle applied to Punkt), and the general Punkt theory.
- **Text reference**: Caption only — "Punkt. Kühle Spannung zum Zentrum."
- **Source**: p.142–143

**FIGURE [Tab-2]**: Tafel 2 — Punkt: Vorsichgehende Auflösung (angedeutete Diagonale d–a) (p.144 / plate verso p.143)
- **Shows**: A rectangular framed field containing numerous black dots of varying sizes, now arranged in a loose diagonal band running from top-right to bottom-left (Diagonale d–a, i.e., from the "dramatic" top-right corner toward the "lyrical" bottom-left). The dots vary considerably in size — the largest appear near the center of the diagonal band; smaller dots flank the main cluster and some isolated dots appear in the lower-left and upper-right corners outside the main band. The diagonal movement is "vorsichgehend" (cautiously advancing) — suggested but not rigidly structured.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The "cautious dissolution" of the cool-central tension into a diagonally directed movement. The diagonal axis (d–a) introduces warmth and directional drama into what was previously concentric. Demonstrates P-07 (diagonal axis) and the transition from cool centripetal (Tab. 1) to directed diagonal Spannung.
- **Text reference**: Caption — "Punkt. Vorsichgehende Auflösung (angedeutete Diagonale d–a)."
- **Source**: p.144–145

**FIGURE [Tab-3]**: Tafel 3 — Punkt: 9 Punkte im Aufstieg (Betonung der Diagonale d–a durch Gewicht) (p.146 / plate verso p.145)
- **Shows**: A rectangular GF (without frame lines — or with very light framing, unframed open plane) containing 9 black circles/dots of significantly varying sizes arranged in a loose ascending diagonal from lower-right to upper-left (d–a direction). The largest dot is prominently placed near the upper-center-left area; a very large dot sits near lower-center; mid-sized dots define the diagonal path; small dots are scattered around the main axis. The arrangement strongly suggests upward movement along the diagonal — "im Aufstieg" (in ascent).
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Weight-emphasis on the d–a diagonal — 9 Punkte organized by size gradient to create a sense of ascending movement. The variation in size translates directly into Gewicht (weight/tension), and the diagonal placement amplifies the dramatic Klang. Demonstrates P-01 (positional tension), P-07 (diagonal axis), and the principle that point size = tension magnitude.
- **Text reference**: Caption — "9 Punkte im Aufstieg (Betonung der Diagonale d–a durch Gewicht)."
- **Source**: p.146–147

**FIGURE [Tab-4]**: Tafel 4 — Punkt: Horizontal-vertikal-diagonales Punktschema zu einem freien Linienaufbau (p.148 / plate verso p.147)
- **Shows**: An unframed GF containing approximately 9–12 black dots/circles of various sizes arranged in a pattern that simultaneously references horizontal, vertical, and diagonal axes — the dots are distributed across the GF in a way that implies multiple directional readings without being locked to any single axis. The arrangement is non-regular but structured — this is a "free linear construction" (freier Linienaufbau) rather than a random scatter.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The synthesis of horizontal, vertical, and diagonal Spannung in a free arrangement — the Punkt-system here acts as the underpinning of a free linear construction, showing how a distribution of Punkte can imply a full Linienaufbau without any lines being drawn. Demonstrates the foundational thesis of the book: point → line → plane as a continuous formal chain.
- **Text reference**: Caption — "Horizontal-vertikal-diagonales Punktschema zu einem freien Linienaufbau."
- **Source**: p.148–149

## Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Experimenting with "oben" and "unten" effects on a free-form
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Take a complex organic or free form and study it "auf den Kopf gestellt" (turned upside down). The student observes how the form's inner Klang changes — the Hartnäckigkeit (stubbornness), Lockerheit (looseness) and Widerstand (resistance) shift according to vertical orientation. Note: experiments of this kind are recommended to be done quickly, leaving only the first impression, because feeling tires quickly and imagination takes over.
- **Purpose**: Trains sensitivity to the vertical positional tension of the GF (P-01); demonstrates that the same form has different formal identities at top vs. bottom.
- **Source**: p.129 (footnote)

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Varying position of form relative to GF border
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Place a simple geometric element (line or plane-form) at varying distances from the GF border — far from border, approaching border, touching border. Observe and record the quality change at each position: free vs. tensioned vs. collapsed.
- **Purpose**: Trains sensitivity to P-04 (Entfernung von den GF-Grenzen) — the fundamental law that proximity to the border amplifies tension and contact eliminates it.
- **Source**: p.130–131

**EXERCISE [E-03]**: Constructing lyrical vs. dramatic compositions from identical elements
- **Task** [INFERRED]: Use the same set of elementary formal elements (lines, dots) and produce two versions: (a) placed centrally, aligned with GF axes → lyrical register; (b) placed eccentrically, with diagonals and border proximity → dramatic register. Compare the results.
- **Purpose**: Demonstrates P-05 and P-06 — that the same elements produce categorically different Klang depending purely on placement and organizational principle.
- **Source**: p.131–132
