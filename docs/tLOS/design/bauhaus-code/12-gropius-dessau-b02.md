## Site Plan, Floor Plans, and the Argument Against Symmetry (pp. 19–21)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Renaissance and Baroque tradition of the symmetrical facade produces a flat, two-dimensional image that the observer reads from a single fixed point. The new building must be grasped by walking around it — it is three-dimensional, not pictorial.
**Evidence**: Abb. 4 (site plan/Lageplan), Abb. 5 (ground floor plan/Grundriss des Erdgeschosses), Abb. 6 (first floor plan/Grundriss des 1. Stockes) — three drawings showing the asymmetric pinwheel organization of the building in relation to the urban grid.
**Conclusion**: Functional planning — Funktionsgliederung — inherently produces asymmetry. Asymmetry is not an aesthetic choice; it is the consequence of letting every zone take the form its function demands.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Symmetriefassade vs. Umgehung / Symmetrical facade vs. circumnavigation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The typical building of the Renaissance and Baroque presents a symmetrical facade. This image, which draws the observer who moves toward it, is flat and two-dimensional ("flächig, zweidimensional"). The new building rejects this: one must walk around it to understand it. It is spatial, not pictorial.
- **Rule**: Do not design a building to be read from a single frontal viewpoint; design it to be understood through movement around and through it.
- **Source**: p.19
- **Quote (DE)**: "der typische bau der renaissance, des barock zeigt die symmetrische fassade, auf deren mittelsachse der zuweg führt. das bild, das sich dem ruhenden beschauer bietet, ist flächig, zweidimensional."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the typical building of the renaissance, of the baroque shows the symmetrical facade, on whose central axis the approach leads. the image that presents itself to the stationary observer is flat, two-dimensional."
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Funktionsgliederung als Planungsgrundlage / Functional articulation as planning basis
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The organisation of a good ground plan follows: correct utilisation of sunlight orientation (richtige Ausnutzung der Sonnenlage), short and adaptable circulation routes (kurze anpassbare Verkehrswege), clear separation of the individual sections of the organism (klare Trennung der einzelnen Abteilungen des Organismus), variation possibilities for about-necessary spatial reorganisations through use of partition walls (Variationsmöglichkeit der Raumfolgen durch hilfe sinnreicher Achsenteilung).
- **Rule**: Ground plan organisation must optimise solar orientation, minimise circulation length, separate functional zones clearly, and build in future flexibility through modular partition systems.
- **Source**: p.20
- **Quote (DE)**: "richtige ausnutzung der sonnenlage, kurze anpassbare verkehrswege, klare trennung der einzelnen abteilungen des organismus, variationsmöglichkeit der raumfolgen mit hilfe sinnreicher achsenteilung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "correct utilisation of the solar orientation, short adaptable circulation routes, clear separation of the individual sections of the organism, variation possibilities for spatial sequences with the help of a clever axis division."
- **Category**: function | space

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: Abb. 4 — Bauhausgebäude, Lageplan der Gesamtanlage (site plan of total complex)
- **Shows**: Black-and-white site plan at city scale. The Bauhaus building complex sits at the intersection of two roads. The building's footprint is clearly asymmetric and pinwheel-shaped — no single frontal elevation. The workshop wing (Werkstattbau), classroom/school wing (Schulgebäude), bridge (Brücke), dormitory (Wohnbau/Atelierhaus), and the connecting low structure are all legible as distinct masses in the plan.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Asymmetry as direct consequence of Funktionsgliederung. The building cannot be approached from a privileged axis — each wing faces a different direction, optimised for its own function and solar orientation.
- **Text reference**: Text on p.19 directly argues the symmetrical facade is the older model; this plan is the counter-argument made visible.
- **Source**: p.19

From left to right: the tall Atelierhaus (student dormitory/studio block) connected via a low intermediate building containing Speisesaal (canteen), Küche (kitchen), Bad (bathroom), Turnraum (gymnasium), Bühne (stage). The Atelierhaus shows the repetitive cell of student studio-rooms along a double-loaded corridor.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The bridge at first floor is a designed Zirkulationsraum — circulation elevated above the street is an architectural decision, not an infrastructure afterthought. The workshop spaces on the upper floors of the Werkstattbau demonstrate that the building is raised on pilotis to free the ground.
- **Text reference**: Cross-reference to abb. 32 and 43 for bridge detail (mentioned later in text, p.25).
- **Source**: p.21

## Construction Phase and Material System (pp. 22–23)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The material system of the Bauhaus building is a direct demonstration of its structural logic. Reinforced concrete frame, infill masonry only where needed, glass curtain wall on the workshop wing, flat roof with membrane waterproofing — each material choice is legible and honest.
**Evidence**: Abb. 7 (construction photograph, east view, 1926) and Abb. 8 (construction photograph, southeast view, 1926) showing the building under construction; text section "material und konstruktion:" listing the complete material specification.
**Conclusion**: Structure is not concealed by cladding. The construction photographs show the concrete frame before infill, making the structural logic transparent. The finished building preserves this transparency.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Material und Konstruktion — Ehrlichkeit der Baukonstruktion / Material and construction — honesty of building construction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The building uses: stamped concrete foundations (gestampfte Betonfundamente); concrete frame (Trägerrippe aus Eisenbetonpfeilern) with tensioned stone-iron deck infill and partial brick wall fill; at the long sides of the workshop building the outer wall is entirely dispensed with — the function of the load-bearing outer wall is taken over by the inner piers. The spaces are closed off by iron window grids; in the basement floor, mushroom slabs (Pilzdecke) are used, the others are cantilevered slabs — an economy measure. Accessible roof surfaces on the studio house are sealed with welded asphalt-felt on insulation layer, the others with lime-wash (Kalklack) on jute-tissue on tar-felt, and levelling concrete. Drainage is via cast-iron pipes concealed inside the building, so the exterior zinc waste pipes become unnecessary. The exterior skin consists of water-repellent smooth cement plaster with a coat of white Keim mineral paint.
- **Rule**: Specify each material according to its structural function; where a structural element replaces a cladding element, make that substitution explicit and visible.
- **Source**: p.23
- **Quote (DE)**: "die außenwand des werkstattbaues entbehrt an seinen längsseiten jedes mauerwerks, die funktion einer tragenden außenwand wird hier von den innenpfeilern aufgenommen. das die räume abschließende eiserne fensterwerk wird von den ausgekragten decken getragen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the outer wall of the workshop building dispenses at its long sides with all masonry; the function of a load-bearing outer wall is taken over here by the inner piers. the iron window framework that closes off the spaces is carried by the cantilevered slabs."
- **Category**: material | transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Pilzdecke als Sparmaßnahme / Mushroom slab as economy measure
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Mushroom slabs (Pilzdecke) are used in the basement floor as an economy measure — "zur Ersparnis zur Bauhöhe." This is not presented as a design weakness but as intelligent material deployment: use the most efficient structural system where visible expression is not required, reserve the more expressive cantilevered slab for the upper floors where it carries the Vorhangfassade.
- **Rule**: Differentiate structural systems by floor and by visibility; use the most economical system where it will not be seen, the most expressive where it carries the architectural argument.
- **Source**: p.23
- **Quote (DE)**: "im kellergeschoß eine 'pilzdecke' zur ersparnis zur bauhöhe"
- **Quote (EN)**: "in the basement floor a 'mushroom slab' to save on building height"
- **Category**: material | function

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: Abb. 7 — Bauhausgebäude im Rohbau 1926, Ostansicht (building under construction 1926, east view)
- **Shows**: Large photograph. The reinforced concrete frame is visible in raw state — piers, cantilevered slabs, the structural skeleton before any infill. The workshop wing (left, taller) and the school wing (right, lower, horizontal) are both visible. Scaffolding covers the facades. The ground is construction rubble. Photo credit: Wedekind, Dessau.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The structural system of the building — the Eisenbetonpfeiler (reinforced concrete piers) and ausgekragte Decken (cantilevered slabs) — is made legible before the skin is added. The construction photograph is an argument for structural honesty: the frame is the building; the glass is only the closure.
- **Text reference**: The "material und konstruktion:" section on p.23 describes this frame in detail.
- **Source**: p.22

**FIGURE [Fig-05]**: Abb. 8 — Bauhausgebäude im Rohbau 1926, Südostansicht (building under construction 1926, southeast view)
- **Shows**: Construction photograph from southeast, showing the workshop wing and the beginning of the bridge connection. Photo credit: Lucia Moholy, Berlin. The building in Rohbau (shell construction) state: concrete frame legible, workshop wing's large-span structural bays visible, the future glass curtain wall location clearly implied by the wide spacing between piers.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The wide pier spacing of the workshop wing — necessary for large workshop spaces — directly produces the wide glass bays of the finished Vorhangfassade. The structure dictates the facade rhythm; the facade does not impose a rhythm on the structure.
- **Text reference**: p.23: "die außenwand des werkstattbaues entbehrt an seinen längsseiten jedes mauerwerks."
- **Source**: p.23

## Elevations and the Glass Facade System (pp. 24–26)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The full-height glass curtain wall (Vorhangfassade) of the workshop wing is a direct consequence of the structural system: the load-bearing piers are set back from the perimeter, the cantilevered slabs project to the facade line, and the glass is hung between slab edges. The glass wraps the corners because structure has retreated from the corners.
**Evidence**: Abb. 9 (west elevation drawing), Abb. 10 (northwest photograph, completed building), Abb. 11 (north elevation drawing with bridge section).
**Conclusion**: The Vorhangfassade is not a stylistic choice — it is the visible consequence of moving the structure inside. Every element of the glass wall (mirror glass, steel profiles, corner wrapping) follows from this structural logic.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Vorhangfassade — Struktur nach innen, Glas nach außen / Curtain wall — structure inward, glass outward
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The outer wall of the workshop building is entirely resolved in window-strips with mirror-glass glazing (Fensterstrossen mit Spiegelglasverglasung). The load-bearing piers stand behind the connection joints of the prefabricated window strips, behind the visible intermediate piers of the basement windows (see abb. 32 and 43). Structure retreats; glass advances to the outer plane.
- **Rule**: When the structure is set back from the facade plane, the facade must be resolved as pure glass closure — the structural logic demands it and the visual result makes the logic legible.
- **Source**: p.25
- **Quote (DE)**: "die außenwand des werkstattbaues ist ganz in fensterstrossen mit spiegelglasverglasung aufgelöst. die tragpfeiler stehen im innern hinter der glaswand."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the outer wall of the workshop building is entirely resolved in window-strips with mirror-glass glazing. the load-bearing piers stand in the interior behind the glass wall."
- **Category**: transparency | material | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Zwei getrennte Schulorganismen / Two separate school organisms
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bridge-like overspanning of the street results from the task of building two separate school organisms with separate entrances: on the left the "technische Lehranstalten" (technical teaching institution), on the right the actual "Bauhaus." The shared circulation spaces are accessible from both sides via the bridge.
- **Rule**: When two organisationally distinct institutions share a site, give each a separate entrance and identity while connecting them at an upper level — circulation is shared but sovereignty is preserved.
- **Source**: p.25
- **Quote (DE)**: "die brückenartige überbauung der straße ergibt sich aus der gestellten aufgabe, zwei getrennte schulorganismen mit gesonderten eingängen (links 'technische lehranstalten', rechts das eigentliche 'bauhaus') zu bauen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the bridge-like spanning of the street results from the given task of building two separate school organisms with separate entrances (left 'technical teaching institution', right the actual 'bauhaus')."
- **Category**: function | space | urbanism

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: Abb. 9 — Bauhausgebäude, Westansicht (west elevation drawing)
- **Shows**: Precise architectural elevation drawing of the west facade. The building reads as a long horizontal bar — the workshop wing occupies the full height with continuous horizontal window bands (Fensterbänder) running its entire length. The facade is almost entirely glass with thin steel dividers. The flat roof line is uninterrupted. Scale bar at lower left. The drawing demonstrates the complete abstraction of the facade to a glass membrane supported by an invisible frame.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The Vorhangfassade in its ideal state — the west elevation is nearly pure glass. The structural rhythm of the piers is only hinted at by the mullion spacing; the piers themselves are invisible because they are set back behind the glass plane.
- **Text reference**: p.25 describes the outer wall as entirely resolved in window-strips.
- **Source**: p.24

**FIGURE [Fig-07]**: Abb. 10 — Bauhausgebäude, Nordwestansicht (northwest view, completed building)
- **Shows**: Photograph by Lucia Moholy, Berlin. The northwest corner of the workshop wing: the glass curtain wall wraps the corner, the steel window profiles catching the light. Behind the glass, the concrete piers are visible as dark vertical elements. The bridge (Verbindungssteg) is visible to the right connecting to the school building. The Atelierhaus (dormitory) rises in the background. Photo captures the tension between transparent glass volume and the solid white rendered wall of the school wing.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The corner wrapping of the glass facade — the glass turns the corner without a solid element — is the definitive visual proof that the structure has moved inside. At a corner, a load-bearing wall would need to be solid; the fact that glass wraps the corner proves there is no corner column, no load at the perimeter.
- **Text reference**: p.25: "die außenwand des werkstattbaues ist ganz in fensterstrossen mit spiegelglasverglasung aufgelöst."
- **Source**: p.25

**FIGURE [Fig-08]**: Abb. 11 — Bauhausgebäude, Nordansicht mit Schnitt durch die Aula (north elevation with section through the Aula)
- **Shows**: Combined elevation and section drawing. The north elevation of the main school building is shown full-width; below it, a cross-section through the Aula (assembly hall) is inset. The elevation shows the bridge at first-floor level crossing the road, connecting the Bauhaus wing (right) to the Technische Lehranstalten wing (left). The flat roof, horizontal window bands, and the differing heights of the workshop wing versus school wing are all legible in this combined drawing.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The section through the Aula confirms the internal spatial organisation — the assembly hall is a double-height space within the school building, positioned at the joint between the bridge and the main building volume.
- **Text reference**: p.25 describes the bridge as giving both school organisms separate entrances while sharing the circulation spaces on either side.
- **Source**: p.26

## Bridge, Dormitory, and Programme Description (pp. 27–31)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The bridge (Brücke/Verbindungssteg) is not a mere connector — it is a designed Zirkulationsraum containing the administration offices (Verwaltungsräume, first floor) and the architecture department (Architekturabteilung, second floor). The bridge is programme, not infrastructure. The dormitory (Atelierhaus/Wohnbau) is a distinct building type demonstrating that the Bauhaus vocabulary scales to the domestic.
**Evidence**: Abb. 12 (northeast view showing dormitory set apart), Abb. 13 (bridge corridor interior), Abb. 14 (bridge exterior, inauguration day), Abb. 15 (longitudinal section), Abb. 16 (north view showing dormitory, canteen, stage).
**Conclusion**: Every element of the complex is programme-full. The bridge contains offices. The low intermediate building contains canteen, kitchen, baths, gymnasium, stage. The dormitory contains 28 student studio-rooms. There is no residual space.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Zirkulationsraum als Programm / Circulation space as programme
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bridge (Verbindungssteg) between the main Bauhaus building and the Technische Lehranstalten building is not merely circulation. First floor: Verwaltungsräume (administration offices). Second floor: Architekturabteilung (architecture department). The shared administration spaces on the bridge are accessible from both wings. Circulation is programmed; no square metre is residual.
- **Rule**: Every circulation element — corridor, bridge, staircase — must carry a programme beyond mere passage; if it does not, it is wasted space.
- **Source**: p.29
- **Quote (DE)**: "erster stock: verwaltungsräume / zweiter stock: architekturanbteilung"
- **Quote (EN)**: "first floor: administration rooms / second floor: architecture department"
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Brückenpfeiler hinter der Innenwand / Bridge piers behind the inner wall
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The load-bearing bridge piers (tragenden Brückenpfeiler) stand behind the inner wall (see abb. 14). The floor is cantilevered (ausgekragt). Steel windows with mirror-glass glazing. The structural logic of the main building — piers set back from the perimeter — is consistently applied to the bridge.
- **Rule**: Apply the same structural principle consistently across every element of the complex; structural inconsistency between building parts breaks the compositional argument.
- **Source**: p.29
- **Quote (DE)**: "die tragenden brückenpfeiler (s.abb.14) stehen unter der inneren wand (rechts im bild), das flurpodest ist ausgekragt, stahlfenster mit spiegelglasverglasung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the load-bearing bridge piers (see abb.14) stand under the inner wall (right in picture), the corridor landing is cantilevered, steel windows with mirror-glass glazing."
- **Category**: transparency | material | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Wohnbau mit 28 Ateliers / Dormitory with 28 studio-rooms
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Wohnbau (dormitory/student residence) is set apart from the Schulbau (school building), visible in the background of the northeast view (abb. 12). It contains 28 Ateliers für Studierende (studios for students). The low intermediate building (Zwischenbau) between dormitory and school building contains Speisesaal (canteen), Küche (kitchen), Bad (bathroom), Turnraum (gymnasium), Bühne (stage), and Vortragssaal (lecture hall).
- **Rule**: Separate residential from educational programme in the massing, but connect them through a shared social programme (canteen, gymnasium, stage) housed in a lower intermediate building.
- **Source**: p.27
- **Quote (DE)**: "links im bild, vom schulbau abgerückt, der wohnbau mit 28 ateliers für studierende; im niedrigen zwischenbau speisesaal, küche, bad und turnraum, bühne und vortragssaal."
- **Quote (EN)**: "left in the picture, set back from the school building, the dormitory with 28 studios for students; in the low intermediate building canteen, kitchen, bath and gymnasium, stage and lecture hall."
- **Category**: function | space

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-09]**: Abb. 12 — Bauhausgebäude, Nordostansicht (northeast view)
- **Shows**: Photograph by Lucia Moholy, Berlin. Left: the Atelierhaus (student dormitory block) as a tall, vertical, white-rendered tower with regular punched windows and projecting balconies. Right: the long horizontal workshop wing of the school building with its glass curtain wall. Between them: the low connecting Zwischenbau. The contrast between the vertical residential tower and the horizontal workshop wing — same white rendered surfaces, different formal language — demonstrates that Funktionsgliederung produces differentiated massing.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Same vocabulary, different configuration — the residential tower is vertical and cellular (28 repetitive cells), the workshop wing is horizontal and open (large workshop bays). The building teaches that function determines form, but a consistent material and structural vocabulary unifies the whole.
- **Text reference**: p.27 identifies the Atelierhaus on the left.
- **Source**: p.27

**FIGURE [Fig-10]**: Abb. 13 — Bauhausgebäude, Verbindungsgang des ersten Stocks in der Brücke (bridge corridor, first floor)
- **Shows**: Full-page interior photograph by Lucia Moholy, Berlin. View along the first-floor corridor of the bridge. The left wall is the full glass curtain wall — floor-to-ceiling steel-framed mirror glass panels, the exterior trees and the school building visible through them. The right wall is solid plaster. The perspective recession into depth is dramatic, emphasising the length and the transparency. The floor is dark; the ceiling is plain; the structural logic is fully exposed. Sunlight enters from the left creating strong shadow patterns on the floor.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The corridor as Zirkulationsraum — not a dark service passage but a light-filled designed space. The full glass wall means the corridor participates in the exterior — you see the weather, the light quality, the other building. Circulation is not separated from architecture; it is architecture.
- **Text reference**: Text p.29 notes the load-bearing bridge piers are behind the inner (right) wall; the corridor photograph confirms the glass is on the outer face.

## Film Stills and the Moving Image of Architecture (pp. 32–33)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The building was documented by the UFA film company at inauguration (September/December 1926). Three film frames of the same view, printed as a filmstrip, demonstrate that architecture must be experienced sequentially — in time, through movement. The still photograph is insufficient; the building exists in the fourth dimension.
**Evidence**: Abb. 17 (three filmstrip frames, northwest view toward bridge) and Abb. 18 (three filmstrip frames, southeast view of glass facade).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Architektur im zeitlichen Erleben / Architecture in temporal experience
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The deliberate use of film-strip format (three sequential frames from the UFA inauguration film, 1926) to document the building makes the argument that architecture is not a static object but a sequence of spatial experiences. The building must be seen in motion, from multiple angles, over time. The filmstrip format is itself a design statement: do not show architecture as a single fixed image.
- **Rule**: Document architecture through sequence, not single image; a building understood only from its canonical photograph is not fully understood.
- **Source**: p.32–33
- **Quote (DE)**: "aus dem einweihungsfilm der 'ufa' (september 1926)" / "aus dem einweihungsfilm der 'ufa' (dezember 1926)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "from the inauguration film by 'ufa' (september 1926)" / "from the inauguration film by 'ufa' (december 1926)"
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-14]**: Abb. 17 — Bauhausgebäude, Nordwestansicht gegen die Brücke, aus dem Einweihungsfilm der "ufa" (September 1926)
- **Shows**: Three film frames arranged vertically as a film strip (with sprocket holes visible on left and right edges). Each frame shows the northwest facade of the school building — the long horizontal workshop wing with its Fensterbänder (window bands) and the bridge connection visible. The three frames are taken from slightly different moments showing the approach to the building, demonstrating the building's appearance at different viewing distances. The filmstrip presentation is a deliberate editorial choice by Gropius to show the building as sequential experience.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The horizontal window bands of the workshop/school building read powerfully in the filmstrip: as a series of continuous lines broken by the vertical piers, they establish a strong rhythmic tempo that is experienced over time in movement.
- **Text reference**: None in immediate text — the image stands alone as evidence.
- **Source**: p.32

**FIGURE [Fig-15]**: Abb. 18 — Bauhausgebäude, Südostansicht, aus dem Einweihungsfilm der "ufa" (Dezember 1926)

The bottom frame shows an extreme close-up looking up at the corner where the curtain wall meets the cantilevered slab.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The corner resolution of the Vorhangfassade — glass wrapping the corner because no structural element occupies the corner — is most clearly visible in this close-up filmstrip sequence. The curtain wall argument is most readable at close range; the filmstrip brings the viewer progressively closer.
- **Text reference**: None in immediate text — image-only page.
- **Source**: p.33

## Technische Lehranstalten: Elevation and Window System (pp. 34–35)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Technische Lehranstalten (technical teaching institution) building, while sharing the bridge and campus with the Bauhaus proper, has a different facade treatment — still steel windows with mirror-glass glazing, but with horizontal window bands in a regular grid rather than the full curtain wall of the workshop wing. The structural logic is the same; the programme demands smaller, more cellular windows.
**Evidence**: Abb. 19 (east elevation drawing of Technische Lehranstalten), Abb. 20 (northwest photograph of same building).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Konstruktionspfeiler hinter dem Fensterband / Structural piers behind the window band
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The load-bearing concrete piers of the skeleton frame stand behind the joints of the prefabricated window bands (Verbindungsstöße der vorgefertigten Fensterbandes), behind the visible intermediate piers of the basement windows. The iron windows with upper and lower ventilation flaps (Lüftungsklappen) offer the most favourable ventilation of the classroom (Lehrsäle). This window type, which has found repeated application in Germany, uses polished mirror glass (poliertem Spiegelglas) glazing.
- **Rule**: Prefabricate the window band as a unit; set the structural piers behind the prefabricated strip so that they are not visible from outside — the facade reads as continuous band, not as pier-and-infill.
- **Source**: p.34
- **Quote (DE)**: "die tragenden betonpfeiler des bauskeletts stehen hinter den verbindungsstößen des vorgefertigten fensterbandes, also über den sichtbaren zwischenpfeilern der kellerfenster."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the load-bearing concrete piers of the building skeleton stand behind the connection joints of the prefabricated window band, thus above the visible intermediate piers of the basement windows."
- **Category**: material | transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Lüftungsklappen als integrale Fensterkomponente / Ventilation flaps as integral window component
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The iron windows (eiserne Fenster) with upper and lower ventilation flaps (Lüftungsklappen) provide the most favourable ventilation for the classrooms. This window type is described as having found "wiederholt Anwendung in Deutschland" (repeated application in Germany) — it is a proven industrial component deployed in a systematic way, not a custom design.
- **Rule**: Use proven industrial window components with integrated ventilation; specify the same component type throughout a building to achieve economic repetition and consistent appearance.
- **Source**: p.34
- **Quote (DE)**: "die eisernen fenster mit oberen und unteren lüftungsklappen gewähren günstige be- und entlüftung der lehrsäle; ein fenstertyp, der seitdem in deutschland wiederholt anwendung fand. verglasung in poliertem spiegelglas."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the iron windows with upper and lower ventilation flaps provide favourable ventilation of the classrooms; a window type that has since found repeated application in Germany. glazing in polished mirror glass."
- **Category**: material | function | standardization

## Material Theory and Industrial Materials (pp. 36–37)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: New industrially-produced materials — iron, concrete, glass — have entered competition with traditional building materials. They surpass traditional materials in performance precisely because their firmness and molecular density allows ever larger, more light-filled structures to be built with ever less construction mass. This development is not a stylistic choice; it is a structural necessity.
**Evidence**: Abb. 21 (composite south elevation drawing), Abb. 22 (south view photograph showing "BAUHAUS" signage on completed building).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Neue Industriebaustoffe übertreffen Naturmaterialien / New industrial materials surpass natural materials
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In the design workshops (gestaltenden Werkwelt), new, industrially produced materials — iron, concrete, glass — have entered into competition with old natural building materials and are beginning to surpass them. These new materials achieve their superior performance precisely through their firmness and molecular density (Festigkeit und molekularer Dichtigkeit). Their properties allow widely-spanning, light-flooded (lichtdurchflutete) spaces to be built that were impossible with the construction materials and techniques of previous ages.
- **Rule**: Select materials on the basis of their structural performance per unit of mass; the material that allows the widest span, the most light, and the least construction mass is the correct material for a building that demonstrates its principles.
- **Source**: p.37
- **Quote (DE)**: "in der gestaltenden werkwelt sind neue, industriell verarbeitete baustoffe in konkurrenz mit den alten naturbaustoffen getreten und beginnen sie zu überholen. diese neuen baumaterialien — eisen, beton, glas — haben es infolge ihrer festigkeit und molekularen dichtigkeit erst ermöglicht, unter größter ersparnis an konstruktionsmasse, weitgespannte, lichtdurchflutete räume und gebäude zu erbauen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "in the designing workshop-world, new, industrially produced building materials have entered into competition with the old natural building materials and are beginning to surpass them. these new building materials — iron, concrete, glass — have, due to their firmness and molecular density, first made it possible, with the greatest economy of construction mass, to build wide-spanning, light-flooded spaces and buildings."
- **Category**: material | function | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Konsequente Vergrößerung der Wand- und Dachöffnungen / Consistent enlargement of wall and roof openings
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: With the conscious aim of reducing the load-bearing body of the building through refined calculation and qualitative maximisation of material firmness to be spatially ever more restricted, leading consistently to the ever-increasing enlargement of wall and roof openings, in order to let daylight in to the rooms that are against weather closed spaces — the old principle of large house-wall surfaces and closed roofs gives way to the inverse principle of large window surfaces and roof openings within the smallest possible framework of construction piers and supports.
- **Rule**: As structural calculation refines the structural member, the freed area must become window; the goal is maximum glass area within minimum structural frame.
- **Source**: p.37
- **Quote (DE)**: "führen konsequent zur sich immerfort steigernden vergrößerung der wand- und dachöffnungen, um das tageslicht ungehemmt in die gegen witterung abgeschlossenen räume dennoch einströmen zu lassen. die alten hauswandungen mit kleinen öffnungen innerhalb großer, undurchbrochener flächen und geschlossener dächer weichen dem umgekehrten prinzip ausgedehnter fensterflächen und dachöffnungen innerhalb schmaler rahmen von konstruktionspfeilern und -stützen von möglichst geringer ausdehnung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "lead consistently to the ever-increasing enlargement of wall and roof openings, in order to let daylight flow unimpeded into spaces that are closed against weather. the old house-walls with small openings within large unbroken surfaces and closed roofs give way to the inverse principle of extensive window surfaces and roof openings within narrow frames of construction piers and supports of the smallest possible extent."
- **Category**: material | transparency | function

### Visual Evidence

- **Shows**: Composite south elevation drawing showing the full complex from the south. From left: the Technische Lehranstalten wing, the bridge at upper level crossing the road, the Bauhaus school wing, and to the right the low Zwischenbau. The bridge connecting element is clearly visible spanning between the two main buildings. The elevation confirms the height differences: school wing 3 storeys, bridge element 2 storeys above street, low Zwischenbau 1 storey. Caption notes reference to abb. 22 (south photograph).
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The south elevation composite makes the full Funktionsgliederung legible in one drawing: each zone has a distinct height profile precisely because each programme demands a different section.

## Atelierhaus Elevation (p. 38)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Atelierhaus (student studio dormitory) is a repetitive cellular building — 28 identical studio units stacked — and its elevation directly expresses this repetition. The facade is a grid: each cell has the same window, the same balcony, the same proportions.
**Evidence**: Abb. 23 (Atelierhaus elevation drawing).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Zelluläre Repetition als Fassadenordnung / Cellular repetition as facade order
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The Atelierhaus elevation is a grid of identical cells — each studio unit is expressed as one bay: one balcony door with full-height glazing, flanked by a narrow side window. The bays repeat vertically across six storeys and horizontally across the full width. The repetition is not monotonous because the balcony projections create shadow and depth at each cell.
- **Rule**: When the programme is inherently repetitive (dormitory, office block, workshop), let the repetition be the facade order — do not disguise it with superficial variation.
- **Source**: p.38
- **Quote (DE)**: [Caption only] "abb. 23 bauhausgebäude, seitenansicht des atelierhauses"
- **Quote (EN)**: "abb. 23 bauhaus building, side elevation of the studio house"
- **Category**: function | space | standardization

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-20]**: Abb. 23 — Bauhausgebäude, Seitenansicht des Atelierhauses (side elevation of studio house)
- **Shows**: Precise architectural elevation drawing of the Atelierhaus. Six storeys. Each bay shows: a projecting balcony with balustrade, a full-height glazed door opening onto the balcony, and to the side a narrower fixed or operable window. The ground floor has a different treatment (service / entrance). The flat roof reads as a thin cap. The proportional system of the facade is a near-square module repeated. Scale bar at lower left (0 to 10 metres).
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The cellular repetition principle. Each of the 28 studio units occupies one bay; the elevation is a direct count of the programme units. The projecting balconies create shadow relief that saves the facade from flatness while remaining entirely determined by function — each student has outdoor space.
- **Text reference**: p.27 mentions "28 ateliers für studierende"; the elevation confirms the spatial configuration.
- **Source**: p.38
