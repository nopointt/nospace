## Atelierhaus — Student Dormitory and Studio Block

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: A school where people work collectively on a shared task — as at the Bauhaus — must simultaneously offer every individual the possibility of withdrawing, undisturbed, from the community. The dormitory building is the architectural resolution of this tension.
**Evidence**: Six-storey Atelierhaus shown from east, south, and southeast facades (abb. 24–28). Each studio unit is a standardized module: 5.17 × 4.35 m (axis dimension), containing a sleeping niche, washstand with running water, and two built-in wardrobes. Every unit has its own small private balcony. The communal kitchen and food lift ("Speiseaufzug") are shared per floor.
**Conclusion**: The Atelierhaus demonstrates that repetition-as-system and individual-as-unit are not in contradiction. The building achieves community through shared infrastructure and privacy through individual spatial allocation.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Gemeinschaft und Rückzug / Community and Retreat
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Die Überlegung, daß Menschen, die an einer gemeinschaftlichen Aufgabe arbeiten, wie im Bauhaus, die Möglichkeit haben müssen, sich zeitweise ungestört außerhalb der Gemeinschaft ganz auf sich selbst zurückziehen zu können, führte dazu, das Ateliergebäude der Studierenden vom übrigen Betrieb abzurücken und jedem einzelnen Atelier möglichste Wohnruhe, ja auch jedem seinen eigenen kleinen Balkon zu geben. / "The consideration that people who work collectively on a shared task — as at the Bauhaus — must have the possibility of withdrawing, undisturbed, entirely to themselves outside the community, led to the decision to separate the students' studio building from the main operations and to give each individual studio the greatest possible domestic quiet, and indeed each person their own small balcony."
- **Rule**: Separate collective workspace from individual living quarters; provide each person a private outdoor threshold, however small.
- **Source**: p.39
- **Quote (DE)**: "die überlegung, daß menschen, die an einer gemeinschaftlichen aufgabe arbeiten, wie im bauhaus, die möglichkeit haben müssen, sich zeitweise ungestört außerhalb der gemeinschaft ganz auf sich selbst zurückziehen zu können, führte dazu, das ateliergebäude der studierenden vom übrigen betrieb abzurücken und jedem einzelnen atelier möglichste wohnruhe, ja auch jedem seinen eigenen kleinen balkon zu geben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The consideration that people working together on a communal task, as at the Bauhaus, must have the possibility of withdrawing undisturbed, entirely to themselves, outside the community, led to separating the students' studio building from the main operations and giving each studio the greatest possible domestic quiet, and each person their own small balcony."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Standardisiertes Ateliermodul / Standardized Studio Module
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every student studio-atelier has the same plan: 5.17 × 4.35 m axis dimension, sleeping niche, washstand with running water, two built-in wall cupboards. Shared kitchen ("Teeküche") on each floor, shared food lift ("Speiseaufzug"). Standardization is the condition of equality and of economy.
- **Rule**: Fix the unit module through programmatic analysis; vary the configuration, not the module.
- **Source**: p.41 (abb. 26 caption)
- **Quote (DE)**: "jedes studierenden-atelier besteht aus einem 5,17 4,35 m (achsenmaß) großen raum, mit schlafnische, waschtisch mit fließendem wasser und zwei wandschränken. gemeinsame teeküche in jedem stock. speiseaufzug."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Every student studio consists of a 5.17 × 4.35 m (axis dimension) room, with sleeping niche, washstand with running water, and two wall cupboards. Shared kitchen on every floor. Food lift."
- **Category**: standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Balkon als privater Schwellraum / Balcony as Private Threshold Space
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The individual balcony performs two functions simultaneously: it is the private exterior threshold of the individual cell, and it is the unit of facade composition that gives the dormitory tower its rhythmic, layered visual identity. Each balcony is structurally cantilevered, with a tubular steel railing — lightweight, transparent to air and light. The south-facing orientation captures maximum sun. The facade reads as a vertical stack of private territories.
- **Rule**: Design balconies as structural cantilevers with minimal railings; orient to sun; make them the generating module of the facade.
- **Source**: p.41–43 (abb. 25, 26, 27, 28)
- **Category**: space | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Südorientierung des Wohnbaus / South Orientation of Residential Building
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The Atelierhaus balconies face south. This is not incidental — it is programmatic. Dormitory/residential functions receive the warm southern light; workshop functions face north for even, diffuse illumination. The building's orientation is its environmental program made visible.
- **Rule**: Orient sleeping and living spaces to the south; orient workshops and workspaces to the north.
- **Source**: p.41–42 (abb. 25, 26 — south and southeast views)
- **Category**: space | function

## Werkstattbau — Workshop Wing: Glass Facade and Structural Expression

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The workshop wing's glass facade is not a stylistic gesture — it is the logical consequence of exposing the concrete frame structure fully and hanging a non-load-bearing curtain of glass from it. The corner wrap of the glass is structural honesty made visible: there is no corner column, because the frame has been pulled back, freeing the glass to wrap continuously.
**Evidence**: Multiple photographs of the Werkstattbau from different angles (abb. 29–37): the approach from the main entrance showing the full glass facade and Querglafront; the northwest view; the corner detail of the northwest workshop showing concrete columns and mass ceiling clearly; the Haupttreppenhaus (main stairwell) with undivided mirror glass; the stairwell corner detail from below.
**Conclusion**: Every element of the workshop wing facade is a structural argument. Glass is not decoration applied to structure — it is the absence of wall where wall is not structurally needed.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Vorhangfassade — Glasfassade als Konstruktionsausdruck / Curtain Wall — Glass Facade as Structural Expression
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Die Ecke des Werkstattbaues läßt das Konstruktionsgerüst der Betonpfeiler und Massivdecken klar erkennen. Zum erstenmal wurde hier das Problem der Auflösung der Wand durch Ausspannen einer durchgehenden Glashaut vor das tragende Bauskelett bis zur letzten Konsequenz durchgeführt. Die Auskragung der Decken über das Kellergeschoß ergibt doppelte Vorteile: die statisch günstige und daher ökonomische Verkürzung der Abstände von Außenpfeilern zu Mittelpfeilern im Innern, und die Ausnutzbarkeit der gesamten Glasfront auch von den Pfeilern für Arbeitsplätze der Werkstätten. / "The corner of the workshop building makes the structural framework of the concrete columns and solid ceilings clearly legible. Here for the first time the problem of dissolving the wall by spanning a continuous glass skin before the load-bearing skeleton was carried through to its final consequence. The cantilever of the ceilings over the basement level gives double advantages: the structurally favorable and therefore economical reduction of the spacing from outer to inner columns, and the usability of the entire glass facade even at the columns as workplaces for the workshops."
- **Rule**: Hang the glass skin from the structural frame, not from the wall; cantilever the floor slabs to free the entire facade surface including the column positions.
- **Source**: p.47 (abb. 32 caption text)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die ecke des werkstattbaues läßt das konstruktionsgerüst der betonpfeiler und massivdecken klar erkennen. zum erstenmal wurde hier das problem der auflösung der wand durch ausspannen einer durchgehenden glashaut vor das tragende bauskelett bis zur letzten konsequenz durchgeführt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The corner of the workshop building makes the structural framework of the concrete columns and solid ceilings clearly legible. Here for the first time the problem of dissolving the wall by spanning a continuous glass skin in front of the load-bearing skeleton was carried through to its final consequence."
- **Category**: transparency | material | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Doppelter Vorteil der Deckenauskagrung / Double Advantage of Slab Cantilever
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Ceilings cantilevered over the basement level produce two simultaneous structural and spatial benefits: (1) structurally favorable reduction of outer-to-inner column spacing — more economical; (2) the entire glass facade, including the column positions, becomes usable as workplaces. Economy and spatial usefulness are achieved by the same structural decision.
- **Rule**: Design the structural cantilever to simultaneously reduce column spans and maximize usable facade-adjacent floor area.
- **Source**: p.47 (abb. 32 caption text)
- **Category**: function | standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Fenstervorhänge unter den Decken / Window Curtains Below Ceilings
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Niedrige Körper vor der ganzen Breite der Glaswand — Fenstervorhänge unter den Decken entlang der gesamten Glasfront — protect against direct sun. The interior shading system runs the full width of the glass wall beneath the ceiling, protecting workplaces from direct solar radiation while maintaining the full glass facade.
- **Rule**: Provide full-width interior curtain shading below the ceiling line along the entire glass wall; do not subdivide the glass to solve the sun problem.
- **Source**: p.47 (abb. 32 caption text)
- **Quote (DE)**: "niedrige körper vor der ganzen breite der glaswand. fenstervorhänge unter den decken entlang der gesamten glasfront gegen sonnenstrahlen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Low elements across the full width of the glass wall. Window curtains below the ceilings along the entire glass front against solar radiation."
- **Category**: function | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Glas als moderner Baustoff / Glass as Modern Building Material
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In der Entwicklung der modernen Bauweise wird das Glas als moderner Baustoff gerade infolge der zunehmenden Vergrößerung der Öffnungen eine wesentliche Rolle spielen. Seine Anwendung wird unbegrenzt sein und nicht auf das Fenster beschränkt bleiben; denn seine edlen Eigenschaften, seine durchsichtige Klarheit, seine leichte, schwebende, wesenlose Stofflichkeit verbürgen ihm die Liebe der modernen Baumeister. / "In the development of modern construction, glass as a modern building material will play an essential role, precisely on account of the increasing enlargement of openings. Its application will be unlimited and will not remain confined to the window; for its noble qualities, its transparent clarity, its light, hovering, immaterial substance guarantee it the love of modern builders."
- **Rule**: Treat glass not as window filler but as a primary building material with its own spatial and formal logic; apply it beyond the window opening.
- **Source**: p.49 (abb. 34 caption text)
- **Quote (DE)**: "in der entwicklung der modernen bauweise wird das glas als moderner baustoff gerade infolge der zunehmenden vergrößerung der öffnungen eine wesentliche rolle spielen. seine anwendung wird unbegrenzt sein und nicht auf das fenster beschränkt bleiben; denn seine edlen eigenschaften, seine durchsichtige klarheit, seine leichte, schwebende, wesenlose stofflichkeit verbürgen ihm die liebe der modernen baumeister."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the development of modern construction, glass as a modern building material will play an essential role, precisely on account of the increasing enlargement of openings. Its application will be unlimited and will not remain confined to the window; for its noble properties, its transparent clarity, its light, hovering, immaterial substance guarantee it the love of the modern builder."
- **Category**: material | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Spiegelglasscheibe — Ungeteilt / Undivided Mirror Glass Pane
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The main stairwell (Haupttreppenhaus) uses undivided ("ungeteilt") mirror glass panes — single uninterrupted sheets of reflective glass spanning the full opening. This is the extreme application of P-08: glass not as multiple panes in a subdivided frame, but as a single continuous surface. The reflection multiplies the space visually; the absence of divisions makes structure and sky continuous.
- **Rule**: Where possible, use the largest available glass pane without subdivision; the undivided glass surface is the formal ideal.
- **Source**: p.51 (abb. 36 caption: "blick vom haupttreppenhausfenster mit ungeteilter spiegelglasscheibe auf den werkstattbau")
- **Category**: material | transparency

## Haupttreppenhaus — Main Stairwell and View Through Glass

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The main stairwell is not a utilitarian circulation shaft — it is a designed visual instrument. The undivided mirror glass pane of its primary window frames the workshop building as a controlled view, while the stairwell landing functions as a viewing platform from which the building reads itself.
**Evidence**: abb. 38 — view from the stairwell landing through the glass to the "technische Lehranstalten" wing, with annotation that the Lüftungsflügel (ventilation panels) are "gekuppelte Drehfenster, die in jeder Stellung stehen bleiben" (paired pivoting windows that remain in any position).
**Conclusion**: Designed circulation = designed views. Every landing is an occasion for a composed visual relationship to the building's other parts.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Zirkulationsraum als Aussichtspunkt / Circulation Space as Viewing Platform
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The stairwell landing (Podest) in the main stairwell building faces the "technische Lehranstalten" (technical teaching institutes) through the full glass facade. The view is the designed content of the landing experience: one is placed at the glass and looks at the building's other wing. Circulation is not movement between rooms — it is the experience of the building's spatial organization.
- **Rule**: Design every major landing as a viewing point; orient it toward the most informative view of the building's composition.
- **Source**: p.53 (abb. 38)
- **Quote (DE)**: "blick vom podest des haupttreppenhauses im bau der 'technischen lehranstalten'. die lüftungsflügel sind gekuppelte drehfenster, die in jeder stellung stehen bleiben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "View from the landing of the main stairwell in the 'technical teaching institutes' building. The ventilation panels are paired pivoting windows that remain in any position."
- **Category**: space | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Lüftungsflügel — Gekuppelte Drehfenster / Ventilation Panels — Paired Pivoting Windows
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The ventilation panels in the large glass facade are "gekuppelte Drehfenster, die in jeder Stellung stehen bleiben" — paired pivoting windows that can be set to any angle and remain there without a latch mechanism. This is a detail that serves the glass facade's integrity: the ventilation opening is flush with the glass plane, does not project, and is mechanically self-positioning.
- **Rule**: Use pivoting (not sliding or casement) ventilation panels within large glass surfaces; design them to remain at any set angle without a latch.
- **Source**: p.53 (abb. 38 caption)
- **Category**: function | material

### Visual Evidence

- **Shows**: Interior photograph from the stairwell landing, looking through the full glass facade toward the "technische Lehranstalten" wing. ## Flachdach — The Flat Roof as Modern Standard

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Based on twenty years of experience with accessible and inaccessible flat roofs, Gropius argues that the pitched roof is becoming obsolete. The flat roof — properly constructed — is not only technically equivalent but architecturally and urbanistically superior. The roof terrace (Dachterrasse) is the formal and social culmination of this argument.
**Evidence**: abb. 39 — the roof terrace of the Atelierhaus with the gymnasium of the student building ("Gymnastiksaal der Studierenden"), the parapet designed as a bench, floor covering of laid stone slabs. Extended written argument on p.55 covering technical virtues, urban implications, and social program. abb. 40 — the roof terrace adjacent to the Speisesaal (dining hall), with people using it as outdoor dining space.
**Conclusion**: The Flachdach is the technically rational, economically superior, and urbanistically progressive roof form. The building at Dessau makes the argument physically: the Dachterrassen are not residual flat surfaces — they are programmed spaces.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Flachdach — Erfahrungsbasierte Überzeugung / Flat Roof — Experience-Based Conviction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Die erfolgreichen Erfahrungen, die in den letzten 20 Jahren mit der Konstruktion begehbarer und nicht begehbarer Horizontaldächer gemacht wurden, geben mir die Überzeugung, daß der technisch fortgeschrittene Mensch sich in der Zukunft ausschließlich des Horizontaldaches bedienen wird. / "The successful experiences made in the last 20 years with the construction of accessible and inaccessible horizontal roofs give me the conviction that the technically progressive person will in the future use exclusively the horizontal roof."
- **Rule**: Build flat roofs; design the accessible ones as programmed outdoor spaces with appropriate surface treatment.
- **Source**: p.55
- **Quote (DE)**: "die erfolgreichen erfahrungen, die in den letzten 20 jahren mit der konstruktion begehbarer und nicht begehbarer horizontaldächer gemacht wurden, geben mir die überzeugung, daß der technisch fortgeschrittene mensch sich in der zukunft ausschließlich des horizontaldaches bedienen wird."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The successful experiences made in the last 20 years with the construction of accessible and inaccessible horizontal roofs give me the conviction that the technically progressive person will in the future use exclusively the horizontal roof."
- **Category**: function | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Dachgärten auf Terrassen und Dächern / Roof Gardens on Terraces and Roofs
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The flat roof recaptures in the city what the garden recaptures in nature. "Sie werden mit ihren Gärten auf Terrassen und Dächern — vom Luftweg aus gesehen — den eindruck eines großen Gartens geben. Der durch den Bau der Häuser verlorene begrünte Boden wird auf den flachen Dächern wiedergewonnen." / "With their gardens on terraces and roofs — seen from the air — they will give the impression of a large garden. The green ground lost through the construction of houses will be recovered on the flat roofs."
- **Rule**: Program flat roofs as gardens and terraces; treat them as ground recovered at height.
- **Source**: p.55
- **Quote (DE)**: "sie werden mit ihren gärten auf terrassen und dächern — vom luftweg aus gesehen — den eindruck eines großen gartens geben. der durch den bau der häuser verlorene begrünte boden wird auf den flachen dächern wiedergewonnen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "With their gardens on terraces and roofs — seen from the air — they will give the impression of a large garden. The green ground lost through building houses will be recovered on the flat roofs."
- **Category**: urbanism | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Technische Vorteile des Flachdachs / Technical Advantages of the Flat Roof
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The flat roof's advantages over the pitched roof: (1) Clear rectangular attic spaces instead of the unusable dead angles under sloped roofs; (2) avoidance of wooden roof structure — fire risk; (3) usability of roof surfaces for habitation (children's playgrounds, laundry drying, etc.); (4) better accessibility and lower maintenance costs; (5) absence of gutters, downpipes, flashings in perishable zinc.
- **Rule**: Enumerate the technical and maintenance advantages of the flat roof; the pitched roof is not merely aesthetically wrong but technically inferior.
- **Source**: p.55
- **Category**: function | standardization

## Leben im Bauhaus — Community Life and the Building as Social Infrastructure

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Bauhaus building is not only an instrument for work — it is the physical container and enabler of a community life that includes music, gymnastics, sport, theater, and collective celebration (Bauhausfeste). The building's formal qualities — the roof terrace, the stage, the open ground between the wings — make this community life possible. Architecture enables sociality.
**Evidence**: abb. 41–47 (six photographs on two pages) — "Leben im Bauhaus": musicians playing on the roof, gymnastics on the roof terrace, ball games with the building in the background, theatrical performance on the Bühne (stage). abb. 48 — night photograph of the entire Bauhaus building illuminated for the Bauhausfest, caption text describing the tradition of Bauhausfeste continued from Weimar, with thousands of participants from inside and outside.
**Conclusion**: The building is the stage for the Bauhaus community's life. The Bauhausfest is the annual demonstration that the architecture works — that it can hold and amplify collective experience.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Architektur als soziale Infrastruktur / Architecture as Social Infrastructure
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The Bauhaus building enables community life not through symbolic gesture but through functional provision: the Dachterrasse provides the outdoor gymnasium and social space; the Bühne provides the performance space; the Speisesaal and its terrace provide the communal dining space. Each programmatic element generates a corresponding social practice. The building does not merely shelter activity — it produces it.
- **Rule**: Provide explicit spatial support for every mode of communal life — work, performance, sport, dining, celebration; do not rely on residual space.
- **Source**: p.56–58 (abb. 41–47)
- **Category**: pedagogy | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Bauhausfest — Gebäude als Veranstaltungsraum / Bauhausfest — Building as Event Space
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Since the Weimar Bauhaus era, the tradition of the "Bauhausfest" had been maintained. Masters and students improvised festivities, stage sets, costumes, and theatrical performances. These events became one of the strongest means of social cohesion within the Bauhaus. Thousands of participants from inside and outside. The building, seen at night with all its floors illuminated, is itself the image of this collective life.
- **Rule**: Design the building to be fully habitable at night and to read as a luminous object; the illuminated building is the event.
- **Source**: p.58–59 (abb. 47 text, abb. 48)
- **Quote (DE)**: "seit den weimarer bauhauszeiten hatte sich eine tradition der 'bauhausfeste' entwickelt: willkommene aufgaben, um die einfälle von meistern und studierenden improvisierten aufzuführen, festräume, kostüme, aufführungen. diese feste waren eins der stärksten mittel der freundschaftlichen atmosphäre im bauhaus und wurden allen teilnehmern zu einem unvergänglichen erlebnis."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Since the Weimar Bauhaus era a tradition of 'Bauhaus festivals' had developed: welcome occasions for improvising the ideas of masters and students, festival rooms, costumes, performances. These festivals were one of the strongest means of the friendly atmosphere at the Bauhaus and became an unforgettable experience for all participants."
- **Category**: pedagogy | space
