## Meisterhäuser — Einzelhaus Gropius: Interior Fittings and Functional Equipment

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The interior of the Einzelhaus Gropius demonstrates that rational spatial organization does not require large rooms — it requires precisely designed storage, fitted furniture, and equipment positioned for minimum effort and maximum utility. Every square meter must work. The building's social argument is made visible in its fittings.

**Evidence**: An extensive photographic sequence (Abb. 109–123), many shot as film strips (Humboldt-Film, Berlin; Foto Lucia Moholy, Berlin), documents the Wäschewandschrank, the built-in Kleiderschrank, the Toilettentisch, the kitchen with Spüle and Geschirrkorb, the kitchen cabinetry by Marcel Breuer (Schränke: M. Breuer), the ironing board cupboard (Schrank für Plättbrett und Plätteisen), the laundry storage cupboard (dreiteiliger eingebauter Schrank für gebrauchte Wäsche), the bathroom with Kristallglasscheiben walls, and the Waschküche with motorized drum washer and centrifuge.

**Conclusion**: Interior equipment is not decoration added after architecture is fixed — it is architecture. The fitted wardrobe between bedrooms, the ironing board that folds out of a wall cupboard, the kitchen with Geschirrkorb and Tellerabtropfregal: these are spatial decisions, not product choices. The building teaches by equipping.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Eingebaut / Built-in furniture as spatial argument
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The main bedroom of the Einzelhaus Gropius contains a Wäschewandschrank (wall-mounted linen cupboard) documented in three film frames showing a woman opening, accessing, and using the unit. The built-in quality is not incidental — it eliminates freestanding furniture mass, keeps the room perceptually open, and assigns each function a permanent, sized location in the wall.
- **Rule**: Build storage into the structure; never add freestanding furniture where a wall niche or fitted unit can serve the same function more efficiently.
- **Source**: p.119
- **Quote (DE)**: "wäschewandschrank im hauptschlafzimmer des einzelhauses gropius"
- **Quote (EN)**: "wall-mounted linen cupboard in the main bedroom of the Gropius detached house"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Kleiderschrank zwischen Schlafzimmern / Wardrobe as acoustic and spatial separator
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Between the two bedrooms of the Einzelhaus Gropius, a built-in Kleiderschrank spans the full height of the partition. It is accessible from both sides and is documented in a still photograph (Abb. 110) showing the view through the open wardrobe from one bedroom into the other. The caption states: "das licht schaltet sich beim öffnen und schließen der tür schlagartig ein und aus" — light switches on and off sharply when the door opens and closes.
- **Rule**: Use the wardrobe or storage element as a room divider: it simultaneously separates two spaces acoustically, provides storage for both, and creates a visual connection when opened.
- **Source**: p.120
- **Quote (DE)**: "zwischen zwei schlafzimmern eingebauter begehbarer kleiderschrank im einzelhaus — das licht schaltet sich beim öffnen und schließen der tür schlagartig ein und aus"
- **Quote (EN)**: "walk-in wardrobe built between two bedrooms in the detached house — the light switches sharply on and off when the door opens and closes"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Begehbarer Kleiderschrank / Walk-through wardrobe as movement sequence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Kleiderschrank is begehbar — you walk through it, not just open a door to it. Three film frames (Abb. 111) show a figure entering, moving within, and exiting the wardrobe space. The movement through the wardrobe is itself a designed sequence: the wardrobe is Zirkulationsraum, not dead storage.
- **Rule**: When space permits, design storage as traversable volume: the act of passing through the storage area becomes functional movement, not wasted circulation.
- **Source**: p.121
- **Quote (DE)**: "begehbarer kleiderschrank im einzelhaus gropius (siehe abb. 110)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "walk-in wardrobe in the Gropius detached house (see fig. 110)"
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Kristallglasscheiben im Badezimmer / Crystal glass wall tiles
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bedroom washbasin area in the Einzelhaus Gropius (Abb. 112) shows a curtain separating the washbasin from the sleeping zone — demonstrating that a single room can serve multiple functions when flexible screening is used. The bathroom proper (Abb. 122) has its walls clad entirely in Kristallglasscheiben (crystal glass tiles/panes). This material decision is explicitly stated: "die wände sind mit kristallglasscheiben belegt."
- **Rule**: Use non-porous, reflective wall surfacing (glass, glazed tile) in wet rooms: hygienic, dimensionally stable, and visually expansive under artificial light.
- **Source**: p.122 (Abb. 122), p.122 (Abb. 112)
- **Quote (DE)**: "badezimmer im einzelhaus gropius — die wände sind mit kristallglasscheiben belegt"
- **Quote (EN)**: "bathroom in the Gropius detached house — the walls are clad with crystal glass panes"
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Toilettentisch mit Spiegeln / Mirror-lined dressing table as multiplication of view
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Gastzimmer (guest room) of the Einzelhaus Gropius contains a Toilettentisch lined with mirrors on multiple faces (Abb. 113). The photograph shows the mirrors multiplying the reflection of the room and the objects placed on the glass shelves. The Breuer-designed cantilevered tubular steel stools (Stahlrohrstühle) are visible. The dressing table is itself a glass-and-steel construction — transparent and lightweight.
- **Rule**: Design dressing/toilet furniture from glass and tubular steel: minimal mass, maximum visual expansion through reflection, hygienically cleanable.
- **Source**: p.123
- **Quote (DE)**: "mit spiegeln ausgekleidete toilettentische im gastzimmer des einzelhauses gropius"
- **Quote (EN)**: "mirror-lined dressing table in the guest room of the Gropius detached house"
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Spüle mit Geschirrkorb und Tellerabtropfregal / Kitchen sink unit as integrated workflow
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kitchen of the Einzelhaus Gropius (Abb. 114–115) is documented in still photograph and three film frames. The caption specifies: "heißwasser-soda-dusche; geschirrkorb; tellerabtropfregal" — hot water/soda spray, dish basket, plate draining rack. These three elements form a continuous workflow: spray → basket → rack. The sequence is spatial: each element is positioned at the correct height and adjacency for the next operation.
- **Rule**: Design the kitchen sink zone as a linear workflow sequence: spray/rinse station → draining basket → drying rack, each element sized and positioned for immediate transfer without lifting or crossing.
- **Source**: p.124–125
- **Quote (DE)**: "spüle des einzelhauses gropius — heißwasser-soda-dusche; geschirrkorb; tellerabtropfregal"
- **Quote (EN)**: "sink of the Gropius detached house — hot water/soda spray; dish basket; plate draining rack"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Geschirrduchrreiche / Pass-through between kitchen and dining room
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kitchen of the Einzelhaus Gropius (Abb. 116) contains a Geschirrduchrreiche zur Spüle — a pass-through opening in the wall between kitchen and dining room, sized for passing dishes. The Küchenschrank is attributed to Marcel Breuer. The photograph shows the white-painted cabinetry, pass-through hatch, and the kitchen oven.
- **Rule**: Insert a Geschirrduchrreiche (dish pass-through) in the wall between kitchen and adjacent dining space: eliminates door-opening when carrying dishes, reduces circulation path by half.
- **Source**: p.126
- **Quote (DE)**: "küche im einzelhaus gropius mit geschirrduchrreiche zur spüle — küchenschrank: m. breuer"
- **Quote (EN)**: "kitchen in the Gropius detached house with dish pass-through to the sink — kitchen cabinet: M. Breuer"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Einflügelige Geschirr- und Besteckschrank / Single-wing cabinet between dining room and kitchen
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Abb. 117 documents in three film frames a Einflügeliger Geschirr- und Besteckschrank positioned between Eßzimmer and Spüle, accessible from both sides. The caption states it opens "beiderseits" — from both sides. This bidirectional access cabinet is the interior-space equivalent of the Vorhangfassade logic: the boundary between two zones becomes the functional element itself, not a barrier.
- **Rule**: Position storage cabinets on the boundary between serving and eating zones, opening to both: the boundary becomes useful, not merely separating.
- **Source**: p.127
- **Quote (DE)**: "einflügeliger geschirr- und besteckschrank zwischen eßzimmer und spüle, beiderseits zu öffnen (m. gropius und m. breuer)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "single-wing crockery and cutlery cabinet between dining room and sink, opening from both sides (M. Gropius and M. Breuer)"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Schränke nach Breuer / Breuer kitchen cabinets — modular standardization

The gas stove (with separate gas oven below) and tile storage cabinet complete the wall. Attribution is explicit: "schränke: m. breuer."
- **Rule**: Standardize all kitchen cabinet units to a single depth, height, and door module: the wall becomes a continuous working surface with clear visual order.
- **Source**: p.128
- **Quote (DE)**: "küche im einzelhaus gropius — schränke: m. breuer"
- **Quote (EN)**: "kitchen in the Gropius detached house — cabinets: M. Breuer"

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Topfschrank mit vertikal gestaffelten Räumen / Pot cupboard with vertically staged shelves
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Abb. 119 (three film frames, Humboldt-Film / Berlin) documents the Topfschrank — pot cupboard — in the kitchen, with vertically staged shelves accessed through a hinged door. The film frames show a woman accessing different heights of the cupboard, demonstrating the ergonomics: the most-used items at mid-height, less-used above and below.
- **Rule**: Stage cupboard shelves vertically by frequency of use: most-used items at elbow height, least-used at top and bottom — the cupboard's interior is an ergonomic sequence, not random storage.
- **Source**: p.129
- **Quote (DE)**: "topfschrank mit vertikal gestaffelten räumen in der küche des einzelhauses gropius (m. breuer)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "pot cupboard with vertically staged compartments in the kitchen of the Gropius detached house (M. Breuer)"

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Schrank für Plättbrett / Ironing board cupboard — fold-out function
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Abb. 120 shows a Schrank für Plättbrett und Plätteisen — a wall cupboard designed to house the ironing board and iron. A composite photograph shows simultaneously the board in stored position and in extended position. When deployed, the board projects horizontally at working height from the open cupboard door. When not in use, it folds back entirely within the cupboard thickness.
- **Rule**: Design fold-out work surfaces (ironing boards, fold-down desks, fold-up beds) as wall cupboard elements: the function is present only when needed, absent from the room when not in use.
- **Source**: p.130
- **Quote (DE)**: "schrank für plättbrett und plätteisen im einzelhaus gropius — die kombinierte aufnahme zeigt gleichzeitig das heraus- und hereingeklappte plättbrett"
- **Quote (EN)**: "cupboard for ironing board and iron in the Gropius detached house — the composite photograph shows simultaneously the extended and folded-in ironing board"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Dreiteiliger Wäscheschrank im Treppenhaus / Three-part laundry cupboard ventilated through the wall
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Abb. 121 (three film frames, Humboldt-Film / Berlin) shows a dreiteiliger eingebauter Schrank für gebrauchte Wäsche (three-part built-in cupboard for used laundry) positioned in the Treppenhaus of the Einzelhaus Gropius. - **Rule**: Locate the used-laundry cupboard in the staircase, ventilate it to outside air through the wall, and subdivide it into labeled compartments by linen type: hygiene by ventilation, order by categorization.
- **Source**: p.131
- **Quote (DE)**: "dreiteiliger eingebauter schrank für gebrauchte wäsche im treppenhaus des einzelhauses gropius — der schrank ist nach außen durch die wand entlüftet; getrennte abteilungen für tisch-, bett- und leibwäsche"

## Meisterhäuser — Doppelhäuser: Exterior, Text, and Plans

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Doppelhäuser (double houses for Bauhaus masters) demonstrate that the same architectural vocabulary governing the main Bauhaus building applies equally at domestic scale. A new spatial feeling — Raumgefühl — replaces the old sense of enclosed, earth-bound mass. The building is experienced as movement and opening, not as shelter and enclosure. The 90° rotation of one unit relative to its twin achieves visual isolation between two identical plans.

**Evidence**: Abb. 124 (view of a Doppelhaus from the roof of the Einzelhaus, showing terraces, balconies, and cantilevered concrete slabs in all storeys), Abb. 125 (east elevation of a Doppelhaus, showing horizontal banding, south-facing garden facade with balconies and large windows), Abb. 126 (ground floor plan of the Doppelhaus, showing the 90° rotation relationship between the two units), Abb. 127 (upper floor plan of the Doppelhaus, showing Atelier spaces and Schlafzimmer with balconies), Abb. 128 (exterior photograph: terrace seating area in front of the Altzimmer of the Doppelhäuser).

**Conclusion**: The Doppelhaus is not two houses side by side. It is a demonstration that spatial economy, the new Raumgefühl, and site independence from the street can be achieved simultaneously through intelligent rotation, cantilevered Massivdecken, and Dachterrasse access from all rooms.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Raumgefühl verändert sich / The spatial feeling has changed
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius articulates a fundamental shift in spatial culture: the old sense of space — expressed in closed, monolithic, earth-bound building forms and individualized interiors — has been superseded. Today's progressive architecture reflects the movement, velocity, and interconnectedness of contemporary life by loosening the building mass and seeking to recover the connection of interior to the infinite spatial continuum.
- **Rule**: Design buildings as open spatial organisms, not as enclosed masses: the boundary between interior and exterior is a designed threshold, not a wall of separation — the Innenraum reaches toward the Allraum.
- **Source**: p.135
- **Quote (DE)**: "das raumgefühl verändert sich; während die alten zeiten abgeschlossener kulturentwicklungen die schwere erdgebundenheit in festen, monolit wirkenden baukörpern und individualisierten innenräumen verkörperten, zeigen die werke der heutigen, richtunggebenden baumeister ein verändertes raumempfinden, das die bewegung, den verkehr unserer zeit in einer auflockerung der baukörper und räume widerspiegelt und den zusammenhang des innenraums mit dem allraum zu erhalten sucht, was die abschließende wand verneint."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The spatial feeling changes; while the old times of closed cultural developments embodied the heavy earth-boundedness in solid, monolith-like building forms and individualized interiors, the works of today's direction-giving architects show a changed spatial sensibility, which mirrors the movement and traffic of our time in a loosening of the building masses and spaces, and seeks to preserve the connection of the interior with the infinite spatial continuum — which the closing wall denies."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Raumgröße kann herabgesetzt werden / Room size can be reduced without loss
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius makes a direct economic and social argument: room sizes can be reduced without disadvantage, given the growing pressure of housing density. This is not poverty — it speaks for the difficulty of the housing question. What decides quality is not square meters but the organization of the Wohnorganismus: how the household is arranged, how organization and technology relieve the housewife so that her energy is available for her own development and for the children.
- **Rule**: Reduce room dimensions only when organization and fitted equipment compensate for the reduction: smaller rooms are acceptable if the Wohnorganismus — the spatial-functional organism of the household — is precisely designed.
- **Source**: p.137
- **Quote (DE)**: "die größe der räume kann sich ohne schaden herabsetzen lassen, zunehmende einer steigerung des wohnkomforts. die zunehmende schwierigkeit der hausgestellungsfrage, die z. b. in amerika schon zu lebensverändernden maßnahmen zwingt, spricht für die disposition des wohnorganismus entscheidend mit. organisation und technik müssen die hausfreu davon bewahren, daß ihre kräfte allein ins hausdienet verbraucht werden, statt daß sie für die eigene geistige entwicklung und für die erziehung der kinder frei bleiben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The size of rooms can be reduced without disadvantage, accompanying an increase in domestic comfort. The growing difficulty of the housing question, which in America, for example, already forces life-changing measures, speaks decisively for the arrangement of the domestic organism. Organization and technology must protect the housewife from having her energy consumed entirely in housework, instead of leaving her free for her own intellectual development and for raising the children."
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: 90°-Drehung des Doppelhauses / 90° rotation of the paired unit for visual isolation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Doppelhaus ground floor plan (Abb. 126) shows the two units: one unit is rotated 90° relative to the other. The caption states the purpose explicitly: "die linke wohnung ist nach außen um 90° von sol nach süd gedreht und ist folglich spiegelbild der anderen; dadurch: verwendung gleicher baustücke, der wechselnde ansichten; optische isolierung."
- **Rule**: When pairing two identical dwelling units, rotate one 90° relative to the other: the result is the use of identical building components (Baustücke), varied exterior elevations, and visual isolation between the two units despite structural identity.
- **Source**: p.136
- **Quote (DE)**: "erdgeschoßgrundriß eines doppelhauses — die linke wohnung ist nach außen um 90° von sol nach süd gedreht und ist folglich spiegelbild der anderen; dadurch: verwendung gleicher baustücke; der wechselnde ansichten; optische isolierung"
- **Quote (EN)**: "ground floor plan of a double house — the left dwelling is rotated 90° outward from east to south and is consequently the mirror image of the other; thereby: use of identical building components; varied elevations; visual isolation"
- **Category**: standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Terrassen und Balkone in allen Etagen / Terraces and balconies at all floor levels
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Abb. 124 shows the view of a Doppelhaus from the roof of the Einzelhaus Gropius. The caption states: "terrassen und balkonvorbauten in allen etagen, austrittsmöglichkeit ins freie von fast allen räumen aus — die balkone sind durch stützenlose auskragungen der massivdecken gebildet."
- **Rule**: Provide terrace or balcony access from every floor level; form balconies as cantilevered extensions of the Massivdecken (solid concrete slabs) — no supporting columns below, no visual obstruction to the ground.
- **Source**: p.134
- **Quote (DE)**: "ostansicht eines doppelhauses vom dach des einzelhauses aus gesehen — terrassen und balkonvorbauten in allen etagen, austrittsmöglichkeit ins freie von fast allen räumen aus — die balkone sind durch stützenlose auskragungen der massivdecken gebildet"
- **Quote (EN)**: "east view of a double house seen from the roof of the detached house — terraces and balcony projections at all floors, possibility of stepping outside from almost all rooms — the balconies are formed by unsupported cantilevers of the solid concrete slabs"
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Atelier im Obergeschoß / Atelier on upper floor
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The upper floor plan (Abb. 127) shows that each unit of the Doppelhaus has an Atelier — a studio — on the upper floor, in addition to Schlafzimmer and Balkon. The Atelier is not a storage room or ancillary space: it is a primary designed room, sized for creative work, positioned at the upper level for light. The Bauhaus masters are not merely teachers — they are practicing artists. Their houses accommodate practice.
- **Rule**: In housing for creative professionals, include a dedicated Atelier room at the upper level: this is a functional room, not a luxury — it recognizes that intellectual and artistic work requires a specific spatial condition distinct from living and sleeping.
- **Source**: p.137
- **Quote (DE)**: "obergeschoßgrundriß eines doppelhauses — [atelier visible on plan for both units]"
- **Quote (EN)**: "upper floor plan of a double house — [atelier visible on plan for both units]"
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Sitzplatz vor dem Altzimmer / Outdoor terrace seat adjacent to the living room
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Abb. 128 shows the Sitzplatz (outdoor seating area) in front of the Altzimmer (living room) of the Doppelhäuser. The photograph shows the terrace level: flat pavement, a low parapet wall, the south-facing garden elevation of the house with its large windows behind. The tree trunks of the pine forest frame the view. The Sitzplatz is not a garden — it is an extension of the interior floor level, a designed outdoor room.
- **Rule**: Position an outdoor Sitzplatz directly adjacent to and at the same floor level as the Wohnraum, separated only by the glazed facade: the outdoor room is continuous with the interior, not separated from it by a step or grade change.
- **Source**: p.138
- **Quote (DE)**: "sitzplatz vor dem altzimmer der doppelhäuser"
- **Quote (EN)**: "outdoor seating area in front of the living room of the double houses"
- **Category**: space
