## Front Matter — Title, Contents, Publication Data

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Dessau Bauhaus building is not merely a school building — it is a complete architectural argument, a built demonstration of the principles the school itself teaches. The book presents this building as a technical and philosophical document simultaneously.
**Evidence**: Cover illustration (axonometric of Meisterhäuser), title page hierarchy, table of contents listing five distinct building projects, colophon crediting the typography and cover design to L. Moholy-Nagy, and the publisher Albert Langen Verlag, München, 1930.
**Conclusion**: Even the book's production is an exercise in Bauhaus method: typography by Moholy-Nagy, printing by Hesse & Becker Leipzig, clichés by C. Dünnhaupt Dessau. The object demonstrates what it describes.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Sachlicher Stil / Objective functional style
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bauhausbücher series adopts a uniform, functional typographic system — lowercase throughout, no ornament, hierarchy established through weight and size alone. Volume 12 continues this systematic approach: the cover uses black and red (Bauhaus's standard two-color system), the number "12" in large bold type, and the author's name in a reduced weight. The book is designed as a product, not a prestige object.
- **Rule**: Establish visual hierarchy through weight, size, and color alone — never through ornamental addition.
- **Source**: p.0 (cover), p.3 (title page)
- **Quote (DE)**: "bauhausbücher 12 / schriftleitung: walter gropius / l. moholy-nagy"
- **Quote (EN)**: "bauhaus books 12 / editorial direction: walter gropius / l. moholy-nagy"
- **Category**: transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Einheit im Maßstab — Buchgestaltung als Bauhausprodukt / Unity of scale — book design as Bauhaus product
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bauhausbücher are themselves Bauhaus workshop products. The cover design (Moholy-Nagy), typography, binding, and printing are all credited explicitly. Every material decision is attributed. The book's production chain is made visible, not hidden — consistent with the building's exposed structural logic.
- **Rule**: Make the production chain visible; credit every collaborator and material decision.
- **Source**: p.5 (copyright page)

## Inhalt — Table of Contents

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The book presents five distinct building projects, not one. The Bauhaus building is the centerpiece, but the volume documents a complete body of work demonstrating Gropius's Dessau practice: school building, masters' houses, worker housing estate, consumer cooperative buildings, and labor office.
**Evidence**: The table of contents lists: vorwort p.7 | bauhausgebäude dessau p.13 | wohnungen der bauhausmeister, dessau p.84 | siedlung dessau-törten und gebäude des konsumvereins dessau p.152 | arbeitsamt, dessau p.201.
**Conclusion**: The five projects together form a single argument about the application of Bauhaus principles across building types: educational institution, domestic housing (individual masters' houses and collective worker settlement), commercial building, and public civic institution. Same vocabulary, five different programs, five different scales.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Funktionsgliederung über Bautypologie / Functional articulation across building typologies
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: By presenting five building types in a single volume, Gropius argues implicitly that the principles demonstrated in the Bauhausgebäude are not type-specific. The same structural logic, the same material vocabulary, the same Lichtführung principles apply whether the building is a school, a house, a workers' settlement, or a labor exchange office.
- **Rule**: Apply functional principles consistently across all building types — the principle precedes the typology.
- **Source**: p.6 (contents page)
- **Quote (DE)**: "inhalt: bauhausgebäude dessau · wohnungen der bauhausmeister, dessau · siedlung dessau-törten · arbeitsamt, dessau"
- **Quote (EN)**: "contents: bauhaus building dessau · apartments of the bauhaus masters, dessau · dessau-törten settlement · labor office, dessau"

## Vorwort — Foreword (pp. 7–12)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Bauhaus was founded not as a style-producing institution but as a method — a synthesis of craft knowledge and industrial technique, directed toward solving the living conditions of modern life. The Dessau building and this book are both products of that method applied to its own organizational context.
**Evidence**: Gropius narrates the founding of the Bauhaus in Weimar (spring 1919), the political pressures, the move to Dessau (1925), the construction and inauguration of the new building (December 1926), and the subsequent projects. He explains why photography and sequential image presentation are necessary to convey spatial experience that a single view cannot capture.
**Conclusion**: The building is a report ("bericht") on a completed phase of work. The book's production method — sequential photographs creating the illusion of movement through space — is itself a designed experience, not a documentation convention.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Bauhaus als Synthese von Kunst und Technik / Bauhaus as synthesis of art and technology
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius states the Bauhaus was founded to achieve "die vereinigung aller werkkünnstlerischen und technischen disziplinen zu einer neuen baukunst" — the unification of all craft-artistic and technical disciplines into a new architecture. This is not collaboration between separate domains; it is a genuine synthesis where neither art nor craft nor engineering is subordinate.
- **Rule**: Design must synthesize technical production knowledge and formal artistic intelligence — neither alone is sufficient.
- **Source**: p.7 (p.8 in book)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die vereinigung aller werkkünnstlerischen und technischen disziplinen zu einer neuen baukunst als deren unablösliche bestandteile, zu einer baukunst also, die dem lebendigen leben dient."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the unification of all craft-artistic and technical disciplines into a new architecture as their inseparable components — an architecture that serves living life."
- **Category**: pedagogy | workshop-integration

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Geringste Mittel, größte Wirkung / Minimum means, maximum effect
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius states explicitly that the requirement of the age is to achieve "mit geringsten mitteln größte wirkung" — with minimum means maximum effect. This is not aesthetic minimalism but an economic and intellectual discipline: the mastery of technical means and the economy of the mind ("ökonomie des geistigen") must work together. Material economy and conceptual economy are one.
- **Rule**: Achieve maximum spatial and functional effect with minimum material and structural means — economy is not reduction but mastery.
- **Source**: p.9 (book p.9)
- **Quote (DE)**: "um diese forderungen zu erfüllen, muß 'mit geringsten mitteln größte wirkung' erreicht werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "to fulfill these requirements, 'maximum effect with minimum means' must be achieved."
- **Category**: function | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Standardisierung als Befreiung / Standardization as liberation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius argues that standardization ("standardisierung der produktionsvorgänge") does not enslave the individual — it frees life from unnecessary ballast. Standardization is the precondition for maximum personal freedom and independence. This inverts the Romantic critique of industrial production: the standard element liberates rather than imprisons.
- **Rule**: Use standardized elements as the basis for design — this frees the designer and user to concentrate on what matters: spatial quality, light, and functional precision.
- **Source**: p.9
- **Quote (DE)**: "die standardisierung der produktionsvorgänge, die zu einem maximum an persönlicher freiheit und unabhängigkeit zu gelangen. [...] befreit das leben von unnötigem ballast, um es desto ungehemmter und reicher sich entfalten zu lassen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the standardization of production processes [leads] to a maximum of personal freedom and independence. [...] it frees life from unnecessary ballast, so that it can unfold all the more uninhibitedly and richly."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Das Kunstwerk als Produkt — künstlerische Gestaltung durch technische Mittel / The artwork as product — artistic design through technical means
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius argues the "Kunstwerk" is in its spiritual dimension exactly like a machine product ("funktioniert"): the self-evident purpose of an airplane is to fly. The designer can learn from the engineer's product. But the Kunstwerk is also a unique result — "ein einmaliges resultat, ein in sich abgeschlossener subjektiver mikrokosmos" — whose moral copyright belongs to its creator. Technical means serve artistic ends; they do not replace them.
- **Rule**: Design to function completely — then and only then does it achieve formal integrity. Function and form are not opposed; incomplete function produces incomplete form.
- **Source**: p.10
- **Quote (DE)**: "das 'kunstwerk' hat im geistigen wie im materiellen sinne genau so zu 'funktionieren' wie das erzeugnis des ingenieurs."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the 'artwork' must 'function' in the spiritual as in the material sense exactly like the product of the engineer."
- **Category**: function | workshop-integration

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Der Raum als Problem — Raumgestaltung aus Abstraktion / Space as problem — spatial design from abstraction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius states that the modern "Raumgestalter" begins from the modern understanding of abstract space ("abstrakte raumes") — not from precedent, not from historical style. This is identified as the cause of their difficulty with "Bildplastikerei" (pictorial decoration): they have moved past representation to the direct design of spatial experience. The Bauhaus teaching of space ("grundlegende kapitel 'der raum' in Moholy-Nagy's 'von material zu architektur'") is the foundation.
- **Rule**: Begin spatial design from the abstract properties of space itself — proportion, light, movement, boundary — not from historical typological precedent.
- **Source**: p.10 (footnote 1 references Moholy-Nagy's "von material zu architektur," bauhausbücher bd.14)
- **Quote (DE)**: "von den produktenmachern unter ihnen waren es die modernen gestalter, die von der auffassung des abstrakten raumes ausgegangen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "among them, the modern designers were those who began from the conception of abstract space."
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Fotografie als Raumerlebnis-Instrument / Photography as spatial experience instrument
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius explicitly states why photography is limited ("die mittel der darstellung von bauten in einem buch sind beschränkt") and how he intends to overcome this: not through single views but through sequential image series that simulate movement through the building — "durch den wechsel der illusion des gedachten räumlichen ablaufs zu vermitteln." The reader is led through the building photographically as they would be spatially.
- **Rule**: Present buildings through sequential photographs that simulate movement and the experience of changing spatial states — not through single definitive views.
- **Source**: p.11–12 (book pp. 11–12)
- **Quote (DE)**: "ich glaubte, die wesentliche dieser bauten, die ordnung der sich in ihnen abspielenden lebensfunktionen und den daraus resultierenden räumlichen ausdruck, aus allen diesen gründen nur dadurch wiedergeben zu können, daß ich den leser nacheinander an zahlreichen bildausschnitten vorbeiführte, um ihm durch diesen wechsel der illusion des gedachten räumlichen ablaufs zu vermitteln."
- **Quote (EN)**: "I believed the essence of these buildings — the organization of the life-functions taking place within them and the spatial expression resulting from them — could only be conveyed by leading the reader successively past numerous image fragments, to communicate through this sequence the illusion of imagined spatial movement."
- **Category**: space | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Das Bauhaus als Gemeinschaftswerk / The Bauhaus as collective work
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius is careful to attribute the building's design not solely to himself but to the collective: "die stadt dessau hatte mir für sämtliche bauten die gesamtregie — planung, vergabe und bauleitung — übertragen, sodaß auch alle einzelarbeit bei mir im bauhaus zusammenfloß." But he also names: the Architektur-Abteilung for plans and execution (with named collaborators including Marcel Breuer, Herbert Bayer, Hinnerk Scheper, Joost Schmidt, Gunta Stölzl), the Weberei, Möbel- und Stahlrohr, Wandmalerei, and Druckereiabteilung — each workshop contributing its domain to the whole building.
- **Rule**: Assign every building component to the workshop most competent to design it — the building is a field for collective competence, not a single designer's total vision.
- **Source**: p.13 (book p.12, footnotes)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die farbige raumgestaltung des gesamten baues wurde von der wandmalereiabteilung des bauhauses durchgeführt, sämtliche beleuchtungskörper stammen in konstruktion und ausführung aus der metallwerkstatt des bauhauses, die stahlrohrmöbel der aula, des speisesaales und der ateliers wurden nach entwürfen von m. breuer hergestellt, die beschriftungen führte die druckereiabteilung des bauhauses aus."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the color design of all spaces was carried out by the wall-painting workshop of the Bauhaus; all lighting fixtures originate in construction and execution from the metal workshop of the Bauhaus; the tubular steel furniture of the aula, dining hall, and studios was made after designs by M. Breuer; the lettering was carried out by the printing workshop of the Bauhaus."
- **Category**: workshop-integration | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Luftbild als neue gestalterische Forderung / Aerial view as new design imperative
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: On p.16, Gropius places a text caption beneath the first aerial photograph that reads: "die verkehrswege in der luft erheben eine neue forderung an die erbauer von häusern und städten: auch das bild der bauten aus der vogelschau, das die menschen in früheren zeiten nicht zu gesicht bekamen, bewußt zu gestalten." Air traffic routes create a new demand on builders of houses and cities: to consciously design the aerial view — the bird's-eye image that people in earlier times never saw.
- **Rule**: Design buildings to be read simultaneously from the ground, at medium distance, and from the air — the roofscape and plan composition are not residual but designed surfaces.
- **Source**: p.16 (book p.16)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die verkehrswege in der luft erheben eine neue forderung an die erbauer von häusern und städten: auch das bild der bauten aus der vogelschau, das die menschen in früheren zeiten nicht zu gesicht bekamen, bewußt zu gestalten."
- **Quote (EN)**: "air traffic routes create a new demand on the builders of houses and cities: to consciously design also the image of buildings from the bird's-eye view — the view that people in earlier times never encountered."
- **Category**: urbanism | space | transparency

## Das Bauhausgebäude Dessau — Program, Construction, Materials (pp. 14–15)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Bauhaus building is a precisely defined program: three functionally distinct wings serving three distinct constituencies (technical school, workshop-laboratory, student residence), connected by designed circulation. The material and construction system is fully integrated with the formal vocabulary — no decoration applied from outside.
**Evidence**: Gropius provides: total floor area (2630 m² Grundfläche), total enclosed volume (ca. 32,450 cbm), total construction cost (902,500 Mark = 27.8 Mk per cbm), procurement cost (126,200 Mk). Building commenced autumn 1925, completed December 1926. Three-part program described in detail. Full material and construction specification provided.
**Conclusion**: The numbers are an argument. At 27.8 Mk/cbm the building is economical. Economy is achieved not through cheapness but through rational organization: the Funktionsgliederung eliminates redundant circulation, the curtain wall eliminates heavy masonry, the flat roof eliminates the pitched roof's wasted volume. Every formal decision is also a cost decision.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Dreigliederung des Baukomplexes / Three-part division of the building complex
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The total building complex consists of three formally and functionally distinct parts: (1) the Flügelbau der "technischen Lehranstalten" (wing of the technical schools — later Berufsschule), containing teaching rooms, library, physics lab, model workshops, and a fully developed basement floor; (2) the Laboratoriumswerkstätten und Lehrräume des Bauhauses (laboratory workshops and teaching spaces of the Bauhaus), containing stage workshop, printing, dyeing, sculpture, packing and storage, canteen, exhibition spaces, large vestibule and stage in the basement; weaving workshop, basic course rooms, a large lecture hall, and the two-story bridge to the technical school wing at first floor; painting, metalwork, two lecture halls connectable to a large exhibition hall, and the second bridge level with the architecture department at second floor; (3) the Atelierhaus (atelier house) containing welfare facilities, stage between aula and dining hall that opens to both sides for performances, 28 studio apartments for students, terraced roof accessible from kitchen.
- **Rule**: Divide a complex building program into functionally distinct wings, each with its own formal language — then connect them through designed circulation that is itself architecturally resolved.
- **Source**: p.14–15
- **Quote (DE)**: "der gesamte baukomplex besteht aus drei teilen: 1 der flügelbau der 'technischen lehranstalten' [...] 2 laboratoriumswerkstätten und lehrräume des bauhauses [...] 3 atelierhaus"
- **Quote (EN)**: "the total building complex consists of three parts: 1 the wing of the 'technical schools' [...] 2 laboratory workshops and teaching rooms of the Bauhaus [...] 3 atelier house"
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Eisenbetongerüst mit Vorhangfassade / Reinforced concrete frame with curtain wall
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius specifies: "eisenbetongerüst mit ziegelmauerwerk, steineisendecken auf unterzügen, im sockelgeschoß 'pilzdecke'" — reinforced concrete frame with brick infill, composite floor slabs on beams, mushroom-column slab in the basement. All windows: double-glazed with Kristallspiegelglas (crystal plate glass). Exposed surfaces: verlöteten Asphaltplatten auf Torfoleumsolierlage (soldered asphalt tiles on cork insulation underlay) and abgeglätteten Beton (smooth concrete). Non-accessible flat roofs: mit Kaltlack auf Jutegewebe über Torfoleumsolierlage und abgeglätteten Beton (cold-applied asphalt on jute fabric over cork insulation on smooth concrete). Not accessible flat roofs: covered with Zinkblech (zinc sheet). Exterior: Zementputz mit keimischer Mineralfarbe (cement render with mineral paint).
- **Rule**: Expose the structural system — concrete frame, flat roof, smooth render — and derive all formal qualities from structural and material logic, not applied decoration.
- **Source**: p.15
- **Quote (DE)**: "eisenbetongerüst mit ziegelmauerwerk, steineisendecken auf unterzügen, im sockelgeschoß 'pilzdecke': sämtliche fenster aus doppelt überfälzten profileisen mit kristallspiegelglas."
- **Quote (EN)**: "reinforced concrete frame with brick infill, composite floor slabs on beams, mushroom-column slab in the basement: all windows from double-rebated steel sections with crystal plate glass."
- **Category**: material | transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Gesamtgestaltung durch Bauhaus-Werkstätten / Total design through Bauhaus workshops
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "die farbige raumgestaltung des gesamten baues wurde von der wandmalereiabteilung des bauhauses durchgeführt" — the color design of all spaces was executed by the wall-painting workshop. All lighting fixtures from the metal workshop. Tubular steel furniture (aula, dining hall, studios) designed by M. Breuer and produced by the metal workshop. All lettering and signage by the printing workshop. The building is a collective Bauhaus product from structure to spoon.
