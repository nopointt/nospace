## Visual Survey: Contemporary Dutch and European Architecture (Abbildungen 40–55)

Pages 80–92 are entirely image-based — a comparative survey of contemporary work by Brinkman & Van der Vlugt, Bijvoet & Duiker, S. van Ravesteijn, Mart Stam, and myself. No explanatory text accompanies these plates; they speak through formal comparison with the De Stijl argument already made.

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The new Dutch architecture, and its closest European counterparts in the Weißenhof exhibition context, achieves a formal synthesis that earlier movements merely approached. These buildings are not illustrated theory — they are theory built.
**Evidence**: Photographs and drawings of the Van Nelle Fabriek (Rotterdam, 1925/26), my own Börse Ideen-Entwurf (Rotterdam, 1926), my Serienhäuser (Stuttgart, 1927), Van Ravesteijn's Verwaltungsgebäude (Rotterdam-Fijnoord, 1927), and water tower proposals by Johansen and Mart Stam.
**Conclusion**: [INFERRED] The comparative placement of these works — from industry to housing to infrastructure — demonstrates that the new formal principles are not limited to a single building type. They constitute a Gesamthaltung, a total architectural attitude applicable at every scale and program.

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: Brinkman und Van der Vlugt — Fabrik "Van Nelle", Rotterdam, Entwurf 1925/26
- **Shows**: Full photograph of the Van Nelle Factory under construction or recently completed. Massive horizontal glass-and-steel curtain wall facade, multiple stories of flush glazing in continuous bands, a large cylindrical silo or stair tower element at one end rising above the roofline, cantilevered loading docks at ground level. The building reads as an assembly of horizontal planes interrupted by the vertical tower accent.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Masse und Öffnung resolved at industrial scale — the wall IS the window. The traditional masonry wall with punched openings is entirely dissolved; glass bands replace solid Fläche. The building demonstrates that constructive ehrlichkeit at maximum scale yields a facade that is entirely legible: structure expresses itself, function expresses itself, nothing is disguised.

**FIGURE [Fig-02]**: J.J.P. Oud — Ideen-Entwurf Büros im Steigerwald, Groningen-Rotterdam, 1926

Upper drawing: a traditional historicist building silhouette with dome, tower, and ornamental roofline superimposed with a schematic horizontal modern grid. Lower drawing: the same site treated in modern orthogonal manner — a long horizontal block with regular bays, a clean flat roofline, standardized window rhythm. The juxtaposition is intentional: old vs. new formal logic on the same program.

By placing both schemes in direct visual contrast, the drawing makes the formal argument without words: the horizontal bar is not impoverished — it is purified. The ornamental profile of the historicist building is revealed as arbitrary addition, not structural necessity.

**FIGURE [Fig-03]**: J.J.P. Oud — Ideen-Entwurf für die Börse in Rotterdam, 1926
- **Shows**: Axonometric drawing of a stock exchange building design. The composition is a complex of interlocking orthogonal volumes — flat-roofed rectangular masses at different heights, with large horizontal window bands cut through them. The roofscape shows terraces and a large flat skylight element. The building reads as a three-dimensional De Stijl composition: planes sliding past each other, volumes interpenetrating, no dominant central axis.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The Eckproblem resolved at the scale of a major public building. Corners are dissolved — volumes overlap rather than meet at traditional corners, so that the edges of the building read as intersections of planes rather than traditional structural corners. The flat roof ensures the building terminates at a horizontal plane, not a pitched or domed silhouette. rechteckige Komposition operating in three dimensions.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Abb. 42 J.J.P. OUD. Ideen-Entwurf für die Börse in Rotterdam, 1926"
- **Source**: p.81 (PDF page 082)

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: STAM — Entwurf für die Bebauung vom "Dam"-Terrain (mit Schwebebebahnstation) in Amsterdam, 1926
- **Shows**: Perspective drawing of an elevated transit station and surrounding development. A long elevated structure carries what appears to be a suspended rail line; below and beside it, horizontal bar buildings with continuous window bands. The composition is dominated by the horizontal datum of the elevated rail infrastructure, with the buildings serving as subordinate horizontal planes beneath and alongside it.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The extension of orthogonal principles to urban infrastructure — not just individual buildings but entire transit-integrated urban fabric. The elevated rail becomes a structural datum against which the buildings are measured, much as a De Stijl composition uses a primary horizontal element to anchor secondary planes.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Abb. 43 le STAM. Entwurf für die Bebauung vom 'Dam'-Terrain (mit Schwebebahnstation) in Amsterdam"

**FIGURE [Fig-05]**: B. Bijvoet und J. Duiker — Sanatorium, Zonnestraal, Hilversum, 1927
- **Shows**: Photograph of the Zonnestraal Sanatorium — a low horizontal building set against trees and landscape. The building appears as a series of long white horizontal wings radiating from a central core. Large expanses of glazing in the facades. The building reads as extremely light and transparent against its natural setting — the structure almost dissolves into the landscape rather than asserting mass against it.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The reconciliation of the abstract orthogonal composition with landscape and with functional program (sanatorium = maximum light, air, openness to nature). The extreme transparency of the Fläche — wall replaced by glass — serves both principle (constructive ehrlichkeit, structural honesty) and function (therapeutic light for tuberculosis patients). Principle and function here are not in conflict; they reinforce each other.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Abb. 44 B. BIJVOET UND J. DUIKER. Sanatorium 'Zonnestraal' Hilversum 1927"

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: J.J.P. Oud — Serienhäuser in Stuttgart, 1927 (exterior)
- **Shows**: Photograph of the row houses at the Weißenhof exhibition, Stuttgart. A terrace of white rendered houses seen from the garden side — two storeys, flat roof, standardized window openings, roof terraces with metal railings. The houses read as a continuous horizontal composition punctuated by the regular vertical rhythm of individual unit divisions. The corner windows and cantilevered roof terrace slabs are visible, with thin metal pipe railings.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Standardisierung in practice — the housing row as a serial composition where the individual unit is identical but the row as a whole achieves an urban scale. The white render surface asserts Fläche: the wall is a plane, not a material texture. The regular window rhythm creates a formal repetition that is the opposite of the Expressionist individuation of each bay. menschliche Proportion is achieved through the scale of the individual unit within the row.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Abb. 45 J.J.P. OUD. Serienhäuser in Stuttgart 1927"
- **Source**: p.84 (PDF page 085)

**FIGURE [Fig-07]**: J.J.P. Oud — Wohnzimmer, Serienhäuser Stuttgart, 1927 (interior)
- **Shows**: Photograph of a living room interior in the Stuttgart Serienhäuser. The room shows built-in furniture: a low sideboard unit runs along one wall, with open shelving above it. A table and tubular-steel chairs in the foreground. The proportions of the room are compact and rational. The finish is entirely plain — white or light-colored walls, no moldings, no ornament. The built-in millwork integrates storage into the wall plane.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The extension of De Stijl formal principles from the building envelope to the interior. The built-in furniture is itself a Fläche treatment — storage integrated into wall planes rather than freestanding objects cluttering the floor. The room demonstrates that menschliche Proportion does not require ornamental richness; the human body is served by spatial clarity and integrated function. The tubular-steel chairs assert the machine-age material vocabulary consistent with the building's structural honesty.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Abb. 46 J.J.P. OUD. Wohnzimmer. Serienhäuser Stuttgart, 1927"
- **Source**: p.85 (PDF page 086)

The structural columns define a regular grid without enclosing partitions — the space reads as a column grid with infill, the spatial logic made visible through the structural rhythm.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The skeletal structural principle — column grid as spatial organizer rather than wall as enclosure — creates interior spaces where the human occupant perceives the building's structural logic directly. This is konstruktive Ehrlichkeit operating inside the building: the structure is not hidden in walls but celebrated as the spatial ordering system.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Abb. 51 S. VAN RAVESTEIJN. Innenraum. Verwaltungsgebäude des Güterbahnhofs Rotterdam-Fijnoord, 1927"

## Der Einfluss von Frank Lloyd Wright auf die Architektur Europas

*New chapter beginning at PDF page 093. No date given on the title page; the text ends with the date "1925" on p.98 (PDF page 099), establishing this essay as written in 1925.*

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Frank Lloyd Wright is one of the greatest figures of 19th- and early 20th-century architecture. His influence on European architecture — particularly Dutch, French, Czech, Belgian, and Polish architecture — is real, important, and at the same time dangerous. The danger lies not in Wright's work itself, but in the way European architects have absorbed it: superficially, imitating his forms rather than understanding his principles.
**Evidence**: Wright's formal characteristics (horizontal mass stacking, interlocking rooms, the importance of line and form-weight), the "Wright School" that emerged in the American West, the reception of Wright's work in Europe through publications, the parallel emergence of Cubism in painting/sculpture vs. Cubism in architecture, the distinction between Wright's American-specific sources (organic richness, "High Life") and the European need for a different abstraction.
**Conclusion**: Wright should be understood as a prophet, not a model. His achievement was to articulate the necessity of a new architecture rooted in the needs of modern life — but the European solution must be different from his, because the European social and cultural conditions (austerity, abstraction, rejection of individual luxuriance) demand a purer reduction than Wright himself achieved. Imitating Wright's forms in Europe is not only artistically wrong — it is morally wrong. The European new architecture must take from Wright's spirit — the demand for a Gesamtkomposition of function, material, and form — while arriving at entirely different formal results.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Geistige Anspannung des Erzeugens / Mental concentration of creation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Wright's work is admired partly for the wrong reasons — his technical mastery makes it appear effortless. "Bewundert man andere um des Talents willen, womit man bei ihnen die Materie beherrscht sieht, Wright verehre ich, weil der Entstehungsprozeß seiner Arbeit mir völlig fremd, gänzlich Geheimnis bleibt." The creation of a true formal synthesis — not a formula, but a living process of thought — is more difficult to grasp and more valuable than mere technical mastery.
- **Rule**: Revere an architect not for what you can imitate but for what you cannot explain — the invisible logic of creation is the sign of genuine synthesis.
- **Source**: p.93
- **Quote (DE)**: "Bewundert man andere um des Talents willen, womit man bei ihnen die Materie beherrscht sieht, Wright verehre ich, weil der Entstehungsprozeß seiner Arbeit mir völlig fremd, gänzlich Geheimnis bleibt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "While one admires others for the talent with which one sees them master the material, I revere Wright because the process of creation of his work remains entirely foreign to me, entirely a mystery."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Fläche als Ausdruck / Surface as expression — Wright's primary contribution
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: What distinguished Wright's architecture from the historicism of the 19th century was the primacy of the plane over the profile. "Die Verschiebungen der Flächen, die weit ausladenden Platten und Gesimse, die wiederholt unterbrochenen und sich wieder fortsetzenden Massen, die dominierende Horizontalentwicklung, alles typische Merkmale der Arbeit Wrights" — these characteristics are identified as what made Wright's influence so immediate and convincing to European architects escaping historicism.
- **Rule**: Identify in any historical source not the details that can be copied but the underlying spatial logic — the handling of the Fläche — that constitutes the real contribution.
- **Source**: p.94–95
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Verschiebungen der Flächen, die weit ausladenden Platten und Gesimse, die wiederholt unterbrochenen und sich wieder fortsetzenden Massen, die dominierende Horizontalentwicklung, alles typische Merkmale der Arbeit Wrights"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The displacements of the planes, the widely projecting slabs and cornices, the repeatedly interrupted and again continuing masses, the dominant horizontal development — all typical characteristics of Wright's work"
- **Category**: surface

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Kubismus und Architektur / Cubism and architecture — parallel but independent
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Cubism of architecture — the decomposition of the building body into its parts, the reassembly into a larger Ganzes, the striving for Zusammenfassung of many smaller elements — arose independently from Wright, not as a copy of Cubism in painting. "Der Kubismus in der Architektur — dies muß man klar einsehen — entstand völlig selbständig, absolut frei von Wright, in analoger Weise wie in der Malerei und Skulptur und durch diese angetrieben, aus einem inneren Drange."
- **Rule**: Never confuse formal resemblance with genealogical dependency. A principle that appears in two independent domains simultaneously is not imitation but a sign that the principle has become historically necessary.
- **Source**: p.97
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der Kubismus in der Architektur — dies muß man klar einsehen — entstand völlig selbständig, absolut frei von Wright, in analoger Weise wie in der Malerei und Skulptur und durch diese angetrieben, aus einem inneren Drange."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Cubism in architecture — this must be clearly understood — arose entirely independently, absolutely free from Wright, in an analogous manner as in painting and sculpture, and driven by the latter, from an inner drive."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Dreidimensionale Architektur / Three-dimensional architecture — the cubist achievement
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Cubist tendency in architecture is the will toward three-dimensionality — decomposing the building body into its parts, reassembling those parts into a Ganzes larger than the sum, striving generally toward synthesis of many smaller elements. "Übereinstimmung scheint es zu geben im Willen zum Rechteckigen, in der Tendenz zum Dreidimensionalen, im Zergliedern der Baukörper und Wiederaufbauen ihrer Teile, im allgemeinen im Streben nach Zusammenfassung vieler kleiner — anfangs durch Auseinandernehmen enthaltener — Stücke zu einem Ganzen."
- **Rule**: The formal synthesis of a building must operate in three dimensions simultaneously — plan, section, and elevation are one composition, not three separate problems.
- **Source**: p.97
- **Quote (DE)**: "Übereinstimmung scheint es zu geben im Willen zum Rechteckigen, in der Tendenz zum Dreidimensionalen, im Zergliedern der Baukörper und Wiederaufbauen ihrer Teile"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Agreement seems to exist in the will toward the rectangular, in the tendency toward the three-dimensional, in the decomposition of building bodies and the rebuilding of their parts"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Das Neue Material / New materials — application and misapplication
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A further convergence between the Cubist tendency and Wright's work is "die Anwendung neuer Materialien, neuer Techniken, das Sich-richten nach neuen Forderungen" — the application of new materials, new techniques, the orientation toward new demands. However, Oud warns that the imitation of Wright's external forms by his European followers corrupts this principle: the result is a "zweiter Hand stammende Äußerliche" — a secondhand superficiality derived from a master's actual work.
- **Rule**: Apply new materials to satisfy new demands, not to replicate the appearance of an admired building. The appearance is the residue of a problem; the problem is what must be understood.
- **Source**: p.97
- **Quote (DE)**: "die Anwendung neuer Materialien, neuer Techniken, das Sich-richten nach neuen Forderungen"
- **Quote (EN)**: "the application of new materials, new techniques, the orientation toward new demands"
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Wright als Prophét, nicht Meister / Wright as prophet, not master
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Zeigte sich Wright in der Praxis doch mehr Künstler als Prophet, so bereitete der Kubismus mehr täglich die Verwirklichung desjenigen vor, was auch seine Theorie war." Wright revealed himself in practice more as artist than prophet; Cubism prepared daily for the realization of what was also Wright's theory. The European architects who truly understood Wright's lesson — that architecture must be rooted in the necessities of modern collective life — moved toward a greater abstraction than Wright himself achieved.
- **Rule**: Learn from a master's intentions, not his results. The results belong to his time and conditions; the intentions, if valid, point beyond.
- **Source**: p.97–98
- **Quote (DE)**: "Zeigte sich Wright in der Praxis doch mehr Künstler als Prophet, so bereitete der Kubismus mehr täglich die Verwirklichung desjenigen vor, was auch seine Theorie war."
- **Quote (EN)**: "If Wright revealed himself in practice more as artist than prophet, then Cubism prepared daily for the realization of what was also his theory."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Neue Baukunst als Übergangsstadium / New architecture as transitional stage
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The new architecture — including Cubism and the De Stijl tendency — is a transitional stage: "ein Stadium der Auflösung vom alten System und des Aufbaues der neuen Ordnung." The concept of the constructive and the value of formal relationships were rediscovered; the importance of Masse and its complement (space) was re-conquered. "Vor allem aber kam im Kubismus — als logische Fortsetzung der früheren Erneuerungsversuche — unmittelbar und stark die Spannung einer größeren und echteren Vitalität zum Ausdruck."
- **Rule**: Accept that any current formal system is provisional. Its value is not permanence but the degree to which it honestly resolves the conflict between the new demands of life and the inherited repertoire of form.
- **Source**: p.98
- **Quote (DE)**: "ein Stadium der Auflösung vom alten System und des Aufbaues der neuen Ordnung"
- **Quote (EN)**: "a stage of dissolution from the old system and of construction of the new order"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Reinheit des Klanges / Purity of tone — the European demand for abstraction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The European new architecture differs from Wright's American work in its demand for a purer reduction. "Das Lyrisch-Mitreißende, das dem Pfeifen dieses architektonischen Rattenfängers eigen ist, zu gleicher Zeit die Reinheit des Klanges zu erheben begann." The American "High Life" context — the richness of individual expression, the organic plasticity — is specifically American. European conditions demand the opposite: sozialaesthetische Notwendigkeit, Einfachheit und Regelmäßigkeit, Gebundenheit, Straffheit, Genauigkeit.
- **Rule**: Identify which formal qualities in a foreign architectural example are universal principles and which are culturally specific. Import only the principles; leave the culturally specific forms behind.
- **Source**: p.95–96
- **Quote (DE)**: "sozialaesthetische Notwendigkeit, Einfachheit und Regelmäßigkeit; Gebundenheit, Straffheit, Genauigkeit"
- **Quote (EN)**: "social-aesthetic necessity, simplicity and regularity; constraint, precision, accuracy"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Imitation als Verderben / Imitation as corruption
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Es ist indessen ein Fehler, der durch die Kritiker vielmals gemacht wurde und worauf nicht nachdrücklich genug hingewiesen werden kann (weil gerade in dieser Mißdeutung der Grund liegt, weshalb man den Einfluß Wrights auf die Architektur Europas mißdeutend bezeichnen muß), es ist ein Fehler, das Entstehen dieser Kennzeichen ausschließlich Wright zuzuschreiben." The superficial imitation of Wright's formal characteristics — the horizontal projecting slabs, the interlocking masses — is not only wrong historically (since the European tendency arose independently) but harmful architecturally: it substitutes appearance for understanding. The section on "Halbheit, welche ärger als öffentliches Plagiat, welche Charakterlosigkeit ist" condemns this half-measure.
- **Rule**: Never derive a building from the photograph of another building. Derive it from the problem that produced that building.
- **Source**: p.96–99
- **Quote (DE)**: "so ist es diese Halbheit, welche ärger als öffentliches Plagiat, welche Charakterlosigkeit ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "it is this half-measure, which is worse than public plagiarism, which is characterlessness."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Theorie als Lebensgrundlage / Theory as basis of life
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Die Theorie dennoch — dies sei betont — ist von großem Wert als Basis im Leben. Immer von Wert, gänzlich unentbehrlich aber in dieser Zeit, da jede ästhetische Richtschnur, jeder traditionelle Stützpunkt fehlt." In a period where every aesthetic guideline is absent and every traditional support point has failed, theory is not an intellectual luxury — it is the necessary substitute for the certainty that tradition once provided. The new architect must know why he acts as he acts, because the formal conventions that previously permitted instinctive action are no longer reliable.
- **Rule**: Know the reason for every formal decision. In an age without tradition, the architect who cannot articulate the principle behind each choice has no principle — only habit.
- **Source**: p.99
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Theorie dennoch — dies sei betont — ist von großem Wert als Basis im Leben. Immer von Wert, gänzlich unentbehrlich aber in dieser Zeit, da jede ästhetische Richtschnur, jeder traditionelle Stützpunkt fehlt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Theory nonetheless — let this be emphasized — is of great value as a basis in life. Always of value, but entirely indispensable in this time, when every aesthetic guideline, every traditional point of support is absent."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Massenproduktion als Vorläufer der Kubismus / Mass production as precursor
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Das Bedürfnis nach Zahl und Maß, an Reinheit und Ordnung, an Regelmaß und Wiederholung, an Vollkommenheit und Abgerundetheit; Eigenschaften der Organe des modernen Lebens, der Technik, des Verkehrs, der Hygiene, innewohnend auch der gesellschaftlichen Beschaffenheit, den wirtschaftlichen Verhältnissen, den Methoden der Massenproduktion, finden ihre Vorläufer im Kubismus." The formal qualities of industrial production — standardization, repetition, regularity, completeness — are not alien to De Stijl architecture; they are its preconditions. The Kubismus in architecture is the application of mass-production logic to the formal problem.
- **Rule**: Design so that each element could be produced by the methods of modern industry — not as a stylistic gesture but as a logical consequence of the contemporary economic and social order.
- **Source**: p.98
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Bedürfnis nach Zahl und Maß, an Reinheit und Ordnung, an Regelmaß und Wiederholung, an Vollkommenheit und Abgerundetheit"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The need for number and measure, for purity and order, for regularity and repetition, for completeness and roundedness"
- **Category**: standardization
