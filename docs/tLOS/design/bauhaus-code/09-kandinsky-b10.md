## Tafel-Abteilung — Illustrated Plates Section

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Tafeln are not decorative appendices. They are the empirical evidence for every claim made in the theoretical body. Each plate isolates a specific formal phenomenon — a line type, a tension configuration, a compositional principle — and makes it visible in pure form. The theory is proved here, or it is not proved at all.
**Evidence**: Ten plates (Tab. 15–24), each presenting a controlled visual experiment: free curves, wavy lines, simple and complex line interactions, diagonal Spannungen, Doppelklang, farbige Vibration, and analytical schemas of actual paintings ("Schwarzes Dreieck" 1925, "Intime Mitteilung" 1925).
**Conclusion**: The plates demonstrate that formal laws — temperature, Spannung, Doppelklang, the interaction of Punkt with line complexes — are not abstract propositions but observable, reproducible phenomena. They can be seen.

## Tab. 15 — Linie: Freie Gebogene zum Punkt — Milklingen Geometrisch-gebogener

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Freie Gebogene / Free curved line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The free curved line is distinguished from the geometrically curved line by its origin in a continuously varying force rather than a constant rotational radius. Its character is warm, lyrical, and individually inflected. When multiple free curves converge upon a Punkt, that point becomes the gravitational center — the focus of all accumulated Spannung.
- **Rule**: Use free curves when the formal intention is lyrical warmth and individual expressivity; use geometric curves when precision, closure, and concentric tension are required. Never conflate the two — their Klang is opposite.
- **Source**: p.170 (book) / PDF p.181 caption; PDF p.180 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Freie Gebogene zum Punkt — Milklingen Geometrisch-gebogener"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Free curved lines toward the point — co-sounding with geometrically curved lines"
- **Category**: line | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Punkt als Spannungszentrum / Point as tension-center in a line complex
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: When a Punkt is placed in relation to a cluster of free and geometric curves, it does not merely occupy space — it gathers the lines toward itself. The point becomes the still center within a field of directed movement. Its silence is active: it terminates, anchors, and measures the surrounding line tensions.
- **Rule**: Deploy a Punkt deliberately within a line complex to create a focal anchor. Its size and placement relative to the lines determines whether it reads as terminal silence or as compositional weight.
- **Source**: p.170 (book) / PDF p.180 image
- **Category**: point | tension | composition

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: Tab. 15 — Free curves toward point; co-sounding with geometric curves (PDF p.180)
- **Shows**: Full-plate composition. Upper zone: arc-fragments and semi-circular curves (geometric, opening upward) distributed across the upper field — approx. 8–10 arc elements ranging from small to medium. Middle zone: large circular arc (open circle, geometric), bold diagonal strokes (straight lines, acutely angled), a triangular form (outline only), a cluster of parallel hatched lines (horizontal, gray-toned), vertical bar elements ("I", "III"), a cross/plus sign. Lower zone: two small open circles (Punkt-scale), one small filled Punkt (dot), offset left-center. The composition mixes free and geometric elements in a deliberate demonstration of their co-sounding (Milklingen).
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-01 (free vs. geometric curve distinction), P-02 (Punkt as tension-center). The filled Punkt in the lower field acts as the gravitational terminus for the diagonal line thrusts above it. The geometric arcs in the upper register and the free curves in the middle demonstrate simultaneous presence of both line-types and their interaction (Milklingen).
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Freie Gebogene zum Punkt — Milklingen Geometrisch-gebogener" / "Free curved lines toward the point — co-sounding with geometrically curved"
- **Source**: PDF p.180–181 (Tab. 15, book p.170)

## Tab. 16 — Linie: Freie Wellenartige mit Nachdruck — horizontale Lage

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Wellenartige Linie / Wavy line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The wellenartige (wavy) line is a particular type of free curved line in which the force alternates rhythmically — swinging between two opposing directions without resolving into either. Its essential quality is the repetition of alternating tension, which generates a lyrical, undulating Klang. "Mit Nachdruck" (with emphasis) indicates that one directional impulse dominates, producing an asymmetric wave rather than a perfectly regular oscillation.
- **Rule**: When using wavy lines, determine whether the wave is symmetric (equal forces, uniform rhythm) or asymmetric (dominant force, mit Nachdruck). The asymmetric wave carries more expressive charge — it is internally unresolved, and its tension is therefore higher.
- **Source**: p.172 (book) / PDF p.183 caption; PDF p.182 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Freie Wellenartige mit Nachdruck — horizontale Lage"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Free wavy line with emphasis — horizontal position"
- **Category**: line | tension | temperature

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Horizontale Lage / Horizontal position and its temperature
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The horizontal orientation of a line element is not neutral. Horizontal = cold (kalt). It aligns with the temperature system established for the Grundfläche: horizontal corresponds to the cold, earthbound, passive axis — the same temperature as black and blue. When a free wavy line is positioned horizontally, its lyrical warmth is tempered and cooled by its orientation. The result is a complex Klang: warm character (wavy, free) in a cold orientation (horizontal).
- **Rule**: Consider orientation as a temperature modifier. A warm line-type placed horizontally is cooled. A cold line-type placed vertically is warmed. Orientation and line-type produce a combined temperature that differs from either alone.
- **Source**: p.172 (book) / PDF p.183 caption
- **Category**: line | temperature | composition

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-02]**: Tab. 16 — Free wavy line with emphasis, horizontal position (PDF p.182–183)
- **Shows**: Near-full-page composition dominated by a single massive free curved line. The line enters from the upper right as a thick, emphatic stroke, curves broadly leftward and downward in a large sweeping arc, then resolves into a thinner undulating tail extending toward lower left. The stroke varies dramatically in weight — heavy/thick at the apex of the dominant curve (the Nachdruck), thinning as it extends. No other elements present. The caption page (PDF p.183) shows a ghost of a large arc (bleed-through from recto), suggesting the plate's dominant arc bleeds through to the verso.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-03 (wellenartige line with asymmetric emphasis), P-04 (horizontal orientation as temperature modifier). The variation in line weight demonstrates that "Nachdruck" (emphasis) is rendered through stroke weight as well as directional dominance.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Freie Wellenartige mit Nachdruck — horizontale Lage" / "Free wavy line with emphasis — horizontal position"
- **Source**: PDF p.182–183 (Tab. 16, book p.172)

## Tab. 17 — Linie: Dieselbe Wellenartige mit Begleitung von Geometrischen

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Begleitung / Accompaniment of free by geometric lines
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The same free wavy line from Tab. 16 is now accompanied by geometric forms. "Begleitung" (accompaniment) is a precisely musical term: the wavy line is the melodic voice; the geometric elements are the harmonic accompaniment. Their co-presence does not cancel either — it enriches the total Klang through contrast. Free and geometric are not opposites to be avoided simultaneously; they are complementary registers that, when combined, produce a richer compositional sound.
- **Rule**: Pair free and geometric line-types as melodic voice and accompaniment. The free line carries expressive individuality; the geometric provides structural counterpoint. The relationship should be deliberate — one leads, the other accompanies.
- **Source**: p.174 (book) / PDF p.185 caption; PDF p.184 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Dieselbe Wellenartige mit Begleitung von Geometrischen"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The same wavy line with accompaniment by geometric [lines/forms]"
- **Category**: line | composition

## Tab. 18 — Linie: Einfacher und einheitlicher Komplex einiger Freier

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Einfacher Komplex / Simple unified complex of free lines
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Several free lines can be combined into a single, unified complex (Komplex) that reads as one compositional entity rather than as multiple independent elements. "Einfacher" (simple) means internally coherent — the lines share a dominant directional intention and together form a gestalt. "Einheitlicher" (unified) means that despite variety in individual line character, the overall complex has formal unity. This is the beginning of composition proper: not accumulation, but organization.
- **Rule**: When combining multiple free lines, establish one dominant direction or tension axis. Let the other lines relate to this axis — some reinforcing, some countering. Unity comes from the dominance of one structural intention, not from uniformity of all elements.
- **Source**: p.176 (book) / PDF p.187 caption; PDF p.186 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Einfacher und einheitlicher Komplex einiger Freier"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Simple and unified complex of several free [lines]"
- **Category**: line | composition

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: Tab. 18 — Simple unified complex of free lines (PDF p.186–187)
- **Shows**: A multi-element composition combining free and geometric line types. Upper register: a bold horizontal gray line (straight, geometric) spans approximately half the page width; two small black dots (Punkte) flank a small open circle (geometrically drawn). Center-left: a large free curved line hooks inward, forming a partial spiral/loop; from this cluster extend diagonal straight lines at acute angles, some heavy, some thin. Center-right: a cluster of parallel diagonal lines (hatched, gray). Far right: curved arcs (geometric) opening rightward. Lower register: wavy free curves in the lower right, and angular free curves in the lower left. The composition has a center-of-gravity in the left-center, pulling multiple line types into a complex with identifiable dominant axis.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-06 (simple unified complex). The mixed free and geometric elements are organized around a central tension axis, demonstrating how disparate line types can achieve compositional unity (Einheitlichkeit) through shared directional intention.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Einfacher und einheitlicher Komplex einiger Freier" / "Simple and unified complex of several free [lines]"

## Tab. 19 — Linie: Derselbe Komplex durch freie Spirale verkompliziert

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Spirale / Spiral as complicating element
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The spiral is a specific type of free curved line that combines centripetal movement (inward) and centrifugal movement (outward) in a single continuous stroke. Adding a free spiral to an existing line complex "verkompliziert" (complicates) it — not in the pejorative sense, but in the precise formal sense: it adds a new layer of tension that interacts with all existing tensions simultaneously. The spiral introduces concentrated, rotational Spannung into a field of predominantly linear tensions.
- **Rule**: Use the spiral to introduce rotational tension into a line composition. Its inward-pull creates a local Spannung center that can anchor or destabilize the surrounding linear field, depending on its placement. Place it where the composition needs a rotational axis.
- **Source**: p.178 (book) / PDF p.189 caption; PDF p.190 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Derselbe Komplex durch freie Spirale verkompliziert"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The same complex complicated by a free spiral"
- **Category**: line | tension | composition

## Tab. 20 — Linie: Diagonale Spannungen und Gegenspannungen mit einem Punkt, der eine äußere Konstruktion zu innerem Pulsieren bringt

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Diagonale Spannung und Gegenspannung / Diagonal tension and counter-tension
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Diagonal lines carry neither the cold passivity of the horizontal nor the warm activity of the vertical — they are the temperature of movement itself, of transition. When diagonals are opposed — Spannung meeting Gegenspannung — they create a field of mutual resistance. This is compositionally productive: the tension does not cancel but intensifies, generating a zone of maximum formal energy at the crossing point.
- **Rule**: Oppose diagonal tensions deliberately to create zones of maximum compositional energy. The crossing-point of opposing diagonals is the hottest formal location in the composition. Use it to locate the most important element.
- **Source**: p.180 (book) / PDF p.191 caption; PDF p.192 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Diagonale Spannungen und Gegenspannungen mit einem Punkt, der eine äußere Konstruktion zu innerem Pulsieren bringt"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Diagonal tensions and counter-tensions with a point that brings an outer construction to inner pulsation"
- **Category**: line | tension | point | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Punkt als Aktivator des inneren Pulsierens / Point as activator of inner pulsation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A critical principle of this plate: a line construction can remain external — formally correct but inert, a dead arrangement — until a Punkt is introduced. The Punkt does not merely occupy a position; it activates the construction. It brings "inneres Pulsieren" (inner pulsation) — the composition begins to breathe. The Punkt is the formal element that converts structure into life.
- **Rule**: In any composition that feels formally complete but inert, introduce a Punkt. Place it where its gravitational relationship to the surrounding lines creates maximum internal tension. The point is not decoration — it is the activating element that triggers Innere Notwendigkeit.
- **Source**: p.180 (book) / PDF p.191 caption
- **Quote (DE)**: "der eine äußere Konstruktion zu innerem Pulsieren bringt"
- **Quote (EN)**: "that brings an outer construction to inner pulsation"
- **Category**: point | tension | inner-necessity | composition

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-07]**: Tab. 20 — Diagonal tensions and counter-tensions with activating Punkt (PDF p.192)
- **Shows**: A complex multi-line composition built on diagonal axes. Dominant elements: a very thick, heavy diagonal line descending from upper-right to lower-left (the dominant Spannung); several thinner diagonal lines crossing it from various angles (Gegenspannungen); curved line elements (partial circles, arc fragments) interspersed among the diagonals; angular forms including a partial triangle or zigzag; a bold filled Punkt (solid circle, medium-large) positioned in the lower center of the composition, isolated from the main line cluster above. - **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-08 (diagonal tension/counter-tension), P-09 (Punkt as activator of inner pulsation). The placement of the Punkt below and separate from the line complex is the key compositional argument: the Punkt does not touch the lines, yet it transforms the whole from "outer construction" to "inner pulsation."
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Diagonale Spannungen und Gegenspannungen mit einem Punkt, der eine äußere Konstruktion zu innerem Pulsieren bringt"

## Tab. 21 — Linie: Doppelklang — kalte Spannung der Geraden, warme Spannung der Gebogenen, Steifes zum Lockeren, Nachgeben zum Dichten

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Doppelklang / Double sound
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Doppelklang is the simultaneous sounding of two formally opposite qualities — not as conflict, but as harmony of opposites. In this plate, four pairs of opposites are sounding simultaneously: cold tension (Geraden / straight lines) with warm tension (Gebogene / curved lines); stiff (Steifes) with loose (Lockeres); yielding (Nachgeben) with dense (Dichtes). Each pair is a Doppelklang in itself; all four sounding together produce a total Doppelklang of the entire composition. The term is musical by design: in music, two notes sounded simultaneously are a interval or chord — their combined resonance is different from either alone.
- **Rule**: Construct Doppelklang by deliberately pairing formal opposites: straight/curved, stiff/loose, dense/sparse. The opposites must both be fully present — if one is suppressed, the Doppelklang collapses into a single tone. Balance does not mean equality of quantity, but equality of formal presence and intention.
- **Source**: p.182 (book) / PDF p.193 caption; PDF p.194 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Doppelklang — kalte Spannung der Geraden, warme Spannung der Gebogenen, Steifes zum Lockeren, Nachgeben zum Dichten"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Double sound — cold tension of the straight lines, warm tension of the curved lines, stiff toward loose, yielding toward dense"
- **Category**: line | tension | temperature | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Temperatur der Linie / Temperature of line types
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Geraden (straight lines) carry cold Spannung; Gebogene (curved lines) carry warm Spannung. This is not metaphor but a formal property arising from their force-origin: the straight line is produced by a single constant force (cold, unchanging); the curved line by a force that continuously modifies itself (warm, variable). The temperature distinction maps directly onto the full temperature system: cold → horizontal → passive → black/blue; warm → diagonal-to-vertical → active → yellow.
- **Rule**: Never treat straight and curved lines as merely different shapes. They carry opposite temperatures and opposite emotional charges. Use this temperature difference as a compositional instrument — as deliberately as a painter uses warm and cool colors.
- **Source**: p.182 (book) / PDF p.193 caption
- **Category**: line | temperature

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Steifes und Lockeres / Stiff and loose as compositional poles
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Beyond temperature, line compositions can be analyzed along the axis of Steifes (stiff, rigid, precise) versus Lockeres (loose, relaxed, free). Stiff elements create dense, controlled zones; loose elements create open, flowing zones. The opposition of these qualities within a single composition produces spatial variety and internal movement — the eye travels from dense-stiff zones to open-loose zones and back.
- **Rule**: Map your composition's zones as stiff or loose. Ensure both poles are present. Use Steifes to create structural anchors; use Lockeres to create release and movement. A composition that is entirely stiff is dead; entirely loose is formless.
- **Source**: p.182 (book) / PDF p.193 caption
- **Category**: line | composition

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-08]**: Tab. 21 — Doppelklang composition (PDF p.194)
- **Shows**: A richly differentiated composition demonstrating all four Doppelklang pairs simultaneously. Left zone: a cluster of thin parallel diagonal lines (cold, stiff, dense — Geraden mit Nachdruck); beside it, a semi-circular arc (geometric curve) partially filled with a crosshatch grid pattern (dense zone); the grid fills the arc, creating maximum density/stiffness. Center-upper: a tall vertical structure built from alternating black and white squares (checkerboard column), topped by a small semicircle — this element is the most structurally geometric and cold. Right zone: a large sweeping geometric arc (quarter-circle, opening rightward) — warm curvature, open, loose. Center: a heavy horizontal straight line (cold) intersects with diagonal elements. Lower zone: a small filled square (Punkt-equivalent in square form), isolated. The composition is divided into a stiff-cold-dense left/center and a warm-open-loose right — the Doppelklang is spatially realized.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-10 (Doppelklang), P-11 (temperature of line types), P-12 (Steifes/Lockeres). The checkerboard column is the densest-stiffest element; the sweeping arc is the loosest-warmest. Their simultaneous presence is the formal argument.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Doppelklang — kalte Spannung der Geraden, warme Spannung der Gebogenen, Steifes zum Lockeren, Nachgeben zum Dichten"
- **Source**: PDF p.194 (Tab. 21, book p.182); caption PDF p.193

## Tab. 22 — Linie: Farbige Vibration im Schematischen durch ein Minimum der Farbe (schwarz) erreicht

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Farbige Vibration durch Minimum der Farbe / Color vibration through minimum of color
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: It is possible to achieve farbige Vibration (color vibration) using only black — the minimum of color. This is achieved not by adding color but by the schematic arrangement of formal elements: the alternation of filled and empty, thick and thin, dense and sparse creates a visual vibration that functions analogously to chromatic vibration. The plate works in strict black-and-white (schematisch) yet achieves vibratory quality through formal organization alone.
- **Rule**: Do not assume that color vibration requires color. Formal vibration — achieved through alternation of tonal contrast, density variation, and element rhythm — is a valid and sometimes more powerful substitute. Use maximum tonal contrast (black on white) with carefully alternated density to produce vibration without hue.
- **Source**: p.184 (book) / PDF p.195 caption; PDF p.196 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Farbige Vibration im Schematischen durch ein Minimum der Farbe (schwarz) erreicht"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Color vibration in the schematic achieved through a minimum of color (black)"
- **Category**: line | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Schematisch als Methode / The schematic as method
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: "Im Schematischen" indicates that this plate works as a schema — a formal diagram that isolates one principle in its purest form, free of painterly qualities (Manier, Faktur). The schematic is not a lesser form of composition; it is the most analytically rigorous form. It demonstrates the principle without the distraction of surface.
- **Rule**: Use schematic compositions to isolate and test formal principles. When a principle can be demonstrated schematically, in pure form, the law is established. When it only works with painterly surface treatment, the principle is weaker.
- **Source**: p.184 (book) / PDF p.195 caption
- **Category**: composition | inner-necessity

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-09]**: Tab. 22 — Color vibration through minimum of color (PDF p.196)
- **Shows**: A schematic, architecturally-organized composition that achieves vibratory effect through tonal alternation. Left cluster: a vertical structure of small elements — crosshatch grids, thin parallel lines, small filled/open circles — stacked with alternating densities; a small cross at top. A bold thick horizontal line (black) cuts across the left-center. Center: a larger open rectangle/square outline. Right zone: a large sweeping arc (thin line, geometric quarter-circle) opens toward upper-right, with a cluster of thin diverging lines (fan-like) at its origin point, and at its midpoint a small filled square. Lower right: three vertical bars (thin, parallel), and an isolated small filled square. The alternation between dense clusters (left) and sparse open arcs (right) creates the vibratory effect — the eye oscillates between zones of maximum density and minimum density.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-13 (farbige Vibration through formal alternation), P-14 (schematic as analytical method). The composition works entirely in black on white; the "color vibration" is achieved purely through density alternation and tonal contrast.
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Farbige Vibration im Schematischen durch ein Minimum der Farbe (schwarz) erreicht"
- **Source**: PDF p.196 (Tab. 22, book p.184); caption PDF p.195

## Tab. 23 — Linie: Innere Beziehung eines Komplexes von Geraden zu einer Gebogenen (links — rechts) zum Bild „Schwarzes Dreieck" (1925)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Innere Beziehung / Inner relationship between formal elements and painting
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The analytical schema (left–right: complex of straights related to curved) is presented as the inner structural law of a specific painting — "Schwarzes Dreieck" (1925). This is the method: take a finished painting, extract its formal skeleton, and show that the painting is a realization of formal laws, not a product of intuition or taste. The schema is not an afterthought but the generative principle. Every painting has an inner formal relationship structure that can be extracted and displayed as pure line.
- **Rule**: Analyze existing paintings by extracting their formal skeleton — the Komplex von Geraden and Gebogenen, the dominant tension axes, the Punkt placements. The schema should be legible as an autonomous formal argument. If it is not, the painting's formal logic is insufficient.
- **Source**: p.186 (book) / PDF p.197 caption; PDF p.198 image
- **Quote (DE)**: "Innere Beziehung eines Komplexes von Geraden zu einer Gebogenen (links — rechts) zum Bild 'Schwarzes Dreieck' (1925)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Inner relationship of a complex of straight lines to a curved [line] (left — right) to the painting 'Black Triangle' (1925)"
- **Category**: line | composition | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Links-Rechts als kompositorische Achse / Left–right as compositional axis
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The parenthetical "(links — rechts)" is not descriptive but analytical: it identifies the primary compositional axis of the schema as a left-right opposition. In Kandinsky's system, left and right of the Grundfläche have different weights and emotional charges — left is lighter, freer, more lyrical; right is heavier, more constrained, more dramatic. The distribution of formal complexes across this axis is therefore a compositional decision with emotional consequence.
- **Rule**: Identify the primary compositional axis (left-right, top-bottom, diagonal) before placing any element. The axis determines how each element will be read emotionally. A complex placed on the left reads differently from the same complex placed on the right — not by convention, but by the formal logic of the Grundfläche.
- **Source**: p.186 (book) / PDF p.197 caption
- **Category**: composition | plane | tension

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-10]**: Tab. 23 — Inner relationship schema for "Schwarzes Dreieck" (1925) (PDF p.198)

## Tab. 24 — Linie: Horizontal-vertikaler Aufbau mit gegensätzlicher Diagonale und Punktspannungen — Schema des Bildes „Intime Mitteilung" (1925)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Horizontal-vertikaler Aufbau / Horizontal-vertical structure
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The compositional skeleton of "Intime Mitteilung" (Intimate Communication, 1925) is built on the horizontal-vertical axis system — the foundational temperature opposition (cold horizontal / warm vertical) — complicated by "gegensätzliche Diagonale" (opposing diagonal). The diagonal functions as a disrupting third force, introducing movement and dynamism into what would otherwise be a static orthogonal structure. This three-axis system (horizontal + vertical + diagonal) is a fundamental compositional architecture.
- **Rule**: Build compositions on the tension between the horizontal-vertical orthogonal structure and its diagonal disruption. The orthogonal provides stability (cold/warm balance); the diagonal provides movement (temperature of transition). Without the diagonal, the composition is static. Without the orthogonal, it is chaotic.
- **Source**: p.188 (book) / PDF p.199 caption
- **Quote (DE)**: "Horizontal-vertikaler Aufbau mit gegensätzlicher Diagonale und Punktspannungen — Schema des Bildes 'Intime Mitteilung' (1925)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Horizontal-vertical structure with opposing diagonal and point-tensions — schema of the painting 'Intimate Communication' (1925)"
- **Category**: line | tension | composition | point

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Punktspannungen im Kompositionsschema / Point-tensions in the compositional schema
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Punktspannungen" (point-tensions) appear explicitly as a named element in the compositional schema — equal in status to line-tensions. The Punkte in the schema are not incidental; they are structural nodes whose gravitational relationships to the surrounding line system are part of the formal architecture of the painting. This confirms that Punkt is always analyzed as Spannung, never as mere marker.
- **Rule**: When analyzing or constructing a composition, map Punktspannungen explicitly as part of the formal skeleton. Name where each point is, what it is in tension with, and what temperature and direction that tension carries. A schema that omits Punktspannungen is incomplete.
- **Source**: p.188 (book) / PDF p.199 caption
- **Category**: point | tension | composition

## Batch Summary — Cross-Plate Observations

### Analytical Schema Method (Cross-plate principle)
[INFERRED] Tabs. 23 and 24 establish a methodological principle that runs throughout the entire book but here receives its clearest demonstration: Kandinsky's paintings are not products of feeling or intuition but realizations of formal laws. The analytical schemas (Tab. 23 for "Schwarzes Dreieck," Tab. 24 for "Intime Mitteilung") are proof. The law exists first; the painting is its sensory embodiment. This is the deepest meaning of Innere Notwendigkeit — not that the artist felt compelled, but that the formal logic was internally necessary.

### Progression of Plate Series (Tabs. 15–24)
[CERTAIN] The plates form a deliberate pedagogical sequence:
1. Tabs. 15–17: Isolation of single line types (free curved, wavy, with geometric accompaniment)
2. Tabs. 18–19: Building to simple complex, then complicating with spiral
3. Tab. 20: Introducing the Punkt as activator within a line complex
4. Tab. 21: Doppelklang — formal opposites in deliberate harmony
5. Tab. 22: Schematic demonstration of vibration through formal means alone
6. Tabs. 23–24: Application to actual paintings — formal law verified against practice
