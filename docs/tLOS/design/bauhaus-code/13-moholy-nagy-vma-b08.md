## Zweierlei des Volumens / Two kinds of volume

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The word "volume" (Volumen) must be understood in two distinct senses that are ultimately a unity: (1) the measurable, mass-defined three-dimensional extent bounded by the three dimensional directions, and (2) the visually perceptible virtual volume — the volume that arises through movement (Bewegung) and is plastically the more essential element.
**Evidence**: The rotating illuminated carousel at Blackpool (abb. 152) produces a clearly visible virtual volume through its movement; the night sky photograph (abb. 153) shows light as boundary-material of volume and space; X-ray image (abb. 154) and film stills (abb. 155a,b) demonstrate light creating space-form; the shadowless lamp (abb. 156) and the light-diagram of the city (abb. 157) extend this argument; Nikolaus Braun's Lichtplastik (abb. 158) is the constructed synthesis.
**Conclusion**: Plastik = the path to sublimation of material, from mass to movement. Virtual volume — the movement-generated, optically graspable, non-corporeal space-body — is not a lesser form but the completion of the sculptural sequence. Licht = boundary zone: it forms both Volumen and Raum simultaneously.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Zweierlei des Volumens / Two kinds of volume
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: On the ground of the impulsions of material as it unfolds in plastic development, we must understand "volume" in two ways — though at bottom they are one unity: the measurable mass-volume defined by the three directional axes, and the visually perceptible virtual volume arising from movement.
- **Rule**: Always distinguish between the mass-volume of a static body and the virtual volume generated by movement or light — both are plastically real.
- **Source**: p.167
- **Quote (DE)**: "auf grund dieses vorstoßes wird man unter „volumen" unter umständen zweierlei verstehen, (was im grunde doch wieder eine einheit ist). wir bestimmten als volumen: 1. den im gewicht meßbaren und durch die drei dimensionsrichtungen abtastbaren massenumfang. 2. den nur visuell erlebbaren, durch bewegung entstehenden virtuellen umfang, der — obwohl körperlos — doch in dreidimensionaler ausdehnung erkennbar, ausgesprochen plastisches gestaltungselement ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "On the ground of these impulsions we shall henceforth understand 'volume' in two ways (which are, at bottom, again a unity). We defined as volume: 1. the mass-extent measurable in weight and traceable through the three directional axes. 2. the purely visually perceptible virtual extent arising from movement, which — though bodiless — is recognizable in three-dimensional extension and is an emphatically plastic design element."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Plastik = Weg zur Sublimierung des Materials / Plastik as path to sublimation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Plastik is the path to the sublimation of material, from mass to movement. The sequence material → surface → volume → virtual volume → light-space traces a single ascending line of dematerialization in which each stage sublimes the materiality of the previous.
- **Rule**: Treat each sculptural stage not as a type but as a step on a single axis of increasing dematerialization — from heavy mass toward pure movement and light.
- **Source**: p.167
- **Quote (DE)**: "plastik = der weg zur sublimierung des materials, von masse zu bewegung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "plastik = the path to the sublimation of material, from mass to movement."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Licht als Grenzgebiet — es bildet Volumen und Raum / Light as boundary-zone
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Light is the boundary-zone: it forms both volume and space. The photograph of the night sky (abb. 153) demonstrates this — light is not decoration or illumination but the very material from which spatial limits are constituted.
- **Rule**: Use light as a primary space-forming material; treat every light boundary as a volumetric and spatial boundary simultaneously.
- **Source**: p.168
- **Quote (DE)**: "licht ist grenzgebiet: es bildet volumen und raum."
- **Quote (EN)**: "light is the boundary zone: it forms volume and space."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Parallelerscheinungen / Parallel phenomena across domains
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Parallel to the loosening tendencies of material in plastic development, phenomena from completely different domains appear with striking resemblance. Natural processes demonstrate the same laws — water in rest, movement, gaseous, liquid, solid states; all such transformations flow from an extraordinary adaptive capacity (Anpassungsvermögen) that corresponds in principle.
- **Rule**: Seek structural parallels across domains — nature, technology, art — as confirmatory evidence for design principles, not as decorative analogy.
- **Source**: p.169
- **Quote (DE)**: "zu den auflockerungstendenzen des materials in der plastischen entwicklung gesellen sich als parallelerscheinungen andere gebiete mit auffallender deutlichkeit. u. a. sind es auch naturvorgänge, die uns das gleiche zeigen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Alongside the loosening tendencies of material in plastic development, other fields appear as parallel phenomena with striking clarity. Among them are natural processes, which show us the same thing."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Lichtplastik — konstruiertes virtuelles Volumen / Constructed virtual volume
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The Lichtplastik (light sculpture) — exemplified by Nikolaus Braun's work of 1923 (abb. 158) — represents the constructed synthesis of the entire sequence: it is neither mass nor surface but pure regulated light, forming volume without solid substance. The artist who attempts "Lichtplastik" must be willing to reach the boundaries where private means no longer suffice and must be expanded into collective organization of space-light.
- **Rule**: Design light not as decoration but as autonomous plastic material constituting virtual volumes in space.
- **Source**: p.171
- **Quote (DE)**: "was für erlebnisse könnte man mit künstlichem, regulierbarem, vorberechnetem licht hervorgerufen werden gegenüber den bisherigen plastiken, die sozusagen 'tagesplastiken' waren — berechnet auf das von oben herunterfließende licht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "What experiences could be produced by artificial, regulable, pre-calculated light, as opposed to the earlier sculptures that were so to speak 'daylight-sculptures' — calculated for light flowing down from above."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Neue Sehen und Fotografie als Licht-Raum-Dokument / Photography as light-space document
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph — particularly the time-exposure — fixes the simultaneously running light-signs and creates a spatial co-ordination (Simultaneität) of the various movement-paths. A spatial interpenetration of time and space arises. Photography is thus not a recording device but an instrument of the Neue Sehen: it discloses the virtual volumes and light-movements invisible to ordinary perception.
- **Rule**: Use the camera's capacity for time-exposure and unusual viewpoints as a pedagogical and analytical instrument for seeing the light-volume relationships in space.
- **Source**: p.175
- **Quote (DE)**: "die fotografie — eine zeitaufnahme — fixiert die hintereinander ablaufenden lichtzeichen und so entsteht eine räumliche gleichordnung (simultaneität) der verschiedenen bewegungsbahnen. eine durchdringung von zeit und raum."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Photography — a time-exposure — fixes the successively running light-signs and thus creates a spatial co-ordination (simultaneity) of the various movement-paths: an interpenetration of time and space."
- **Category**: perception

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Die Zusammenhänge sind nicht immer erkennbar / Connections are not always visible
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In every cultural period a phalanx-like synthesis of all gestaltung forces emerges; this state does not last long — the others stream in quickly and the phalanx is aligned again. Therefore connections between plastic development, natural science, technology, and social conditions are not always immediately recognizable.
- **Rule**: Do not expect visible, linear causal chains between design developments and their cultural context — the connections are structural and emerge only in retrospect.
- **Source**: p.176
- **Quote (DE)**: "die zusammenhänge sind nicht immer erkennbar"
- **Quote (EN)**: "the connections are not always recognizable"
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Erkenntnisstufe ≠ Ausdrucksintensität / Level of knowledge ≠ intensity of expression
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The various developmental stages of absolute expressive forms mark different levels of knowledge (Erkenntnisstufen), but they are not a measure of expressive intensity (Ausdrucksintensität). To produce or receive an intensity of expression requires not only a specific knowledge-faculty but our whole being.
- **Rule**: Do not confuse the sophistication of formal development with expressive power — a more abstract stage of Plastik is not automatically more intense than an earlier one.
- **Source**: p.177
- **Quote (DE)**: "eine erkenntnisstufe ist noch kein maßstab für die ausdrucksintensität. die verschiedenen entwicklungsstufen der absoluten ausdrucksformen kennzeichnen wohl unterschiedliche erkenntnisstufen, sind aber kein maßstab für die ausdrucksintensität."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A level of knowledge is not yet a measure of expressive intensity. The various developmental stages of absolute expressive forms mark different levels of knowledge, but they are not a measure of expressive intensity."
- **Category**: perception

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Eine Deutung — Brancusi als Paradigma der Entwicklung / Brancusi as paradigm
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Brancusi is cited as a paradigm of the full plastic development — from the anatomical study (Abb. 166, 1900–1902) through the material-driven intermediate stages to the final pure volume-form. His development traverses all five stages: from mass to surface to volume activation, to opening and perforation, toward virtual volume and light-integration. Notably: he was astonished by new materials, had no idea of the volumetric law at the start, but arrived at it by the logic of material and process. His work teaches that one does not deduce the law — one finds it through material.
- **Rule**: Trace the developmental logic of any sculptural or design work through its material stages, not through its stylistic affiliations.
- **Source**: p.178
- **Quote (DE)**: "um einige heutige beispiele zu geben: brancusis entwicklung ist erschütternd und groß zugleich. abb. 166 ist eine arbeit des zwanzigjährigen aus seiner akademischen zeit 1900–1902."
- **Quote (EN)**: "To give some contemporary examples: Brancusi's development is shattering and grand at the same time. Abb. 166 is a work of the twenty-year-old from his academic period 1900–1902."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Holz als Grundlage aller plastischen Stadien / Wood traverses all stages
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Brancusi works through all stages of plastic development in wood — from the first crude distinctions of elevation and depression through pointed and rounded forms, through the light and shadow plays in surfaces, to the faktur-values and above all the penetrations of straight and curved surfaces (planes). Wood is the medium through which he traverses the full pedagogical sequence.
- **Rule**: A single material, deeply worked across all formal stages, reveals the complete logic of plastic development better than multiple materials superficially sampled.
- **Source**: p.178
- **Quote (DE)**: "er macht in holz alle stadien der plastischen entwicklung durch — mit ausnahme des kinetischen. das merkwürdige oder richtigere: das biologisch gesetzmäßige, in seiner arbeit: daß er bei der verwendung neuer materialien, die eine andere bearbeitungsweise beanspruchen als das holz, immer wieder von der ersten stufe der bearbeitung — innerhalb des betreffenden materials — die ganze entwicklung durchmacht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "He works through in wood all the stages of plastic development — with the exception of the kinetic. The remarkable or more correct thing: the biologically lawful in his work: that when using new materials, which require a different manner of working than wood, he always begins again from the first stage of working — within the material in question — and works through the entire development."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Neue Wendung — der perforierte Block / The breakthrough: perforated block
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A new turning-point (neue Wendung) in plastic history is marked by Lipchitz's work from 1926 onward: the perforated block — a system of ribs bounding the air-volume. The hollowing-out becomes ever deeper. The sculpture is no longer assembled but is "grown" from a single piece ("gezogen"). This radical opening makes the air-volume itself the plastic element, bounded by the rib-system.
- **Rule**: When a form is perforated and its interior void becomes the primary plastic element, the work has crossed into a new category — the space enclosed by the rib-system is the sculpture, not the material.
- **Source**: p.183–184
- **Quote (DE)**: "der perforierte block: ein system von rippen begrenzt das luftvolumen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the perforated block: a system of ribs bounds the air-volume."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Das Erkennen einer plastischen Entwicklungsstufe ist noch kein Erlebnis der Plastik
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Recognizing a stage of plastic development is not yet an experience (Erlebnis) of the plastik. To recognize (erkennen) the five stages and their logic is a necessary beginning — but it is not the terminal goal. The total experience of a plastic work requires far more than knowledge of its development stage: it requires knowledge of stereometric and biotechnical constructional elements, of measure (Maß), proportion (Verhältnis), structure (Struktur), texture (Textur), movement (Bewegung), and faktur-relationships.
- **Rule**: Design education must distinguish between recognition of developmental stages (necessary but initial) and full experiential understanding of the plastic work (the actual goal).
- **Source**: p.186
- **Quote (DE)**: "das erkennen einer plastischen entwicklungsstufe ist noch kein erlebnis der plastik. das problem der plastischen gestaltung ist mit einer solchen behandlung bei weitem nicht erschöpfend dargestellt. zum wirklich durchdringenden erlebnis sind damit nur die ersten schritte getan."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The recognition of a stage of plastic development is not yet an experience of the plastik. The problem of plastic design is far from exhaustively presented by such a treatment. For a truly penetrating experience, only the first steps have been taken."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Darstellung — die primitive Form / The primitive (primordial) form of representation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: For primitive peoples (primitive Völker) representation was largely synonymous with graspability (Greifbarkeit) and seizability (Besitzergreifung). The artist positioned the object for them in its moving, overwhelming reality, or even in a reality surpassing human scale (überwältigende Realität). In the death-mask the incomprehensible fact of dying becomes primitively graspable — the dead man is mummified, the root-fiber of the family, preserved as mummy in their real possession.
- **Rule**: Understand primitive representation not as naive art but as an epistemological act: making graspable what would otherwise remain beyond cognitive reach.
- **Source**: p.186–187
- **Quote (DE)**: "für den primitiven war die darstellung höchstwahrscheinlich gleichbedeutend mit greifbarkeit, besitzergreifung. er stellte die ihn bewegende oder ihn überwältigende realität sozusagen in menschenmaßen vor sich hin."
- **Quote (EN)**: "For primitive peoples representation was in all probability largely synonymous with graspability, seizability. He positioned the reality that moved or overwhelmed him so to speak in human-scale dimensions before him."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Zur Frage der Harmonielehren / On harmonic systems
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Since ancient times humanity has tried to find the formulas of human expression, to break down the components of experience into knowable elements. Harmonic systems (Harmonielehren) became ever more refined. We know today that harmony is not mechanically producible as art — harmony exists not in an aesthetically formal formula but in the undisturbed running of a function of existence (Existenzfunktion). Any work of this type is governed not by its own pleasure but according to the law of its own nature (Eigengesetzlichkeit).
- **Rule**: Do not construct harmonic systems as self-sufficient rules; harmonic form arises from the undisturbed running of function, not from the application of proportion-formulas.
- **Source**: p.188
- **Quote (DE)**: "wir sind gegen die so gearteten harmonielehren skeptisch geworden. wir wissen heute, daß die harmonie nicht in äshetischer formel, sondern in der organischen, ungestört ablaufenden funktion einer jeden existenz ruht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "We have grown skeptical of such harmonic systems. We know today that harmony resides not in an aesthetic formula, but in the organically undisturbed running of a function of every existence."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Funktionslehre — biologischer Aufbau als Quelle / Functional doctrine — biological structure as source
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The biological construction of the human organism is the proper source of every organic expression. From this insight one could attempt to grasp the motivating grounds of expression in a functional doctrine (Funktionslehre): to register the movement of the expression in biological laws. Old proportion-doctrines work with biological preconditions — they treat the sizes of the human body according to the golden section and other proportions.
- **Rule**: Ground any proportional or spatial system in the biological preconditions of human functioning, not in abstract numerical ratios abstracted from those conditions.
- **Source**: p.188
- **Quote (DE)**: "der biologische aufbau des menschen ist die eigentliche quelle jedes organischen ausdrucks. von dieser einsicht her könnte man versuchen, die beweggründe eines solchen ausdrucks in einer funktionslehre zu erfassen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The biological construction of the human organism is the proper source of every organic expression. From this insight one could attempt to grasp the driving grounds of such expression in a functional doctrine."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Elementenlehre — Psycho-physische Wirkung der Elemente / Elemental doctrine — psycho-physical effect
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every element (Farbe, Ton, etc.) possesses a psycho-physical (sinnlich-sittliche) effect. For the sinnlich-sittliche effect of elements we have today no fixed formulas, though a few have been established. For example: yellow = color of envy; green = hope; red = love. Animals grow irritated by it. The effect of color-complexes on people is such that a period can cause falsely conceived associations, and can also shift human disposition.
- **Rule**: Account for the psycho-physical effect of every formal and material element in design — color, texture, proportion, sound — not only their geometric relationships.
- **Source**: p.190
- **Quote (DE)**: "die psycho-fyischen wirkung der sinnlich faßbaren elemente (farbe, ton usw.) ist die grundlage unserer beziehungen zu gegenstand und ausdruck."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The psycho-physical effect of the sensorially graspable elements (color, tone, etc.) is the basis of our relationships to object and expression."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Allgemeine Elementenlehre — Grundstruktur / General elemental doctrine — foundational structure
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A general elemental doctrine of plastic (bildnerische) design is built on the relationships of existing forms. It must distinguish between: I. Vorhandene Formen (existing forms): mathematisch-geometrische Formen; biotechnische Formen. II. Neu erzeugte Formen (newly generated forms): freie Formen. The generation of new forms can be grounded in: (1) Verhältnissen der Maße (golden section and other proportions), Lage (measurable in angles), Bewegung (velocity, direction, shift, crossing); (2) Materialwerte: Struktur, Textur, Faktur (häufung); (3) Licht (Farbe, optische Täuschungen). The relations between forms can work through: (1) Kontraste; (2) Abweichungen; (3) Variationen: (a) Schiebung (Reihung), (b) Drehung, (c) Spiegelung.
- **Rule**: Any design system must be built on this three-part structural taxonomy: form-types, form-generating principles, and relational operations.
- **Source**: p.192
- **Quote (DE)**: "zu einer allgemeinen elementenlehre / eine allgemeine elementenlehre wird aufgebaut auf den beziehungen der / I vorhandenen formen: / mathematisch-geometrische formen / biotechnische formen; / II neu erzeugten formen und formkomplexe: / freie formen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Toward a general elemental doctrine / A general elemental doctrine is built on the relationships of / I existing forms: / mathematical-geometrical forms / biotechnical forms; / II newly generated forms and form-complexes: / free forms."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Sinnlich-sittliche vs. Psycho-physische Wirkung / Sensory-moral vs. psycho-physical effect
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Goethe's designation "sinnlich-sittlich" (sensory-moral) for the psycho-physical effect of sensorially graspable elements (color, tone, etc.) is the ground of our relationships to object and expression. For the sinnlich-sittliche effect of the elements we today possess no certain formulas for most — though from some of them we can speak with certainty. The psycho-physical dimension of color and form is the material basis of design, not a decorative supplement.
- **Rule**: Treat the sensory-moral (psycho-physical) dimension of every design element as primary material fact — not as subjective association.
- **Source**: p.190
- **Quote (DE)**: "die sinnlich-sittliche (psycho-physische) wirkung der sinnlich faßbaren elemente (farbe, ton usw.) ist die grundlage unserer beziehungen zu gegenstand und ausdruck."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The sensory-moral (psycho-physical) effect of the sensorially graspable elements (color, tone, etc.) is the ground of our relationships to object and expression."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Zur neuen Realität — Entmaterialisierung des Ausdrucks / Toward new reality
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The sovereign organization of the relationships between the volumes, the figures, the measures, the positions, and the lights — this is what gives rise to the neue Realität. Plastik is not representation (Darstellung) in the mimetic sense. The highest plastic achievement is not graspability but the sovereign organization of relations.
- **Rule**: Shift design judgment from mimetic fidelity to relational sovereignty: the work succeeds when the relations between volumes, measures, positions, and lights are sovereignly organized.
- **Source**: p.188
- **Quote (DE)**: "daraus entsteht die neue realität."
- **Quote (EN)**: "from this arises the new reality."
- **Category**: space

### Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Analyse der Brancusi-Entwicklung als Lehrsequenz / Analysis of Brancusi's development as teaching sequence
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: The student examines the full developmental sequence of Brancusi's work — from anatomical academic study (Abb. 166) through material-driven wood-working, to the final dematerialized volumes — and locates each work on the Material→Volume→Light axis. Special attention: when Brancusi switches materials, he restarts from Stage 1 within the new material, re-working the entire sequence. The student must identify this restart behavior and understand why it is biologically (organically) lawful.
- **Purpose**: Trains the ability to read formal stages from works already made; illustrates P-10 and P-09; demonstrates that the pedagogical sequence is not artificial but inherent in the material logic.
- **Source**: p.178

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Lipchitz-Analyse — Perforierter Block / Lipchitz analysis — perforated block
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: The student studies the Lipchitz series (Abb. 166–174): first the Stilleben in stone (1918), then the Kunststein Plastik (1918), then the Liegende Frau (1925), then the two 1926 bronzes (Abb. 170–171), then the 1927 bronzes (Abb. 172–173), finally the 1928 bronze (Abb. 174). The student notes: (a) the progressive deepening of hollows; (b) the transition from assembled to "grown" (gezogen) piece; (c) the moment when the air-volume bounded by ribs becomes the primary plastic element.
- **Purpose**: Trains recognition of the neue Wendung (P-11); demonstrates that the breakthrough to virtual volume is not arbitrary but follows from the internal logic of the hollowing-out process.
- **Source**: p.183–185

**EXERCISE [E-03]**: Skizze einer allgemeinen Elementenlehre / Sketch of a general elemental doctrine
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: The student constructs a skeleton of a general elemental doctrine for plastic design, working with the three-part taxonomy given at p.192: (I) existing forms (mathematical-geometrical; biotechnical), (II) newly generated free forms, built from the three generative axes (Verhältnisse/Lage/Bewegung; Materialwerte; Licht), and operated on by the three relational operations (Kontraste; Abweichungen; Variationen). The student must be able to place any plastic form within this taxonomy and identify which generative axis produced it and which relational operation connects it to adjacent forms.
- **Purpose**: Trains the systematic thinking that underlies design (P-17); demonstrates that the skeleton of an elemental doctrine is not a formula-book but a method of systematic investigation.
- **Source**: p.191–192

**EXERCISE [E-04]**: Psycho-physische Wirkung der Farbelemente / Psycho-physical effect of color elements
- **Task** [INFERRED]: The student is asked to identify and record the psycho-physical (sinnlich-sittliche) effects of color elements through systematic observation — not through memorizing formulas (since few are established with certainty), but through deliberate experiential testing: placing the same form in yellow, green, and red environments and observing the behavioral and felt responses. Cross-reference with animal-behavior studies (irritability of animals to specific colors) to confirm the material basis of the effect.
- **Purpose**: Trains recognition of elemental psycho-physical effects as design material (P-16, P-18); counters the false view that color-effect is merely subjective.
- **Source**: p.190

- **Shows**: A free-standing figurative sculpture in artificial stone (Kunststein) — a human figure reduced to angular, geometric volumes: sharp contrasts between elevations and depressions (Erhöhungen und Vertiefungen), pointed and rounded forms, and above all pronounced penetrations of straight and curved surfaces (planes). - **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-10, P-12 — "diese arbeit ist von seltenem reichtum in der verwendung ihrer mittel: in kontrasten der erhöhungen und vertiefungen, des spitzen und runden, kleinen und großen, des lichtu und schattens und in den durchdringungen der geraden und gebogenen flächen (platten)."
- **Text reference**: Yes — directly cited with detailed formal analysis.

**FIGURE [Fig-18]**: Abb. 169 — Jacques Lipchitz 1925, Liegende Frau / Reclining woman
- **Shows**: A horizontal reclining sculpture — a broadly spread form with deep hollowing: "die Aushöhlungen werden immer tiefer." The form is massive, resting on a rectangular base, with the upper surface carved into increasingly deep concave hollows. The concavities now threaten to break through the material entirely.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-11 — The progressive deepening of the hollows is the key transitional stage before the breakthrough to the perforated block. "Die Aushöhlungen werden immer tiefer" — this is the verge of the neue Wendung.

- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-11 — The approach to the neue Wendung in bronze: the form begins to open, with air-spaces introduced between elements. Caption: "eine neue wendung: durchbrochen."
- **Text reference**: Yes — captioned "eine neue wendung: durchbrochen."
- **Source**: p.183

**FIGURE [Fig-20]**: Abb. 171 — Jacques Lipchitz 1926, Plastik (Bronze)
- **Shows**: A bronze figure-sculpture with more pronounced opening — the form is a seated or crouching figure whose internal voids are clearly visible, with material forming arches and bridges across open space.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-11 — Further progression of the neue Wendung. Together with Abb. 170, demonstrates the decisive opening of the solid block toward the perforated form.
- **Text reference**: Yes — cited as part of the neue Wendung sequence.
- **Source**: p.183

**FIGURE [Fig-21]**: Abb. 172 — Jacques Lipchitz 1927, Plastik (Bronze)
- **Shows**: A bronze figure composed largely of open curves and voids — the figure is built from S-curved and spiraling elements with extensive open air-space. The solid material now frames the void rather than filling it.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-11 — "wie sehr das blockhafte bei lipchitz noch maßgebend ist, zeigen abb. 173 und 174. lipchitz versichert dem besucher, daß die plastik nicht montiert, sondern aus einem stück 'gezogen' ist."
- **Text reference**: Yes — cited in the analysis of the "gezogen" (grown from one piece) construction method.
- **Source**: p.184

**FIGURE [Fig-22]**: Abb. 173 — Jacques Lipchitz 1927, Plastik (Bronze)
- **Shows**: A bronze figure-sculpture with a grid-like open front plane — the upper body forms a lattice/grille of rectangular openings, while the lower body remains more solid. The air-volume is explicitly contained within a rib-system. Caption: "der perforierte Block: ein System von Rippen begrenzt das Luftvolumen."
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-11 — The perforated block fully realized: the air-volume bounded by ribs is now the primary plastic element.
- **Text reference**: Yes — directly cited with the key formulation "der perforierte Block."
- **Source**: p.184

**FIGURE [Fig-23]**: Abb. 174 — Jacques Lipchitz 1928, Plastik (Bronze)
- **Shows**: A tall, complex bronze figure-sculpture of considerable verticality — a dense weaving of curved, spiraling, and angular wire-like bronze elements with extensive open space. The overall form is figure-like but entirely open; the solid material is reduced to a network of linear elements enclosing multiple air-volumes.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-11 — The culmination of the Lipchitz development on these pages: the solid mass has been almost entirely sublimated into a network of material bounding multiple air-volumes simultaneously. This is the closest approach to virtual volume while remaining in solid material.
- **Text reference**: Yes — final image in the Lipchitz series.
- **Source**: p.185
