## Section: Lichtgestaltung — Eggeling, Hirschfeld-Mack, and the Color-Sound Problem (pp. 19–22)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The kinetic color image — moving light composition — represents the highest development of the reflektorische Lichtgestaltung (reflective light design), surpassing the static tableau. The central problem is the Zeitproblem: light composition must operate in time, just as music does.

**Evidence**: Eggeling developed the Zeitproblem scientifically from Futurist beginnings; he built movement sequences from linear elements to demonstrate the organic development of forms. Hirschfeld-Mack and Schwerdtfeger at the Bauhaus worked with reflective light and shadow plays; their instruments use prisms, rotating color discs, and iris shutters. Hans Richter approached the theoretical side of space-time continuity in the Lichtsynthese. Alexander László's Farblichtmusik ("color-light-music") approached the problem from the acoustic-kinetic direction, though Moholy-Nagy notes his work rests too heavily on subjective statements about colored hearing rather than scientific investigation of optophonetic principles.

**Conclusion**: The essence of the single static image is the Produktion von Spannungen (production of tensions) in color and form relationships. The essence of the reflective image in motion is these same tensions in kinetic form — as an optical time-flow in equally weighted equilibrium. A new, more demanding active state of the viewer is created: Meditation before a static image is replaced by an accelerated, engaged kinetic experience.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Zeitproblem / The Time-Problem of Light Composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kinetic Lichtgestaltung is not merely accelerated painting — it demands its own laws. Eggeling was the first to develop the Zeitproblem scientifically, using constructed movement sequences from the simplest linear elements to make visible the developing complexity of Tempo, Wiederholung (repetition), Sprung (leap), and Richtungsvarianten (directional variants) — the ABC of movement phenomena in light-dark and directional relations.
- **Rule**: Design kinetic light compositions from the Zeitproblem outward — sequence, tempo, and direction are the primary compositional parameters, not the individual static frame.
- **Source**: p.19
- **Quote (DE)**: "Er fotografierte auf dem Tricktisch eine aus einfachsten linearen Elementen aufgebaute Bewegungsfolge und versuchte das aus dem Einfachen sich entwickelnde Komplizierte durch Auswägen der Entwicklungsverhältnisse in Größe, Tempo, Wiederholung, Sprung usw. den Augen faßbar zu machen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "He photographed on the animation stand a movement sequence built from the simplest linear elements and attempted to make visible, through the weighing of developmental proportions in size, tempo, repetition, leap, etc., the complexity developing out of simplicity."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Gleichgewichtiger Zustand / Equally Weighted Equilibrium
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The essence of the single static image is the Produktion von Spannungen in Farben- und Formverhältnissen (production of tensions in color and form relations), the production of new, more colorful harmonies in equally weighted equilibrium. The essence of the reflective image is these same tensions in kinetic form, as an optical time-flow in equally weighted equilibrium.
- **Rule**: In reflective color-image composition, the simultaneous and sequential color-form tensions must be held in gleichgewichtigem Zustand (equally weighted equilibrium) — neither static rest nor chaotic flux.
- **Source**: p.22
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Wesen des Einzelbildes ist die Produktion von Spannungen in Farben- und (oder) in Formverhältnissen auf der Fläche, die Produktion neuer, farbiger Harmonien in gleichgewichtigem Zustande."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The essence of the single image is the production of tensions in color and (or) form relations on the surface, the production of new, colorful harmonies in equally weighted equilibrium."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Aktiver Zustand des Zuschauers / Active State of the Viewer
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kinetic Lichtgestaltung creates a heightened active state in the viewer — instead of Meditation before a static image, it creates a gesteigerter aktiver Zustand (heightened active state), because the viewer must simultaneously grasp new life-events at the optical tempo of the work. The kinetic design form is thus justified without doubt as Notwendigkeit der Gestaltung von optischen Erlebnissen (necessity of design of optical experiences). The polar question is: static or kinetic — and it is the question of the polaren Ausgleichs (polar balance) and the rhythm governing life.
- **Rule**: Design kinetic light works that demand active, not passive, reception — the tempo and complexity of optical events must require simultaneous apprehension, not leisurely contemplation.
- **Source**: p.22
- **Quote (DE)**: "Hier schafft das neu auftretende Zeitmoment und seine immer weiter lautende Gliederung einen gesteigerten aktiven Zustand des Zuschauers, der — statt einer Meditation über ein statisches Bild — statt eines Hineinsinkens, woraus seine Selbstvergessen gegeben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Here the newly emerging time-element and its ever-more-articulated structure creates a heightened active state in the viewer, who — instead of a meditation on a static image — instead of a sinking-into, from which self-forgetting arises."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Farbe-Ton-Forschung / Color-Sound Research
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The investigation of the relationships between color and sound (Farbe-Ton-Forschung) is a legitimate and necessary area, but it must be grounded in scientific-objective investigation of optophonetic principles, not subjective claims about colored hearing. László's Farblichtmusik rests too much on subjective statements of farbigen Tonens (colored hearing) instead of scientific objective investigation of optophonetic principles. The work of Hirschfeld-Mack and Schwerdtfeger at the Bauhaus, using reflective lights and shadow plays with prismatic instruments, is closer to the correct scientific path, as it is grounded in the properties of the photographic light-sensitive surface.
- **Rule**: Color-sound research must proceed from scientific-objective investigation of the optophonetic, not from subjective analogies between sensory modes.
- **Source**: pp.20–21
- **Quote (DE)**: "Er fußt zu sehr auf subjektiven Aussagen des farbigen Tonsehens statt auf wissenschaftlicher objektiver Erforschung der optofonetischen Grundsätze."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It rests too much on subjective statements about colored hearing instead of scientific objective investigation of optophonetic principles."
- **Category**: light

## Section: HAUS-PINAKOTHEK — Democratic Distribution of Color Images (pp. 23–25)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The equal right of every person to the highest-level satisfaction of their needs — including color image experience — is today a goal of progressive technical development. The machine of production and multiplication (Vervielfältigungsmöglichkeiten) has already democratized literature; now it must democratize color imagery.

**Evidence**: Printing, the Schnellpresse (fast press), and multiplication techniques have made books, films, and magazines accessible to virtually everyone. Color multiplication is still imperfect but developing. Farbige Harmonien (colored harmonies) are already being distributed through reproductions, lithos, and illustrated prints. Color film slides may be stored at home like gramophone records. Projectors with 4 large and 4 small projectors allow prismatic decomposition and additive/subtractive mixing of colors from 8 basic tones — creating feinster Übergänge (finest transitions) and surprising exchanges of colored surfaces, oscillating, dissolving, flowing.

**Conclusion**: The "Haus-Pinakothek" — the home picture gallery of projected color images — is the democratic destination of the kinetic Lichtgestaltung. In China and Japan equivalent Hauspinakotheken are already common. In Europe it will come when the need is felt.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Vervielfältigungsmöglichkeiten / Multiplication as Democratic Access
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The machine and its multiplication possibilities for consumer goods have already achieved a general leveling of the quality of life. Through the invention of printing, the Schnellpresse, the photograph, and film, colored harmonic images — formerly only for the wealthy — are now within reach of almost everyone. The future will likely present color-film slides in the home as gramophone records are now stored.
- **Rule**: Design color and light works that can be mechanically reproduced and widely distributed — Vervielfältigungsmöglichkeiten (multiplication possibilities) are not degradation but democratic necessity.
- **Source**: p.23
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Technik, und ihre Vervielfältigungsmöglichkeiten von Gebrauchsgegenständen haben in weitem Maße eine Nivellierung und gleichzeitige Nivohebung der Menschheit durchgesetzt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Technology and its multiplication possibilities for consumer goods have to a great extent achieved both a leveling and a simultaneous elevation of quality for humanity."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Freischwebende Farbengarben / Free-Floating Color Projections Without Screen
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kinetic projective Lichtgestaltung will give the greatest value to projection without a direct screen surface — freischwebende, einander durchdringende Farbengarben und -massen ohne direkte Projektionsfläche (free-floating, mutually interpenetrating color sheaves and masses without a direct projection surface). This will achieve the most developed static image — the richest tension of the space-time-span. It follows that in future periods only the "MALER" (painter) who produces genuinely sovereign and maximum achievements will remain.
- **Rule**: The highest form of color-light design projects into space without a screen surface — the medium itself is luminous air, not a reflective plane.
- **Source**: pp.24–25
- **Quote (DE)**: "Es ist wahrscheinlich, daß die künftige Entwicklung den größten Wert auf die kinetische projektorische Lichtgestaltung legen wird, sogar wahrscheinlich mit im Raume freiwebenden, einander durchdringenden Farbengarben und -massen ohne direkte Projektionsfläche."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It is probable that future development will place the greatest value on kinetic projective light design, very likely with free-floating, mutually interpenetrating color sheaves and masses in space, without a direct projection surface."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Technische Kenntnisse des Malers / Technical Knowledge Required of the Painter
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A correct handling of the reflective (film) and projective Lichtgestaltung — which will be the dominant color art of the near future — requires thorough knowledge of optics and the mechanical-technical aspects of their use, exactly as the Fotografie demands. It is astonishing that the "geniale" (genius) painter today has so little scientific knowledge compared to the "nüchternen" (sober) Techniker. The Techniker already works long with interference phenomena, subtraction and addition mixes of light, polarization phenomena, etc. That these are not applied to color composition with the same matter-of-factness seems a provisional utopia.
- **Rule**: The painter who works with light must acquire scientific optical knowledge as rigorously as technical mastery — intuition without technical grounding is insufficient for Lichtgestaltung.
- **Source**: pp.24–25
- **Quote (DE)**: "Es ist überraschend, daß der heutige 'geniale' Maler gegenüber dem 'nüchternen' Techniker recht wenig wissenschaftliche Kenntnisse besitzt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It is astonishing that today's 'ingenious' painter possesses comparatively little scientific knowledge compared to the 'sober' technician."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

## Section: FOTOGRAFIE (pp. 25–27)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: Since the invention of photography, nothing essentially new has been found in the principle and technique of the process. All innovations since have been based on the Daguerreian reproductive conception: Wiedergabe (Kopie) — reproduction (copy) — of Nature in the perspective mode of the Camera obscura. Each period has merely developed its own "fotografische Manier" in the image of the dominant painterly manner (cf. p.47).

**Evidence**: Humans invent new instruments and methods, but at first the new function is pressed into the old form — the new is blocked by the old. The futuristic (static) painting that later became self-evidently known was already a failed attempt at Bewegungssimultanität (simultaneity of movement) — attempting what film would naturally achieve. The Konstruktivisten's painting, which abandoned reflective (reproduktive) Lichtgestaltung altogether, already pointed the way to a new optical development — yet this is barely yet recognized.

**Conclusion**: The photographic apparatus, as technical instrument and the most important Produktionsfaktor (production factor) of the Filmgestaltung, copies nature "getreu" (faithfully) — and thereby loses sight of the essential form elements: the spans of Form, the Durchdringung (interpenetration), the Hell-Dunkel-Zusammenhänge (light-dark relations), the Bewegung (movement), the Tempo. This is why average film has remained at Reproduktion of nature and stage presentations.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Lichtempfindlichkeit als Grundelement / Light-Sensitivity as Foundation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The fact that the light-sensitive surface (glass, metal, paper, celluloid, etc.) is one of the fundamental elements of the photographic process was completely neglected earlier. The light-sensitive surface was always directed away from its original path — the Camera obscura — subordinating it to the perspective laws governing it, to the fixed holding (Reproduzieren) of single objects in their property to reflect or absorb light. Not even the possibilities of this combination were sufficiently exploited.
- **Rule**: Design photographs that exploit the full properties of the light-sensitive surface — its reaction to light itself, not merely its capacity to record perspective views of objects.
- **Source**: p.26
- **Quote (DE)**: "Man vernachlässigte früher in der Fotografie vollkommen die Tatsache, daß die Lichtempfindlichkeit einer chemisch präparierten Fläche (Glas, Metall, Papier, Zelluloid usw.) eines der Grundelemente des fotografischen Verfahrens ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Earlier, photography completely neglected the fact that the light-sensitivity of a chemically prepared surface (glass, metal, paper, celluloid, etc.) is one of the fundamental elements of the photographic process."
- **Category**: photogram

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Fotografischer Apparat als Ergänzung des Auges / The Camera as Complement to the Eye
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The awareness of the possibilities of the camera should have led to recognizing existences that are imperceptible or barely perceptible to our unaided instrument, the eye — making them sichtbar (visible) with the help of the photographic apparatus. That is: der fotografische Apparat kann unser optisches Instrument, das Auge, vervollkommnen bzw. ergänzen (the photographic apparatus can perfect or complement our optical instrument, the eye). This principle has been applied in some scientific experiments (motion study of walking, gallop) and in zoological, botanical, and mineralogical investigations of single phenomena whose Zusammenhänge cannot be established in secondary sense terms (see pp.48–54, pp.55–59). We have not yet used the capabilities of the apparatus in secondary sense terms — the "fehlerhaften" (faulty) photographs already show this.
- **Rule**: Use the camera as a prosthetic extension of human vision — to reveal what the unaided eye cannot see, not merely to record what it already sees.
- **Source**: p.26
- **Quote (DE)**: "d. h. der fotografische Apparat kann unser optisches Instrument, das Auge, vervollkommnen bzw. ergänzen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "That is: the photographic apparatus can perfect or complement our optical instrument, the eye."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Vorstellungsbild / The Mental Image Complemented by the Photo
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Through one hundred years of photography and two decades of film, we have been informed in this respect by an immeasurable number of reports on the world. We see — through the agency of these instruments — with completely different eyes. One can say: we die Welt mit vollkommen anderen Augen sehen (we see the world with completely completely different eyes). Yet the total result is still only a visual encyclopedic achievement. What is needed: not reproduction of the world but the production of new Relationen (relations) that are important for life.
- **Rule**: The goal of photography is not encyclopedic documentation of the world as it appears, but the production of new visual relations (neue Relationen) that illuminate aspects of life otherwise imperceptible.
- **Source**: p.27
- **Quote (DE)**: "Man kann sagen, daß wir die Welt mit vollkommen anderen Augen sehen. Trotzdem ist das Gesamtergebnis bis heute nicht viel mehr als eine visuelle enzyklopädische Leistung. Das genügt uns aber nicht. Wir wollen planmäßig produzieren, da für das Leben das Schaffen neuer Relationen von Wichtigkeit ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "One can say that we see the world with completely different eyes. Yet the total result to date is not much more than a visual encyclopedic achievement. This is not enough for us. We want to produce planfully, since for life the creation of new relations is what matters."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

## Section: PRODUKTION REPRODUKTION (pp. 28–29)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The development of human beings consists in the synthesis of all their functional apparatuses. Art achieves this — and it is one of its most important tasks, since it depends on the completeness of all functional Wirkungskomplexe (effect complexes). Art attempts to establish and enrich in the perceptual domain new, far-reaching Beziehungen (relations) between the known and still-unknown optical, acoustic, and other functional appearances. Under this perspective, Gestaltungsversuche (design experiments) are valuable only when they produce neue, bisher unbekannte Relationen (new, hitherto unknown relations). Reproduktion (repetition of already existing relations) without enriching Gesichtspunkte (perspectives) from the standpoint of creative design is at best a virtuosic affair.

**Evidence**: The Produktion (productive design) of the human apparatus must now extend the Reproduktionszwecken (reproduction purposes) to productive purposes. This has been applied to Grammofon and Fotografie (and can be applied to other reproduction media such as the Fernsehen/television and the speaking film).

**Conclusion**: If in the area of photography we wish a revaluation toward productive purposes, we must use the light-sensitivity of the photographic plate — from ourselves (with mirrors or lens devices, passing light through crystals, liquids, etc.) — to fixate light appearances that are gestalteten Lichterscheinungen (designed light appearances). Precursors for this type of design can be seen in astronomical and X-ray images.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Produktion vs. Reproduktion — The Fundamental Distinction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every apparatus that has been used only for Reproduktionszwecken (reproduction purposes) can be turned to Produktion (productive design). Reproduktion — the repetition of already existing relations — without enriching viewpoints from the standpoint of creative design is at best only a virtuosic matter. Produktion — productively designed Gestaltung — creates new, hitherto unknown relations. The goal is to expand the Reproduktionsapparat to Produktionsmittel.
- **Rule**: When using any technical apparatus (camera, gramophone, film), ask first: is this Reproduktion of something that could exist without this apparatus, or is it Produktion — something only this apparatus can create? Only Produktion advances the medium.
- **Source**: p.28
- **Quote (DE)**: "Unter diesem Gesichtspunkte sind die Gestaltungsversuche nur dann wertvoll, wenn sie neue, bisher unbekannte Relationen produzieren. Damit ist wiederum gesagt, daß die Reproduktion (Wiederholung bereits existierender Relationen) ohne bereichernde Gesichtspunkte aus dem besonderen Gesichtspunkt der schöpferischen Gestaltung im besten Falle nur als virtuose Angelegenheit zu betrachten ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "From this perspective, design experiments are valuable only when they produce new, hitherto unknown relations. This says again that Reproduktion (repetition of already existing relations) without enriching perspectives from the standpoint of creative design is at best only a virtuosic affair."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Lichtempfindlichkeit der Platte als Produktionsmittel / Light-Sensitivity as Production Instrument
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: If we wish a revaluation in the area of photography toward productive purposes, we must use the light-sensitivity of the photographic plate — from ourselves (using mirrors or lens devices, passing light through crystals, liquids, etc.) — to fixate gestalteten Lichterscheinungen (designed light appearances). The precursors for this kind of Lichtgestaltung are the astronomischen and Röntgenaufnahmen (X-ray images).
- **Rule**: Exploit the light-sensitivity of the photographic surface directly — use it to design light, not merely to capture light reflecting off pre-existing objects.
- **Source**: pp.28–29
- **Quote (DE)**: "Wenn wir auf dem Gebiete der Fotografie eine Umwertung in produktiver Richtung vornehmen wollen, müssen wir die Lichtempfindlichkeit der fotografischen (Bromsilber)-Platte dazu benutzen: die von uns selbst (mit Spiegeln oder Linsenvorrichtungen aus, durchleuchteten Kristallen, Flüssigkeiten usw.) gestalteten Lichterscheinungen (Lichtspiel-Elemente) darauf zu fixieren."
- **Quote (EN)**: "If we wish a revaluation in the domain of photography in a productive direction, we must use the light-sensitivity of the photographic (silver bromide) plate to: fixate on it the light appearances (light-play elements) that we ourselves have designed (with mirrors or lens devices, by passing light through crystals, liquids, etc.)."
- **Category**: photogram

## Section: FOTOGRAFIE OHNE KAMERA — DAS „FOTOGRAMM" (p. 30)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The camera-less photograph — the Fotogramm — is the most direct form of photographic Lichtgestaltung. It proves that light is a plastic medium, a new Gestaltungsmittel (design medium) that can be handled like color in painting or tone in music.

**Evidence**: Practically, the Fotogramm is produced by allowing light to fall through objects of various refractive indices onto a screen (photographic plate, light-sensitive paper), or by deflecting it from its original path using various lens-devices — covering parts of the screen with shadow, etc. This process can proceed with or without a camera apparatus. In the second case (cameraless), the technique is the fixation of a differentiated Licht- und Schatten-Spiels (light and shadow play). This leads to the possibilities of Lichtgestaltung, where light is handled as a new Gestaltungsmittel — as color is in painting, as tone in music. This type of Lichtgestaltung is called the Fotogramm (see pp.69–76).

**Conclusion**: Another path of Ausbau (development) toward Produktion would be the investigation and use of various chemical mixtures — for the eye imperceptible light appearances (electro-magnetic oscillations) to fixate them (as with X-ray photography). A further path is the construction of new apparatus: first, those that use the Camera obscura while eliminating perspective presentation; second, apparatus with lens and mirror devices that comprehend the object from all sides simultaneously; and apparatus built on different optical laws than our eye.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Das Fotogramm als Lichtgestaltung / The Photogram as Light Design
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Fotogramm is the direct camera-less photograph — light falls onto the light-sensitive surface through objects of varying refraction, or is deflected from its original path; parts of the surface are covered with shadow. The result is a fixation of a differentiated Licht- und Schatten-Spiels. This leads to the possibilities of Lichtgestaltung, where light functions als neues Gestaltungsmittel (as a new design medium) — as color is handled in painting, as tone in music. This type of Lichtgestaltung I call the Fotogramm.
- **Rule**: The Fotogramm — cameraless photography — is Produktion in its purest form: an image that exists only because of the medium of light acting on a light-sensitive surface, not a record of anything pre-existing.
- **Source**: p.30
- **Quote (DE)**: "Dieser Weg führt zu Möglichkeiten der Lichtgestaltung, wobei das Licht als ein neues Gestaltungsmittel, wie in der Malerei die Farbe, in der Musik der Ton, zu handhaben ist. Ich nenne diese Art Lichtgestaltung Fotogramm."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This path leads to possibilities of light design, in which light is to be handled as a new design medium — as color is in painting, as tone is in music. I call this type of light design the Fotogramm."
- **Category**: photogram

## Section: DIE ZUKUNFT DES FOTOGRAFISCHEN VERFAHRENS (pp. 31–35)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The creative application of photography's principles and laws will lead to the end of photography as mere "Kunst" (art) in the traditional sense. The human spirit creates creative domains wherever it can be creatively active — so in the next period, photography too will experience a great flowering.

**Evidence**: Photography as a Darstellungskunst (art of representation) is not a simple nature copy. The rarity of truly "good" photographs proves this. The mysterious and simultaneously convincing quality of good photography — the extraordinary brilliance of light-filled whites, the exact magic of finest fabric texture, the finest grey transitions, the precision of steel construction just as in sea-foam — all fixated in the hundredth or thousandth of a second. Since light phenomena in motion offer generally higher differentiation possibilities than in the static state, all photographic procedures reach their highpoint in film (Bewegungsbeziehungen der Lichtprojektionen / movement relations of light projections). Existing film practice has limited itself to Reproduktion of dramatic actions, without using the possibilities of the photographic apparatus creatively.

**Conclusion**: The film apparatus as technical instrument and most important Produktionsfaktor of Filmgestaltung copies nature "naturgetreu" (faithfully to nature) — but loses the essential form elements: Form spans, Durchdringung, Hell-Dunkel-Zusammenhänge, Bewegung, Tempo. It is now revolutionary to link the development of color-and-form-free photography and film — in order to free the pure form relationships from the dominance of the handmade (manuellen) image, bringing color photography and the tönende Bild (sounding image) and optophonic Gestaltung to a new, fully sound basis.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Fotografie als Darstellungskunst / Photography as Representational Art
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Photography as Darstellungskunst (art of representation) is not a simple nature copy. Good photography is rare. Its quality lies in a new Qualitätsgefühl für das Hell-Dunkel, the radiant white, the exact magic of finest transitions, the precision of steel construction in the foam of the sea — all fixated in the hundredth or thousandth of a second.
- **Rule**: Judge a photograph not by its fidelity to a pre-existing subject but by whether it creates a new Qualitätsgefühl für das Hell-Dunkel — a quality of light-dark that could not be produced by any other means.
- **Source**: p.31
- **Quote (DE)**: "Es entsteht ein neues Qualitätsgefühl für das Hell-Dunkel, das strahlend Weiße, die mit flüssigem Licht gefüllten schwarz-grauen Übergänge, den exakten Zauber feinsten Gewebes: in den Rippen der Stahlkonstruktion ebenso wie in dem Schaum des Meeres — und das alles festgehalten im hundertsten oder tausendsten Teil einer Sekunde."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A new quality-sense for light-dark arises — the radiantly white, the black-grey transitions filled with liquid light, the exact magic of the finest fabric: in the ribs of the steel construction as well as in the foam of the sea — and all of this fixated in the hundredth or thousandth of a second."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Der Fotografische Apparat als Produktionsfaktor / The Camera as Production Factor
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photographic apparatus as technical instrument and most important Produktionsfaktor (production factor) of Filmgestaltung copies the objects of the world "naturgetreu" (faithfully to nature). Aber hat man bisher damit gerechnet, daß die wesentlichen Elemente der Filmgestaltung nicht genügend geläutert wurden — the essential form elements: the spans of Form, Durchdringung, Hell-Dunkel-Zusammenhänge, Bewegung, Tempo. Without these, the average film remained at the Reproduktion of natural and stage presentations.
- **Rule**: The camera's capacity for faithful reproduction is its weakness, not its strength — the filmmaker must override this tendency and design the essential form-elements (Form, Durchdringung, Hell-Dunkel, Bewegung, Tempo) deliberately.
- **Source**: p.32
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der fotografische Apparat als technisches Instrument und wichtigster Produktionsfaktor der Filmgestaltung kopiert 'naturgetreu' die Gegenstände der Welt. Damit muß man rechnen; aber bisher hatte man vielzuviel damit gerechnet und so kam es, daß die wesentlichen Elemente der Filmgestaltung nicht genügend geläutert wurden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The photographic apparatus as technical instrument and most important production factor of film design copies the objects of the world 'faithfully to nature.' This must be taken into account; but hitherto it has been taken into account far too much, and so it came about that the essential elements of film design were not sufficiently refined."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Verwendungsmöglichkeiten der Fotografie / Applications of Photography
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The application possibilities of photography are already countless, since through it the finest effects of Helligkeitswerte (brightness values) can be fixated — with further advances through color values. These include: Festhalten von Situationen, von Realität (pp.84–89); Zusammenfügen und Aufeinander- und Nebeneinander-Projizieren; Durchdringung; organisierbare Szenenverwandlung; Überrealität, Utopie und Scherz (here is the new wit) (pp.98–105); objektive, also expressive Porträts (pp.92–97); Werbemittel; Plakat; politische Propaganda (pp.104–111); Gestaltungsmittel für Foto-Bücher, d.h. Fotografien an Stelle des Textes: Typofoto (pp.36 and pp.122–135); Gestaltungsmittel für flächige oder räumliche gegenstandslose absolute Lichtprojektionen; simultanes Kino.
- **Rule**: Recognize the full spectrum of photography's applications: from documentary reality-capture through surrealist montage, portrait, advertising, Typofoto (photography as text substitute), and absolute light projection — each demands its own photographic laws.
- **Source**: pp.33–34
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Verwendungsmöglichkeiten der Fotografie sind schon jetzt unzählig, da durch sie die gröbsten und feinsten Wirkungen der Helligkeitswerte — bei weiterem Fortschreiten auch der Farbenwerte — fixiert werden können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The application possibilities of photography are already countless, since through it the coarsest and finest effects of brightness values — with further progress also color values — can be fixated."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Fotoplastiken / Fotomontage as Synthetic Vision
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Among the application possibilities of film, the Wiedergabe verschiedener Bewegungen in their dynamic — scientific as well as observation-based of functional, chemical kind; time-lapse and slow-motion; wirelessly projected film images — are most important. To illustrate one of these use possibilities: Fotoplastiken (pp.106–110) — assembled from various photographs into a new simultaneous Darstellung (presentation); compressed interpenetration of the visual and verbal (Wortwitz); uncanny, imaginarily growing simultaneously narrating and tangible: "als das Leben selbst" (as life itself). This still-primitive manual work will be achievable with projection and new copying processes mechanically.
- **Rule**: The Fotoplastik (photomontage) is a valid form of Produktion — it creates simultaneous visual-verbal meaning that neither a single photograph nor text alone could produce.
- **Source**: pp.34–35
- **Quote (DE)**: "Sie sind — aus verschiedenen Fotografien zusammengesetzt — eine Versuchs-Methode der simultanen Darstellung; komprimierte Durchdringung von visuellem und Wortwitz; unheimliche, ins Imaginäre wachsende Verbindung der allerrealen, imitativen Mittel. Aber sie können gleichzeitig erzählend, handfast sein; veristischer 'als das Leben selbst'."
- **Quote (EN)**: "They are — assembled from various photographs — an experimental method of simultaneous presentation; compressed interpenetration of the visual and wordplay; uncanny, imaginarily growing connection of the most realistic, imitative means. But they can simultaneously be narrative, palpable; more realistic 'than life itself.'"
- **Category**: simultaneity

## Section: TYPOFOTO (pp. 36–38)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: Not mere curiosity, nor economic considerations alone, but a deep human interest in the events of the world has produced the enormous reach of the news-media: typography, film, and radio. The concentrated work of organization is the spirit-filled consequence that synthesizes all elements of human creation: Spieltrieb (play instinct), Anteilnahme (participation), Erfindungen (inventions), Werkzeuge (tools).

**Evidence**: Typography serves communication. Photography is the visual representation of the optically graspable. Das Typofoto ist die visuell exakteste dargestellte Mitteilung — "The Typofoto is the visually most exact presented message." Each era has its own optical attitude. Our era's is that of film, Lichtreklame (light advertising), Simultanität of sinnlich wahrnehmbarer Ereignisse (sensory-perceptible events). Typography alone functions in linear dimension. Through the inclusion of photographic procedures, it has expanded to an entirely new — heute als total bekannten — dimensionality. Illustrated newspapers, placards, and display printing pioneered this. Typographic practice was formerly held rigidly to one material and one type of setting-technique. An effective loosening can only be achieved through the most far-reaching, comprehensive use of photographic-zincographic-galvanoplastic etc. techniques.

**Conclusion**: Linear, message-conveying typography is only a mediating stopgap between the content of a message and the receiving human. The schema: MITTEILUNG → TYPOGRAFIE → MENSCH (MESSAGE → TYPOGRAPHY → HUMAN). Typography moves now toward the aufnehmendem Menschen (the receiving human) — employing the optical possibilities of its subjective existence in the work. A Fotografie as typographic material uses the Objektivität (objectivity) of photography in its presentation, which permits no individual interpretation — building up to a visuell-assoziativ-begrifflich synthetischen Kontinuität (visually-associatively-conceptually synthetic continuity) — to an eindeutigen Darstellung in optisch gültiger Gestalt. The Typofoto regulates the new Tempo of the new visual literature.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Typofoto — Definition and Necessity
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Was ist Typofoto? Typografie ist in Druck gestaltete Mitteilung. Fotografie ist visuelle Darstellung des optisch Faßbaren. Das Typofoto ist die visuell exakteste dargestellte Mitteilung. ("What is Typofoto? Typography is message shaped in print. Photography is the visual representation of the optically graspable. Typofoto is the visually most exact presented message.") The Typofoto is not an aesthetic choice — it is the logical consequence of the visual literacy of the modern age.
- **Rule**: When designing a message for print, ask whether the addition of photographic material can make the communication more exact and less dependent on individual interpretation — if yes, use Typofoto.
- **Source**: p.37
- **Quote (DE)**: "Was ist Typofoto? Typografie ist in Druck gestaltete Mitteilung. Fotografie ist visuelle Darstellung des optisch Faßbaren. Das Typofoto ist die visuell exakteste dargestellte Mitteilung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "What is Typofoto? Typography is message shaped in print. Photography is the visual representation of the optically graspable. Typofoto is the visually most exact presented message."
- **Category**: typophoto

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Optische Einstellung der Zeit / The Optical Attitude of Each Era
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Each era has its own optical Einstellung (attitude). Our time is that of film, Lichtreklame (light advertising), the Simultanität sinnlich wahrnehmbarer Ereignisse (simultaneity of sensory-perceptible events). This has also produced a continuously developing new creative basis in typography. Typography alone moves in almost exclusively linearer Dimension (linear dimension). Through the inclusion of photographic procedures it has expanded to a completely new — today already totally familiar — dimensionality. The work done by illustrated newspapers, placards, and display printing initiated this.
- **Rule**: Typography must not remain in the linear dimension of pre-photographic culture — it must expand to the simultaneous, synoptic dimensionality that the era of film and Lichtreklame demands.
- **Source**: p.37
- **Quote (DE)**: "Jede Zeit hat ihre eigene optische Einstellung. Unsere Zeit: die des Films, der Lichtreklame, der Simultanität sinnlich wahrnehmbarer Ereignisse."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Each era has its own optical attitude. Ours: that of film, light-advertising, the simultaneity of sensory-perceptible events."
- **Category**: typophoto

**PRINCIPLE [P-20]**: Typografie als Vermittlungsnot / Typography as Mediating Stopgap
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Linear, message-conveying typography is only a vermittelndes Notglied (mediating stopgap) between the content of a message and the receiving human: MITTEILUNG → TYPOGRAFIE → MENSCH. Photography as typographic material — used alongside or in place of words as "Fototext" — employs the Objektivität of its presentation, which permits no individual interpretation. It builds up to a visuell-assoziativ-begrifflich synthetischen Kontinuität: to a eindeutigen Darstellung in optisch gültiger Gestalt (unambiguous representation in optically valid form). Das Typofoto regelt das neue Tempo der neuen visuellen Literatur.
- **Rule**: Use photography as a structural text-substitute ("Fototext") in page design — not as decoration alongside text, but as an element that achieves optically what text achieves conceptually, creating a synthetic visual-verbal continuity.
- **Source**: pp.37–38
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die lineare, gedankenmitteilende Typografie ist nur ein vermittelndes Notglied zwischen dem Inhalt der Mitteilung und dem aufnehmenden Menschen: MITTEILUNG — TYPOGRAFIE — MENSCH."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Linear, thought-communicating typography is only a mediating stopgap between the content of a message and the receiving human: MESSAGE — TYPOGRAPHY — HUMAN."
- **Category**: typophoto

**PRINCIPLE [P-21]**: Typofoto als neues Tempo / Typofoto Sets the New Tempo
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Today one tries to use typography no longer merely as an objective tool, but to involve the Wirkungsmöglichkeiten (effect possibilities) of its subjective existence in the work. The photographic materials themselves contain strong optical graspabilities and are able, through the content of the message, also visually — not only mediately intellectually — to express it. Photography as typographic material can appear alongside or as "Fototext" in place of words — in its Objektivität of presentation, which permits no individual interpretation. In future, every printing house will have its own Klischeanstalt and it will be a certainty that the future of typographic processes belongs to the fotomechanischen (photomechanical) methods.
- **Rule**: The Typofoto sets the new visual tempo — adopt it for all visual communication that must reach mass audiences at speed.
- **Source**: p.38
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Typofoto regelt das neue Tempo der neuen visuellen Literatur."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The Typofoto regulates the new tempo of the new visual literature."
- **Category**: typophoto
