## Section: Reflektorische Lichtspiele / Farbensonatine — Hirschfeld-Mack
*(Book pages 79–83, PDF pages 80–84)*

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Reflektorisches Lichtspiel (Reflective Light Play) as developed at the Bauhaus by Hirschfeld-Mack is a new synthetic art form: it composes in light, color, movement, and acoustic rhythm simultaneously — a true Lichtgestaltung that cannot be reduced to painting, theater, or music alone.

**Evidence**: A written "score" (Partitur) for the Farbensonatine (3 Takte) is presented with nine rows (Reihen) corresponding to lamps, colors, movements, musical intervals, and lamp-shade positions. Two photographic stills from the live performance (photographed by Eckner, Weimar) show frozen moments of the moving light composition. The score itself is visual — a grid notation fusing musical and spatial syntax.

**Conclusion**: The formal means of this light-play are: the moving colored point, the line, and the surface form — each adjustable in size, brightness, and boundary sharpness. The Blendvorrichtung (dimming device) allows continuous transition from black ground to maximum brightness. This is a new, genuinely konstruktive compositional medium, and it is being built from scratch at the Bauhaus.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Lichtgestaltung / Light Design as Compositional Medium
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The formal elements of the Farbensonatine are the moving colored point (bewegter farbiger Punkt), the line (Linie), and the surface form (Flächenform). Each can be projected larger or smaller, brighter or darker, with sharp or unsharp boundaries. Light is not decoration; it is plastic material shaped by the artist.
- **Rule**: Treat light as a primary formal element with the same degrees of freedom as form, color, and line — controllable in size, intensity, edge quality, and temporal position.
- **Source**: p.79
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die formalen Gestaltungsmittel sind: der bewegte farbige Punkt, die Linie und die Flächenform. Jedes dieser Elemente kann in beliebiger Geschwindigkeit und Richtung bewegt, vergrößert oder verkleinert, heller oder dunkler, mit scharfen und unscharfen Begrenzungen projiziert werden, kann die Farbe wechseln, sich mit anderen Farbformen durchdringen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The formal design means are: the moving colored point, the line, and the surface form. Each of these elements can be moved at any speed and direction, enlarged or reduced, brighter or darker, projected with sharp or unsharp boundaries, can change color, interpenetrate with other color-forms."
- **Category**: light

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Simultanität / Simultaneity of Light-Color Complexes
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: By dimming light sources and switching individual lamps, individual and larger color complexes can slowly emerge from the black ground, rise to maximum brightness, while other complexes simultaneously disappear — thus generating continuous variation. The light-play produces an endless variety of forms without repeating itself, always strictly organized as a Farbformthema (color-form theme).
- **Rule**: Structure visual compositions so that multiple elements evolve simultaneously at different rates — some brightening as others fade — creating a continuous, non-repeating field of variation governed by a single unifying theme.
- **Source**: p.79
- **Quote (DE)**: "Durch Ab- und Aufblendung der Lichtquellen und durch Einschaltungen von Widerständen ist es uns möglich, einzelne und größere Farbformkomplexe langsam aus dem schwarzen Grunde bis zur höchsten Helligkeit und farbiger Leuchtkraft zu steigern, während andere Komplexe gleichzeitig langsam verschwinden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "By dimming and brightening the light sources and by switching in resistances, we are able to raise individual and larger color-form complexes slowly from the black ground to maximum brightness and chromatic luminance, while other complexes simultaneously slowly disappear."
- **Category**: simultaneity

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Optischer Rhythmus / Optical Rhythm Scored like Music
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Farbensonatine has a Partitur (score) with nine rows (Reihen): lamp positions, colors, movements, Horizontallinien (horizontal lines) overlappping one another, Geschlossenheit (closure) and Überschneidungen (intersections), Steigerungen (intensifications), Höhepunkte (climaxes), and so on. The score makes visible the temporal structure of the light-play — just as musical notation makes audible structure visible. The optical rhythm is conducted, not improvised.
- **Rule**: Score light and movement compositions with explicit notation that maps time, intensity, color sequence, and spatial position — treating optical tempo as a compositional parameter no less rigorous than musical rhythm.
- **Source**: p.79–80
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die formalen Gestaltungsmittel werden entsprechend der musikalischen Bewegung geführt, so daß die zeitliche Gliederung durch den akustischen Rhythmus eindeutig geführt, die optischen Bewegungen, Einfügungen, Zusammenlegungen, Überschneidungen, Steigerung, Höhepunkte usw. unterstützen und gefördert werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The formal design means are guided in correspondence with the musical movement, so that the temporal articulation is unambiguously led by the acoustic rhythm, supporting and promoting the optical movements, insertions, superimpositions, intersections, intensifications, climaxes, etc."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Reflektorisches Lichtspiel / Reflective Light Play as New Synthetic Art Form
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In the reflective light-plays one sees — through the impressive physio-psychic effect of directly colored light beams combined with simultaneous rhythmic music — the possibility of developing a new Kunstgattung (art genre), and also the appropriate means for bridging the gap between the many ratios of abstract painters and the new attitude from which they arise. The note appended reads: "Diese Ausführungen gebe ich wörtlich wieder, ohne mich damit in allen Punkten zu identifizieren. L. M.-N." — Moholy-Nagy quotes Hirschfeld-Mack verbatim but marks his critical distance.
- **Rule**: When incorporating light as a compositional medium in a new art form, acknowledge its dual character: it acts both physiologically (on the retina) and psychologically (on the mind) — this dual action is the source of its power as a synthetic medium.
- **Source**: p.79
- **Quote (DE)**: "In den reflektorischen Farbenspielen sehen wir durch die eindrucksvolle physisch-psychische Wirkung der direkten farbigen Strahlen in Verbindung mit einer gleichlaufenden rhythmischen Musik die Möglichkeit zu einer neuen Kunstgattung zu entwickeln."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the reflective color plays we see, through the impressive physical-psychological effect of direct colored rays in combination with simultaneous rhythmic music, the possibility of developing a new art genre."
- **Category**: light

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Partitur / The Score as Design Document
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Farbensonatine score (p.81, Erläuterung zur Partitur: Farbensonatine, 3 Takte) uses nine rows: (1) Takteinteilung (bar division), (2) Lampen (lamps), (3) Farben (colors), (4) Die Stellung der Lampen (lamp positions), (5) Häupte und Lampenschalter (main switches), (6. und 7.) Die Widerstände (resistances), (8.) Verdunkelung (dimming), (9.) Das lineare Gesamtbild (overall linear image). The score functions simultaneously as notation, instruction, and visual composition — a Typophoto object in itself.
- **Rule**: A design notation system for time-based light and color work must track at minimum: timing, light source positions, color states, intensity gradients, and the emergent overall image — all in a single readable document.
- **Source**: p.80–81
- **Quote (DE)**: "Erläuterung zur Partitur: Farbensonatine (3 Takte)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Explanation of the score: Color Sonatina (3 bars)"
- **Category**: optical-rhythm

## Section: Neue Sehen — Photographic Modes Demonstrated
*(Book pages 84–98, PDF pages 85–99)*

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The following sequence of photographs demonstrates the eight perceptual modes that the camera enables and the uneducated eye cannot access. Each photograph represents a mode of Neue Sehen: extreme distance with pattern-dissolution, steep oblique angle, extreme close-up, bird's-eye view (Aufnahme von oben), negative-print tone reversal, simultaneous portrait, and spherical wide-angle reflection.

**Evidence**: A curated sequence of photographs — from multiple photographers including Moholy-Nagy himself, Lucia Moholy, Renger-Patzsch, and press services — each illustrating a distinct perceptual category. Captions are minimal but analytical: they state what the photographic means produces, not simply what the subject is.

**Conclusion**: The camera does not merely record what is there — it constructs a new kind of seeing. The objective test: would this image exist without the camera's specific technical capability? In every case presented here: no. These are all Produktion.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Extreme Distance + Pattern Recognition — Neue Sehen Mode 1
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The photograph of zebras and gnus in East Africa (p.84) demonstrates how extreme photographic distance dissolves individual animal identity into pure surface pattern. The striped hides of massed zebras become an abstract tonal field — the kind of visual information that normal human vision, limited by proximity and selectivity, cannot assemble. Distance, in the camera, is a formal tool.
- **Rule**: Use extreme distance not to document subjects but to reveal the surface patterns, textures, and rhythmic structures that only emerge when individual identity is suppressed by scale.
- **Source**: p.84
- **Quote (DE)**: [No explicit caption text beyond subject identification: "Zebras und Gnus in Ostafrika. Foto: Martin Johnson. Aus der Berliner Illustrierten Zeitung."]
- **Quote (EN)**: "Zebras and Gnus in East Africa."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Oblique Angle (Schräge Aufnahme) — Neue Sehen Mode 2
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The oblique photograph of the Teichwirtschaftliche Versuchsstation, Bayern (p.85, photo: Lohöfener) demonstrates that the camera used at a steep diagonal transforms the industrial grid of the facade — metal beams, ventilation panels, structural members — into an abstract geometric composition. The subject's utilitarian identity dissolves; what remains is the formal tension between diagonal thrust and grid structure.
- **Rule**: When a subject contains latent geometric order, approach it obliquely — the diagonal angle releases the abstract compositional potential that the frontal, "natural" view suppresses.
- **Source**: p.85
- **Category**: neue-sehen

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Extreme Close-Up (Herausnahme eines Teiles) — Neue Sehen Mode 3
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph of a marabu eye (p.86, "Auge eines Marabus," photo: Press-Foto-New-Service, from Frankfurter Illustrierten Zeitung) demonstrates "außerordentliche Konzentration" (extraordinary concentration) through the isolation of a fragment. The eye, surrounded by a vast landscape of wrinkled skin-texture, becomes a world unto itself. Normal vision would never isolate this fragment with such precision. The caption names this explicitly: "In der Herausnahme eines Teiles liegt eine außerordentliche Konzentration." / "In the extraction of a part lies an extraordinary concentration."
- **Rule**: Isolate a fragment of the subject — strip away context until the fragment fills the frame and reveals a visual world that the whole subject conceals.
- **Source**: p.86
- **Quote (DE)**: "In der Herausnahme eines Teiles liegt eine außerordentliche Konzentration."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the extraction of a part lies an extraordinary concentration."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Psychologisches Problem / The Psychological Moment in Film
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The film still from "Dr. Mabuse" (Ufa film) showing hands around a table (p.87, "Tischrücken") isolates the psychological problem of hands — "das Ziel vieler Maler seit Leonardo — in dem Bruchteil einer Sekunde gefaßt" / "the goal of many painters since Leonardo — captured in the fraction of a second." The camera captures in an instant what painting pursues over months. This is a decisive argument for the camera's unique temporal capability: the instantaneous fixing of a psychologically charged moment.
- **Rule**: The camera's fraction-of-a-second capture is not a shortcut — it is a formal capability unavailable to any static medium. Use it to fix psychologically charged states that cannot be constructed through deliberate composition.
- **Source**: p.87
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das psychologische Problem der Hände — das Ziel vieler Maler seit Leonardo — in dem Bruchteil einer Sekunde gefaßt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The psychological problem of hands — the goal of many painters since Leonardo — captured in the fraction of a second."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Extreme Close-Up / Makrofotografie — Faktur Revealed
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The two Renger-Patzsch photographs — "Blühender Kaktus" (p.88) and "Kaktus" (p.89) — demonstrate through extreme close-up how the photographic lens transforms biological structure into abstract formal composition. The flowering cactus (p.88) produces a dense web of tendrils and spines against a dark mass: pure linear complexity. The cactus detail (p.89) — spined leaf segments against total black — converts a botanical specimen into a study in formal tension: jagged triangular thorns, curved leaf edges, deep black voids. Neither image would exist for the eye at natural scale.
- **Rule**: Use macro close-up to reveal the Faktur (material surface quality, texture, structural pattern) hidden within natural and industrial objects — not to document the object but to construct a formal image from it.
- **Source**: p.88–89
- **Category**: neue-sehen

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Hell-Dunkel-Gliederung / Chiaroscuro as Spatial Construction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph "Puppen" (p.90, photo: Moholy-Nagy) places two dolls seen from above on a sunlit surface crossed by a diagonal shadow grid. Caption: "Durch die Hell-Dunkelgliederung, durch die Schattenliniatur wird das Spielzeug ins Fantastische gerückt." / "Through the chiaroscuro articulation, through the shadow-line pattern, the toy is shifted into the fantastic." Chiaroscuro here is not atmospheric rendering (as in Rembrandt) — it is a structural grid imposed by light and shadow that transforms the ordinary object's spatial identity.
- **Rule**: Deploy light and shadow not as atmospheric effect but as a geometric structuring agent — shadow lines and tonal fields that divide the picture plane and transform the spatial identity of the depicted object.
- **Source**: p.90
- **Quote (DE)**: "Durch die Hell-Dunkelgliederung, durch die Schattenliniatur wird das Spielzeug ins Fantastische gerückt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Through the chiaroscuro articulation, through the shadow-line pattern, the toy is shifted into the fantastic."
- **Category**: light

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Aufnahme von oben / Bird's-Eye View — Neue Sehen Mode 4
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph "Aufnahme von oben" (p.91, photo: Moholy-Nagy) shows a woman seated in a courtyard with stairs and a folding chair, shot from directly above. Caption: "Der Reiz der Aufnahme liegt nicht im Objekt, sondern in der Sicht von oben und in den abgewogenen Verhältnissen." / "The appeal of the photograph lies not in the object but in the view from above and in the balanced proportions." The bird's-eye view removes all vertical perspective recession and converts the scene into a flat pattern of interlocking shapes and proportions.
- **Rule**: The bird's-eye view is not an elevated documentary angle — it is a formal operation that eliminates recession, equalizes depth, and converts three-dimensional space into two-dimensional proportion and pattern.
- **Source**: p.91
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der Reiz der Aufnahme liegt nicht im Objekt, sondern in der Sicht von oben und in den abgewogenen Verhältnissen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The appeal of the photograph lies not in the object but in the view from above and in the balanced proportions."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Faktur / Surface Quality as Photographic Subject
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The film still from "Zalamort" (National Film, p.92) is described as "Porträtaufnahme mit Darstellung von Fakturenwirkungen. (Faktur = Art und Erscheinung des Herstellungsprozesses)." / "Portrait photograph with representation of Faktur effects. (Faktur = the nature and appearance of the production process.)" This is a critical definition: Faktur is not mere texture — it is the visible evidence of how something was made. A photograph that shows Faktur is showing process, not just surface.
- **Rule**: Photograph surfaces in a way that reveals the evidence of their making — the weave of fabric, the grain of material, the marks of process — because Faktur is the subject, not the object that bears it.
- **Source**: p.92
- **Quote (DE)**: "Porträtaufnahme mit Darstellung von Fakturenwirkungen. (Faktur = Art und Erscheinung des Herstellungsprozesses)."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Portrait photograph with representation of Faktur effects. (Faktur = the nature and appearance of the production process.)"
- **Category**: neue-sehen

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Objektives Porträt / The Objective Portrait
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The portrait by Lucia Moholy (p.94) is captioned: "Ein Versuch des objektiven Porträts: den Menschen so sachlich aufzunehmen, wie einen Gegenstand, um das fotografische Ergebnis nicht mit subjektivem Wollen zu belasten." / "An attempt at the objective portrait: to photograph the person as objectively as an object, in order not to burden the photographic result with subjective intention." The objective portrait is a disciplined refusal of photographic interpretation — the camera's capacity to record without the painter's expressive will.
- **Rule**: Approach the portrait subject with the same dispassionate attention you would give an object — remove all theatrical lighting, forced pose, and compositional flattery — so that the photographic result is determined by the subject, not by the photographer's expressive intention.
- **Source**: p.94
- **Quote (DE)**: "Ein Versuch des objektiven Porträts: den Menschen so sachlich aufzunehmen, wie einen Gegenstand, um das fotografische Ergebnis nicht mit subjektivem Wollen zu belasten."
- **Quote (EN)**: "An attempt at the objective portrait: to photograph the person as objectively as an object, in order not to burden the photographic result with subjective intention."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Amerikanische Beleuchtungskultur / Lighting Culture in Portrait Photography
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Gloria Swanson portrait (p.95, photo: Paramount) exemplifies what the caption calls "Amerikanische Kultur der Porträtaufnahme: die raffinierte Wirkung der Beleuchtung, Materialien, Fakturen, Rundungen und Kurven." / "American culture of portrait photography: the refined effect of lighting, materials, Fakturen, roundings and curves." This is the opposite of the objective portrait — it is a deliberate construction of glamour through light. Moholy-Nagy presents it descriptively, not approvingly: it is Reproduktion elevated to high craft.
- **Rule**: Recognize the difference between photographic construction through lighting — which produces a crafted image that expresses intent — and objective photography, which suppresses intent to let the subject speak. Both are legitimate modes; the artist must know which they are practicing.
- **Source**: p.95
- **Quote (DE)**: "Amerikanische Kultur der Porträtaufnahme: die raffinierte Wirkung der Beleuchtung, Materialien, Fakturen, Rundungen und Kurven."
- **Quote (EN)**: "American culture of portrait photography: the refined effect of lighting, materials, Fakturen, roundings and curves."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Negativ / Tone Reversal as Formal Operation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph "Negativ" (p.96, photo: Moholy-Nagy) shows a standing figure, but the tonal values are reversed — the bright luminous white head floats against a dark grey ground, with the suit rendered in deep black. Caption: "Die Umkehrung des Tonwerts kehrt auch die Verhältnisse um. Die geringe Menge von Weiß tritt überaus aktiv in Erscheinung und bestimmt dadurch das ganze Bild." / "The reversal of tonal value also reverses the relationships. The small quantity of white appears exceedingly active and thereby determines the entire picture."
- **Rule**: Tonal reversal (negative printing) is not a novelty effect — it is a formal operation that restructures the hierarchy of visual elements. A small area of reversed tone becomes dominant. Use it to test which formal elements carry the composition, independent of their natural luminosity.
- **Source**: p.96
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Umkehrung des Tonwerts kehrt auch die Verhältnisse um. Die geringe Menge von Weiß tritt überaus aktiv in Erscheinung und bestimmt dadurch das ganze Bild."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The reversal of tonal value also reverses the relationships. The small quantity of white appears exceedingly active and thereby determines the entire picture."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Simultanes Porträt / Multiple-Exposure Portrait — Neue Sehen Mode 5
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The amateur photograph of Hannah Höch (p.97, captioned "Anfangsform des simultanen Porträts" / "Initial form of the simultaneous portrait") shows a double or multiple-exposure portrait: two superimposed images of the same person at slightly different positions, creating a ghosted, simultaneous presence. Moholy-Nagy marks this as a beginning (Anfangsform) — not yet fully realized, but pointing toward the possibility of representing a person across multiple moments or viewpoints in a single image.
- **Rule**: The simultaneous portrait is not a technical accident — it is a formal program: use multiple exposures to construct a representation of a subject that exists in time, not just at an instant. This is the photographic equivalent of Cubist simultaneity, but achievable through purely photographic means.
- **Source**: p.97
- **Quote (DE)**: "Anfangsform des simultanen Porträts."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Initial form of the simultaneous portrait."
- **Category**: simultaneity

---

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Sphärische Reflexion / Spherical Mirror — Neue Sehen Mode 6
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph "Atelier in der Gartenkugel" (p.98, photo: Muche, Bauhaus) shows a studio space reflected in a polished garden ball (Gartenkugel). The spherical mirror compresses the entire surrounding space — walls, windows, the photographer's hand, the room — into a single circular image with extreme wide-angle distortion. This is a view the unaided eye cannot achieve: simultaneous all-around vision compressed into one frame.
- **Rule**: Spherical and convex reflective surfaces are optical instruments that produce a mode of vision radically different from normal perspective — compress entire spatial environments into a single field and use the resulting wide-angle compression as a compositional device.
- **Source**: p.98
- **Category**: neue-sehen

- **Shows**: Film still showing a circular or oval dark table surface surrounded by approximately twelve pairs of hands reaching in from the frame edges, fingertips touching the table. - **Text reference**: "Das psychologische Problem der Hände — das Ziel vieler Maler seit Leonardo — in dem Bruchteil einer Sekunde gefaßt."

**FIGURE [Fig-15]**: Gloria Swanson — American Portrait Culture

**FIGURE [Fig-16]**: Negativ (Tone-Reversed Portrait)
- **Shows**: Full-length figure of a man standing, photographed as a negative print — tonal values reversed. The suit (normally dark) is rendered in deep near-black; but the head is rendered in brilliant, glowing white — a luminous orb against the grey ground. The ground itself is a mid-grey, and the figure is darker than the ground in the body but brighter at the head. A small amount of white completely dominates the image. Photo: Moholy-Nagy.
- **Type**: Produktion — the negative print is a specifically photographic operation (darkroom inversion) with no equivalent in other media. The formal result — the dominance of a small white area — is the analytical finding.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-16 (Negativ / tone reversal). Caption states the analytical principle directly: "Die Umkehrung des Tonwerts kehrt auch die Verhältnisse um."
- **Text reference**: "Die geringe Menge von Weiß tritt überaus aktiv in Erscheinung und bestimmt dadurch das ganze Bild."
- **Source**: p.96

**FIGURE [Fig-17]**: Hannah Höch — Simultanes Porträt (Amateur photograph)
- **Shows**: A medium close-up photograph of Hannah Höch, seated or reclining in an outdoor setting (grass, trees visible). The photograph is a double or multiple exposure: the subject appears twice (or slightly more) at different positions, creating a ghosted, superimposed image. The second image is slightly offset and translucent, giving the impression of simultaneous presence at two moments or positions. Captioned as "Anfangsform des simultanen Porträts" / "Initial form of the simultaneous portrait." Source: Amateuraufnahme (amateur photograph).
- **Type**: Produktion — the multiple exposure is a specifically photographic operation. The result (simultaneous presence at two moments) is unachievable in single-moment media.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-17 (simultanes Porträt as a new photographic mode). Moholy-Nagy designates it as a beginning form — not yet fully developed but showing the direction.
- **Text reference**: "Anfangsform des simultanen Porträts."
- **Source**: p.97

**FIGURE [Fig-18]**: Atelier in der Gartenkugel (Studio in the Garden Ball)
- **Shows**: Full-page photograph of a studio interior reflected in a highly polished spherical garden ornament (Gartenkugel / mirror ball). The entire surrounding space is compressed into the circular reflection: windows, room geometry, walls, and the photographer's own hand holding the camera are all simultaneously visible within the sphere. The image has extreme wide-angle distortion — straight lines curve, the room wraps around the sphere. Photo: Muche. Bauhaus.
- **Type**: Produktion — the spherical mirror is an optical instrument producing a mode of vision (all-around simultaneous compression) unavailable to the unaided eye.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-18 (sphärische Reflexion as Neue Sehen mode). Also demonstrates P-02 (Simultanität) — the entire room is seen from all angles simultaneously in a single frame.
- **Text reference**: No extended caption. Placement in the Neue Sehen sequence is the argument.
- **Source**: p.98
