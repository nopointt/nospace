## Die Grundfläche (The Basic Plane) — Continued: Linienpaare, Oben/Unten, Links/Rechts, Formate, Kompositionslehre

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Grundfläche (GF) is not a neutral substrate but an active organism with differentiated internal forces. Each of its four borders, four parts, two diagonals, and center generates its own Spannung (tension), which enters into compositional relationship with any element placed upon it. The GF must be understood analytically before any form is placed on it.

**Evidence**: Kandinsky provides schematic weight ratios (2/4 vs 4/6) for "Dramatisierung" vs "Ausgleich"; empirical correspondences between top/bottom/left/right and temperature, freedom, and "literary" associations (Himmel/Erde, Ferne/Haus); diagrams of the four boundary tensions of the square (Fig. 77–79), the diagonal axis (Fig. 80), center tensions (Fig. 81), weight distribution (Fig. 82), harmonic and disharmonic diagonals (Fig. 83–84), and opposed vertical/horizontal form-pairs (Fig. 85–88).

**Conclusion**: Composition is the science of combining elements with the GF's own forces. Ignorance of the GF's inner character leads either to accidental harmony or to unresolvable contradiction. Only through exact knowledge of the GF can the artist exercise Innere Notwendigkeit.

## Begriff (Definition of the Grundfläche)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Grundfläche — materielle Fläche / basic plane — material surface
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Under "Grundfläche" is understood the material surface that is called upon to receive the content of the work. It is designated here as GF. The schematic GF is bounded by two horizontal and two vertical lines and thereby set off from its environment as a self-sufficient being ("selbständiges Wesen").
- **Rule**: Treat the basic plane as a living, self-sufficient organism — never as a passive container.
- **Source**: p.109
- **Quote (DE)**: "Unter der Grundfläche wird die materielle Fläche verstanden, die berufen ist, den Inhalt des Werkes aufzunehmen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Under the basic plane is understood the material surface that is called upon to receive the content of the work."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Linienpaare — Grundklang der GF / line-pairs — fundamental resonance of the GF
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: After the characteristics of the horizontal and the vertical are established, the fundamental resonance (Grundklang) of the GF can be clarified: two elements of cold repose and two elements of warm repose are two double-lengths of repose that determine the quiet — objective — Klang of the GF.
- **Rule**: Every rectangular GF carries four boundary lines of two types: cold (horizontal) and warm (vertical); together they constitute the GF's objective Klang before any element is placed.
- **Source**: p.109
- **Quote (DE)**: "zwei Elemente der kalten Ruhe und zwei Elemente der warmen Ruhe sind zwei Doppellänge der Ruhe, die den ruhigen — objektiven Klang der GF bestimmen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "two elements of cold repose and two elements of warm repose are two double-lengths of repose that determine the quiet — objective — resonance of the GF."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Das Überwiegen — temperature domination / predominance
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The predominance of one or the other pair — i.e., the predominating height or width of the GF — determines either the atmosphere of cold or the warmth of the objective Klang. Individual elements placed on the GF are thus brought into a colder or warmer atmosphere in advance, and this state can later be counteracted by the largest number of opposing elements — a fact that must never be forgotten. For example, a tall format (Hochformat / portrait) placed on a colder GF will "dramatize" the tensions by driving them upward against resistance. This creates ever more compositional possibilities.
- **Rule**: Choose the GF's proportions deliberately: a dominant horizontal atmosphere suppresses vertical elements; a dominant vertical atmosphere enhances their Spannung.
- **Source**: p.109
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Überwiegen des einen oder des anderen Paares, d.h. die überwiegende Breite oder die überwiegende Höhe der GF bestimmt entweder das Überwiegen der Kälte oder der Wärme des objektiven Klanges."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The predominance of one or the other pair, i.e. the predominating width or height of the GF, determines either the predominance of cold or the warmth of the objective resonance."
- **Category**: plane

## Natur der GF (Nature of the Basic Plane)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Natur der GF als unabhängige Tatsache / Nature of the GF as independent fact
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The GF hangs not only from the nature of the individual elements but from the nature of the GF itself — and from the forces of the artist, as an independent fact that must be apprehended. This fact is also a source of great compositional possibilities — a means to an end.
- **Rule**: Study the GF's nature as an independent force, not merely as background for placed elements.
- **Source**: p.110
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Klänge der schematischen GF / Resonances of the schematic GF
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every schematic GF, produced by 2 horizontal and 2 vertical lines, thereby generates 4 sides. Each of these 4 sides develops its own Klang, which goes beyond the borders of cold and warm repose. Added to each is the Klang of the warm or cold repose — a second Klang that is inseparably and organically bound to the position of the line = border.
- **Rule**: Every side of a rectangular GF carries two simultaneous Klänge: one from its orientation (horizontal/vertical) and one from its position (top/bottom/left/right). Both are active compositional forces.
- **Source**: p.110
- **Quote (DE)**: "Jede schematische GF, die durch 2 horizontale und 2 vertikale Linien erzeugt wird, hat dementsprechend 4 Seiten. Und jede dieser 4 Seiten entwickelt einen nur ihr eigenen Klang."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Every schematic GF, generated by 2 horizontal and 2 vertical lines, has correspondingly 4 sides. And each of these 4 sides develops its own exclusive resonance."
- **Category**: plane

## Oben und Unten (Top and Bottom)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Oben = Lockerheit, Leichtigkeit, Freiheit / Top = looseness, lightness, freedom
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Oben" evokes the idea of greater looseness ("Lockersein"), a feeling of lightness, a release, and finally a freely colored resonance. From these three interrelated properties each gives always something differently colored Mitklang. "Lockersein" negates density. The nearer to the upper boundary of the GF, the more scattered the individual smallest surfaces appear, and they lose weight; the Note of heaviness is reduced. Any heavier form gains more weight ("Note") at the upper part of the GF.
- **Rule**: Place lighter, more open forms toward the top of the GF; heavier forms gain disproportionate weight at the top and should be used there sparingly or for dramatic emphasis.
- **Source**: p.111, p.112
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das 'Oben' erweckt die Vorstellung eines größeren Lockerseins, ein Gefühl der Leichtigkeit, einer Befreiung, und schließlich der Freiheit."
- **Quote (EN)**: "'Top' awakens the idea of greater looseness, a feeling of lightness, a release, and finally freedom."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Freiheit — Hemmung ist auf das Minimum reduziert / Freedom — resistance reduced to minimum
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Freiheit" generates the impression of lighter "Bewegung" (movement), and the Spannung can here play more easily. "Steigen" (ascending) or "Fallen" (falling) gains an intensity. Die Hemmung ist auf das Minimum reduziert.
- **Rule**: The top zone of the GF is where upward-moving tensions are least resisted; ascending compositional forces belong naturally here.
- **Source**: p.112
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Hemmung ist auf das Minimum reduziert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The resistance is reduced to the minimum."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Unten = Verdichtung, Schwere, Gebundenheit / Bottom = density, weight, constraint
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Unten" works in the exact opposite direction: densification, weight, constraint. The nearer to the lower boundary of the GF, the denser the atmosphere; the individual smallest surfaces lie ever closer together, and the larger and heavier forms are carried with ever increasing lightness. These forms lose weight, and the Note of heaviness diminishes. The feeling of "Bewegung" is increasingly constrained. Die Hemmung erreicht ihr Maximum.
- **Rule**: Place forms requiring grounding and stability at the bottom; ascending tensions are most suppressed there and require maximum force to overcome.
- **Source**: p.111, p.112
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das 'Unten' wirkt vollkommen entgegengesetzt: Verdichtung, Schwere, Gebundenheit."
- **Quote (EN)**: "'Bottom' works in the completely opposite direction: densification, weight, constraint."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Dramatisierung und Ausgleich / Dramatization and Equilibrium
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The properties of top and bottom, together forming the highest contrast pair, can be intensified toward "Dramatisierung" or brought to "Ausgleich." The schematic formula:

  **Fall 1 — "Dramatisierung":**
  - oben: GF-Gewicht 2 / Formen-Gewicht 2 → 4/4 = 4:8
  - unten: GF-Gewicht 4 / Formen-Gewicht 4

  **Fall 2 — "Ausgleich":**
  - oben: GF-Gewicht 2 / Formen-Gewicht 4 → 6:6
  - unten: GF-Gewicht 4 / Formen-Gewicht 2

- **Rule**: For maximum dramatic contrast, reinforce the GF's natural weight with matching form weight (light forms at top, heavy at bottom). For equilibrium, compensate against the GF's natural weight (heavy forms at top, light at bottom).
- **Source**: p.112
- **Quote (DE)**: "1. Fall — 'Dramatisierung': oben GF-Gewicht 2, Formen-Gewicht 2 / unten GF-Gewicht 4, Formen-Gewicht 4 = 4:8"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Case 1 — 'Dramatization': top GF-weight 2, form-weight 2 / bottom GF-weight 4, form-weight 4 = 4:8"
- **Category**: plane

## Rechts und Links (Right and Left)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Links = Lockerheit, Leichtigkeit, Freiheit, Ferne / Left = looseness, lightness, freedom, distance
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The left side of the GF internally resembles "Oben" (top): it is the continuation of "Unten" (bottom) with the same abatement. Density, weight, and constraint decrease; looseness, lightness, and freedom grow as the eye moves from center toward "Links." The degree of these three properties can vary in "Links," and grows in the direction toward "Oben" (top) and diminishes toward "Unten" (bottom). "Links" carries a "literary" quality — the movement toward "Links" is a movement into the "Ferne" (distance). The forms that have their tensions directed to the left tend toward "Links," and their movement gains ever more intensity and an "adventurous" quality. "Bewegung" toward left = movement toward the distance.
- **Rule**: Forms aimed leftward carry a sense of departure, adventure, and spatial expansion; use left orientation for forms that must read as moving away, opening up, or becoming free.
- **Source**: p.113, p.114, p.115
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das nach 'Links' — Insfreiengehen — ist eine Bewegung in die Ferne."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Moving toward 'Left' — going into freedom — is a movement into the distance."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Rechts = Verdichtung, Schwere, Gebundenheit, Haus / Right = density, weight, constraint, home
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: "Rechts" inwardly resembles "Oben" — specifically a continuation of "Unten" with the same abatement, just as "Links" = continuation of "Oben" with the same abatement. Density, weight, and constraint increase; the tensions of forms directed rightward grow ever smaller and the freedom of movement is increasingly bounded. Movement toward "Rechts" = movement toward "Haus" (home). The nearer to "Rechts," the greater the hardness and resistance — harder than "Links."
- **Rule**: Forms aimed rightward carry a sense of arrival, settlement, and constraint; use right orientation for grounding, resolution, and formal stability.
- **Source**: p.114, p.115
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das nach 'Rechts' — Innsgebundengehen — ist eine Bewegung nach Hause."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Moving toward 'Right' — going into constraint — is a movement toward home."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Vierfache Charakteristik der GF — Reihenfolge / Four-part character of the GF — sequence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Thus the four borders of the GF are to be presented in the following sequence of Spannung and "literary" association:

  | Reihenfolge | Spannung | "Literarisch" |
  |---|---|---|
  | 1. oben | zur | Himmel (heaven) |
  | 2. links | zur | Ferne (distance) |
  | 3. rechts | zur | Haus (home) |
  | 4. unten | zur | Erde (earth) |

  One should not imagine that these relationships can be composed in a formulaic way. The purpose is to bring to consciousness the inner Spannungen of the GF, as much as is known, and to introduce these Spannungen as an important component of the future Kompositionslehre.
- **Rule**: When composing, consciously account for the directionality of all four GF borders: top → heaven (light, free), left → distance (adventurous), right → home (stable), bottom → earth (heavy, constrained).
- **Source**: p.116
- **Quote (DE)**: "Reihenfolge: 1. oben – Spannung zur – Himmel / 2. links – zur – Ferne / 3. rechts – zur – Haus / 4. unten – zur – Erde"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Sequence: 1. top – tension toward – heaven / 2. left – toward – distance / 3. right – toward – home / 4. bottom – toward – earth"
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Literarisch — tiefe Verwandtschaft der Künste / Literary — deep kinship of the arts
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: There is a specific feeling connected to the described properties — "literary" in character — which discloses the deep kinship between various arts: the far-reaching general "root" of all arts and all spiritual domains. This feeling is the result of the two possible directions of human movement. This should only be named as a pointer for the Kompositionslehre: in the last decades the wave of concentration and the wave of eccentricity rose and fell again. This is linked to various causes, partly temporal phenomena, partly deeply rooted necessities.
- **Rule**: The associations of the GF's directions (Himmel/Erde/Ferne/Haus) are not literary impositions — they are empirically established correspondences between spatial forces and human experience; use them as data.
- **Source**: p.115, p.126
- **Category**: plane

## Das Quadrat — Formate (Square — Formats)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Das Quadrat — objektivste Form der GF / The Square — most objective form of the GF
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The objective form of the schematic GF is the square — both pairs of borders possess the equal force of Klang. Kälte and Wärme are relatively balanced. An arrangement of the most objective GF with a single element that carries the highest objectivity, which thus is best able to bear the highest objectivity of the GF, yields coldness — it can even be a symbol of death, resembling nothing so much as death. Not without purpose are such examples around us today.
- **Rule**: The square GF carries the maximum equilibrium between cold and warm — use it when absolute formal objectivity or neutrality is required; but recognize that absolute objectivity approaches death.
- **Source**: p.109, p.118
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die objektivste Form der schematischen GF ist das Quadrat — die beiden Paare der Grenzlinien besitzen die gleiche Kraft des Klanges. Kälte und Wärme sind relativ ausgeglichen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The most objective form of the schematic GF is the square — both pairs of boundary lines possess equal force of resonance. Cold and warmth are relatively balanced."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Quadrat ist farblos / The square is colorless (relative)
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The isolated Horizontal or Vertical possesses a so-to-speak colored repose (warm or cold). The square offers only the point ("Nur der Punkt") of repose, as long as it remains isolated. The isolated Horizontal or Vertical has a so-to-speak colored repose. So can the square also be designated as a colorless form.
- **Rule**: A square GF is the most neutral ground for compositional experiment — it neither reinforces warm nor cold; place it where the content alone must carry the temperature.
- **Source**: p.118
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die isolierte Horizontale oder Vertikale verfügt über eine sozusagen gefärbte 'Ruhe'... So darf auch das Quadrat als eine farblose Form bezeichnet werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The isolated horizontal or vertical has a so-to-speak colored 'repose'... So can the square also be designated as a colorless form."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Relatives — Relativität der Objektivität / Relativity of Objectivity
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The analysis of the square has clearly shown that objectivity in this case is only relative. With other means: complete "Ruhe" offers only the point, as long as it remains isolated. The circle, as the result of two equally-acting forces, inclines toward the most unfigurative "Ruhe," because the central Punkt of the circle is the most complete rest, not merely isolated points.
- **Rule**: Absolute objectivity cannot be reached in composition; every form — even the square — retains a relative temperature and tension. Design for the relative balance your content requires, not for impossible absolute neutrality.
- **Source**: p.118
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Formate — Diagonale Achse / Formats — Diagonal Axis
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: When a diagonal is drawn through the square GF, this diagonal stands at an angle of 45° to the horizontal. At the transition of the square GF into other rectangular planes, this angle becomes either larger or smaller. The diagonal increasingly inclines either toward the vertical or toward the horizontal. Therefore the diagonal can be grasped as a certain Spannungsmesser (tension-measurer) — a gauge of the internal tension between horizontal and vertical dominance. So are formed the so-called Hoch- und Langformate (portrait and landscape formats), which in "conventional" painting have a purely naturalistic meaning — and through this purely formal analysis gain an abstract compositional meaning.
- **Rule**: Treat the diagonal angle as a readout of the GF's temperature tension: near 45° = balanced; steeper = vertical (warm) dominance; shallower = horizontal (cold) dominance. Choose format to control this pre-compositional tension.
- **Source**: p.119
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Diagonale bekommt eine zunehmende Neigung entweder zur Vertikalen oder zur Horizontalen. Deshalb kann sie als ein gewisser Spannungsmesser aufgefaßt werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The diagonal acquires an increasing inclination either toward the vertical or toward the horizontal. Therefore it can be grasped as a certain tension-measurer."
- **Category**: plane

## Weitere Spannungen — Zentrum und Grenzen (Further Tensions — Centre and Borders)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Widerstandskräfte der Grenzen — vier primäre Teile / Resistance forces of the borders — four primary parts
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The intersection point of the two diagonals determines the center of the GF. The horizontal and vertical conducted through this center divide the GF into vier primäre Teile, each of which has a specific character. They all touch in the "equal-valid" center, from which Spannungen radiate in diagonal directions. The numbers 1, 2, 3, 4 designate the Widerstandskräfte (resistance forces) of the borders. a, b, c, d = the border designations of the four primary parts.
- **Rule**: Map every composition against the four-part division of the GF; each quadrant has a distinct tension character radiating from the center, and element placement in a given quadrant activates that quadrant's specific forces.
- **Source**: p.120, p.121
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Gegensätze — höchste Konsequenz der Zusammenstellung / Contrasts — highest consequence of arrangement
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: From the four primary parts certain necessary consequences result:
  - Parts a and d lie in the highest opposition to one another (höchsten Gegensatz).
  - Part b has the greatest resistance upward (größten Widerstand nach oben).
  - Part c inclines toward the most moderate (gemäßigten) resistance and develops a mild Gegensatz with part b.
  - In combination with the resistance forces of the surface-borders, a Gewichtsschema (weight schema) results (see Fig. 82 — Gewichtsverteilung).
- **Rule**: Quadrant a and quadrant d are the maximum contrast pair in the GF; placing opposed elements in these quadrants creates the most extreme dramatic tension. Quadrants b and c form the moderate contrast pair.
- **Source**: p.121
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Teile a und d liegen im höchsten Gegensatz zueinander. Teil b — Spannung zu 1–3 = lockerter Widerstand nach oben, Teil c — Spannung zu 2–4 = gemäßigter Widerstand und lässt sich ihre Verwandtschaft erkennen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Parts a and d lie in the highest opposition to one another. Part b — tension to 1–3 = loosened resistance upward. Part c — tension to 2–4 = moderate resistance, and their kinship is recognizable."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-20]**: Innere Parallele — Richtung aus dem Zentrum / Inner Parallels — direction from center
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Here the kinship of both opposition pairs (a-d above; b-c below) becomes apparent. Both are examples in which the forms lie in a parallel relationship to the GF; that is to say, the forms lie in the direction of the harmonic diagonal OR in the direction of the disharmonic diagonal. These four pairs provide also many possibilities for compositional constructions.
- **Rule**: When aligning forms with the GF's diagonal axis, distinguish between harmonic diagonal (from top-left to bottom-right) and disharmonic diagonal (from bottom-left to top-right); these create opposite compositional dynamics.
- **Source**: p.125
- **Category**: plane

## Gewicht und Diagonale (Weight and Diagonal)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-21]**: Harmonische und Disharmonische Diagonale / Harmonic and Disharmonic Diagonal
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The diagonal that runs from bottom-left to top-right is the "disharmonic" diagonal (disharmonische Diagonale) — Fig. 84. It concentrates its tension especially on point d (bottom-right), whereby the diagonal seems to acquire a tendency to deviate from point a and from the center; then from point a the diagonal seems to displace itself upward. The tension cb is of more complicated nature — toward the purely diagonal direction it acquires an inclination toward the horizontal or vertical, a deflection toward "Oben." The diagonal cb: "lyrische" Spannung. The diagonal da: "dramatische" Spannung.
- **Rule**: The cb-diagonal (harmonic, top-left to bottom-right) carries lyrical Spannung — calm, inward; the da-diagonal (disharmonic, bottom-left to top-right) carries dramatic Spannung — active, outward. Choose the diagonal axis of your composition accordingly.
- **Source**: p.123
- **Quote (DE)**: "cb — 'lyrische' Spannung, da — 'dramatische' Spannung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "cb — 'lyrical' tension, da — 'dramatic' tension."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-22]**: Gewicht der Diagonale / Weight of the Diagonal
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The diagonal lies lighter on the lower (bottom-right), as the triangle abc — which exerts a certain pressure and weight on the lower part — lays harder and heavier on the lower (point d). This pressure concentrates especially on point d, whereby the diagonal seems to acquire the tendency to deviate from point a and to displace itself from the center. These designations are self-evidently only arrows to point toward the inner content — they are bridges from the outer to the inner.
- **Rule**: When placing diagonal elements, account for their differential weight: the "dramatic" diagonal (da) concentrates weight at the bottom; the "lyrical" diagonal (cb) distributes weight more evenly.
- **Source**: p.122–123
- **Category**: plane

## Inhalt, Methode, Anwendung (Content, Method, Application)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-23]**: Inhalt — Spannungen zeigen innere Richtungen / Content — tensions point toward inner directions
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: These designations (lyrisch/dramatisch) are self-evidently only arrows to point toward inner directions — indicators of the directions which point toward inner content. They are bridges from the outer to the inner. In any case it must be repeated: every spot of the GF is individual and possesses its alone-belonging voice and inner coloring.
- **Rule**: Every point on the GF has an individual "Stimme" (voice) and inner Färbung (coloring); treat position as a compositional variable with the same precision as form and color.
- **Source**: p.123
- **Quote (DE)**: "Jedenfalls darf wiederholt werden: jede Stelle der GF ist individuell mit der ihr allein gehörenden Stimme und inneren Färbung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In any case it must be repeated: every spot of the GF is individual and possesses its alone-belonging voice and inner coloring."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-24]**: Methode — wissenschaftliche Theorie als Grundlage / Method — scientific theory as foundation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The analysis of the GF here employed is an example of the principled scientific method that is to contribute to the construction of the young Kunstwissenschaft (art science). The simple examples point toward the path of practical application.
- **Rule**: Every formal analysis must proceed from the principle of scientific method — thesis, evidence, conclusion — not from taste or convention.
- **Source**: p.123
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die hier verwendete Analyse der GF ist ein Beispiel der prinzipiellen wissenschaftlichen Methode, die zum Aufbau der jungen Kunstwissenschaft beitragen soll."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The analysis of the GF employed here is an example of the principled scientific method that is to contribute to the construction of the young art science."
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-25]**: Anwendung — die Linie an der Grenze zwischen Linie und Fläche / Application — the line at the border between line and plane
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A simple form that represents the transition from line to plane and thereby unites the properties of the line and the plane is brought into the most "objective" GF. What consequences arise? This transition-form is to be brought into the discussed directional tensions (harmonic/disharmonic diagonal etc.) as the most "objective" GF.
- **Rule**: At the transition between line and plane, the element simultaneously carries line-tensions and plane-tensions; analyze both systems to determine the compositional result.
- **Source**: p.123
- **Category**: line

## Gegensätze der Formen auf der GF (Contrasts of Forms on the GF)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-26]**: Höchster und gemäßigter Gegensatz — Paare A und B / Highest and moderate contrast — Pairs A and B
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: From the four pairs of the Gegensatz (contrast), two arise:
  - **Das erste Paar** (A) = example of the highest Gegensatz: the form directed toward the looser resistance (top) and the form directed toward the hardest resistance (bottom).
  - **Das zweite Paar** (B) = example of a mild Gegensatz: both forms lie toward the milder resistance, and both are directed toward each other — the forms lie most parallel to one another and must distinguish themselves by their Maß (measure/proportion).
- **Rule**: For maximum compositional tension, use Pair A contrast (top-directed vs. bottom-directed); for calm parallel composition, use Pair B contrast (both forms directed toward each other from mid-zones).
- **Source**: p.124–125
- **Category**: composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-27]**: Äußere und Innere Parallele / Outer and Inner Parallels
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In both cases the forms exist in a parallel relationship to the GF — either an outer parallelism (äußere Parallelität), where the outer borders of the GF are taken as the base; or an inner parallelism (innere Parallele), where the forms lie in a direction parallel to the harmonic or disharmonic diagonal.
- **Rule**: Distinguish outer parallelism (alignment with GF edges) from inner parallelism (alignment with GF diagonals); they activate different tension systems and must not be confused.
- **Source**: p.125
- **Category**: composition

## Kompositionelle Konstruktionen (Compositional Constructions)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-28]**: Konstruktionsmöglichkeiten — unbegrenzt / Constructional possibilities — unlimited
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: These four pairs provide compositional constructions — foundations on which further main directions of forms can be built, either remaining central or with their center in different directions. The number of constructional possibilities is unlimited. The inner atmosphere of the time, the nation, and even the inner content of personality determine the fundamental Klang of the GF. These can only be named here as pointers for the Kompositionslehre.
- **Rule**: Use the four-pair contrast system as a generative schema for construction — not as a constraint, but as the foundation from which unlimited compositional variants can be derived.
- **Source**: p.125
- **Category**: composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-29]**: Aufbau — Harmonieaufbau vs. Aufbau nach dem Prinzip des Gegensatzes / Construction — harmonic construction vs. construction by the principle of contrast
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: All individual tensions of the GF, once they land on the GF, take on other directions and are colored differently each time. Through the form-complexes they are pressed either toward "Oben" or drawn lengthwise. With unfortunate choice of format a common, shared ordering can become a repelling disorder. "Ordnung" here does not mean mathematical "Harmonieaufbau" alone, but also "Aufbau nach dem Prinzip des Gegensatzes" — e.g., the Langformat can "dramatize" elements by bringing them into the zone of resistance. This is mentioned only as a waymarker for the Kompositionslehre.
- **Rule**: Construction by harmonic accumulation and construction by the principle of contrast are both valid — choose consciously; do not mistake accidental harmony for formal necessity.
- **Source**: p.120
- **Quote (DE)**: "nicht allein den mathematischen 'Harmonieaufbau', in dem die sämtlichen Elemente in klarabgemessenen Richtungen liegen, sondern auch den Aufbau nach dem Prinzip des Gegensatzes."
- **Quote (EN)**: "not only the mathematical 'harmonic construction' in which all elements lie in clearly measured directions, but also construction according to the principle of contrast."
- **Category**: composition

## Kunstgeschichte und Komposition (Art History and Composition)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-30]**: Kunst und Zeit — drei Grundlagen / Art and Time — three foundations
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The connection between art history and "cultural history" (to which "Unkultur" belongs) is based on three foundations:
  1. The art underlies the time — either a) the time is strong and concentrically-minded, and the art follows it unreservedly; or b) the time is strong, but internally dissolved, and the weak art follows the dissolution.
  2. The art opposes the time and brings the time's counter-possibility to expression.
  3. The art crosses the borders of the time, and provides the content of the future.
- **Rule**: Recognize the art of any era by its relationship to contemporary concentration or eccentricity; only art at the level of principle 3 (crossing the time's limits) transcends its historical moment.
- **Source**: p.126
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-31]**: Relativer Klang der GF — Doppel- und Mehrklänge / Relative Resonance of the GF — double and multiple resonances
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: This pulsing of the GF transforms itself into double and multiple Klänge when the simplest element is placed on the GF. A freely curved line, which on one side consists of two waves and on the other side of three waves, has through its uppermost thick tip a stubborn "Gesicht" (face) and ends with an increasingly energetic wave. This line gathers itself from below, gains an ever increasing energy of becoming-waves, until the "Hartnäckigkeit" (stubbornness) reaches its maximum. What happens when this line is directed first toward the left, then toward the right?
- **Rule**: A single element placed on the GF immediately generates double Klang — the element's own Spannung plus the GF's directional tension at that position; always calculate both when placing any element.
- **Source**: p.128
- **Quote (DE)**: "Dieses Pulsieren der GF verwandelt sich, wie gezeigt wurde, zu Doppel- und Mehrklängen, wenn das einfachste Element auf die GF gebracht wird."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This pulsing of the GF transforms itself, as has been shown, into double and multiple resonances when the simplest element is placed on the GF."
- **Category**: plane

### Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Dramatisierung vs. Ausgleich — Gewichtsformel
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Apply the weight formula to place forms on a GF: for Dramatisierung, reinforce GF-weight with form-weight (oben: GF=2, Form=2; unten: GF=4, Form=4); for Ausgleich, compensate (oben: GF=2, Form=4; unten: GF=4, Form=2). Verify the resulting weight ratio (4:8 vs. 6:6).
- **Purpose**: Trains conscious management of compositional weight distribution; makes the top/bottom contrast system operable.
- **Source**: p.112

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Richtungspaare auf der GF — Lyrisch / Dramatisch
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Place a transition-form (line→plane) on the most objective (square) GF, first aligned with the "lyrische" diagonal (cb, top-left to bottom-right), then with the "dramatische" diagonal (da, bottom-left to top-right). Observe the Klang difference.
- **Purpose**: Trains the student to distinguish lyrische from dramatische Spannung in diagonal placement; makes the diagonal system empirically graspable.
- **Source**: p.123

**EXERCISE [E-03]**: Gegensatz-Paare — Vertikale Lage vs. Horizontale Lage
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Create the two contrast-pairs: Pair A (highest Gegensatz — forms directed toward loose resistance / hard resistance), Pair B (mild Gegensatz — both forms directed toward each other from mid-zones). First use portrait format (Vertikale Lage — "Warme Ruhe"), then landscape format (Horizontale Lage — "Kalte Ruhe"). Compare the character of each pair in each format.
- **Purpose**: Trains awareness of how format temperature (warm/cold) modulates the Gegensatz intensity of form-pairs.
- **Source**: p.124
