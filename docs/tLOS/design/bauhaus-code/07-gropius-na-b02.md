## Tischlerei — Furniture Workshop (continued)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The furniture workshop produces objects that are simultaneously demonstrable as Prototypen for industrial manufacture and as coherent spatial systems — each piece is not a standalone object but a member of a type-family, combinable into room furnishings.

**Evidence**: Pages 17–34 document the full range of Tischlerei output from 1922–1924: storage systems (shelves, bookcases, display cabinets), seating (armchairs, children's chairs, desk chairs), beds, tables, and kitchen furniture — across four designers: Gropius, Albers, Breuer, Dieckmann.

**Conclusion**: The workshop demonstrates that Reduktion to geometric component logic — rectangular frame, flat plane, sliding or hinged panel — yields objects legible across every room type. The type-logic is transferable: the same structural grammar produces a bookcase, a kitchen cabinet, a display case.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Spiegelglas als Materialwahrheit / Mirror glass as material honesty
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Glass used in furniture — Spiegelglas (mirror glass), Opakglas (opaque glass) — is not decorative cladding but functional surface. The mirror glass sliding door on Albers's display cabinet and Breuer's and Dieckmann's bookcases allows visual access to contents while maintaining enclosure. The material is chosen for what it does, not for visual richness.
- **Rule**: Use glass in furniture only where transparency or reflection serves a functional purpose (content visibility, spatial lightness); never as surface decoration.
- **Source**: p.19, p.25, p.30, p.31
- **Quote (DE)**: "Opakglas, Nickel, Spiegelglas, Eiche hell und dunkel"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Opaque glass, nickel, mirror glass, oak light and dark"
- **Category**: material-honesty

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Komponentenlogik / Component logic in storage systems
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The shelving and storage units shown across pages 17–21 and 30–31 operate on a consistent component grammar: upright members (vertical), shelf members (horizontal), glass sliding panels. The same grammar produces a magazine rack, a sheet-music shelf, a display cabinet, a wardrobe-scale bookcase. The Typus is the component set, not the finished object.
- **Rule**: Design the component type, not the object instance — the same uprights, shelves, and panels should combine into any storage configuration required.
- **Source**: p.17, p.18, p.19, p.21, p.30, p.31
- **Category**: standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Elastischer Sitz / Elastic-supported seating surface
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Breuer's Sessel (Kreuzgurt, 1922) uses cross-webbing (Kreuzgurt) and a fabric seat surface (Stoff) rather than a padded cushion. The seat is elastic and angled, supporting the full upper thigh (ganzen Oberschenkel). The back is also angled and held by two straps (Gurte) positioned to contact shoulder blades and cross-spine, deliberately leaving the sensitive spine free.
- **Rule**: The seat structure must follow the body's loading geometry — angle the seat for thigh support, angle the back for shoulder-blade contact, leave the spine unloaded by design.
- **Source**: p.28, p.29
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der elastische und schräge Sitz stützt den ganzen Oberschenkel. Der Rücken ist danach ebenfalls schräg und elastisch gehalten, durch zwei Gurte, die nur Schulterblätter und Kreuz — flache Teile des Rückens — berühren, das empfindliche Rückgrat frei lassen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The elastic and angled seat supports the entire upper thigh. The back is accordingly also angled and elastically held by two straps that touch only the shoulder blades and the cross — flat parts of the back — leaving the sensitive spine free."
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Farbig lackiert als Typus-Kennzeichnung / Color lacquer as type identification
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Several Breuer pieces (Kinderstühle, Küchenschrank, Schreibtisch) are described as "farbig lackiert" — color lacquered. In the context of the children's furniture and kitchen furniture, this is not decoration but type-differentiation: color distinguishes functional categories and makes the object legible to its user-type (child, cook).
- **Rule**: Apply color to differentiate object types or user contexts; color is a functional signal, not ornament.
- **Source**: p.32, p.33, p.34
- **Category**: function

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Versuchshaus als Prototyp-Kontext / The experimental house as prototype context
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Multiple objects in this batch are identified as belonging to the Versuchshaus des Bauhauses (experimental house) — Dieckmann's bed, Breuer's bookcase (from the Versuchshaus in Weimar). The experimental house is the testing ground: objects are installed in actual use conditions before being proposed for serial production.
- **Rule**: The prototype must be tested in real spatial context — not in isolation as a studio object — before being designated as a Typus for Serienfertigung.
- **Source**: p.24–25, p.31
- **Quote (DE)**: "Aus dem Versuchshaus des Bauhauses in Weimar"
- **Quote (EN)**: "From the experimental house of the Bauhaus in Weimar"
- **Category**: craft-industry

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Sperrholz für geringes Gewicht / Plywood for low weight
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Breuer's Kinderstühlchen (children's small chairs) are made with Sperrholzplatten (plywood panels) specifically for geringes Gewicht — low weight. The material choice is dictated by the user requirement: a child must be able to move the chair.
- **Rule**: Let the user's physical capability determine the material choice; weight is a design parameter, not an afterthought.
- **Source**: p.32
- **Quote (DE)**: "Kinderstühlchen mit Sperrholzplatten (geringes Gewicht). Farbig lackiert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Small children's chairs with plywood panels (low weight). Color lacquered."
- **Category**: material-honesty

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Bewegliche Elemente / Movable components within a fixed frame
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Breuer's Toilettentisch (dressing table, 1923) features bewegliche Spiegel (movable mirrors) and a sliding Frisierkastendeckplatte (hairdressing-box cover plate). Dieckmann's Schreibsessel has a bewegliche Rückenlehne (movable backrest). Mobility of specific elements within an otherwise rigid frame is designed-in, not added-on.
- **Rule**: Identify which elements of a piece require movement for function; make only those elements mobile, leaving the structural frame fixed and stable.
- **Source**: p.23, p.27
- **Quote (DE)**: "Toilettentisch mit beweglichen Spiegeln, Nußbaum und Zitronenholz. Die Deckplatte des Frisierkastens (links) ist nach rechts verschiebbar."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Dressing table with movable mirrors, walnut and lemon wood. The cover plate of the hairdressing box (left) is slidable to the right."
- **Category**: function
