## Siedlung Dessau-Törten — Rationalized Housing Construction and Type Development

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Siedlung Dessau-Törten is not simply a housing estate — it is a laboratory for the industrialization of dwelling. Each construction cycle (1927, 1928) represents an iterative refinement of a Typus: the type is not found once and frozen, but developed through systematic research combining technical, economic, and artistic forces. The settlement demonstrates that the problem of housing is, at its root, an industrial problem.

**Evidence**: Construction photographs showing the assembly of Füllwände (infill walls) from Schlackenbetonplatten (slag concrete panels) and Wärmeisolierende Zellbetonplatten (thermally insulating cellular concrete panels); exterior views of completed row-house sequences (Typen 1927 and 1928); photographs of the garden and rear elevations; interior photographs of the furnished living room, kitchen (Küche mit Einrichtung), and bathroom (Spülküche); plans of the Konsumverein building (ground floor and upper floors); exterior photographs of the Konsumverein building. The sequence moves from shell construction → completed exterior → furnished interior → civic building.

**Conclusion**: The Siedlung proves that a coherent formal language can be produced by rationalized, industrialized means, that the Typus improves through repetition and research, and that an entire neighborhood — including its commercial and civic building — can be unified under the same sachlicher Stil. The building is the argument. Its existence is the proof.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Schlackenbetonplatten und Zellbetonplatten / Slag concrete panels and cellular concrete insulating panels
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The infill walls (Füllwände) are constructed from Schlackenbetonplatten on the interior and Wärmeisolierende Zellbetonplatten on the exterior face. Simultaneously, the square concrete frames (quadratische Betonrahmen) for the staircase glazing are set in place. This is not sequential trades work — it is simultaneous, coordinated assembly.
- **Rule**: Design the wall assembly so that thermal insulation, structural fill, and window-frame installation proceed as a single coordinated operation, not as sequential trades.
- **Source**: p.179
- **Quote (DE)**: "ausmauern der füllwände aus schlackenbetonplatten auf der innenseite und wärmeisolierenden zellbetonplatten auf der außenseite. gleichzeitig werden die quadratischen betonrahmen zur aufnahme der treppenhausverglasung mitversetzt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "filling the infill walls with slag concrete panels on the interior side and thermally insulating cellular concrete panels on the exterior. Simultaneously the square concrete frames for the staircase glazing are set in place."
- **Category**: standardization | material | workshop-integration

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Terrazzo-Fensterbrüstung / Prefabricated terrazzo window sill/parapet
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The prefabricated terrazzo window parapets (gestampfte Terrazzo-Fensterbrüstbrik) are fitted from outside during the exterior completion phase. This prefabricated element condenses multiple functions: weather seal, visual datum line across the facade, and finished interior sill — all in a single factory-produced component.
- **Rule**: Combine weather protection, structural datum, and interior finish into a single prefabricated facade element; installation sequence must allow exterior fitting before interior finishing is complete.
- **Source**: p.180
- **Quote (DE)**: "von den außen werden die gestampften terrazzo-fensterbrüstbrik vorsezt"
- **Quote (EN)**: "from outside the prefabricated pressed-terrazzo window parapets are fitted"
- **Category**: standardization | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Einsetzen der Stahlrahmen und Stahlblättern vor Ausmauern der Füllwände / Frames first, walls second
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The steel frames (Stahlrahmen) and steel panels (Stahlblättern) are inserted prior to filling the infill walls. The structural frame precedes and determines the infill — the frame is not hidden by the wall, it organizes it. This is the constructional logic of the Vorhangfassade applied at domestic scale.
- **Rule**: Install all frame elements before infill; the frame must be legible as the organizational logic of the wall even after the infill is complete.
- **Source**: p.181 (abb. 166)
- **Quote (DE)**: "einsetzen der stahlrahmen und stahlblättern vor ausmauern der füllwände"
- **Quote (EN)**: "installation of steel frames and steel panels before filling the infill walls"
- **Category**: transparency | function | standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Eigenheim mit Garten als Wohnform / The garden house as dwelling type for the many
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius argues explicitly that the owner-occupied house with garden (Eigenheim mit Garten) delivers essential values for family life — above all for children — that the Großhaus (large apartment block) cannot provide. However, this Wohnform (dwelling type) is economically more demanding to build and manage, and can only be justified where genuine need for the type exists. The type is not imposed sentimentally; it is justified functionally.
- **Rule**: Choose dwelling type based on the actual needs of the inhabitants, not on ideological preference for either the individual house or the collective block; justify the choice on functional and economic grounds.
- **Source**: p.182
- **Quote (DE)**: "es steht außer frage, daß über die wirtschaftlichen fragen hinaus das teurere und schwerer zu bewirtschaftende eigenheim mit garten für viele kreise der bevölkerung wesentliche werte für das familienleben der bewohner, insbesondere für die kinder, mit sich bringt, daß also überall da, wo echte bedürfnisse zum eigenheim vorliegen, dem planmäßigen bau dieser wohnform vorschub geleistet werden muß"
- **Quote (EN)**: "it is beyond question that beyond the economic questions the more expensive and more difficult-to-manage owner-occupied house with garden brings essential values for the family life of the inhabitants, especially for children, so that wherever genuine need for the individual house exists, the systematic construction of this dwelling type must be promoted"
- **Category**: function | urbanism

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Balkon und Garten an der Rückseite / Balcony and garden on the rear face
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The rear elevation of the Törten row houses carries balconies and gardens — the private, inhabited exterior face. The front elevation is the public, civic face; the rear is the domestic face. This functional differentiation of front and rear produces two distinct facade languages from the same building type.
- **Rule**: Differentiate front and rear facades according to their public/private role; the rear face carries the domestic program (balcony, garden access, service) while the front carries the civic address.
- **Source**: p.182 (abb. 167, 1927) and p.183 (abb. 168, 1926)
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Typisierte Serienmöbel / Standardized serial furniture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The living room (Wohnzimmer) is furnished with standardized serial furniture (typisierte Serienmöbel, 1926). The furniture vocabulary — cubic forms, dark-stained wood, simple joinery — is consistent with the formal language of the architecture. Interior and exterior are designed as a single system; the furniture type is as much a product of Typus research as the building type.
- **Rule**: Design furniture and fittings as a type-series using the same formal logic as the architecture; do not treat interior furnishing as a separate discipline from building design.
- **Source**: p.184 (abb. 169)
- **Quote (DE)**: "wohnzimmer, typisierte serienmöbel 1926"
- **Quote (EN)**: "living room, standardized serial furniture 1926"
- **Category**: standardization | workshop-integration

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Haustür in Stahlrahmen, Glasbausteine / Entry door: steel frame with glass block
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The house entrance (Hauseingang) uses a door set in a steel frame (Stahlrahmen) with glass blocks (Glasbausteine) forming the sidelight and overhead panel. Glass blocks transmit light into the entry without transparency — a material that is simultaneously structural infill, light conductor, and privacy screen. The same logic as the Vorhangfassade at the scale of a domestic threshold.
- **Rule**: At the domestic threshold, use glass block with steel frame to admit light while maintaining the mass of the wall; the entry must be readable as a designed, articulated element, not a hole in the wall.
- **Source**: p.185 (abb. 170)
- **Quote (DE)**: "hauseingang. haustür in stahlrahmen, glasbausteine"
- **Quote (EN)**: "house entrance. front door in steel frame, glass blocks"
- **Category**: material | transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Küche mit rationeller Einrichtung / Kitchen as rationalized work space
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kitchen (Küche mit Einrichtung, 1926) is organized as a rationalized work space: continuous counter surface, wall-mounted storage, minimal circulation path between sink, preparation surface, and stove. The kitchen is not decorated — it is organized. Every element has a function and a position determined by the work sequence.
- **Rule**: Design the kitchen as a work-sequence diagram made physical; the spatial organization must minimize unnecessary movement; no decorative elements that do not contribute to the work function.
- **Source**: p.186 (abb. 171)
- **Quote (DE)**: "küche mit einrichtung, 1926"
- **Quote (EN)**: "kitchen with fittings, 1926"
- **Category**: function | workshop-integration

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Waschtrog und Sitzbadewanne aus weißem Terrazzo / Bathroom as rationalized hygiene space
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bathroom (Spülküche) contains a wash trough (Waschtrog) and sitting bathtub (Sitzbadewanne) in white terrazzo, with a shared swivel board (gemeinsamer Schwenkhahn) and a fold-down work surface (Arbeitsklappplatte) above the tub, manufactured by Junkers. The bathroom is organized by the same logic as the kitchen: rational sequence, integrated fittings, factory-produced components. The terrazzo is white — hygiene expressed as material choice.
- **Rule**: Standardize bathroom fittings as factory-produced components in a single material (white terrazzo); integrate all hygiene functions within a minimum floor area using combinable, swiveling fittings.
- **Source**: p.187 (abb. 172)
- **Quote (DE)**: "waschtrog und sitzbadewanne aus weißem terrazzo, gemeinsamer schwenkhahn, arbeitsklappplatte über der wanne, junkers terme"
- **Quote (EN)**: "wash trough and sitting bathtub in white terrazzo, shared swivel tap, fold-down work surface above the tub, Junkers [heating]"
- **Category**: standardization | function | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Typus 1927 / The 1927 type — first public appearance
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Häuser des Typs 1927 (houses of the 1927 type) are shown from two angles: from a distance with the electrical pylon as vertical counterpoint (abb. 173), and in close frontal view showing the two-storey composition with horizontal window strips (Fensterbänder) alternating with rendered wall panels (abb. 174). The type is defined by: flat roof, horizontal window bands, white rendered surfaces, cubic massing. The industrial pylon in the background is not incidental — it places the housing in the landscape of industrial modernity.
- **Rule**: Position the housing type within its actual landscape — including infrastructure — not in a sanitized void; the Siedlung is part of an industrial territory, and its formal language must be congruent with that territory.
- **Source**: p.188 (abb. 173), p.189 (abb. 174)
- **Category**: function | urbanism | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Verbesserter Typus 1927 (Baujahr 1928) / Improved type — iterative refinement
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The "verbesserten Typs 1927, Baujahr 1928" (improved 1927 type, built 1928) shows the characteristic street view of the Törten settlement: a receding perspective of identical two-storey houses flanking a paved road, with a street lamp as the only vertical accent. The iteration from 1927 to the 1928 construction cycle represents explicit type refinement. The formal language is identical; the refinement is constructional and economic, not stylistic.
- **Rule**: Iterate the type across construction cycles; refinement must be documented and the improved version supersedes the previous without abandoning the formal vocabulary.
- **Source**: p.190 (abb. 175)
- **Quote (DE)**: "häuser des verbesserten typs 1927, baujahr 1928"
- **Quote (EN)**: "houses of the improved 1927 type, construction year 1928"

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Gartenseite als Ansicht / Garden elevation as architectural face
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The garden elevation (Gartenseite) of the 1927 type (abb. 176, 177) shows the private rear face: the same formal vocabulary as the street face — flat roof, horizontal windows, white render — but with garden plots, fences, and informal occupation visible. The formal language does not change at the rear. The sachlicher Stil is not a public performance; it is the consistent language of the entire building and its setting.
- **Rule**: Apply the same formal language to all faces of the building including the rear garden elevation; do not reserve formal discipline only for the public street face.
- **Source**: p.191 (abb. 176), p.192 (abb. 177)

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Typus als radikale Ausarbeitung / The type as radical, sustained work
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius states directly that the right sense of tradition does not build on individual willfulness (eigenbrödlerische Wollen) but on the common standard — the thing that the most people can best satisfy themselves with, that most possess in quality. This standard can only be achieved through new and strong technical means. The elaboration (Ausarbeitung) of the type requires the strongest and most radical work; it is not a one-time discovery but an ongoing problem.
- **Rule**: Treat type development as a continuous research problem requiring radical technical commitment; do not mistake formal consistency for completed work — the type must be constantly improved and verified.
- **Source**: p.192
- **Quote (DE)**: "der richtige sinn für die tradition baut nicht auf das mutwillige, eigenbrödlerische wollen, sondern auf das gemeinsame, den standard, der viele zu befriedigen in der lage ist, am meisten inhalt, am meisten qualität besitzt. eine solche gedrängtheit an inhalt läßt sich nur dann erreichen, wenn neue und starke technische mittel angewendet werden, deren aufwand sich erst in der vervielfältigung überhaupt lohnen kann. die ausarbeitung des typus braucht die stärkste und radikalste arbeit, wenn er sich bewähren und erhalten soll, ist das problem eines objekts und seine herstellung bis ins letzte auch auf längere zukunft hinaus durchdacht, so wird dieses zum traditionellen standard, denn nur das bessere ist des guten feind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the right sense of tradition builds not on willful, individualistic wanting, but on the common standard — the one that is able to satisfy the most people, that contains the most content and the most quality. This density of content can only be achieved when new and strong technical means are applied, whose expenditure only pays off in multiplication. The elaboration of the type requires the strongest and most radical work; if it is to prove itself and endure, the problem of an object and its manufacture must be thought through to the last detail for the longer future as well — for only the better is the enemy of the good."
- **Category**: standardization | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Behausung als Massenbedarfsartikel / Housing as mass-consumption article
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius argues that the chaotic non-uniformity of our dwellings proves how confused our ideas are about what is appropriate housing for the present-day human. The majority of the population of civilized peoples have equal and uniform living and life requirements. Human dwelling is therefore a matter of mass need (Massenbedarf). The population no longer has it in mind to have things made to measure — they buy stock products (Vorrätsprodukte), and refined manufacturing methods satisfy most individual needs. Therefore, the fundamental restructuring of all construction toward the industrial side is the most important demand of a simultaneous solution.
- **Rule**: Design housing as a mass-production problem, not as an artisanal problem; the formal vocabulary must be compatible with industrial repetition and factory production of components.
- **Source**: p.193
- **Quote (DE)**: "die chaotische uneinheitlichkeit unserer wohnungen beweist die verschwommenheit unserer vorstellungen von der richtigen, die heutigen menschen angemessenen behausung. die mehrzahl der bürger zivilisierter völker hat gleichartige wohn- und lebensbedürfnisse. die menschliche behausung ist also eine angelegenheit des massenbedarfs. genau so wie es aber heute 90% der bevölkerung nicht mehr einfällt, sich ihre beschuhung nach maß anfertigen zu lassen, sondern vorrätsprodukte bezieht, die infolge verfeinerter fabrikationsmethoden die meisten individuellen bedürfnisse befriedigen, so wird sich in zukunft der einzelne auch die ihm gemäße behausung vom lager bestellen können. grundlegende umgestaltung der gesamten bauwirtschaft nach der industriellen seite hin ist wichtigste forderung für eine zeitgemäße lösung des problems."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the chaotic non-uniformity of our dwellings proves the confusion of our ideas about appropriate housing for today's person. The majority of the citizens of civilized peoples have equal and uniform living and life requirements. Human dwelling is therefore a matter of mass need. Just as today 90% of the population no longer thinks of having their shoes made to measure, but orders stock products that through refined manufacturing methods satisfy most individual needs, so in future the individual will also be able to order their appropriate dwelling from stock. Fundamental restructuring of all construction toward the industrial side is the most important demand for a contemporary solution to the problem."
- **Category**: standardization | urbanism | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Dreiseitige Gleichzeitigkeit / Three-domain simultaneity for housing reform
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius insists that the solution to housing must proceed simultaneously from three different areas: from the economic-organizational (volkswirtschaftlich-organisatorisch), from the technical, and from the formal-artistic (gestalterisch). All three areas are interdependent; simultaneous action from all three is the only path to success. This is not a hierarchy — it is a triad.
- **Rule**: Never approach housing design as purely a technical problem, or purely an economic problem, or purely a formal problem; all three must be addressed simultaneously, and solutions that advance only one domain while ignoring the others are incomplete.
- **Source**: p.193
- **Quote (DE)**: "dieses muß gleichzeitig von drei verschiedenen gebieten aus, vom volkswirtschaftlich-organisatorischen, vom technischen und vom gestalterischen abhängig, angepaßt werden. alle drei gebiete sind voneinander abhängig. nur gleichzeitiges vorgehen von allen drei gebieten aus wird zum erfolg führen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "this must be approached simultaneously from three different areas: from the economic-organizational, from the technical, and from the formal-artistic. All three areas are interdependent. Only simultaneous action from all three areas will lead to success."
- **Category**: function | pedagogy | urbanism

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Fix-und-fertig-Wohnhaus vom Lager / The ready-made house ordered from stock
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Gropius projects that the fully fitted-out (fix und fertig eingerichtete) house ordered from stock (vom Lager) will in a short time become a main product of industry. The implementation of this comprehensive problem requires the determined, joint action of state and municipal authorities, professional organizations, states, communes, large industry. The large building organizations, states, communes, and large industry have the duty to undertake the necessary trials, to finance the housing production. Public trial sites with public funding are urgently needed.
- **Rule**: The industrialized housing type requires public trial infrastructure and public funding; private initiative alone cannot produce the volume needed for type development — it must be a joint action of public and industrial actors.
- **Source**: p.195
- **Quote (DE)**: "das fix und fertig eingerichtete wohnhaus vom lager wird in kürze ein hauptprodukt der industrie werden. die durchführung des umfassenden problems erfordert allerdings entschlossenes gemeinsames vorgehen der staats- und kommunalbehörden, der fachleute und der konsumenten. die großen bauherrenorganisationen, staaten, kommunen, großindustrie, haben die pflicht, die notwendigen versuche, die der hausproduktion vorausgehen müssen, zu finanzieren: öffentliche versuchsplätze mit hilfe öffentlicher mittel sind dringendes erfordernis."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the fully fitted-out house from stock will in a short time become a main product of industry. The implementation of the comprehensive problem requires determined, joint action of state and municipal authorities, professionals, and consumers. The large building organizations, states, communes, large industry have the duty to finance the necessary trials that must precede housing production: public trial sites with public funding are an urgent necessity."
- **Category**: standardization | urbanism

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Typisierte Bauteile als Forschungsprojekt / Standardized building components require systematic research at large scale
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Just as industry subjects every object to systematic preliminary investigation, with buyers, technicians, and artists equally involved, before multiplying it in large numbers — so the manufacturing of standardized building components demands systematic trial work on a large scale, combining economic, industrial, and artistic forces.
- **Rule**: Treat the standardized building component as an industrial research object: involve economic, technical, and artistic expertise from the start; do not scale up production until systematic trials are complete.
- **Source**: p.195
- **Quote (DE)**: "so wie die industrie jeden gegenstand, den sie vervielfältigt, zahllosen versuchen systematischer vorarbeit unterwirft, an der kaufleute, techniker, künstler gleichermaßen beteiligt sind, ehe sein 'typus' gefunden wird, so verlangt auch die herstellung typisierter bauteile systematische versuchsarbeit in großzügigem zusammengehen der wirtschaftlichen, industriellen und künstlerischen kräfte."
- **Quote (EN)**: "just as industry subjects every object it multiplies to countless systematic preliminary trials, in which merchants, technicians, and artists are equally involved, before its 'type' is found, so the manufacture of standardized building components demands systematic trial work in a generous combination of economic, industrial, and artistic forces."
- **Category**: standardization | workshop-integration | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Konsumverein als Siedlungsbaustein / The cooperative store as settlement building block
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Haus für den Konsumverein für Dessau und Umgebung (cooperative store building for Dessau and surroundings) is part of the Siedlung complex. It is not a stand-alone commercial building but a civic building designed as part of the total settlement. The building combines commercial ground floor with residential upper floors — mixed use as principle.
- **Rule**: Include civic and commercial buildings in the settlement plan from the start; the cooperative store (or equivalent civic program) must be designed with the same formal vocabulary as the housing and sited to serve the settlement's daily life.
- **Source**: p.196 (abb. 181–183 plan)
- **Quote (DE)**: "grundriß des erdgeschosses und des obergeschosses für den bau des konsumvereins für dessau und umgebung im fachbereich: fleischereien, kolonialwarenläden und kaffee. im erdgeschoß: 2 stageerwohnungen (2 zimmer, bad und küche)"
