## Section: Dichtung (Poetry)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The rhythmic shaping of a verse (Vers) finds its expression in a curving and straight line (gerade und gebogene Linie), where the lawful alternation (gesetzmäßige Wechsel) can be graphically precisely designated as Versmaß. The line is fundamental to poetry's inner law, not merely decorative.
**Evidence**: Beyond rhythmic measurement, the verse carries a musikalisch-melodische Linie — ascending/descending dynamics in the voice — expressed through variable form. This line is organically bound (gesetzmäßig) to the literary content of the verse: Spannung (tension) and Entspannung (relaxation) are content-bearing in nature.
**Conclusion**: For an abstract poem, a Notensystem (notation system) that precisely indicates the Höhenlinie (height-line) with musical precision is theoretically possible but practically hazardous. The point in poetry is — Schweigen (silence).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Gesetzmäßige Linie / Lawful line of rhythm
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The rhythmic form of a verse can be graphically designated exactly. The Versmaß (meter) maps to a line whose alternation of tension and relaxation is lawful, not arbitrary. The variable is bounded — it depends on the degree of freedom of the reciter, just as musical dynamics depend on the performing artist.
- **Rule**: Map rhythmic tension to line form — regular alternation = consistent curvature; freely interpreted performance = variable, irregular line.
- **Source**: p.95
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die rhythmische Gestaltung des Verses findet ihren Ausdruck in gerader und gebogener Linie, wobei der gesetzmäßige Wechsel graphisch genau bezeichnet wird — Versmaß."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The rhythmic shaping of the verse finds its expression in straight and curved line, where the lawful alternation is graphically precisely designated — meter."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Musikalisch-melodische Linie / Musical-melodic line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Beyond the rhythmic measurement, the verse carries a musikalisch-melodische Linie that manifests as rising and falling (das Steigen und Fallen) in only slightly changing variable form. This line is grundsätzlich (fundamentally) bound to the literary-inner content of the verse: Spannung and Entspannung are of content-bearing nature.
- **Rule**: The melodic arc of content — its tension and release — must be read as a line phenomenon, not merely as semantic meaning.
- **Source**: p.95
- **Quote (DE)**: "Diese Linie ist grundsätzlich gesetzmäßig, da sie mit dem literarischen Inhalt des Verses verbunden ist — Spannung und Entspannung sind inhaltlicher Natur."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This line is fundamentally lawful, as it is bound to the literary content of the verse — tension and relaxation are content-bearing in nature."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Der Punkt — Schweigen / The point as silence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In poetry, the point (Punkt) is — Schweigen (silence). This is Kandinsky's direct statement, placed at the conclusion of the poetry section as the terminal case of all punctuation applied to the Punkt concept.
- **Rule**: The point marks absolute cessation — it is not decoration but the embodiment of held tension without release.
- **Source**: p.95
- **Quote (DE)**: "Wie oben schon gesagt wurde, ist der Punkt — Schweigen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "As already stated above, the point is — silence."
- **Category**: point

## Section: Technik (Engineering / Technology)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: In the neighboring domain of engineering (Ingenieurkunst) and the closely related Technik, the line increasingly gains in significance.
**Evidence**: The Eiffel Tower in Paris was the first notable attempt to expose particularly high buildings as skeletal structures — the line has displaced the plane (Fig.65, Fig.66). The statistical method that reduces number usage — the line partially replaces the number — creates tables accessible even to laypeople (Fig.67). The same method is used in other sciences (e.g., astronomy: "Lichtkurve").
**Conclusion**: The line's role in technical representation is to achieve maximum information economy: minimal expression to maximal result. The technical application of the line as graphical numerical expression (Zahlenausdruck) is precise, automatic, and maximally efficient.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Linie ersetzt Zahl / Line replaces number
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In technology and its related domains, the line increasingly gains significance. The specific technical construction of the line as graphical numerical expression is an important case. The automatic Linienzeichen (line signs) used in meteorological observations are a precise graphical representation of increasing and decreasing force (zu- und abnehmende Kraft). The line here replaces the number: clearer, more accessible, more precise.
- **Rule**: Where quantitative change must be read at a glance, substitute line-form for numerical table: the eye grasps trend and magnitude simultaneously through line direction and amplitude.
- **Source**: p.95 (footnote), p.96
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die entstehenden Tabellen sind übersichtlich, und auch dem Laien zugänglich."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The resulting tables are clear, and also accessible to the layperson."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Skelettierung / Skeletonization
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Eiffel Tower was the first significant attempt to reveal particularly high buildings as skeletal structures — die Linie hat die Fläche verdrängt (the line has displaced the plane). In marine engineering, the skeleton of a motor freight ship (Fig.66) likewise exposes line-construction in three-dimensional space, not on the plane.
- **Rule**: When structural honesty demands it, expose the skeleton: the line-construction of a form reveals its actual generative forces more truthfully than a surface (Fläche) would.
- **Source**: p.95–96
- **Quote (DE)**: "Soviel ich weiß, war der Eiffelturm in Paris der bedeutendste erste Versuch, aus Linien ein besonders hohes Gebäude herauszustellen — die Linie hat die Fläche verdrängt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "As far as I know, the Eiffel Tower in Paris was the most significant first attempt to expose a particularly high building as lines — the line has displaced the plane."
- **Category**: line

## Section: Konstruktivismus (Constructivism)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The "constructivist" works of recent years are largely composed of pure or approximate constructions in space, without practical purpose (zweckmäßig).
**Evidence**: What compels us to also count these works in the domain of "pure" art is: the energetic use of the line (energetische Verwendung der Linie) and the strong emphasis on Punkt-nodes (Punktknoten) are striking in these works (Fig.70 — Constructivist exhibition, Moscow 1921).
**Conclusion**: Constructivist art belongs to the realm of pure art, but arrives at it from the engineering side.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Energetische Verwendung der Linie / Energetic use of the line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The constructivist works of recent years use the line energetically — this energetic use and the strong emphasis on Punkt-nodes (Punktknoten) are conspicuous characteristics of these works. This differentiates them from decorative line use and positions them within the domain of pure art.
- **Rule**: Distinguish energetic line use (line as force-vector, tension-bearer) from decorative line use (line as outline or ornament). Only the former has claim to pure formal validity.
- **Source**: p.97
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die energetische Verwendung und die starke Betonung des Punktknotens sind in diesen Werken auffallend (Fig.70)."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The energetic use and the strong emphasis on the point-node are conspicuous in these works (Fig.70)."
- **Category**: line

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: Fig.70 — Ein Raum der Konstruktivisten-Ausstellung in Moskau 1921.
- **Shows**: Photograph of an exhibition room with three-dimensional constructivist sculptures/constructions on stands — diagonal and vertical rod-constructions in space, abstract spatial line compositions filling the room.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Three-dimensional Linie-Punkt constructions in art (not engineering) — the Konstruktivismus domain. The energetic use of line and Punkt-nodes as formal elements. Demonstrates P-06.
- **Text reference**: "Die energetische Verwendung und die starke Betonung des Punktknotens sind in diesen Werken auffallend (Fig.70)."
- **Source**: p.97

## Section: Natur (Nature)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The use of the line in nature is extraordinarily abundant (überaus zahlreich). This deserves a special synthetic investigation that only a natural scientist of artistic sensibility could accomplish.
**Evidence**: For the artist it would be especially important to see what the elements of nature are from the Grundelement perspective — what properties they possess and in what way they assemble into configurations (Gebilden). Composition laws open to the artist the possibility of not merely imitating external appearances, but discovering in apparent randomness the Gesetze (laws). Within this domain, two principles stand out: the Prinzip der Parallele (principle of the parallel) and the Prinzip des Gegensatzes (principle of contrast) — both were stimulated through line combinations as shown in examples like the Mastenwald (Fig.69). The laws thus isolated come to the full understanding of the total Gesetze (laws) of the great domains — Kunst and Natur — and clarify the autonomous activity of both as a higher synthetic Ordnung (order): Äußeres + Inneres.
**Evidence cascade (biology, mineral, plant, animal)**:
- Mineral realm: the schematic structure of crystals (Fig.71 — "Trichiten"/crystalline skeletons) is a pure Linienbildung, e.g., in Flächenform — ice crystals.
- Plant realm: the plant in its whole development from seed to root follows the Prinzip der ansetzenden Stamm (attaching stem), going from point to line (Fig.72 — leaf Grundspirale). From point to line to more complex line-complexes, to independent line-constructions (Fig.74 — Clematis blossom).
- Animal realm: plant-oscillation movements (Fig.73 — plant oscillation through "Gleiten"), animal nervous constructions (Fig.77 — vertebral extremity), and the organic line leadership of the arm, which from the Gewebestruktur (Fig.76 — loose connective tissue of rat) goes always from the same Grundprinzip (basic principle): the concentric construction.
**Conclusion**: The organic line leadership of the arm proceeds always from the same Grundprinzip of concentric construction — it shows in varied assembled forms the unendlichen Zusammenstellungen (infinite combinations) while the Grundprinzip (basic principle) remains unified.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Parallel und Gegensatz als Naturgesetz / Parallel and Contrast as natural law
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The two principles — das Prinzip der Parallele (principle of parallel) and das Prinzip des Gegensatzes (principle of contrast) — emerge as the fundamental laws governing all line combinations in nature and in art. Both laws were stimulated through line combinations (as in the Mastenwald example). Isolated this way, the laws of the great domains — art and nature — become finally understood as a higher synthetic order: Äußeres + Inneres (External + Internal).
- **Rule**: In any line composition, identify whether its governing principle is the parallel (repetition, similarity, accumulation) or the contrast (opposition, differentiation, counterforce). Only one or the other will dominate; the combination is the compositional resolution.
- **Source**: p.97
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die auf diese Weise abgesonderten und selbständig lebenden Gesetze der beiden großen Reiche — der Kunst und der Natur — werden schließlich zum Verständnis des Gesamtgesetzes der Weltkomposition führen und die selbständige Betätigung der beiden an einer höheren synthetischen Ordnung — Äußeres + Inneres — klarlegen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The thus isolated and independently living laws of the two great realms — art and nature — will finally lead to the understanding of the total law of world-composition and clarify the autonomous activity of both within a higher synthetic order — External + Internal."
- **Category**: composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Punkt zu Linie zu Komplex / Point to line to complex
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The plant in its whole development from seed (Samen) to root proceeds according to the Prinzip der ansetzenden Stamm (attaching stem) — going from point to line (Fig.72 — leaf Grundspirale). From point it goes via line to more complicated Linienkomplexen (line complexes), to independent line-constructions, as with the eccentric construction of fir trees (Fig.74). The organic line-leadership of the arm similarly proceeds always from the same Grundprinzip, showing unendliche Zusammenstellungen (infinite combinations) while the Grundprinzip remains unified.
- **Rule**: Trace any organic form to its originating Grundprinzip: find the point of origin, the primary line of growth, and the complex that emerges from it. The concentric principle and the eccentric principle will each generate fundamentally different complexes.
- **Source**: p.98–99
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Pflanze in ihrer ganzen Entwicklung vom Samen zur Wurzel geht nach dem Prinzip der ansetzenden Stamm."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The plant in its whole development from seed to root proceeds according to the principle of the attaching stem."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Konzentrische Konstruktion / Concentric construction as organic principle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The die organische Linienführung der Arme (organic line leadership of the limbs) always proceeds from the same Grundprinzip of concentric construction (Fig.77 — vertebral extremity schema). The schöpferische Kraft (creative force) sets itself to keep to a certain natural law, wherein the eccentric principle cannot definitively prevail (bestimmen). Natural laws of this kind are essential for art — the Exzentrische also finds its place.
- **Rule**: Concentric constructions pull toward a center — use this as an inward-resolving compositional force. Eccentric constructions push outward toward corners and boundaries — use for expansive, externally-directed compositions. Both are derived from natural formal law and carry Innere Notwendigkeit when used knowingly.
- **Source**: p.101–102
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die organische Linienführung der Arme geht stets vom selben Grundprinzip aus, weist aber dabei die verschiedensten Zusammenstellungen auf."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The organic line leadership of the limbs always proceeds from the same basic principle, but shows in this the most varied combinations."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Abstrakte Kunst und Innere Natur / Abstract art and inner nature
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The line appears in nature in innumerable appearances — mineral, plant, and animal. The schematic construction of crystals is a pure Linienbildung (Fig.71). The abstract standpoint has until now become clear only in abstract art (abstrakte Kunst), which recognizes its rights and duties no longer in the external shell (äußere Schale) of natural appearances, but in the inner purposes (inneren Zwecken). The relationship between art and nature is not one of imitation but of analogy: both serve the same Grundgesetze.
- **Rule**: Do not imitate the surface of natural forms; extract their inner formal law (Gesetz) and apply that law to pure composition. The crystal is not a motif — it is evidence that Linienbildung is a universal generative principle.
- **Source**: p.98, p.101
- **Quote (DE)**: "Dieser Standpunkt ist bis heute ausschließlich in der abstrakten Kunst deutlich geworden, die ihre Rechte und Pflichten erkannt hat und sich nicht mehr auf die äußere Schale der Naturerscheinungen stützt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This standpoint has until now become clear exclusively in abstract art, which has recognized its rights and duties and no longer relies on the external shell of natural appearances."
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Geometrischer und lockerer Aufbau / Geometric and loose construction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In the plant realm, some complexes are of a clear, exact geometrical kind (geometrische Konstruktionen) — recalling the überraschende (surprising) configurations of spider webs, made by animals. Others are of a "looser" nature (lockerer Natur), forming from free lines (freien Linien) where the abstract-artistic construction shows no exact geometric construction (Fig.76 — loose connective tissue). Both construction types are present in abstract painting: the exact-geometric and the free.
- **Rule**: Recognize the two construction poles — geometric (closed, exact, derived from fixed principle) and loose (open, free, derived from organic variation). Both are valid; neither is superior. The choice between them must arise from Innere Notwendigkeit.
- **Source**: p.100
- **Quote (DE)**: "Dabei ist aber natürlich das Feste und Exakte nicht ausgeschlossen, sondern nur auf eine andere Weise verarbeitet. Ebenso sind in der abstrakten Malerei beide Konstruktionsarten vorhanden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "But in this the fixed and exact is not excluded, only processed in a different way. Similarly, in abstract painting both construction types are present."
- **Category**: composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Kunst und Natur — Innere Beziehung / Art and Nature — inner relationship
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The finger grows from the hand exactly as a twig from a branch (Fig.77), according to the Prinzip der allgemelichen Entwicklung (principle of general development) without considering the whole, growing freely without a Gesamt-Plan (total plan) except the inner one (inneren). In painting, a line can have a higher relationship to its center — die äußere Beziehung (the outer relationship) must not be confused with the inner. This simple fact must not be underestimated in the analysis of the relationships between art and nature.
- **Rule**: Distinguish inner relationship (structural analogy, shared formal law) from outer relationship (surface resemblance, imitation). Only inner relationship provides valid formal grounding.
- **Source**: p.102
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der Finger wächst aus der Hand genauso, wie ein Zweig aus dem Ast — nach dem Prinzip der allgemelichen Entwicklung. In der Malerei kann eine Linie eine höhere Beziehung zu ihrem Zentrum haben — die äußere Beziehung soll nicht mit der inneren verwechselt werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The finger grows from the hand exactly as a twig from a branch — according to the principle of general development. In painting, a line can have a higher relationship to its center — the outer relationship must not be confused with the inner."
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Thematischer Aufbau — Sprünge (Thematic construction — jumps)
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The skeletons of various animals point in their rise to the highest — beim besten Form (the best form) — at densely packed line-constructions (dichteste Linienkonstruktionen), where these variations allow nothing of "beauty" to be desired and surprise each time through their Mannigfaltigkeit (variety/multiplicity). The remarkable fact here is that these jumps — from giraffe to toad (vom Elefant zur Maus, von Fisch zur Kröte) — are nothing more than variations of one Theme (Thema), and the unlimited possibilities lie exclusively in one Prinzip der konzentrischen Konstruktion (principle of concentric construction). The creative force sets itself to keep a certain natural law (Naturgesetz), while the Exzentrische cannot definitively prevail.
- **Rule**: Use thematic variation through the single Grundprinzip: hold the concentric principle constant while varying all surface configurations. The Grundprinzip unifies; variation enriches.
- **Source**: p.101
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Skelette verschiedener Tiere weisen in ihrer Steigerung bis zur höchsten, bei den meisten Form — beim Gewebe des Blattes oder wie bei der exzentrischen Konstruktion der Nadelbäume (Fig.74) — auf... die schöpferische Kraft hat sich hier an bestimmte Naturgesetze zu halten, die das Exzentrische ausschließen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The skeletons of various animals point in their rise to the densest line-constructions... the creative force must here hold to certain natural laws that exclude the eccentric."
- **Category**: composition

## Section: Graphik (Graphic arts — printmaking media)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The material properties discussed in the section on the Punkt for "Graphik" apply equally to the Linie: the first natural consequence of the Punkt is the line — easy production in engraving (especially in etching), careful and difficult work in woodcut, and light on-the-plane-lying in lithography.
**Evidence**: Ranked by artistic interest and corresponding technical affinity for the elements:
1. Holzschnitt (woodcut) — Fläche as lightest result
2. Radierung (etching) — Punkt, Linie
3. Lithographie (lithography) — Punkt, Linie, Fläche
**Conclusion**: The technical medium selects for which elements (Punkt, Linie, Fläche) are most natural to it. The artist must understand this correspondence to achieve Innere Notwendigkeit in choice of medium.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Medium-Element Affinität / Medium-element affinity
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Each graphic medium has a natural affinity (Affinität) for specific formal elements. Woodcut: Fläche as the lightest result. Etching: Punkt and Linie. Lithography: Punkt, Linie, and Fläche — the most complete. The level of artistic interest in these elements roughly corresponds to the technical affinity: artistic interest for the elements determines the choice of technique.
- **Rule**: Select the printing medium whose natural technical tendency aligns with the formal elements you intend to use. Do not fight the medium's material law — this violates Innere Notwendigkeit.
- **Source**: p.103
- **Quote (DE)**: "So ungefähr stuft sich das künstlerische Interesse für die Elemente und für die entsprechenden Verfahren ab."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Thus roughly the artistic interest for the elements and for the corresponding techniques grades itself."
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Holzschnitt — Fläche als Urmedium / Woodcut — Plane as primal medium
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Woodcut initially had long-standing contempt (Verachtung) in many cases, but now the appreciation of graphic materials has suddenly awakened — particularly the appreciation of the German woodcut. The woodcut was first treated as a lower form of art until it spread wider and more victoriously and finally created the special type of German graphics. It is intimately internally connected to the Fläche (plane) — the plane is the Hauptausdrucksmittel (main means of expression) of the then-painting (damaligen Malerei), which the woodcut conquered as Flächenplastik (plane-sculpture).
- **Rule**: Understand woodcut as the medium of plane — its natural expression is surface, mass, and the boundary between filled and empty plane. The line in woodcut is the residue of the plane, not the primary element.
- **Source**: p.104
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Fläche, das Hauptausdrucksmittel der damaligen Malerei, eroberte bald darauf die Plastik, da zur Flächenplastik wurde."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The plane, the main means of expression of the painting of that time, soon thereafter conquered sculpture, since it became plane-sculpture."
- **Category**: plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Linie in der Malerei / Line in painting
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: It is self-evident that painting should again occupy itself with its primary means of expression (Ausdrucksmittel) — the Linie. This happened (and still happens) in the form of a normal evolutionary development of expression, first received as Revolution (as many theoreticians still do), and is being done in particular by abstract art, from which this use of the line in graphics is judged favorably — but its use in painting judged as contrary to its nature and therefore as unlawful. This kind of this medium's contradictions is characteristic: what should be separated from each other is intermingled (Kunst, Natur), and what belongs together is carefully separated (this Fläche/Malerei, Graphik division).
- **Rule**: Do not treat the line as belonging exclusively to graphic media. The line is a primary means of expression in painting with full rights — its use in painting is as internally necessary as in any other medium.
- **Source**: p.104
- **Quote (DE)**: "Es ist selbstverständlich, daß die Malerei sich wieder mit ihrem primären Ausdrucksmittel — der Linie — befassen mußte."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It is self-evident that painting had to concern itself again with its primary means of expression — the line."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Radierung — Präzision der dünnen Linie / Etching — precision of the thin line
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Etching (Radierung) is under the existing techniques most capable of producing the thinest line with the greatest precision. Also noted: the old seeking in Vorratskästen (supply boxes) for elementary forms that lead unambiguously to the thinnest elementary form — the abstract pure line Klang (sound/resonance). On the other side: the Radierung draws toward the naked application of a half of the total form under exclusion of color — the restriction to the purely "zeichnerische" (graphic/drawing) form, which makes Radierung a specifically black-white technique.
- **Rule**: Use etching when maximum line precision and thinness are required. The etching line achieves the most "absolute Klang" (absolute sound/resonance) of all graphic line forms.
- **Source**: p.105
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die fest im Material liegende, und ganz besonders die sehr dünne Linie zu erzeugen, dazu ist unter den vorhandenen Techniken am präzisesten die Radierung imstande."
- **Quote (EN)**: "To produce the firmly material-bound, and particularly the very thin line, the etching is the most precise among the available techniques."
- **Category**: line

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Lithographie — Demokratische Vollständigkeit / Lithography — democratic completeness
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Lithography is the last invention in the series of graphic procedures (graphischen Verfahren), and grants in handling the highest Biegsamkeit (flexibility) and Elastizität (elasticity). With almost unimaginable speed of production, combined with an almost invulnerable firmness of the plate, it fully corresponds to the "Geist unserer Zeit" (spirit of our time). Punkt, Linie, Fläche, Schwarz-Weiß, Farbe — everything is achievable with highest economy. Flexibility in lithographic stone handling, i.e., the easy correction of mistakes (Herausnehmen von fehlerhaften Stellen) — which neither woodcut nor etching tolerates — corresponds to the highest degree with today's Notwendigkeit (necessity), not the outer but the inner. The democratic nature of lithography: unlimited production of copies (Abzüge), each copy unique, and increasingly approaches the handpainted image through mechanical application of color.
- **Rule**: Use lithography when the composition demands maximum formal completeness (Punkt + Linie + Fläche simultaneously), flexibility of execution, and when the work must reach broad audiences through multiple copies.
- **Source**: p.105–106
- **Quote (DE)**: "Punkt, Linie, Fläche, Schwarz-Weiß, Farbe — alles wird mit höchster Ökonomie erreicht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Point, line, plane, black-white, color — everything is achieved with the highest economy."
- **Category**: composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Punkt — Ruhe, Linie — Bewegung (formal summary statement)
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: At the conclusion of the section on the line in graphic media, Kandinsky states the formal summary: "Punkt — Ruhe. Linie — innerlich bewegte Spannung, aus der Bewegung entstanden." The two elements — crossings, combinations, assemblies — form an own "Sprache" (language), which creates configurations that words cannot achieve. The exclusion of "Zutaten" (additions) that dull and obscure the inner Klang of this language gives the painterly expression the highest precision (Knappheit) and highest Präzision. And the pure form places itself at the service of the living content.
- **Rule**: The Punkt gives rest; the Linie gives inner-moved tension born from movement. These two formal categories are irreducible — do not blur the distinction. Their combinations form a visual language of maximum precision.
- **Source**: p.106
- **Quote (DE)**: "Punkt — Ruhe. Linie — innerlich bewegte Spannung, aus der Bewegung entstanden. Die beiden Elemente — Kreuzungen, Zusammenstellungen, die eine eigene 'Sprache' bilden, die durch Worte nicht erreichbar ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Point — rest. Line — inwardly moved tension, arisen from movement. The two elements — crossings, combinations, forming their own 'language' that is unreachable by words."
- **Category**: point

## Section: GRUNDFLÄCHE (Basic Plane — title page)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Page 107 (PDF page 118) is a section divider page bearing only the title "GRUNDFLÄCHE" in large type on an otherwise blank page. This signals the transition from the analysis of Linie to the analysis of the Basic Plane (Grundfläche) — the surface on which compositions are made.
**Evidence**: Page 119 (PDF page 119) is blank — the verso of the title page.
**Conclusion**: The Grundfläche section begins on the next page (not within this batch). The title page itself is a compositional act: the single word centered on a white ground — the Grundfläche demonstrating itself.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-20]**: GRUNDFLÄCHE als neue Analyseebene / The Basic Plane as new analytical level
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The section title "GRUNDFLÄCHE" appearing alone on its page signals that the analysis of the Basic Plane (Grundfläche) is a distinct and equally fundamental analytical domain as the Punkt section and the Linie section. The Grundfläche is not background or neutral support — it is an active compositional element deserving its own full systematic analysis.
- **Rule**: Treat the Grundfläche as a primary compositional force, not a neutral substrate. Its boundaries, weight-distribution (top/bottom, left/right), and dimensions all have formal values that affect every element placed upon it.
- **Source**: p.107 (section title page)
- **Category**: plane
