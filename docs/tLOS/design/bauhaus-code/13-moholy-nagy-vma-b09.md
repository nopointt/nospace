## IV. Der Raum (Architektur) — Definition des Raumes · Raumerlebnis

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Space (Raum) is not a mystical or metaphysical entity — it is a real, fathomable constituent of human experience, accessible through both body and movement. The route from raw material through surface, volume, and touch leads inevitably to space as the culminating problem of design; and architecture is nothing other than the total organization of space for human life.
**Evidence**: A taxonomic enumeration of all known types of "space" (mathematical, physical, geometric, topological, etc.); physiological and kinesthetic accounts of how the human organism experiences spatial relations; photographs of kinetic sculptural systems, aerial views, race tracks, ship bridges, film sets, and theatre structures; Moholy-Nagy's own 1922 kinetic konstruktives System; student work at the Bauhaus (volume/space study in glass and cardboard, Bauhaus semester 1923 and 1926); and engineering precedents (ocean liners, stairwell shafts, layered traffic infrastructure).
**Conclusion**: Architecture must be approached not as decoration of structure or stylistic dressing of mass, but as the deliberate organization of spatial experience — of movement, tension, relationship, light, and living. All previous training (tactile, material, surface, volume) was preparation for this single end.

## Definition des Raumes

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The word "Raum" is used so carelessly in everyday language that it has lost its capacity to carry a real, graspable meaning. Before architecture can be discussed, the concept must be stripped of its vagueness and given a precise, tangible foundation.
**Evidence**: A boxed typographic table enumerating all known designations of "Raum" across disciplines: mathematical, physical, geometric (Euclidean, non-Euclidean), isotropic, topological, homogeneous, absolute, relative, fictive, abstract, real, imaginary, finite, infinite, borderless, universal, interior, exterior, movement-space, hollow, air-empty, formal, three-dimensional, two-dimensional, one-dimensional; footnote references to Rudolf Carnap's *Der Raum* (1922, Berlin) and *Der logische Aufbau der Welt* (Weltkreisverlag, Berlin-Schlachtensee, 1928).
**Conclusion**: The multiplicity of meanings is itself diagnostic: we lack a single experiential definition. The correct ground for architecture is not any of these abstract variants but the space the living human body inhabits and moves through.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Definition des Raumes / Definition of space
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Space in everyday language is radically underspecified. The proliferation of technical qualifiers — from Euclidean to topological to imaginary — reveals that no consensus definition exists. Architecture cannot build on this fog.
- **Rule**: Before designing space, define which kind of space — at minimum distinguish between abstract mathematical space and the embodied, kinesthetic space of human movement.
- **Source**: p.194 (PDF p.199)
- **Quote (DE)**: "bei der definition des raumes herrscht heute eine große unsicherheit. schon an unserem sprachgebrauch wird diese unsicherheit deutlich, und eben dieser sprachgebrauch vergrößert sie noch."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the definition of space there reigns today a great uncertainty. Our linguistic usage makes this uncertainty visible — and at the same time amplifies it."
- **Category**: space | perception

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Raum ist Realität / Space is reality
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Space has its own reality, governed by its own laws. It is not reducible to the objects within it or to the walls that bound it. It must be grasped and worked as an independent material, like wood or glass.
- **Rule**: Treat space as a primary material with its own properties — not as a residue left over after walls are placed.
- **Source**: p.195 (PDF p.200)
- **Quote (DE)**: "raum ist eine realität, nach eigenen gesetzen faßbar, nach eigenen gesetzen gliederbar, er muß nun in seiner fundamentalen substanz erfaßt werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Space is a reality, comprehensible by its own laws, organizable by its own laws; it must now be grasped in its fundamental substance."
- **Category**: space | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Raum ist Lagebeziehung von Körpern / Space is the positional relationship of bodies
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A space definition derived not from abstract geometry but from the relation of bodies to each other — their relative positions, tensions, distances. From this follows the key formula: Raumgestaltung is the Gestaltung of the positional relationships of bodies (Volumen).
- **Rule**: Design space by governing the relationships between volumes — not by styling surfaces or selecting materials.
- **Source**: p.195 (PDF p.200)
- **Quote (DE)**: "demnach: raumgestaltung ist die gestaltung von lagebeziehungen der körper (volumen)."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Accordingly: spatial design is the design of the positional relationships of bodies (volumes)."
- **Category**: space | volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Das organische Raumerlebnis / The organic spatial experience
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The human being comes to know space primarily through the consciousness of the body's own location — through Tastsinn (tactile sensation), movement, hearing, and the sixth sense of positional awareness. Space is not perceived by the eye alone; it is experienced through the whole organism in motion.
- **Rule**: Design for movement through space, not for the static frontal view. The spatial experience unfolds in time, not in a single glance.
- **Source**: p.195 (PDF p.200)
- **Quote (DE)**: "dem menschen wird der raum — die lagebeziehungen der körper — zuerst mittels seines gesichtssinnes bewußt. dieses erlebnis der sichtbaren lagebeziehungen seines körpers kann durch bewegung: veränderung der eigenen lage — und mittels des tastsinnes parallel erlebt, kontrolliert werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The human being becomes aware of space — the positional relationships of bodies — first through the sense of sight. This experience of the visible positional relationships of his own body can be experienced and controlled in parallel through movement: change of one's own position — and through the sense of touch."
- **Category**: space | tactile | perception

## Raum ist Realität — Das organische Raumerlebnis (continued, p.196)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Space experience is not a luxury of aesthetes — it is the most immediate, biologically prior form of human experience. The architectural profession fails because it does not start from this biological ground.
**Evidence**: Enumeration of all channels through which the human organism perceives space: from Gesichtssinn (sight) registering perspectives, sharp edges, surfaces, light/shadow; from Gehörsinn (hearing) registering acoustic dimensions; from kinesthetic sense registering one's own movement through volume; from Tastsinn. Cross-references to figures 182, 201–208, 186, 201 for Neue Sehen perspectives; references to figures 179, 183 for movement; footnote citing Adolf Behne "das buch der 1000 wunder" (Verlag Hesse & Becker, Leipzig) and various experiments on sensory space.
**Conclusion**: Space experience is biologically primary. Its fuller enumeration — visual, acoustic, kinesthetic, tactile — reveals how narrow conventional architectural training has been. The Bauhaus correction starts from re-educating the whole sensory apparatus.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Raumerlebnis ist biologische Funktion / Spatial experience is biological function
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The capacity to experience space is not an aesthetic achievement — it is a biological given, present in every human being more richly than any current education exploits. Like the perception of colour or tone, it is universal and trainable.
- **Rule**: Ground spatial education in the biological equipment of the organism — all senses, movement, proprioception — before introducing any formal or stylistic categories.
- **Source**: p.196 (PDF p.201)
- **Quote (DE)**: "raumerlebnis ist kein privileg begabter menschen, sondern biologische funktion."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Spatial experience is not the privilege of gifted people, but a biological function."
- **Category**: space | perception | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Primitiv formuliert erfaßt der Mensch auch den Raum / Even in its most primitive formulation, the human being grasps space
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Space perception begins with Gesichtssinn registering perspectives (Abb. 182), sharp faces, edges (Abb. 183), penetrations (Abb. 186–208), proportional relationships, light; from Gehörsinn; from movement through space in various orientations — horizontal, vertical, diagonal, crossing (Abb. 179, 183); from Gleichgewichtssinn (balance sense) registering curves, rotations, spirals (Abb. 135, 136, 199, 201).
- **Rule**: Design for the full register of perceptual channels — not sight alone.
- **Source**: p.196 (PDF p.201)
- **Category**: space | perception

**FIGURE [Fig-02]**: Abb. 175 — Der Eiffelturm (aerial photograph, p.201/PDF p.206)
- **Shows**: An extreme aerial (bird's-eye) photograph of the Eiffel Tower shot from directly above, showing the tower foreshortened to almost a flat iron-cross shape at the center, surrounded by the radial patterns of the Champ de Mars paths, lawns, and the Seine beyond. - **Source**: p.201 (PDF p.206)

## Architektonische Grundfragen

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Architecture has a social and biological responsibility that dwarfs the interests of stylistic discussion or engineering efficiency alone. The living conditions of human beings — their spatial experience — are a life-and-death matter, not a decorative problem.
**Evidence**: The observation that despite the urgency of the housing problem, architects still debate style. The argument that the home is not a box for storage but a living organism; that a future architecture must be conceived as a Gesamtkonzeption addressing biological, functional, and spatial dimensions simultaneously. Footnote references to Adolf Behne and Josef Frank (Josef Frank, "Neues Wohnen," Verlag Klinkhardt, Leipzig) and Moholy's own assertion from the Bauhaus.
**Conclusion**: Architectural education must shift from stylistic historicism (Doric column, Corinthian capital, etc.) to the understanding of space as the primary product. Only then can the housing problem be addressed at its root.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Konstruktion vor Komposition / Construction before composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The root of architecture lies in Raumgestaltung as Konstruktionsproblem — a problem of governing law, not of taste. The "Bildungsweg" of conventional architectural training, obsessed with stylistic markers (Doric, Corinthian, Romanesque, Gothic), reaches only the surface dressing — never the spatial law beneath.
- **Rule**: Begin every architectural project from its spatial governing law (Konstruktionsprinzip), not from its stylistic vocabulary.
- **Source**: p.199 (PDF p.204)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die wurzel der architektur liegt in der beherrschung der raum-problematik, die praxis im konstruktionsproblem."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The root of architecture lies in the mastery of the spatial problem; its practice lies in the problem of construction."
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Erlebnis von Architektur / Experience of architecture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The path to understanding architecture leads through biological experience of space — not through formal analysis of built precedents or stylistic categorization. The "gebildeter" (educated) person typically lacks direct spatial experience and substitutes historical knowledge for it — which is inadequate preparation for design.
- **Rule**: Educate the spatial sense directly — through body, movement, material — before introducing historical precedent.
- **Source**: p.199 (PDF p.204)
- **Quote (DE)**: "der weg zum erlebnis der architektur geht also über ein bestimmtes biologisch-funktionelles erfassungsvermögen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The path to the experience of architecture goes through a determinate biological-functional capacity of apprehension."
- **Category**: space | perception | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Wohnung als lebendiger Raum / The dwelling as living space
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The dwelling is not a container. It must be understood as a living, mobile entity — a spatial organism in which the occupant does not merely reside but actively lives: moves, breathes, regenerates, develops. The future architecture must conceive the dwelling as a Gesamtkonzeption, not as an assemblage of style-marked rooms.
- **Rule**: Design the dwelling as a total spatial organism — conceive Wohnen (dwelling) as a form of life, not as a product of construction industry.
- **Source**: p.198 (PDF p.203)
- **Quote (DE)**: "über die erfüllung leiblicher elementarer bedürfnisse hinaus soll der mensch in seiner wohnung auch die tatsache des raumes erleben können. nicht ein zu-rückweichen vom raum soll die wohnung sein, sondern ein leben im raum."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Beyond the satisfaction of elementary bodily needs, the human being should also be able to experience the fact of space in his dwelling. The dwelling should not be a retreat from space, but a life within space."
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

## Die Grenze zwischen Architektur und Plastik

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Architecture and sculpture (Plastik) are frequently confused in the current discourse, but they can be rigorously distinguished: Plastik is always Volumengestaltung (design of volume from outside), while Architecture is always Raumgestaltung (design of enclosed, inhabited space from within).
**Evidence**: The Eiffelturm (Abb. 175) cited as a boundary case — structurally a Plastik (Volumengestaltung, pierced block) rather than architecture, despite its scale. The argument that geometry alone does not determine the boundary: the distinction lies in whether the spatial interior is designed for human inhabitation and movement.
**Conclusion**: The boundary between Plastik and Architektur is not scale, not material, not structure — it is the presence or absence of enclosed, organized space for human experience. Raumgestaltung is the exclusive province of architecture.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Plastik ist Volumengestaltung, Architektur ist Raumgestaltung
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Sculpture (Plastik) always remains at the level of volume — it organizes mass seen from outside. Architecture goes further: it creates an interior space designed for human movement and inhabitation. This distinction is fundamental and must not be blurred.
- **Rule**: Distinguish at every stage between designing a volume (Plastik) and designing a space (Architektur): only the latter is the task of architecture.
- **Source**: p.200 (PDF p.205)
- **Quote (DE)**: "plastik ist immer gestaltung von volumen. (selbst bei dem virtuellen volumen der kinetischen plastik.) (abb. 176–178)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Sculpture is always the design of volume. (Even in the virtual volume of kinetic sculpture.) (Abb. 176–178)"
- **Category**: volume | space

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-03]**: Abb. 176 — Charlotte Victoria, Bauhaus erstes Semester 1923 — Volumen- und Raumstudie (Glas und Kaliko) (p.202/PDF p.207)
- **Shows**: A student Bauhaus exercise — a three-dimensional study constructed from glass and calico (lightweight fabric), photographed in Weimar. The structure consists of transparent glass planes and opaque calico strips arranged to articulate both solid volumes and enclosed voids. Several horizontal glass shelves are held by vertical calico connectors; the whole reads simultaneously as volume and proto-space.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The pedagogical transition from Volumengestaltung (Plastik) toward Raumgestaltung — the student work enacts the boundary between the two. The transparent glass makes visible the interior spatial relationships rather than presenting an opaque mass. Supports P-10.
- **Text reference**: Caption only — "charlotte victoria (bauhaus, erstes semester 1923) volumen- und raumstudie (glas und kaliko)." Referenced in the running text as an example of kinetic plastic expression: "die tatsache einer kinetischen plastischen ausdrucksform führt zu der anerkennung eines raumzustandes, der nicht das ergebnis der lagebeziehungen von starren volumen ist, sondern von sichtbaren und unsichtbaren wirksamkeiten der bewegungstatsachen und bewegungsformationen, unter umständen also aus beziehungen von kraftfeldern besteht."
- **Source**: p.202 (PDF p.207)

## Eine Analogie

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The kinetic plastic form is not merely an art-world curiosity — it is an analogy for the spatial organization principles of modern life: force-fields, movement trajectories, dynamic equilibrium rather than static mass.
**Evidence**: The kinetic student work (Abb. 176) cited; the argument that modern scientific understanding of "stoff gleich kraft" (matter equals force) — a reference to field-physics and the equivalence of mass and energy — has already transformed the physical understanding of spatial bodies. The architectural profession has not yet absorbed this shift.
**Conclusion**: The future Raumgestaltung will not be organized around static, hierarchical walls and rooms but around dynamic relationships of forces — exactly as kinetic sculpture already demonstrates at a small scale. The architect must learn to think in force-fields, not in load-bearing masses.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Kinetik statt Statik / Kinetics instead of statics
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Architecture's obsession with static, closed, hierarchically arranged volumes is a historical holdover. The scientific understanding that matter and force are equivalent ("stoff gleich kraft") implies that spatial relationships are dynamic and relational, not fixed and monumental. The move from Statik to Kinetik in spatial thinking corresponds to the move from dead mass to living organization.
- **Rule**: Design spatial relationships as dynamic equilibria — not as static compositions of inert volumes.
- **Source**: p.203 (PDF p.208)
- **Quote (DE)**: "diese auslegung von 'lagebeziehungen der körper' (gleich raum) für die schaffung neuer raumrelationen ist durch die heutigen wissenschaftlichen erkenntnisse wie 'stoff gleich kraft' durchaus gegeben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This interpretation of 'positional relationships of bodies' (equals space) for the creation of new spatial relations is fully warranted by today's scientific knowledge such as 'matter equals force.'"
- **Category**: space | perception

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-05]**: Abb. 178 — Moholy-Nagy 1922, Kinetisches konstruktives System — Bau mit Bewegungsbahnen für Spiel und Beförderung (durchkonstruiert von Dipl.-Ing. Stefan Sebök 1928) (p.204/PDF p.209)
- **Shows**: A large architectural-scale perspectival drawing or reconstruction of Moholy-Nagy's 1922 kinetic constructive system. The structure is a double-helix or spiral tower form — two interlocking spiraling ramps wound around a central vertical axis, with human figures shown at various points on the ramps indicating human scale. The outer form is an elongated ovoid/torpedo shape; the inner structure shows interpenetrating rings and diagonal movement paths. The drawing is technically executed (Dipl.-Ing. Sebök, 1928).
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The architectural-scale application of kinetic spatial thinking — a building organized entirely around movement trajectories rather than static rooms. The structure is both the Volumen (the spiral form) and the Raumgestaltung (the organized movement paths within). Directly illustrates P-11 and the section "statt statik: kinetik."
- **Text reference**: Referenced in running text on p.203–205 as illustration of the kinetic approach to spatial organization; footnote: "siehe auch den entwurf der mechanischen exzentrik im heft 4 der bauhausbücher."
- **Source**: p.204 (PDF p.209)

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: Abb. 178a — Moholy-Nagy 1922 — Die erste Fassung des kinetischen Systems (detail drawing, p.205/PDF p.210)
- **Shows**: A detail/section drawing of the same 1922 kinetic system, showing the circulation logic more clearly. An outer bahn (path) with spiral ascent for the Publikum (audience/users); a horizontal Ringplattform serving as an intermediate level; an inner spiral connected to a Fahrstuhlschacht (elevator shaft) with a smooth upper surface. - **Text reference**: "der bau enthält eine äußere bahn mit spiraler steigung zur beförderung des publikums, daher mit geländer; statt stufen vollrampe. abschluß oben und zugleich verbindung zur äußeren spiralrampe: eine horizontale halbringplattform, der ein fahrstuhlschacht eingegliedert ist."

## Statt Statik: Kinetik

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Spatial design today needs to move from the ordering principle of static, closed, hierarchical form to the ordering principle of kinetic, dynamic, multi-directional spatial relationships. The materials and science of the age already demand this shift.
**Evidence**: The student work in Bauhaus semesters 1923–1926 (Abb. 176–177); the kinetic system (Abb. 178–178a); reference to "utopian Vorstellungswelt" (abb. 178) representing available Baumaterialien; reference to abb. 179, 180 as examples from other fields. The central argument: earlier architects built sichtbare, meßbare, wohlproportionierte, geschlossene Körper (visible, measurable, well-proportioned, closed bodies) — today the goal is the ein- und ausströmen (flowing in and out) of spatial relations in simultaneous multi-directional movement, expressing force-relationships latent in materials.
**Conclusion**: The architectural design task is to master the dynamic Bewegungsbeziehungen (movement relationships) of space — in opposition to the static, hierarchical fixations of all earlier periods.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Bewegungsbeziehungen als Grundlage / Movement relationships as foundation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Earlier architecture built closed, measured, proportioned bodies and called it space. The correct object of architectural design is not the closed body but the dynamic field of spatial relationships — the invisible forces, the trajectories of movement, the tensions between volumes. These are what must be organized.
- **Rule**: Organize movement paths first; let the enclosing elements be derived from the movement diagram.
- **Source**: p.203 (PDF p.208)
- **Quote (DE)**: "früher schuf man aus sichtbaren, meßbaren, wohlproportionierten baumassen geschlossene körper, die man raumgestaltung nannte; heutige raumerlebnisse beruhen auf dem ein- und ausströmen räumlicher beziehungen in gleichzeitiger durchdringung von innen und außen, oben und unten, auf der oft unsichtbaren auswirkung von kräfteverhältnissen, die in den materialien gegeben sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Earlier one created from visible, measurable, well-proportioned building masses closed bodies which one called spatial design; today's spatial experiences rest on the flowing in and out of spatial relationships in simultaneous penetration from inside and outside, above and below, on the often invisible effects of force-relations that are given in the materials."
- **Category**: space | material

- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Spatial relationships "größtenteils durch gespannte Drähte" — the space is defined not by solid enclosure but by the field of tensions between two bodies. ## Raumgestaltung ist nicht in erster Linie eine Frage des Baumaterials

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: It is a common misconception that spatial design is primarily a problem of building materials — of combining stone, steel, glass, concrete. In fact, spatial design operates at a level prior to material: it is the organization of invisible, immeasurable movement-relationships and force-fields latent in spatial configurations.
**Evidence**: The argument that today's spatial organization is defined not by assembling single cells into volumes or by lining up volumes in sequence, but by invisible — though measurable through body — Bewegungsbeziehungen across all dimensions simultaneously. Material is the medium, not the subject.
**Conclusion**: Raumgestaltung is a question of Knotenpunkt-Gestaltung (node-design) — the points where spatial flows converge, separate, and interact. Baumaterial is the instrument through which this is realized, not the object of the design itself.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Raumgestaltung als unsichtbare Kraftfeldorganisation / Spatial design as organization of invisible force-fields
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The governing structure of a space is not its walls, ceilings, or columns — these are material instruments. The governing structure is the system of Bewegungsbeziehungen in all dimensional directions simultaneously: up-down, in-out, left-right, diagonal. These cannot be seen statically; they are grasped only through movement.
- **Rule**: Before specifying any material, map the Bewegungsbeziehungen of the space — the paths, flows, convergences, and tensions. Let the material decisions follow.
- **Source**: p.211 (PDF p.216)
- **Quote (DE)**: "raumgestaltung ist heute vielmehr ein verwobenes sein von raumteilen, die meist in unsichtbaren, aber deutlich spürbaren bewegungsbeziehungen aller dimensionsrichtungen und in fluktuierenden kräfteverhältnissen verankert sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Spatial design today is rather an interwoven being of spatial parts, mostly anchored in invisible but clearly perceptible movement-relationships of all dimensional directions and in fluctuating force-relations."
- **Category**: space | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Stufenweise Entfaltung / Step-by-step unfolding
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The mastery of spatial design cannot be leaped to directly. The human organism must unfold its spatial faculties gradually — in the early stages only very limited capabilities succeed. The general problem is visible only at the later stages — after step-by-step deployment. The Bauhaus pedagogical sequence (material → surface → volume → space) is not arbitrary but biologically grounded in this stufenweise Entfaltung.
- **Rule**: Teach spatial design in strict developmental sequence — do not introduce architectural space problems before the student has fully internalized material, surface, and volume.
- **Source**: p.211 (PDF p.216)
- **Quote (DE)**: "in den ersten versuchen zur gestaltung des raumes ist es natürlich nur in sehr beschränktem maße gelungen, das spezifische mittel der raumgestaltung, den raum selbst, zu beherrschen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the first attempts at spatial design it has naturally succeeded only to a very limited degree to master the specific means of spatial design — space itself."
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Historische Folge / The historical sequence
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The historical sequence of architectural development is not a sequence of styles but a sequence of spatial conceptions, each more liberated from static mass than the last. The task is to identify where in this sequence the contemporary moment stands — and push forward.
- **Rule**: Read architectural history as a sequence of spatial discoveries, not as a catalogue of styles to quote.
- **Source**: p.211 (PDF p.216)
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

## Raumgestaltung auf der Bühne und im Film

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Theatre and film are the closest contemporary approximations to an architecture of free spatial design — but both remain incomplete: theatre is not yet liberated from illusionistic representation, and film is still too far from a non-illusionistic, self-organized spatial solution.
**Evidence**: Abb. 187 (Filmbau — film studio interior photographed from above, showing scaffolding, crew, and set construction) and Abb. 188 (eine Filmtreppe — a film staircase construction, photographed from outside during construction, showing the scaffolding, plaster surfaces, and timber framework that constitute the temporary architectural space of a film set).
**Conclusion**: Film and theatre provide raumgestaltung als Ausdrucksform of the human organism — expressions of inner drive. The freedom achieved there (constructing spatial sequences not constrained by permanent structure) should transfer to permanent architecture. But the current practice remains "vorläufig ziemlich entfernt von einer nichtillusionistischen, selbstgestalterischen Lösung."

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Film und Theater als Laboratorien der freien Raumgestaltung / Film and theatre as laboratories of free spatial design
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Raumgestaltung as an expressive form of the human organism finds more freedom in theatre and film than in permanent architecture — because the temporary, constructive nature of stage and film set liberated designers from the constraints of permanent structure. This freedom is a model for architecture to learn from.
- **Rule**: Use theatrical and cinematic spatial construction as research laboratories for architectural spatial ideas — test free spatial sequences before committing to permanent structure.
- **Source**: p.215 (PDF p.220)
- **Quote (DE)**: "raumgestaltung als ausdrucksform des menschen, aus innerem trieb — wie malerei, plastik, musik, dichtung — gibt es nach landläufigen begriffen nicht. doch wäre erst eine solche konzeption die richtige umkreisung des räumlichen gestaltungsproblems."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Spatial design as an expressive form of the human being, from inner drive — like painting, sculpture, music, poetry — does not exist according to common understanding. Yet only such a conception would be the correct circumscription of the spatial design problem."
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Befreiung von zweckverkoppelter Architektur / Liberation from purpose-coupled architecture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The deepest aspiration of spatial design is to find the spatial language that corresponds not to programme alone but to the full biological energies of the human being — those oriented toward light, air, and freeing breadth. Purpose-coupled architecture (zweckverkoppelte Architektur) can never fully address this. The liberation of spatial energies is ultimately the liberation of the human being in the everyday built world.
- **Rule**: Design beyond programme — understand the biological spatial drives (toward light, movement, openness) as co-equal design inputs with functional requirements.
- **Source**: p.216 (PDF p.221)
- **Quote (DE)**: "aber über diese möglichkeiten hinaus ist eine eingehende beschäftigung mit diesem vorstellungskreis wichtig, weil sich hier neue sfären der befreiung für den heutigen menschen auftun und von der befreiung dieser menschlichen energien her auch der zweckverkoppelten architektur hilfe kommen kann; d. h. letzten endes dem menschen, der in seinem alltag dieser zweckverkoppelten architektur eingeordnet ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "But beyond these possibilities an intensive engagement with this sphere of ideas is important, because here new spheres of liberation open up for the contemporary human being — and through the liberation of these human energies help can also come to purpose-coupled architecture; that is, ultimately to the human being who in his everyday life is subordinated to this purpose-coupled architecture."
- **Category**: space | pedagogy

### Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-12]**: Abb. 184 — Badeanstalt Halensee (Berlin) (p.212/PDF p.217)
- **Shows**: An aerial photograph (Foto: Lufthansa) of the Halensee open-air bathing establishment in Berlin. The complex is photographed from directly above, showing the outdoor pools, bathing platforms, diving structures, lawns, and access paths as a flat geometric plan. Human figures are visible as tiny white and dark dots at the water's edge and on the lawns. The figure/ground of water (dark) and land (light) creates a strong abstract pattern.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Used to illustrate the developmental sequence from closed plastic form (Hohlkörper) to open spatial form. The caption text provides a six-point typology of spatial liberation stages: (1) einzellig, geschlossen (Hohlkörper), Zelt; (2) einzellig, eine Seite offen; (3) mehrzellig, geschlossen, durch ungebrochene Wände verbunden; (4) geometrische Formung der Zellendurchdringung (Wright, Abb. 184); (5) offen, fluktuierend, in der Horizontale hin (geöffnet); (6) dasselbe auch nach der Vertikale hin (geöffnet); Typ: Schiffsbrücken. The bathing establishment is cited as an example of type (4) — geometric cellular interpenetration. Supports P-14.
- **Text reference**: "dazu genügt die fixierung, daß die entwicklung vom geschlossenen zum offenen raum lief: reiche plastische außengliederung, zur faßbaren machung der beziehungen trotz starrer körperhaftigkeit" with the six-point list.
- **Source**: p.212 (PDF p.217)

**FIGURE [Fig-13]**: Abb. 185 — Kommandobrücke eines Ozeandampfers (p.213/PDF p.218)

The bridge deck is shown from the side — a horizontal, multi-level open structure with railings, instrument housings, and cantilevered decks. The structure reads as pure functional horizontal spatial extension: no walls, no enclosure, only structured open decks projecting over the ship's hull.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: "Die seit den neunziger Jahren gebauten überseeeschiffe sind die vorläufer der heutigen Architektur. Die Notwendigkeit mit geringstem Gewicht größten Rauminhalt und vollkommene Stabilität zu erzielen, zwang den Schiffbauingenieur wie den heutigen Architekten der Funktionsbegriff — zu ähnlichen Lösungen." The ocean liner bridge as precedent for functional, open-space architecture — form follows the spatial and functional requirement without stylistic overlay. Supports P-07.
- **Text reference**: Direct caption: "die seit den neunziger jahren gebauten überseeeschiffe sind die vorläufer der heutigen architektur."
- **Source**: p.213 (PDF p.218)

**FIGURE [Fig-14]**: Abb. 186 — Treppenschacht des Maschinenraumes auf dem Hapagdampfer "Albert Ballin" (von unten hinausfotografiert) (p.214/PDF p.219)
- **Shows**: A photograph (Foto: uhu) taken from directly below looking upward through the stairwell shaft of the engine room of the HAPAG steamship "Albert Ballin." Caption: "alle gänge des treppenschachtes, der zum maschinenraum hinabführt, liegen auf rosten, damit die heiße luft aus den kesseln ungehindert abziehen kann. die erfüllung der funktion schafft gleichzeitig harmonische form." Directly illustrates the convergence of functional necessity and spatial beauty. Supports P-07.
- **Text reference**: "alle gänge des treppenschachtes, der zum maschinenraum hinabführt, liegen auf rosten, damit die heiße luft aus den kesseln ungehindert abziehen kann. die erfüllung der funktion schafft gleichzeitig harmonische form."

- **Text reference**: "film und teater ermöglichen freie raumgestaltung. doch wir sind in der praxis vorläufig ziemlich entfernt von einer nichtillusionistischen, selbstgestalterischen lösung."
