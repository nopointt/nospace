## EINFÜHRUNG (Introduction) — pp. 5–8

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Photography and film are not merely tools for recording the world; they are instruments that have placed entirely new means of optical Gestaltung (visual shaping/design) in our hands — means that are only now beginning to be understood and exploited.
**Evidence**: The photographic apparatus offers surprising capabilities that are only beginning to be evaluated. The camera's lens is already not bound to the narrow limits of our eye; no manual design tool (pencil, brush) can produce comparably seen sections of the world; nor can manual tools fix a movement at its core. The distortion possibilities of the lens — Untersicht (worm's-eye), Obersicht (bird's-eye), Schrägansicht (oblique view) — are not simply negative; they generate an optics freed from the associative laws that bind the human eye. And at a single point: the fineness of gray gradations produces a sublimated value whose differentiation, outside of one's own sphere of effect, can benefit the formal design itself.
**Conclusion**: Although photography has existed for a hundred years, the developmental process has only in recent years permitted recognition — beyond the specific — of the design consequences. Only recently has our seeing ripened for grasping these connections.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Fotografie als Gestaltungsmittel / Photography as a design instrument
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photographic apparatus delivers surprising possibilities whose evaluation we are only beginning to undertake. The expansion of the field of vision is no longer bound to the narrow limits of our eye.
- **Rule**: Treat photography not as a recording device but as a design instrument with autonomous optical capabilities unavailable to the hand.
- **Source**: p. 5
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der fotografische Apparat hat uns überraschende Möglichkeiten geliefert, mit deren Auswertung wir erst beginnen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The photographic apparatus has delivered surprising possibilities to us, with the evaluation of which we are only beginning."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Neue Sehen — the camera frees vision from biological constraint
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The camera's Untersicht, Obersicht, Schrägansicht are by no means only negatively to be valued; they do not simply deform, rather they give an optics freed from the associative laws that bind our eyes. The fineness of the gray gradations produces a sublimated value whose differentiation comes outside the author's own sphere of effectiveness.
- **Rule**: Use camera angles and lens distortions not to correct them toward the "normal" view but to exploit their capacity to produce vision the unaided eye cannot perform.
- **Source**: p. 5
- **Quote (DE)**: "die Verzerrungsmöglichkeiten des Objektivs — Untersicht, Obersicht, Schrägansicht — sind keineswegs nur negativ zu werten, sondern geben eine von unserem Auge nicht leistbare unvoreingenommene Optik"
- **Quote (EN)**: "the distortion possibilities of the lens — worm's-eye, bird's-eye, oblique — are by no means only negatively to be valued; rather they give an unprejudiced optics not achievable by our eye"
- **Category**: neue-sehen

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Zeitgemäße Mittel / Means appropriate to the time
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: One need not pass judgment on the various kinds of painterly design; but a classification of currently existing and possible optical designs in the sense of the use of means is appropriate. It seems indispensable to participate in the design of one's own time with zeitgemäßen (time-appropriate) means.
- **Rule**: Every medium must be used for what only it can do in its own time; do not use the camera to do what the brush has already mastered.
- **Source**: p. 8 (continuation from p. 8 — full sentence: "Immerhin erscheint es mir unumgänglich, an der Gestaltung der **eigenen Zeit** mit **zeitgemäßen** Mitteln mitzuarbeiten.")

- **Quote (EN)**: "Nevertheless it seems to me indispensable to participate in the design of one's own time with time-appropriate means."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

## II. — Die Spaltung: Fotografie und Malerei (The Split: Photography and Painting) — p. 6

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: It is first necessary to clarify and demonstrate the relationship of photography to painting — specifically, that the development of photographic technique has contributed substantially to the emergence of new forms in the optical design (optischen Gestaltung) and has split the hitherto undivided territory of optical expression.
**Evidence**: Before the invention of photography, painting had united in itself the tasks of Darstellung (representation/depiction) and farbigen Ausdrucks (color expression). Since the split, painting encompasses two territories: (1) GESTALTUNG DER FARBE — the pure relations of colors and lightness-values with one another, analogous in music to the design of acoustic relations; this is the design of the generally valid, of laws rooted biologically, independent of climate, race, temperament, education; (2) GESTALTUNG DER DARSTELLUNG — relations of external, imitative, representational content with associative meanings, analogous in music to language alongside acoustic design.
**Conclusion**: The split does not destroy what human intelligence has previously achieved; on the contrary: the pure forms of expression crystallize and are brought to a more penetrating effect in their own law-nature (Eigengesetzlichkeit).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Gestaltung der Farbe vs. Gestaltung der Darstellung — the fundamental split
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Since the invention of photography, painting has been split into two distinct territories. GESTALTUNG DER FARBE covers the pure relationships of colors and lightness-values among themselves — biologically rooted, independent of climate, race, temperament, education — analogous to the design of acoustic relationships in music. GESTALTUNG DER DARSTELLUNG covers the relationships of external, object-derived, imitative content with associative meanings — the same kinds of relationships that, in acoustic design, language has alongside music.
- **Rule**: Distinguish at all times whether a visual work is operating in the territory of pure color-form relations (biologically grounded, non-representational) or in the territory of representational/associative content; these are different instruments requiring different criteria.
- **Source**: p. 6
- **Quote (DE)**: "GESTALTUNG DER FARBE: Die reinen Beziehungen der Farben und Helligkeitswerte untereinander, wie wir ähnliches in der Musik als Gestaltung der akustischen Beziehungen kennen; das ist die Gestaltung der allgemeingültigen, von Klima, Rasse, Temperament, Bildung unabhängigen, im biologischen wurzelnden Gesetzmäßigkeiten; GESTALTUNG DER DARSTELLUNG: Beziehungen des von außen nachbildend Hergeleiteten, Gegenständlichen mit assoziativen Inhalten"
- **Quote (EN)**: "DESIGN OF COLOR: the pure relationships of colors and lightness-values among themselves, as we know analogously in music as the design of acoustic relationships; this is the design of the generally valid, biologically rooted laws independent of climate, race, temperament, education; DESIGN OF REPRESENTATION: relationships of the externally derived, imitative, object-based content with associative meanings"
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Die Spaltung macht beide Gebiete reiner / The split purifies both territories
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The split does not destroy what human intelligence has previously achieved. On the contrary: the pure forms of expression crystallize and are brought to more penetrating effect in their Eigengesetzlichkeit (own law-nature).
- **Rule**: Recognize that when photography takes over representation, painting is freed — not impoverished; use each medium for its own law-nature, not for tasks the other can perform better.
- **Source**: p. 7
- **Quote (DE)**: "Durch diese Spaltung wird das von menschlichem Geiste bisher Errungene nicht zerstört; im Gegenteil: die reinen Formen des Ausdrucks werden kristallisiert und in ihrer Eigengesetzlichkeit zu durchschlagenderer Wirkung gebracht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Through this split, what has previously been achieved by the human spirit is not destroyed; on the contrary: the pure forms of expression crystallize and are brought to more penetrating effect in their own law-nature."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

## III. — Die zeitgemäßen Konsequenzen (The Contemporary Consequences) — p. 7

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Three consequences follow from the Fotografie/Malerei split for contemporary practice.
**Evidence**:
1. In photography and film we now possess a mechanically-exact instrument that functions incomparably better as a means of Darstellung than the manual procedures of hitherto-known painterly practice. Painting can henceforth attend exclusively to the reinen Gestaltung der Farbe.
2. The pure design of color demonstrates that the "theme" of color design (painting) is color itself — that one can, without representational references, achieve a purely constructed, primary expression.
3. The newly invented optical and technical instruments give valuable stimuli to the optical designer; among other things, alongside the pigment image they create the light-image (Lichtbild), alongside the static image the kinetic (das kinetische). Next to the panel painting (Tafelbild) — films in all dimensions; naturally also outside the Filmtheaters.
**Conclusion**: It is necessary to classify the currently existing and possible optical designs in the sense of the use of means. Do not make value judgments about different kinds of painterly design — but clarify the means.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Fotografie übernimmt Darstellung / Photography takes over representation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In photography and film we possess a mechanically exact instrument that functions as a means of Darstellung incomparably better than the manual procedures of hitherto-known painterly practice. Painting can from now on attend exclusively to the reinen Gestaltung der Farbe.
- **Rule**: Do not use photography to do what painting does better (pure color design); do not use painting to do what photography does better (accurate representation). Each medium to its own law.
- **Source**: p. 7
- **Quote (DE)**: "Wir besitzen in dem mechanisch-exakten Verfahren der Fotografie und des Films ein unvergleichlich besser funktionierendes Ausdrucksmittel für die Darstellung als in dem manuellen Verfahren der bisher bekannten malerischen Malerei. Die Malerei kann sich von nun an mit der reinen Gestaltung der Farbe befassen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the mechanically exact procedure of photography and film we possess an incomparably better functioning means of expression for Darstellung than in the manual procedures of hitherto known painterly painting. Painting can from now on attend to the pure design of color."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Das Lichtbild und das kinetische Bild als neue Gestaltungsgebiete
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The newly invented optical and technical instruments create, alongside the pigment image, the Lichtbild (light-image); alongside the static image, the kinetic image. This produces new territories of design with their own law-natures.
- **Rule**: Treat the Lichtbild and the kinetic image not as extensions of painting but as autonomous design territories governed by the properties of light and motion.
- **Source**: p. 7
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die neu erfundenen optischen und technischen Instrumente geben dem optischen Gestalter wertvolle Anregungen; unter anderem schaffen sie neben dem Pigmentbild das Lichtbild, neben dem statischen Bild das kinetische."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The newly invented optical and technical instruments give the optical designer valuable stimuli; among other things they create alongside the pigment image the light-image, alongside the static image the kinetic."
- **Category**: light

## ÜBER DAS GEGENSTÄNDLICHE UND GEGENSTANDSLOSE
### (On the Representational and the Non-Representational) — pp. 11–13

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The biological functions of color and their psychophysical effectiveness are barely investigated. One thing is certain: to perceive color, to work with color is for people an elementary, biologically conditioned necessity. The absolute Malerei (absolute painting) draws on the elementary tension relations of colors, forms, directions, positions — biologically rooted tension-and-relationship values that are common to all people via the physiological apparatus.
**Evidence**: Color-form relationships — complementary colors, centric/eccentric, centrifugal/centripetal ordering possibilities of colors, lightness/darkness values, warm/cold, near/far movement of colors, light/heavy — all rooted in biological necessity. These relationships are in fact at all times the actual content of farbiger Gestaltung (color design). Paintings of every period must have been designed from these original tension relationships. Formal differences in painting of different periods can not be reduced to temporal changes of form alone; rather an image could be read upside down, still providing sufficient basis for assessing its painterly worth.
**Evidence (continued)**: Human beings have always tried to express problems unresolvable internally — to depict, represent their environment and the ideas of their spirit. This produced the great drive to capture natural appearances and fix the imagined. The associative tension values and the elementary color-design values that came into being on other paths (clothing, household goods, etc.) were here captured with intensity and organized into Darstellungsergebnisse (depiction-results). This enormously amplified the imitative intentions, which now touched the elementary biological basis.
**Evidence (continued, p. 13)**: A progressive and progressing development of photography and film will soon make clear that these techniques enable a completely perfected Durchführung (execution) of Darstellung — incomparably better than manual painterly practice has been able to achieve. If clarity about this had penetrated through to the current era, the time-rooted intuitive recognition next to other factors (footnote) had given "gegenstandslose," "abstract" painting an enormous boost.
**Conclusion**: The non-representational painter needs no special courage to turn to the design tasks that photography and film offer; the entire human interest in coming to know the world has, so to speak, been switched into this: to participate in the service of Darstellung at every moment and in every situation. Since we cannot speak against a representational art, we must instead demand that time-appropriate consequences be drawn from it: the world-interest and the world-sensibility corresponding to our time must be drawn out. Design methods that only breathe past times and past ideologies must be set alongside the mechanical Darstellungsverfahren with its still unsurpassable Erweiterungsmöglichkeiten (expansion possibilities). In such a situation, the discussion over representational or non-representational painting will no longer be of importance; one must treat the whole problematic in absolute (not "either/or" but "and") terms: darstellerische und optische Gestaltung.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Absolute Malerei — biologically rooted color design
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The biological functions of color and their psychophysical effectiveness are barely investigated. But one thing is certain: to take up color, to work with color is for people an elementary, biologically conditioned necessity. The concept of "absolute painting" draws on the elementary tension-relationship values of colors, forms, positions, directions — tension values rooted biologically that are common to all people through the physiological apparatus. These relationships are in fact at all times the actual content of farbiger Gestaltung.
- **Rule**: Ground color design in biologically universal tension relationships (complementary, centric/eccentric, warm/cold, light/heavy, near/far) rather than in imitative or associative content — these are the irreducible tools of painting after the photographic split.
- **Source**: p. 11
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die biologischen Funktionen der Farbe, ihre psychofysische Wirksamkeit sind noch kaum untersucht. Eins ist aber sicher: Farbe aufzunehmen, Farbe zu erarbeiten ist für den Menschen eine elementare, biologische Notwendigkeit."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The biological functions of color, their psychophysical effectiveness, have barely been investigated. But one thing is certain: to take up color, to work with it, is for people an elementary, biological necessity."

## TAFELBILD, ARCHITEKTUR UND "GESAMTKUNSTWERK" — pp. 14–17

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The role of the Tafelbild (panel painting) and its relationship to architecture and the Gesamtkunstwerk must be clarified. As long as humans demand optical experiences as life-sustaining foundations, farbige Gestaltung (color design) of farbiger Harmonien (color harmonies) cannot be eliminated on biological grounds.
**Evidence (historical)**: Not long ago, the view was that the Tafelbild was destined for decline — that it was surrendered to the anarchy of subjective seeing. The view was that collective thinking and acting demands objective design laws. In the demand after collective design, with the specific application that the painter's only task is to support the architects' color intentions for buildings.
**Evidence (Gesamtkunstwerk critique)**: This demand, in contrast to the isolated l'art pour l'art painting, was a reaction-formation quickly replaced by a more general and more convincing conviction: that no material — no purely artistic territory — can be judged by the standards of other materials or territories; that painting, or optical design in general, has its own special laws and tasks above all others.
**Evidence (Lebensauffassung critique)**: The Lebensauffassung (life-philosophy) of the previous generation said: The human has his Alltagsleben (daily life) and his Mußestunden (leisure hours); in leisure he occupies himself with the phenomena of "artistic" design, with "through-spiritualized" works. This view led over time to unbearable conditions. Against this came the Kubismus, Konstruktivismus, which tried to purify the elements and means of expression themselves — without the Absicht ("Kunst" to make) — but strove for Ausdruck (expression). The second way was to bring the isolated design territories together into an Einheit (unity) — the Gesamtkunstwerk: architecture as the sum of all arts (Stijl-Gruppe, Holland; early Bauhaus period). The idea was easily comprehensible and the Gesamtkunstwerk, through specializations that had cut all territories into pieces, had destroyed belief in the possibility of experiencing Totalität des Lebenserfahrens (the totality of life-experience). Since the Gesamtkunstwerk is only an addition (Addierung), even if organized, it cannot satisfy us today.
**Evidence (Synthesis demand, pp. 15–16)**: What we need is not the "Gesamtkunstwerk" (Leben) as an additive but a self-constructing Synthese aller Lebensmomente (synthesis of all life-moments) — a comprehensive Gesamtwerk that brings every Isolierung to an end; in which all individual Leistungen arise from biological necessity and flow into a universal Notwendigkeit (necessity). The main task of the next period must be: to design each Work according to its own law-nature and its own particularity. Not artificially blending the boundaries of designs but allowing a design to emerge from its fully effective forces.
**Evidence (Architecture and the painter, pp. 16–17)**: On the basis of a design theory that only proclaims what a work designates from its own means and own forces — the next consequence would be an architectural design as synthesis of the functional elements of architecture — Material-Farbe (material-color) — and so an architectonic Zusammenspiel (interplay) of equally ordered forces. Such a design must enable the painter to make visible in today's sense the Farbigkeit (colorfulness) of spaces and building complexes with the participation of painters. He will work in the domain of Farbdetermination arising from baulichen (constructive) conditions. From a sovereign farbiger design it can therefore only be a matter of Außenarchitektur and Innenarchitektur (interior architecture) as support of the atmospheric "wohnlichen" (habitable) effect. About the Tafelbild: it is to be countered that, contrary to the view that it represents a decline from fresco painting, codex and bible illustrations, panel paintings, landscapes, portraits — seen from the vantage point of Darstellung — have been revolutionary acts. Since the invention of perspective, the Tafelbild has completely neglected color as such and turned itself entirely to the service of Darstellung. This reached its peak of Darstellung in photography and also its low point of farbiger Gestaltung. Only the Impressionists returned color to the Kulturmensch (cultivated person). This development of the Tafelbild has led to a clear separation between farbiger and darstellerischer Gestaltung.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Das Gesamtkunstwerk ist eine Addierung, keine Synthese / The Gesamtkunstwerk is an addition, not a synthesis
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Gesamtkunstwerk is only an Addierung (addition), even if organized; it cannot satisfy us today. What we need is not the "Gesamtkunstwerk" as additive synthesis but a self-constructing Synthese aller Lebensmomente in all-encompassing Gesamtwerk that ends all Isolierung — from biological necessity, flowing into universal Notwendigkeit.
- **Rule**: Do not design by assembling separate arts into a "total work"; design by allowing each moment of life and each material's own law-nature to construct a synthesis from within — the synthesis must be organic, not additive.
- **Source**: p. 15
- **Quote (DE)**: "Da das Gesamtkunstwerk aber nur eine Addierung ist, auch wenn eine organisierte ist, können wir uns heute damit nicht begnügen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Since the Gesamtkunstwerk is only an addition, even if an organized one, it cannot satisfy us today."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Konstruktivismus vs. l'art pour l'art — Reinigung der Mittel
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Kubismus, Konstruktivismus tried to purify the elements and means of expression themselves without the Absicht to "make Kunst" — to want to create "Kunst" means, d.h., when it is its Optimum, it is to be in its Höchstintensität biologically simple, self-evident, unambiguous, pure. The second way was to unify isolated design territories into a Einheit — the Gesamtkunstwerk.
- **Rule**: Purify the means of design from the intention to "make art" — the purest design arises from biological necessity, not from artistic ambition; aim for Optimum: biologically simple, self-evident, unambiguous, pure.
- **Source**: p. 15
- **Quote (DE)**: "die Elemente und Mittel des Ausdrucks selbst zu läutern, ohne die Absicht, damit 'Kunst' schaffen zu wollen. 'Kunst' entsteht, wenn der Ausdruck im Optimum ist, d. h. wenn er in seiner Höchstintensität im Biologischen wurzelnd, zielbewußt, eindeutig, rein ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "to purify the elements and means of expression themselves without the intention of wanting to 'make art.' 'Art' arises when expression is at the Optimum, i.e., when it in its highest intensity, rooted in the Biological, is purposeful, unambiguous, pure."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Die Einheit durch vollkommen auswirkende Kräfte / Unity through fully effective forces
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Einheit (unity) must rather come about through design emerging from its fully effective forces. Not artificially blending the boundaries of designs: a Gestaltung that is completed and brought forward by people whose entire life-attitude gives every individual the possibility of their highest productivity, allowing them to express themselves most personally. By this the human learns again to react with the slightest stirrings of their being to the laws of the material.
- **Rule**: Design unity does not come from assembling parts but from allowing each element to be fully what its material-law demands; personal expression at highest productivity emerges when the designer is not constrained by external goals.
- **Source**: p. 16
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Einheit müßte vielmehr dadurch geschaffen werden, daß eine jede Gestaltung aus ihrer sich vollkommen auswirkenden Tendenz und Eignung heraus aufgefaßt und durchgeführt wird."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Unity must rather be achieved by grasping and carrying through each design from its fully effective tendency and aptitude."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Tafelbild und Fotografie — Darstellung reaches its peak and its low point simultaneously
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Since the invention of perspective, the Tafelbild completely neglected color as such and turned entirely to Darstellung. Photography reached the peak/culmination (Gipfelpunkt) of Darstellung but also the low point (Tiefpunkt) of farbiger Gestaltung. Only the Impressionists returned color to the Kulturmensch.
- **Rule**: Recognize that when a medium achieves perfection in one territory (Darstellung in photography), it simultaneously reaches its lowest point in another (farbige Gestaltung); design accordingly — assign each task to the medium whose law-nature fits it.
- **Source**: p. 17
- **Quote (DE)**: "Diese hat den Gipfelpunkt ihrer darstellerischen Absichten in der Fotografie erreicht, allerdings damit auch den Tiefpunkt farbiger Gestaltung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This [the Tafelbild] reached the peak of its representational intentions in photography, but thereby also the low point of color design."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

## DIE STATISCHE UND KINETISCHE OPTISCHE GESTALTUNG
### (Static and Kinetic Optical Design) — pp. 18–19 (beginning)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The emergence of new technical means has produced new design territories. The technical production of today (die heutige technische Produktion) — projectors (Scheinwerfer), reflectors (Reflektor), light advertising (Lichtreklame) — creates not only new forms and areas for Darstellung but also for farbige Gestaltung. Until now pigment was the dominant means of farbiger Gestaltung; it functioned as "Baumaterial" (building material) in architecture. Its middle-age handling, though inconsistently pursued, was one way; alongside the colorfulness of surfaces a spatial-reflective Strahlung (radiation) also operates. The consciously reflectoric or projective thrown moving farbige Gestaltung (continuous Lichtgestaltung) opens new expression possibilities and brings new Gesetzmäßigkeiten (law-natures) with it.
**Evidence (historical development of projected light)**: For centuries people have tried to create a Lichtorgel (light-organ) or a Farbenklavier (color keyboard). Newton and his pupil Pater Castel were the first known. Different scholars followed. Walter Ruttmann (Germany) in New York 1916 accompanied the Prometheus Symphony with simultaneous projectors throwing Farbgarben (color sheaves) into the space. Thomas Wilfred's (America) Clavilux (ca. 1920) was a Laterna magica-like apparatus that presented gegenstandslose (non-representational) picture-variations with changeable content. An important advance was Ruttmann's work, which already placed the Filmapparat in the service of the Tricktisch (trick-table drawing tool). His finely drawn forms are the beginning of film-like, still unimaginably developable kinetic design.
**Evidence (Viking Eggeling)**: Most importantly mentioned are the works of the early deceased VIKING EGGELING (Sweden), who superseded all previous aesthetics —
**Conclusion**: [continues in next batch — p. 19 ends mid-sentence]

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Kinetische Gestaltung als neues Gestaltungsgebiet / Kinetic design as a new design territory
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The emergence of new technical means brings new design territories. The today's technical production — projectors, reflectors, light advertising — creates not only new forms and areas for Darstellung but also for farbige Gestaltung. Alongside pigment, light becomes a material for design. The consciously reflectoric or projective thrown moving farbige Gestaltung (continuous Lichtgestaltung) opens new expression possibilities and new Gesetzmäßigkeiten.
- **Rule**: Treat projected, reflectoric, and kinetic light as a design material with its own law-natures — not as decoration or illumination but as a plastic medium in motion, capable of Gestaltung in its own right.
- **Source**: p. 18
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die bewußt reflektorisch oder projektorisch geworfene bewegliche farbige Gestaltung (kontinuierliche Lichtgestaltung) öffnet dagegen neue Ausdrucksmöglichkeiten und damit neue Gesetzmäßigkeiten."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The consciously reflectorically or projectively thrown moving color design (continuous light design) opens new expression possibilities and thereby new law-natures."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Lichtgestaltung = historische Notwendigkeit / Light design as historical necessity
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The long history of attempts to create a Lichtorgel or Farbenklavier — from Newton through Castel, Scriabin, Ruttmann, Wilfred, Eggeling — demonstrates that kinetische Lichtgestaltung is not a novelty or a curiosity but a historically persistent necessity finally receiving its proper technical instruments.
- **Rule**: Design with light in motion not as experiment but as the fulfillment of a centuries-old necessity; the technology (film projector, reflectors) has at last provided the means to achieve what earlier attempts (color organ, color keyboard) only approximated.
- **Source**: p. 18–19
- **Quote (DE)**: "Seit Jahrhunderten versucht man, eine Lichtorgel oder ein Farbenklavier zu schaffen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "For centuries people have tried to create a light-organ or a color-keyboard."
- **Category**: light
