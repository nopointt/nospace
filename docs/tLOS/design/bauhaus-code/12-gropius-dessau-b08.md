## Section A — Wohnungen der Bauhausmeister: Interiors and Domestic Design Argument

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Meisterhäuser are not merely staff accommodation. Their interiors are a proof of principle: that the new architecture produces a new Wohnkultur — a way of living stripped of historicist ornament, furnished with objects designed by the Bauhaus workshops, governed by the same sachlicher Stil that governs the building's exterior. The identical floor plan does not produce identical rooms: color, furnishing, and personal arrangement produce variety. This shows that standardization of structure is compatible with individuality of use.

**Evidence**: Photographs of six interiors across multiple Doppelhäuser — the atelier of Moholy-Nagy (Abb. 130), the studio window with Kippflügel detail (Abb. 131), the living room of Muche (Abb. 132), the view from one Doppelhaus window across to another (Abb. 133), the living room of Moholy-Nagy with Breuer tubular steel furniture (Abb. 134/135), the living room from street side (Abb. 135 continuation, p.146), the dining room with Bauhaus wall painting by L. Moholy-Nagy and Breuer furniture (Abb. 136), the sliding storage unit between dining room and kitchen (Abb. 137), the bedroom corner of the Moholy-Nagy Doppelhaus with Breuer furniture (Abb. 138), the east-facing bedroom window framing the neighboring Doppelhaus in birch trees (Abb. 139), the bathroom on the first floor (Abb. 140), and the kitchen larder cabinet with access from the staircase landing to the basement pantry (Abb. 141).

**Conclusion**: The interior photographs demonstrate that the Bauhaus building vocabulary — flat surfaces, clear geometries, industrial materials, maximal light — extends without discontinuity from the architectural envelope into every domestic object. The building and its furnishings are a unified argument.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Gleichheit der Grundrisse / Verschiedenartigkeit der Wirkung — Identity of plan, variety of effect
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.141 states explicitly that the differing treatment of colors and furnishings in individual apartments produces, despite the identical floor plans, such varied effects that the identity of the rooms across different apartments is not consciously perceived by the viewer ("die abweichende behandlung in der farbigen gestaltung und in der möblierung der einzelnen wohnungen bringt trotz gleichheit der grundrisse eine so verschiedenartige wirkung, daß die gleichheit der räume in den verschiedenen wohnungen dem beschauer nicht bewußt wird").
- **Rule**: Design the standard plan and the standard structure; allow color, furniture arrangement, and personal use to produce differentiation. Do not design the variation itself — design the system that makes variation possible.
- **Source**: p.141 (PDF p.141)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die abweichende behandlung in der farbigen gestaltung und in der möblierung der einzelnen wohnungen bringt trotz gleichheit der grundrisse eine so verschiedenartige wirkung, daß die gleichheit der räume in den verschiedenen wohnungen dem beschauer nicht bewußt wird"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The differing treatment of the color design and the furnishing of the individual apartments produces, despite the identical floor plans, such a varied effect that the identity of the rooms in the different apartments is not consciously perceived by the viewer."
- **Category**: standardization | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Befreiung der Wohnung von nutzlosen Gegenständen / Liberation of the dwelling from useless objects
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.145 develops the domestic design argument at length. Gropius addresses the modern housewife directly: she will recognize the advantages of the new apartments most quickly — because the new dwelling is liberated from useless objects and cluttered, pointless decorations ("befreit von sinnlosem, raum-sperrendem kram und überflüssigen verzierungen"). The willfulness of styles has made us saturated; we now seek clear, simple forms that correspond to the essential character of our present domestic life.
- **Rule**: Remove every object from the dwelling that does not serve a clear function. Decoration that exists only as decoration is an obstacle to living.
- **Source**: p.145 (PDF p.145)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die wohnung, befreit von sinnlosem, raumsperrendem kram und überflüssigen verzierungen, von der willkür der stile sind wir satt geworden, von der laune zur regel geschritten und suchen nun in klaren, knappen und einfachen formen, die der art unseres heutigen lebens entsprechen, den wesentlichen und sinnfälligen ausdruck unserer häuslichen umgebung"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The dwelling, freed from meaningless, space-blocking clutter and superfluous decorations — we have become saturated by the arbitrariness of styles, have moved from caprice to rule, and now seek in clear, concise, and simple forms that correspond to the character of our present life the essential and self-evident expression of our domestic surroundings."
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Sachlicher Stil gegen historische Nachahmung / Objective style against historical imitation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.146 states that even today we still find newly furnished apartments in Rococo or Renaissance style, and it is incomprehensible that people of the present day can be persuaded that this is distinguished and refined for domestic use ("selbst heute noch finden wir neu eingerichtete wohnungen im rokoko- oder renaissancestil, und es ist fast unbegreiflich, daß menschen der heutigen zeit sich einreden lassen, dies sei der erstrebenswerte und 'vornehmste' rahmen für das häusliche dasein"). The architects of the present generation have broken thoroughly with this attitude.
- **Rule**: Never imitate historical formal languages in contemporary domestic design. The forms of living must correspond to the means of the present time.
- **Source**: p.146 (PDF p.146)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die architekten der heutigen generation haben mit dieser auffassung gründlich gebrochen und sehen ihre hauptaufgabe darin, die bedürfnisse ihrer eigenen zeit mit den mitteln der heutigen technik zu erfüllen und sich nicht mit schwächlicher nachahmung der vorfahren zu begnügen"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The architects of the present generation have broken thoroughly with this attitude and see their main task in fulfilling the needs of their own time with the means of present-day technology, and not in contenting themselves with weak imitation of their predecessors."
- **Category**: function | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Möbel als Bauhaus-Werkstattprodukt / Furniture as Bauhaus workshop product
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The photograph captions consistently identify the furniture throughout the Meisterhäuser interiors as products of the Bauhaus workshops — specifically Marcel Breuer's tubular steel furniture (Stahl-Rohr-Möbel). This is not incidental; the building and its furnishings are documented together as a unified demonstration. The caption for Abb. 134/135 names "möbel: m. breuer"; Abb. 136 names "tisch und sitzmöbel: m. breuer"; Abb. 138 names "möbel: marcell breuer". The building does not end at the wall — it continues into the object.
- **Rule**: Design the interior furnishings with the same formal principles that govern the building's architecture. The chair, the table, the storage unit, and the wall are part of one design decision.
- **Source**: p.145, p.147, p.149 (PDF pp.145, 147, 149)
- **Quote (DE)**: "möbel: m. breuer" [caption]; "tisch und sitzmöbel: m. breuer" [caption]
- **Quote (EN)**: "furniture: M. Breuer" [caption]; "table and seating furniture: M. Breuer" [caption]
- **Category**: workshop-integration | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Wandmalerei als integrierter Raumbestandteil / Wall painting as integrated room element
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The dining room photograph (Abb. 136, p.147) shows a Bauhaus wall painting ("wandmalerei des bauhauses") alongside Breuer furniture and a built-in storage cabinet for dishes. The caption identifies the color design as by L. Moholy-Nagy. The wall painting is not decoration applied to a finished room; it is part of the room's spatial and chromatic composition. This follows from the Bauhaus principle that painting, architecture, and craft are one.
- **Rule**: Treat the painted wall surface as an architectural element, not as ornament added after the building is complete. Color is a spatial instrument.
- **Source**: p.147 (PDF p.147)
- **Quote (DE)**: "farbige gestaltung: l. moholy-nagy und wandmalerei des bauhauses"
- **Quote (EN)**: "color design: L. Moholy-Nagy and wall painting of the Bauhaus"
- **Category**: workshop-integration | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Atelierfenster mit Kippflügeln / Studio window with tilt-sash
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The studio window (Abb. 131, p.142) of the Doppelhaus shows full-height glazing with Kippflügel (tilt-opening sash elements) and a Eternitplatten breastwork/railing. The structural system is exposed: diagonal steel tension rods are visible in the upper part of the frame. The window is not a hole in the wall — it is a structural and ventilation device, designed as such and left visible.
- **Rule**: Design windows as technical objects — size them for maximum light, detail them for controlled ventilation, and expose the structural logic of their framing rather than concealing it behind trim.
- **Source**: p.142 (PDF p.142)
- **Quote (DE)**: "atelierstahlfenster eines doppelhauses mit kippflügeln. brüstung aus ezernitplatten"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Studio steel window of a double house with tilt-sashes. Breastwork from Eternit panels."
- **Category**: material | transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Gleitender Geschirrschrank / Sliding storage between rooms
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photograph Abb. 137 (p.148) shows a sliding (gleitender) storage unit between the dining room and the kitchen, with a pass-through to the pantry. The caption reads "gleitender geschirrschrank zwischen eßzimmer und spüle eines doppelhauses (siebe abb. 136)". This is a built-in functional element that organizes the spatial sequence of dining → serving → kitchen → pantry as an unbroken logistical chain.
- **Rule**: Design storage and service elements as spatial connectors, not as freestanding furniture. The boundary between rooms is an opportunity for functional integration.
- **Source**: p.148 (PDF p.148)
- **Quote (DE)**: "gleitender geschirrschrank zwischen eßzimmer und spüle eines doppelhauses"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Sliding dish cabinet between dining room and sink of a double house."
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Speisekammer vom Podest der Kellertreppe aus zugänglich / Pantry accessible from stair landing
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The kitchen larder photograph (Abb. 141, p.152) documents a specific spatial-functional solution: the pantry (Speisekammer) is located behind the kitchen, accessible from the landing of the basement stair. The open cabinet shows shelving for both kitchen items and wine/cellar storage. This is a section-design decision, not a plan decision — the vertical sequence of spaces is organized for functional efficiency.
- **Rule**: Design vertical circulation as a functional organizer, not only as a connector of floors. The stair landing is a spatial node that can distribute access to adjacent service spaces.
- **Source**: p.152 (PDF p.152)
- **Quote (DE)**: "speiseschrank in der küche, dahinter speisekammer, vom podest der kellertreppe aus zugänglich"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Food cabinet in the kitchen, behind it the pantry, accessible from the landing of the basement stair."
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Blick aus dem Fenster als Raumerlebnis / View through the window as spatial experience
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Two photographs make a paired argument about windows and views: Abb. 133 (p.144) shows the view from inside one Doppelhaus through a large glazed opening framed by a projecting overhang, looking across the gap between houses to the neighboring Doppelhaus in the birch forest. Abb. 139 (p.150) shows the east-facing bedroom window of one Doppelhaus: a four-pane horizontal window through which the neighboring Doppelhaus is visible among birches. In both cases the window is a frame for a specific spatial relationship — not a generic opening for light and air, but a designed view connecting interior to exterior to the other house.
- **Rule**: Orient windows to frame specific relationships between interior, landscape, and neighboring built forms. The window is not merely a source of light — it is a spatial connection to the world outside.
- **Source**: p.144, p.150 (PDF pp.144, 150)
- **Category**: transparency | space | Lichtführung

## Section B — Siedlung Dessau-Törten und Gebäude des Konsumvereins Dessau (1926–28)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Dessau-Törten housing estate is a demonstration that industrial construction methods can produce housing at significantly reduced cost and time, while maintaining quality of spatial organization and equipment. The Törten Siedlung is not a social housing typology chosen for cheapness — it is a research project in construction rationalization, deliberately selected as a testing ground for new building systems, new materials, and new site organization methods. The recorded costs and timings are evidence, not self-promotion.

**Evidence**: Textual documentation in three sections — Die Aufgabe (the program), Das Konstruktionsprinzip der Reihenhäuser (the structural system), Die Durchführung (the construction process) — plus Die Anordnung der einzelnen Räume (spatial organization of rooms) and Auf Grund neuer Erfahrungen (findings from research). A cost table (p.157) gives exact floor areas, enclosed volumes, and building costs per year (1926–1928). A Konsumgebäude (consumer cooperative building) section (p.158) adds a commercial building to the estate documentation.

**Conclusion**: Industrialized construction — systematic site organization, prefabricated elements, flow-line working — produces measurable efficiency gains without sacrificing the spatial and technical standards that define the new architecture. The cost data proves the argument quantitatively.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Rationalisierung und Industrialisierung des Bauwesens / Rationalization and industrialization of building
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.153 (section title page) introduces the Törten project as an experiment in industrial construction rationalization: "erbaut in den jahren 1926–28". The program text on p.154 states that the total construction of 3 phases produced 316 Einfamilienreihenhäuser as Reichsheimstättenhäuser, with the explicit aim of reducing tenancy costs through rationalization and economies of construction ("ziel war, die mieten der häuser unter zusammenfassung aller rationalisierungsmöglichkeiten herabzudrücken").
- **Rule**: Approach housing construction as an industrial production problem. Minimize cost through systematic rationalization of construction, not through reduction of standards.
- **Source**: p.154 (PDF p.154)
- **Quote (DE)**: "ziel war, die mieten der häuser unter zusammenfassung aller rationalisierungsmöglichkeiten herabzudrücken. dieses ziel billiger mieten wurde durch ökonomische komposition der pläne, rechtzeitige arbeitsvorbereitung, sorgfältige vergabe und ökonomie des gewählten konstruktionsprinzips erreicht"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The goal was to reduce the rents of the houses by combining all possible rationalization measures. This goal of lower rents was achieved through economical plan composition, timely work preparation, careful tendering, and the economy of the chosen construction principle."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Konstruktionsprinzip der Schlackenbeton-Reihenhäuser / Structural principle of the slag-concrete row houses
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.155 describes the structural system in detail. Loadbearing party walls (Brandwände) of Schlackenbetonhohlkörpern (slag-concrete hollow blocks), 22.5 × 25 × 50 cm, one block thick, laid in bond. Front facades of Betonrapidbalken (rapid-set concrete beams), non-loadbearing, filling between beams with Schlackenbetonhohlwänden (slag-concrete hollow wall elements). A crucial choice: the slag aggregate (Koks-Schlacke — coke slag) was locally available and required only transport of cement and glowing coke slag for the hollow block manufacture.
- **Rule**: Choose structural materials that minimize transport by maximizing use of locally available industrial by-products. Calculate the full logistics chain before selecting a construction system.
- **Source**: p.155 (PDF p.155)
- **Quote (DE)**: "tragende brandwände aus schlackenbetonhohlkörpern von 22,5 25·50 cm, also von einer größe, die beim versetzen kann. decken frei gespannt von brandwand zu brandwand aus betonrapidbalken, die ohne zwischenfüllung, balken neben balken, trocken verlegt werden"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Loadbearing party walls of slag-concrete hollow blocks measuring 22.5 × 25 × 50 cm, a size manageable when laying. Floors spanning freely from party wall to party wall using rapid-set concrete beams, laid dry side by side without infill."
- **Category**: material | standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Fließbandartige Arbeitsvorgänge auf der Baustelle / Flow-line operations on the construction site
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.155 (Die Durchführung) describes the site organization in detail. A group of 8 houses formed a production unit: the slag-concrete hollow blocks were manufactured on site by 8 machines in accordion-line production; a team of 2 workers increased output gradually to 250 pieces per day once the supply for one group of 8 houses was prefabricated. The Rapid beams were manufactured in dedicated machines at the site, with the formwork, reinforcement and cement operations placed along the production axis. The beams were bundled in groups of 6 for transport by crane trolley to the next position.
- **Rule**: Organize the construction site as a factory: continuous flow, grouped operations, machine-based production, crane logistics. The building site is not a craft workshop — it is a production line.
- **Source**: p.155 (PDF p.155)
- **Quote (DE)**: "der rohbau geschah auf grund eines vorher aufgestellten eingehenden werkplatzplanes, derartig, daß die baueinheiten, wandkörper und betondeckenbalken auf der baustelle selbst mit maschinen in fließarbeitsartigen vorgängen so hergestellt wurden, daß leerungs- und reibungsverluste plan mäßig eingeschränkt wurden"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The shell construction was carried out on the basis of a previously prepared detailed site plan, such that the building elements — wall blocks and concrete floor beams — were produced on site itself with machines in flow-line operations, so that losses from empty runs and friction were systematically minimized."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Gleichbleibender Arbeiter pro Hausgruppe / Constant worker per house group
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.155 states a key organizational principle: the same worker was always redeployed for the same operation in each successive house group — and through this interlocking of individual construction operations, his performance improved. "grundsatz bei der arbeit an der baustelle war es, ein und denselben mann immer wieder für die gleiche bauphase in jeder hausgruppe einzusetzen und dadurch die leistung zu steigern. um das ineinandergreifen der einzelnen bauarten sicherzustellen" — by familiarity and repetition, speed and quality increase simultaneously.
- **Rule**: Assign each worker to one repeated operation across the full production run. Learning by repetition is a design tool — build it into the site organization plan.
- **Source**: p.155 (PDF p.155)
- **Quote (DE)**: "grundsatz bei der arbeit an der baustelle war es, ein und denselben mann immer wieder für die gleiche bauphase in jeder hausgruppe einzusetzen und dadurch die leistung zu steigern"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The principle in the site work was to assign one and the same worker repeatedly to the same construction phase in every house group, and thereby to increase output."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Zeitplan nach Eisenbahnbetriebsplänen / Construction schedule modeled on railway timetables
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.156 states that to secure the sequence of shell construction and fit-out from the outset, a precise schedule was prepared modeled on railway operating timetables — initially theoretical, then supplemented from practice, set up to show the interlocking of different construction operations over time ("wurde ein genauer zeitplan nach art der eisenbahnbetriebspläne zunächst vor arbeitsbeginn theoretisch — nach fertigstellung der praxis entsprechend ergänzt — aufgestellt"). This schedule made it possible to see the overlapping of different operations and to take timely corrective measures.
- **Rule**: Prepare a full construction sequence schedule before any work begins, structured as a time-operation matrix (not a simple list). Make the interdependencies of operations visible so that delays in one phase can be corrected before they propagate.
- **Source**: p.156 (PDF p.156)
- **Quote (DE)**: "wurde ein genauer zeitplan nach art der eisenbahnbetriebspläne zunächst vor arbeitsbeginn theoretisch — nach fertigstellung der praxis entsprechend ergänzt — aufgestellt. der zeitplan ermöglicht es, auf einen blick das ineinandergreifen verschiedener arbeitsvorgänge zu überschauen und rechtzeitige maßnahmen zu ergreifen"
- **Quote (EN)**: "A precise schedule in the manner of railway operating timetables was prepared initially before the start of work in theoretical form — then supplemented from practice as it was completed. The schedule makes it possible to see at a glance the interlocking of different work operations and to take timely measures."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Anordnung der einzelnen Räume nach Grundstücksnutzung / Room organization following rational plot use
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.156 (Die Anordnung der einzelnen Räume) states that the plots are laid out as allotments (torfklosett angle). Each house has 350–400 qm floor area per unit. The kitchen and utility areas are connected as a functional group; the toilet block is at the garden end for sanitation reasons. Central heating is provided — first period warm-air, second and third periods hot-water central heating, with gas connection for the cooker. All houses have bathrooms with shower; some periods have bath installations. All apartments are equipped with gas boiler.
- **Rule**: Set the equipment and service standard before designing the plan. Central heating, gas cooking, bathroom — these are the minimum technical content of the new housing. Plan around them, not after them.
- **Source**: p.156 (PDF p.156)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die häuser enthalten zentralheizung, in der ersten bauperiode warmluftfeizung, in der zweiten und dritten bauperiode warmwasserzentralheizung. herd kohlenfeuerung, daneben gasanschluß für gasherd. sämtliche häuser sind mit bädern ausgestattet, in der ersten bauperiode mit gasanschluß für junkers terme, in der zweiten und dritten bauperiode mit boileranlagen"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The houses contain central heating, in the first building period warm-air heating, in the second and third building periods hot-water central heating. Hob with coal firing plus gas connection for gas cooker. All houses are equipped with bathrooms — in the first building period with gas connection for Junkers water heaters, in the second and third building periods with boiler installations."
- **Category**: function | standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Flache Dächer aus Korkestrichschicht / Flat roofs with cork screed insulation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.156 specifies that flat roofs are constructed using a layer of cork screed (Korkestrichschicht) or dry-laid cellular concrete panels (Zellbetonplatten) for thermal insulation ("die flachen dächer sind durch auflage einer korkestrichschicht bzw. trocken verlegte zellbetonplatten gegen wärmeverlust geschützt").
- **Rule**: Specify insulation material for flat roofs at the design stage — the choice of insulation system is a design decision, not a contractor's option.
- **Source**: p.156 (PDF p.156)
- **Quote (DE)**: "die flachen dächer sind durch auflage einer korkestrichschicht bzw. trocken verlegte zellbetonplatten gegen wärmeverlust geschützt"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The flat roofs are protected against heat loss by a layer of cork screed or dry-laid cellular concrete panels."
- **Category**: material | function | Flachdach

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Leistungssteigerung durch industriellen Übergang / Performance increase through industrial transition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text on p.159 develops the argument for the gradual industrialization of construction at a macro-economic scale. The previous craft character of building trades is shifting toward the industrial side: the flying workshop at the building site is being replaced by the stationary industrial workshop — the factory. The seasonal character of building (Saisoncharakter des Bauens) is disadvantageous for both employers and workers and for the whole national economy. The transition to new construction methods is slow — precisely because of the enormous scale of the building sector and because it is the key sector of the economy; therefore the tempo of transition must be chosen carefully so as not to endanger existing economic groups, especially craft.
- **Rule**: The industrialization of construction is a process, not a switch. Plan the tempo of transition as carefully as the construction process itself.
- **Source**: p.159 (PDF p.159)
- **Quote (DE)**: "der bisherige handwerkliche charakter des baugewerbes wandelt sich allmählich nach der industriellen seite hin. der fliegen­de werkstatt an der baustelle wird mehr und mehr durch die stationäre industrielle werkstatt — die fabrik — abgenommen. bisheriger saisoncharakter des bauens mit seinen nachteilen für arbeitgeber und arbeitnehmer sowie für die gesamte volkswirtschaft weicht allmählich dem dauerbetriebe"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The previous craft character of the building trade is gradually shifting toward the industrial side. The flying workshop at the construction site is more and more being replaced by the stationary industrial workshop — the factory. The previous seasonal character of building, with its disadvantages for employers, workers, and the whole national economy, is gradually giving way to continuous operation."
- **Category**: standardization | function

## Cross-References

- P-01 (identical plan → varied effect through color and furnishing) connects to P-10 (standardization of plan as cost tool) — standardization at the scale of the Siedlung produces the same benefit as at the scale of the individual Doppelhaus interior: the structure is standard, the differentiation is in use.
- P-06 (Kippflügel as designed technical window element) applies at two scales: the large studio window of the Meisterhäuser (Abb. 131) and the bathroom window of the same houses (Abb. 140). The same element, the same logic, two scales.
- P-12 (flow-line site operations) and P-13 (same worker for same operation) are the micro-scale and the organizational-scale expression of the same principle: learning and efficiency through repetition and system.
- P-14 (railway timetable construction schedule) is the time-management analog of P-12 (flow-line operations in space). Together they constitute Gropius's complete theory of industrial site management.
- P-16 (flat roof with cork/Zellbeton insulation) connects back to the main Bauhaus building's Flachdach — the Törten houses apply the same flat-roof principle at the mass-housing scale, with specified insulation to prove the system is technically serious, not merely formal.
