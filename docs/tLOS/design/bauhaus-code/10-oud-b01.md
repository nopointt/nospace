## ZUR NEUAUFLAGE (Preface to the Second Edition, 1929)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The original text is preserved unchanged; the book is expanded with new images and two additional essays that clarify what has since become clearer.
**Evidence**: The essays "Ja und Nein: Bekenntnisse eines Architekten, 1925" and "Wohin führt das neue Bauen: Kunst und Standard, 1927" are added.
**Conclusion**: The deeper connection between the essays is internal (innerlich), not superficial — the whole must be understood as a connected argument, not isolated texts. Some apparent paradoxes are intentional.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Kontinuität des Textes / Continuity of the original argument
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Oud explicitly refuses to revise the text to match changed circumstances, because the argument's integrity depends on its original formulation.
- **Rule**: Preserve the original logic structure; do not retroactively modernize to avoid apparent paradoxes.
- **Source**: p. 6
- **Quote (DE)**: "Da ich Wert lege auf die Form der nachfolgenden Aufsätze, ist deren Originaltext beibehalten und auf Änderungen (auch der Abbildungen) verzichtet."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Since I attach value to the form of the following essays, their original text is retained and changes (including to the illustrations) have been avoided."
- **Category**: form

## GESTÄNDNIS (Confession / Declaration of Intent, p. 8)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: I am not an art historian but a builder (Baumeister). My orientation is toward the future, not the past. The future concerns me more than historical documentation.
**Evidence**: The material presented exceeds Dutch architecture alone; it points toward the general European architectural idea of our time.
**Conclusion**: Words compel action. The moral debt of theorizing is paid by showing my own work.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Zukunftsorientierung / Future orientation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Oud explicitly declares himself not an art historian but a Baumeister. The future — what architecture will become — matters more than what it has been.
- **Rule**: Architecture is not documented history; it is a project for the future. Extract the developmental line and hold to it.
- **Source**: p. 8
- **Quote (DE)**: "ICH BIN KEIN KUNSTHISTORIKER, SONDERN BAUMEISTER: MIR GILT DIE ZUKUNFT MEHR ALS DIE VERGANGENHEIT, UND AUFZUSPÜREN, WO KOMMEN SOLL, LIEGT MIR NÄHER, ALS NACHZUFORSCHEN, WAS GESCHEHEN IST."
- **Quote (EN)**: "I AM NOT AN ART HISTORIAN BUT A MASTER BUILDER: THE FUTURE MATTERS MORE TO ME THAN THE PAST, AND TRACING WHERE THINGS ARE HEADED IS CLOSER TO MY HEART THAN RESEARCHING WHAT HAS BEEN."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Eigene Haut / Personal skin in the game
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Oud acknowledges being accused of presenting his own ideas under the guise of a documentary film of Dutch architecture. His response: "aus der eigenen Haut kann niemand heraus" — nobody can step outside their own skin.
- **Rule**: Theoretical argument is inseparable from the architectural position of the arguer. Objectivity is impossible; declare your position.
- **Source**: p. 8
- **Quote (DE)**: "AUS DER EIGENEN HAUT KANN NIEMAND HERAUS!"
- **Quote (EN)**: "NOBODY CAN STEP OUTSIDE THEIR OWN SKIN!"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Entwicklungslinie / The line of development
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Where the presented developmental line appears crooked to others, Oud insists on maintaining his own right to hold to it. The line is the main thing; the material presented is secondary.
- **Rule**: Identify the main developmental line of architecture; hold to it even when contested. The material is evidence for the line, not the line itself.
- **Source**: p. 8
- **Quote (DE)**: "MIR ABER ERSCHEINT DIE DARGESTELLTE LINIE DER ENTWICKLUNG GERADE; SOLLTE SIE SICH ANDEREN KRUMM ZEIGEN, SO BESTEHE ICH AUF MEINEM RECHTE, SIE FÜR GERADE ZU HALTEN. DIE LINIE NÄMLICH IST MIR HAUPTSACHE, DAS VORGEFÜHRTE MATERIAL NEBENSACHE."
- **Quote (EN)**: "BUT TO ME THE PRESENTED LINE OF DEVELOPMENT APPEARS STRAIGHT; IF IT SEEMS CROOKED TO OTHERS, I INSIST ON MY RIGHT TO HOLD IT TO BE STRAIGHT. THE LINE IS MY MAIN CONCERN; THE PRESENTED MATERIAL IS SECONDARY."
- **Category**: form

## ÜBER DIE ZUKÜNFTIGE BAUKUNST UND IHRE ARCHITEKTONISCHEN MÖGLICHKEITEN (Ein Programm) — pp. 9–21

> Written 1921, though already superseded by 1929. Preserved without correction except for the significance of painting (Malerei), which the later essays make clearer.

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Architecture is in a critically important developmental period, whose significance is not yet recognized in the right direction. After overcoming Academicism, a renewal has begun — but only its first traces are visible.
**Evidence**: The persistence of ornament as universal remedy for inadequate compositional control; the failure of Cubism and Futurism to adequately transform architecture; the new productive possibilities of iron, plate glass, reinforced concrete (Eisenbeton); machine production as formal influence.
**Conclusion**: The architecture of the future will emerge from: (1) the elimination of ornament, (2) the rational exploitation of new materials, (3) the integration of color as spatial structure, (4) the acceptance of machine production as formal discipline.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Keine Ornamentik / Elimination of ornament
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Ornament is the universal remedy (Universal-Heilmittel) for inadequate architectural composition — it conceals the failure to achieve genuine compositional balance through formal means alone.
- **Rule**: Never use ornament to compensate for compositional failure. Every element of the building must contribute to the overall balance through its own formal proportion, not through decoration.
- **Source**: pp. 14–15
- **Quote (DE)**: "Ornament ist das Universal-Heilmittel für baukünstlerische Impotenz!"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Ornament is the universal remedy for architectural impotence!"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Ornamentlose Baukunst / Ornament-free architecture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: An ornament-free architecture demands the greatest possible purity of architectural composition.
- **Rule**: Remove all ornament; then the compositional task becomes fully visible and must be solved by form alone.
- **Source**: p. 15
- **Quote (DE)**: "Eine ornamentlose Baukunst erfordert die größtmögliche Reinheit der baukünstlerischen Komposition."
- **Quote (EN)**: "An ornament-free architecture demands the greatest possible purity of architectural composition."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Lebensgefühl als Richtlinie / Life-feeling as guideline
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The life-feeling (Lebensgefühl) of an era is the guideline for its art — not formal tradition.
- **Rule**: Derive architectural form from the actual life-feeling of the present, not from inherited formal conventions.
- **Source**: p. 11
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Lebensgefühl einer Zeit ist Richtlinie für ihre Kunst, nicht die Formtradition!"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The life-feeling of an era is the guideline for its art — not formal tradition!"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Baukunst als materiell-geistiges Doppelziel / Architecture's dual goal
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Architecture's goal is double: useful and beautiful simultaneously. Spiritual and material factors interact; neither can be reduced to the other.
- **Rule**: Architecture serves both utility and beauty — neither at the expense of the other. The beautiful must emerge from the organization of the useful, not be applied over it.
- **Source**: p. 13
- **Quote (DE)**: "Ihr Ziel ist ein doppeltes: nützlich und schön zugleich."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Its goal is double: useful and beautiful simultaneously."
- **Category**: construction

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Verzierung als Mangel / Decoration as deficit
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: All ornamentation in art is secondary — external compensation for an internal deficit (Anhängsel, kein Organ). In architecture, it is only necessary as a compositional substitute for as long as the building has not achieved aesthetic self-sufficiency through form.
- **Rule**: Treat any impulse to add ornament as a diagnostic signal: the composition has not yet solved its problem by formal means.
- **Source**: p. 15
- **Quote (DE)**: "Alle Verzierung in der Kunst ist nebensächlich: äußerlicher Ausgleich für ein innerliches Defizit: Anhängsel, kein Organ; in der Baukunst nur so lange notwendig als Gestaltung mit eigenen Mitteln in ästhetischer Hinsicht versagt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "All ornamentation in art is secondary: external compensation for an internal deficit: an appendage, not an organ; in architecture only necessary for as long as formal composition fails aesthetically by its own means."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Neue Materialien als Formgeber / New materials as form-givers
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Iron (Eisen), plate glass (Spiegelglas), reinforced concrete (Eisenbeton) — these materials have their own intrinsic formal logic that architecture must express, not conceal with traditional masonry treatment.
- **Rule**: Let the material speak its own formal language. Iron demands homogeneous surface, uninterrupted horizontal extension, precise mass-and-void articulation. Do not apply brick logic to iron construction.
- **Source**: pp. 18–19
- **Quote (DE)**: "Reine Verwendung von Eisen und Spiegelglas auf diesen Grundlagen wird das offene Element in der Architektur derartig verstärken können, daß die zukünftige Baukunst auf rationelle Weise ihre äußerliche Schwere in hohem Maße wird überwinden können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Pure use of iron and plate glass on these foundations will be able to strengthen the open element in architecture to such a degree that future architecture will be able to rationally overcome its external heaviness to a great extent."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Spiegelglas als Öffnung / Plate glass as architectural opening
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A pure plate glass window, used without wooden mullions breaking the surface, acts as an opening — but one that maintains the visual continuity of the wall plane by reflecting light from its surface. The glass pane is both transparent and a reflective plane; its correct use creates the illusion of wall-continuation while admitting light.
- **Rule**: Window openings in the new architecture must be glazed to maintain the reading of the wall plane as continuous surface. Avoid wooden grids that fragment the opening.
- **Source**: p. 18
- **Quote (DE)**: "Eine Spiegelscheibe, rein verwendet, wirkt in der Architektur als Öffnung an sich. Da wo ihre Abmessungen derartig sein müssen, daß Untereinteilung notwendig ist, werden die Teile so groß, daß nur eiserne Sprossen genügende Stabilität verbürgen: ihr offener Charakter geht also auch dann nicht verloren."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A plate glass pane, used purely, acts in architecture as an opening as such. Where its dimensions must be such that subdivision is necessary, the parts are made large enough that only iron mullions can guarantee sufficient stability: its open character is therefore not lost even then."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Eisenbeton als plastische Möglichkeit / Reinforced concrete as plastic possibility
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Reinforced concrete (Eisenbeton) allows homogeneous assembly of load-bearing and carried elements, pure horizontal extension of large dimensions, and uninterrupted surface planes (Flächen- und Massenbegrenzung). It can be built both bottom-up and top-down, creating a new architectural plasticity in combination with iron and glass.
- **Rule**: Use reinforced concrete for its distinctive formal properties: horizontal extension, homogeneous surface, freedom from the compression logic of masonry. These properties are not incidental — they are the material's contribution to the new architecture.
- **Source**: p. 19
- **Quote (DE)**: "In Eisenbeton ist eine homogene Zusammenfassung von tragenden und getragenen Teilen sowohl als auch horizontale Ausbreitung von bedeutenden Abmessungen und reine Flächen- und Massenbegrenzung möglich."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In reinforced concrete, a homogeneous assembly of load-bearing and carried parts is possible, as well as horizontal extension of significant dimensions and pure surface and mass definition."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Kubismus als Übergangsform / Cubism as transitional form
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Cubism contains developmental possibilities of a more general type that can stamp it as the preliminary stage toward a new monumental painting (monumentalen Malerkunst). It has found the rhythm of the new life-feeling formally — but only in the domain of painting, not yet in architecture.
- **Rule**: Do not apply Cubist pictorial solutions directly to architecture. Architecture must find its own three-dimensional resolution of the same compositional problem.
- **Source**: p. 12
- **Quote (DE)**: "So enthält der Kubismus Entwicklungsmöglichkeiten allgemeinerer Art, die ihn zum Übergangsstadium zu einer neuen, zu einer monumentalen Malerkunst stempeln können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Thus Cubism contains developmental possibilities of a more general type that can stamp it as a transitional stage toward a new, monumental painting."
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Maschinenwerk als Formgebung / Machine work as formal discipline
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The replacement of handwork by machine work is a social and economic necessity. Machine-produced components begin to dominate construction. The machine product, in contrast to the handmade detail, is formally determined by uniformity (Gleichförmigkeit) rather than individual variation — equal formal units in equal-sized sub-elements.
- **Rule**: Accept the formal discipline of machine production: uniform components, standardized sub-elements, repetition as formal virtue not as compromise.
- **Source**: p. 17
- **Quote (DE)**: "Im Gegensatz zum handwerklichen Detail, welches — verhältnismäßig unbestimmt in der Form und der Farbe — endlose Variationen über ein Hauptmotiv erlaubt, ist es das Kennzeichnende der maschinellen Details, daß es, verhältnismäßig bestimmt in der Form und der Farbe, vollkommen gleichförmig ist mit gleichartigen — gleichzeitig verfertigten — Details."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In contrast to the handmade detail, which — relatively indeterminate in form and color — allows endless variations on a main motif, it is characteristic of machine-made details that they, relatively determinate in form and color, are completely uniform with like — simultaneously produced — details."
- **Category**: standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Gleichgewicht der Kräfte / Equilibrium of forces
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The present (1921) architecture of the highest developed form knows no Gespanntheit (tensioned equilibrium) — it achieves external harmony but no inner energy, no contrast, no tension. Like Renaissance surface harmony or intimate Gothic feeling — but never contrast, never Gespanntheit.
- **Rule**: Seek tensioned equilibrium (Gespanntheit) in composition — not surface harmony but the balance of opposed forces held in dynamic tension.
- **Source**: p. 13
- **Quote (DE)**: "kennt nicht die Gespanntheit, wie sie sich ästhetisch verwirklicht in dem großen Rhythmus, in dem gleichgewichtigen Komplex voneinander sich gegenseitig beziehenden und einander beeinflussenden Teilen, wovon der eine die ästhetische Absicht des anderen unterstützt, wo weder etwas hinzugefügt noch etwas ab-"
- **Quote (EN)**: "knows not the tensioned quality as it realizes itself aesthetically in the great rhythm, in the balanced complex of parts that mutually relate to and influence one another, where the aesthetic intention of one supports the other, where neither anything is added nor anything—"
- **Category**: form

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Handwerk vs. Maschinenwerk — Detail als Verfall / Handcraft vs. machine — detail as decline
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The flourishing era of handicraft was the flourishing era of detail. Detail was the expressive medium par excellence — the instrument of greatest inner movement. The most subjective artist, the most expressive detail. The decline of handicraft means the decline of detail as individualized expression.
- **Rule**: Do not attempt to replicate handcraft detail through machine production. Accept that the era of the individualized detail is over; design for machine uniformity.
- **Source**: p. 17
- **Quote (DE)**: "Was der Strich ist für den Geigenspieler, ist das Detail für den Baukünstler der Gegenwart: Ausdrucksmittel par excellence innerer Bewegtheit."
- **Quote (EN)**: "What the stroke is for the violinist, the detail is for the architect of the present: the expressive medium par excellence of inner movement."
- **Category**: standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Formtradition als Hemmnis / Formal tradition as impediment
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The development of architectural history demonstrates that the first impulse in every emergence is to ornament — to decorate the first hut, then lay the foundations for the inequality of use and beauty across centuries. This inequality eventually constituted a confused congruence of beauty-ornament which blocked the emergence of a new Baukunst.
- **Rule**: Do not derive architectural form from historical tradition. Tradition is form-memory, not life-feeling. Derive form from life-feeling.
- **Source**: p. 16
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Entwicklungsgeschichte der Baukunst lehrt, daß die Art ihres Entstehens in erster Instanz zugleich die Keime ihres Verderbens enthält."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The developmental history of architecture teaches that the manner of its origin simultaneously contains the seeds of its decline."
- **Category**: form
