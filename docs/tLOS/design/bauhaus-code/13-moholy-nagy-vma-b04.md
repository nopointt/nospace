## II. Das Material — Schlusskapitel: Tradition · Ornament · Komposition vs. Konstruktion · Ein Beispiel: Kubismus/Picasso · Abstrakte Malerei · Statt Farbe: Licht

This batch covers the closing sections of Chapter II (Das Material), running from the critique of ornament and tradition through the extended analysis of Cubism as a case study in Faktur discovery, and concluding with the turn from color to light as primary design material. The argument culminates in the Projektionsschirm and reflektorische Lichtspiele — the point at which painting dissolves into spatial light-organization.

---

## Ornament

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: A correct and creative use of material clarifies the questions of organic form (funktionelle Formen); ornament in the historical sense is a secondary, additive act — it obscures rather than reveals the material's intrinsic character.

**Evidence**: Moholy-Nagy argues that the question of ornament is tied to the question of organic construction. He states that ornament at its best was always subordinate to function: it was ein Präventivornament — a preventive ornament — applied to wood where splitting along the grain was a risk, its decorative lines coinciding with structurally necessary ones. A gramophone record (abb. 30, referenced from earlier batch) as a modern example of surface decoration that could "schöner" (more beautiful) and "wertvoller" (more valuable) be made.

**Conclusion**: True ornament is not decoration applied from outside, but the visible surface of structural necessity. Applied ornament that does not coincide with structural logic is ornamental fraud.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Präventivornament / Preventive ornament
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Ornament is only legitimate when it coincides with structural necessity — when the decorative line IS the functional line. Historical ornament at its best was Präventivornament: applied where the material's own grain or weakness required reinforcement, so that beauty and protection were identical acts.
- **Rule**: Never apply surface decoration that does not derive from the material's own structural logic. If an ornament can be removed without structural consequence, it is not ornament — it is noise.
- **Source**: p.70
- **Quote (DE)**: "eine richtige und schöpferische verwendung des materials klärt die fragen über funktionelle und ornamentale form."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A correct and creative use of material clarifies the questions about functional and ornamental form."
- **Category**: material | surface

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Ornament subordinate to function in the frühen Perioden
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In the early historical periods ornament was often subordinated to function ("in der frühzeit waren ornamente oft der funktion untergeordnet"). Ornament became detached from structural purpose only later, degenerating into applied surface decoration that no longer carried any functional meaning.
- **Rule**: Locate the historical moment when an ornamental tradition lost its structural motivation — that is the moment it became a Kunststil (style) rather than a Konstruktionsprinzip (constructive principle).
- **Source**: p.70
- **Quote (DE)**: "in der frühzeit waren ornamente oft der funktion untergeordnet"
- **Quote (EN)**: "In early periods ornament was often subordinated to function"
- **Category**: material | surface | pedagogy

## Flächengliederung

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The concept of Ornament belongs to the concept of "Flächengliederung" (surface articulation / organization of surfaces). What matters in architecture and design is how surfaces are articulated — proportioned, scaled, divided — not whether they are decorated.

**Evidence**: Surface organization shows itself in architecture in the appearance of walls, openings, larger and smaller elements: "zu dem kapitel 'ornament' gehört auch der Begriff der 'Flächengliederung'. ihre verwendung z. b. in der Architektur läßt eine wand durch aufteilung größer, kleiner, schmaler usw. erscheinen, als sie es in der Wirklichkeit ist."

**Conclusion**: Surface articulation is a genuine constructive act — it can make a wall appear larger or smaller than it actually is, modifying spatial experience without changing physical dimension.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Flächengliederung als optische Transformation / Surface articulation as optical transformation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The articulation of a surface — through proportional subdivision, scale contrast, rhythm of openings — can make a wall appear larger, smaller, narrower or wider than its actual dimensions. This is not illusion in the pejorative sense; it is the controlled use of optical reality.
- **Rule**: Before applying any surface treatment, determine the desired spatial perception: what must this surface feel like in the room? Then derive the articulation from that perceptual target, not from decorative convention.
- **Source**: p.71
- **Quote (DE)**: "ihre verwendung z. b. in der architektur läßt eine wand durch aufteilung größer, kleiner, schmaler usw. erscheinen, als sie es in der wirklichkeit ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "its use in architecture, for example, makes a wall appear larger, smaller, narrower etc. through articulation, than it actually is."
- **Category**: surface | space | perception

## Komposition, Konstruktion

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Komposition and Konstruktion are not synonyms. Komposition means arranging given elements. Konstruktion means building from a governing law — the structural principle comes first, the elements are derived from it.

**Evidence**: "unter komposition verstand man die höchste ausponderierung von elementen und ihren beziehungen. diese ausponderierung konnte oft noch im laufe der arbeit durch einschieben von neu hinzutretenden elementen, durch änderung der gesamtkomposition erreicht werden. eine konstruktion dagegen müßte als idealfall in allen punkten ihrer technischen und geistigen relationen von vornherein bestimmt sein."

**Conclusion**: Konstruktion is the higher mode. It demands that every decision be logically necessary from the governing principle. Nothing is added for balance, taste, or accident. The weakness of Komposition is that it can always accept one more element; Konstruktion is closed by its own law.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Konstruktion vor Komposition / Construction before Composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Komposition works by rearranging and balancing elements after the fact — it is always open to the next adjustment. Konstruktion establishes the governing law first; every element is logically necessary, derivable from that law. A change anywhere would require changing everything.
- **Rule**: Begin with the governing structural law, not with the arrangement of parts. If you find yourself balancing elements by eye, you are composing, not constructing. Stop. Return to the law.
- **Source**: p.71
- **Quote (DE)**: "eine konstruktion dagegen müßte als idealfall in allen punkten ihrer technischen und geistigen relationen von vornherein bestimmt sein. eine änderung in der durchführung würde die gesamte vorgesehene kräfteverteilung zunichte machen. darum erfordert die konstruktion — im verhältnis zur komposition — ein plus an wissensmäßigen, intuitiven schwungkraft fehlen darf."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A construction, by contrast, must ideally be determined in advance in all its technical and intellectual relations. A change in execution would destroy the entire planned distribution of forces. This is why construction — in relation to composition — requires an additional measure of knowledge-based intuitive driving force."
- **Category**: pedagogy | surface | space

## Tradition

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Tradition is tragically transmitted as unexamined delivery — content and validity are passed on without scrutiny. The student learns to revere tradition not because it has proven its value to them personally, but because it is tradition.

**Evidence**: Moholy-Nagy identifies two forms of failure: (1) handwerkliche Tradition — craft tradition transmitted without understanding, producing objects of proportionally correct form but intellectually vacant; (2) maschinelle Produktion — industrial production that reproduces historical forms mechanically, multiplying ornamental fraud at scale. The machine is capable of extraordinary precision; it is not capable of originating a living form. "Die Zahl der hergestellten Objekte ist verhältnismäßig beschränkt. aber die Maschine ist in ihrer Unbegrenztheit von grausamer Aufrichtigkeit."

**Conclusion**: The machine is ruthlessly honest — it will repeat any form, including a dead one, infinitely. The human designer must supply the living law; the machine will execute it faithfully or betray it faithfully.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Tradition als unverantwortliche Überlieferung / Tradition as irresponsible transmission
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Tradition transmits form without transmitting the knowledge that produced it. The student receives the result without the generative process. This is dangerous because it produces not understanding but reverence — and reverence is the enemy of new construction.
- **Rule**: Treat every received form as a problem to be reconstructed from first principles. Do not ask "what does tradition say?" Ask "what law generated this form, and does that law still hold?"
- **Source**: p.72
- **Quote (DE)**: "es ist tragisch, wie oft tradition, auf inhalt und gültigkeit hin ungeprüfte überlieferung, als unverückbare richtlinien des schönen oder richtigen geehrt werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It is tragic how often tradition — unexamined delivery of content and validity — is honored as an immovable guideline for the beautiful or the correct."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Die Maschine als ehrlicher Multiplikator / The machine as honest multiplier
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The machine does not lie about what it is given. If it is given a living constructive principle, it multiplies living form. If it is given dead ornament, it multiplies dead ornament with ruthless consistency. The machine's honesty is a diagnostic instrument: the quality of industrial production is a direct index of the quality of the constructive thinking that preceded it.
- **Rule**: Before committing any form to industrial production, verify that the form derives from a living constructive law — not from stylistic convention. The machine will expose the difference at scale.
- **Source**: p.72–73
- **Quote (DE)**: "aber die maschine ist in ihrer unbegrenztheit von grausamer aufrichtigkeit."
- **Quote (EN)**: "But the machine, in its limitlessness, is of cruel honesty."
- **Category**: material | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Kunst baut auf — Art builds on life
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Even today the individual carries the burden of confronting all the new possibilities — every form of experience stacks up against the native capacity of the living person. Art cannot be a museum; it must be a method for living. "Kunst — wie wir sie verstehen — stirbt nicht, sie ist wirklich 'ewig', weil sie Energieform, weil sie mit den Sinnen faßbare Ordnung ist."
- **Rule**: Design must be grounded in the full sensory and biological life of the human being. Any form that cannot be experienced with the whole body — with eye, hand, motion, weight — is not fully alive as design.
- **Source**: p.72–73
- **Quote (DE)**: "kunst — wie wir sie verstehen — stirbt nicht, sie ist wirklich 'ewig', weil sie energieform, weil sie mit den sinnen faßbare ordnung ist und dadurch — durch unterbewußte analogien — eine an der gemeinschaft bauende arbeit verrichten kann."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Art — as we understand it — does not die; it is truly 'eternal', because it is a form of energy, because it is order graspable by the senses, and through this — through unconscious analogies — it can perform a work that builds community."
- **Category**: pedagogy | perception

## Die "Ismen"

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The proliferation of artistic "isms" (Impressionism, Cubism, Futurism, Expressionism, Constructivism, Neoplasticism, Suprematism, Dadaism, Surrealism) is the result of fragmentation — each ism isolating one dimension of visual experience and pursuing it exclusively. The task now is to understand the unifying law beneath all of them.

**Evidence**: "das arbeitsproblem des heutigen schöpferischen menschen besteht darin, daß er als sammelbecken zeitlicher gefühle und wünsche seinen ausdruck mit heutigen mitteln formulieren muß. das ist im grunde der generalnenner der vielen kunstismen, die in den letzten hundert jahren auftauchten."

**Conclusion**: Every ism was a valid partial research program. The problem is not that they existed, but that their results were hoarded as proprietary property rather than synthesized into a common educational vocabulary. The student today should be able to extract the constructive law from each ism without becoming a convert to any one.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Die Ismen als Teilforschungen / The Isms as partial research programs
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Each artistic movement of the past hundred years was a research program focused on one dimension of visual-material experience. None was complete; all were necessary. The error is mistaking a partial result for a total answer — becoming an "Ist" (an adherent of an ism) rather than extracting the principle.
- **Rule**: Study every major movement for its constructive contribution. Extract the governing law. Discard the ideological packaging. Do not become a follower of any ism — become the person who knows what each ism discovered.
- **Source**: p.73–74
- **Quote (DE)**: "für die meisten ist es heute kaum möglich, das durcheinander, die dutzende von kunstismen — selbst im sinne klassifizierender, rationalisierter kunstgeschichte — auseinanderzuhalten."
- **Quote (EN)**: "For most people today it is barely possible to sort out the confusion, the dozens of art-isms — even in the sense of a classifying, rationalized art history."
- **Category**: pedagogy

## Ein Beispiel: Der Kubismus (Picasso)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Cubism is the paradigm case for understanding how Faktur was rediscovered as a primary design concern after centuries in which painting had suppressed it. Picasso's development, tracked across abb. 54–65, demonstrates the complete trajectory: from the first analytical decomposition of natural form into geometric volumes, through the direct introduction of material surface as pictorial element, to the point where the picture-plane dissolves into a projection screen for light.

**Evidence**: The sequence of Picasso works cited (abb. 54–65) spans 1909–1926 and is read as a single argument about the progressive foregrounding of Faktur. Moholy-Nagy identifies four phases in Picasso's Cubist development, each representing a different relationship between material, surface, and the painted image.

**Conclusion**: Cubism is not primarily an aesthetic movement. It is a research program that recovered the lost knowledge of Faktur and demonstrated — for the first time in Western painting since the Gothic — that the material surface of the artwork is itself a primary bearer of meaning.

### Die erste Phase des Kubismus

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Kubismus als analytische Zerlegung / Cubism as analytical decomposition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The first phase of Cubism (c. 1909–1910) was motivated by Cézanne's instruction: "tout dans la nature se modèle selon la sphère, le cône et le cylindre." Picasso and his circle took this as a method of analysis, not a stylistic formula. They decomposed observed forms into their geometric volumes — sphere, cone, cylinder — and then represented all visible faces simultaneously rather than from a single viewpoint. This was an anti-illusionistic act: it exposed the structural geometry beneath the atmospheric surface of things.
- **Rule**: When analyzing any form, ask: what are its generating volumes? Sphere, cone, cylinder, prism. Decompose before you compose. Know the geometry before you paint, model, or build.
- **Source**: p.75 (abb. 54, 55), p.77
- **Quote (DE)**: "picasso hielt sich daran — samt seinen kameraden: braque, gleizes, juan gris, metzinger, léger usw. sie erlebten die welt 'stereometrisch'. doch bald trat eine merkwürdige wandlung ein, die der freude an neuen wider·sprechien schien."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Picasso held to this — together with his companions: Braque, Gleizes, Juan Gris, Metzinger, Léger etc. They experienced the world 'stereometrically'. But soon a remarkable change occurred that seemed to contradict the joy in what was new."
- **Category**: surface | perception | pedagogy

### Faktur in der Malerei

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Faktur des Bildes / Faktur of the painted surface
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Faktur is not a property only of craft materials (wood, stone, metal). It is equally a property of the painted surface. The Impressionists had already discovered this: their brushwork — the physical trace of the brush's movement — was itself a carrier of meaning, independent of the depicted subject. But they never fully theorized this discovery. Picasso, in the second phase of Cubism, did: he introduced actual material surfaces (papier collé, sand, sawdust, pasted newspaper, gesso relief) into the picture, making the Faktur of the pictorial surface an explicit theme.
- **Rule**: Treat the physical surface of your medium as a primary expressive material, not a neutral support for depicted content. The surface IS the first content.
- **Source**: p.78–79 (abb. 56), p.81–83
- **Quote (DE)**: "das ist es, was man objektive faktur nennen kann: die wiederentdeckten eigenwerte von material und werkzeug, im späteren kubismus — von darstellungsabsichten losgelöst — auch in sich, für sich wirken."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This is what one can call objective Faktur: the rediscovered intrinsic values of material and tool, in later Cubism — detached from representational intentions — acting in and for themselves."
- **Category**: surface | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Werkzeugfaktur / Tool-Faktur
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every tool leaves a characteristic trace on a surface. Brush, palette knife, spray gun, roller, finger — each produces a different Faktur. The Impressionists made this visible for the first time: "ihr technisches kernproblem war: die farben zu ihrer elementaren intensität, zum leuchten, zu bringen." But they could not separate the material fact of brushwork from the atmospheric representation it served. The later development (abb. 57–59) demonstrates that the same pigment applied to the same ground in different ways produces entirely different light effects.
- **Rule**: Before selecting a technique, determine what Faktur it will produce on the surface. The tool-Faktur must be chosen to serve the light behavior required, not the convenience of the maker.
- **Source**: p.79–81 (abb. 56, 57, 58, 59)
- **Quote (DE)**: "durch die bewegung des pinsels, verschiedene licht·wirkungen der farbe entstehen können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "through the movement of the brush, different light effects of the color can arise."
- **Category**: surface | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Papier collé — das direkte Material / Papier collé — the direct material
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Picasso's introduction of pasted paper (papier collé) and relief elements (abb. 60, 61) was a decisive step: it replaced the painted simulation of Faktur with actual material Faktur. The newspaper strip in the picture has its own printed Faktur — it does not represent texture, it IS texture. This is the crossing point between painting and material construction.
- **Rule**: Where possible, replace the painted simulation of a material property with the actual material property. A real texture is always more specific — and more honest — than its painted likeness.
- **Source**: p.82–83 (abb. 60, 61)
- **Quote (DE)**: "der vorwurf des bildes beugt sich nämlich hier den gesetzen einer bildordnung, die sich aus den beziehungen der farbe, des hell-dunkels, der lagen, richtungen, proportionen von linien und flächen und der faktur kristallisiert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The subject of the picture submits here to the laws of a pictorial order that crystallizes from the relationships of color, light-dark, the positions, directions, proportions of lines and surfaces and the Faktur."
- **Category**: surface | material

### Abstrakte Malerei

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Die objektive Faktur als Befreiung / Objective Faktur as liberation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The third phase of Cubism (abb. 62–63, c. 1913–1917) marks the moment when Faktur became fully autonomous — no longer in service of representation, but acting for itself. Moholy-Nagy calls this the "kurze Zusammenfassung der gegenwärtigen optischen Gestaltungsproblematik": the entire development of contemporary visual form-giving can be understood as the progressive discovery of the intrinsic values of material, surface, and light.
- **Rule**: Recognize the moment in any design process when a material quality begins to work autonomously — when it no longer needs to represent anything outside itself. At that point, give it full authority. Do not suppress it back into service of representation.
- **Source**: p.85–86 (abb. 62, 63)
- **Quote (DE)**: "diese entwicklungsgeschichte der verschiedenen werkstoffe und der ihnen — in verbindung mit den werkzeugen — innewohnenden fakturbildenden fähigkeiten kann natürlich in ganz ähnlicher weise für die späteren maler dargestellt werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This developmental history of the various materials and the Faktur-forming capacities inherent in them — in connection with the tools — can of course be presented in a quite similar way for the later painters."
- **Category**: surface | material | perception

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Neoplastizismus, Suprematismus, Konstruktivismus als Tradition-Klärung / Neoplasticism, Suprematism, Constructivism as tradition-clarification
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Neoplasticism (Mondrian, Doesburg), Suprematism (Malevitch), and Constructivism (Tatlin, Rodchenko, Lissitzky, Moholy-Nagy) recognized what Cubism had made visible about tradition and representation, and proclaimed it clearly. They declared the tradition in regard to Darstellung — representation — exhausted. The "strong similarity with the photographic Faktur in the flowing intermediate values" (abb. 65, 66) was recognized as the next step: optical design instruments must be used purely, without the bending of their meaning.
- **Rule**: Use optical design means — color, line, surface, light — according to their intrinsic optical laws, not according to the conventions of representation inherited from a prior period.
- **Source**: p.87
- **Quote (DE)**: "neoplastizismus (mondrian, doesburg), suprematismus (malewitsch), konstruktivismus (tatlin, rodtschenko, lissitzky, moholy-nagy) haben dies klar erkannt und proklamiert. sie haben die tradition in bezug auf die darstellung — spiegelung der natur — aufgegeben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Neoplasticism (Mondrian, Doesburg), Suprematism (Malevitch), Constructivism (Tatlin, Rodchenko, Lissitzky, Moholy-Nagy) recognized and proclaimed this clearly. They abandoned the tradition in relation to depiction — the mirroring of nature."
- **Category**: surface | perception | pedagogy

## Statt Farbe: Licht

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: Color (Farbe) as pigment is a means for simulating light, not light itself. The final consequence of the Cubist-Constructivist line of development is to abandon pigment altogether and work directly with light — the only medium that can actually produce the flowing, interpenetrating surface relationships that painting sought to simulate.

**Evidence**: Abb. 65 (Moholy-Nagy 1926, Faktur mit Spritzapparat) demonstrates the closest approach to light-flow possible with pigment and spray technique. Abb. 66 (Fotogramm, 1925) demonstrates the direct photographic registration of light on the light-sensitive layer — a surface whose Faktur IS light.

**Conclusion**: The direction is clear: from pigment surface toward light surface, from static Faktur toward flowing light-space. The Projektionsschirm (projection screen) is the logical endpoint of the painted surface's development.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Farbe als unvollkommenes Licht / Color as imperfect light
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Impressionists wanted to capture the flowing quality of light, but they were imprisoned by the opacity and permanence of pigment. Pigment can simulate the appearance of light, but it cannot actually flow, interpenetrate, or change over time. The logical conclusion of Impressionism is to abandon pigment and work with actual light.
- **Rule**: When a medium cannot achieve the effect required by the design law, do not compensate with more skill — change the medium. If flowing light is required, use light. If pigment cannot flow, do not use pigment.
- **Source**: p.88
- **Quote (DE)**: "heutige bemühungen zielen dahin, selbst den farbstoff (das pigment) zu überwinden oder ihn wenigstens soweit wie möglich zu sublimieren, um aus dem elementaren material der optischen gestaltung, aus dem direkten licht, den ausdruck zu realisieren."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Contemporary efforts are directed toward overcoming the pigment (the color material) itself, or at least sublimating it as far as possible, in order to realize expression from the elementary material of optical form-giving — from direct light."
- **Category**: surface | light | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Spritztechnik als Übergang / Spray technique as transition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The spray gun (Spritzapparat) allows pigment to be applied in thin, iridescent, flowing color layers on polished surfaces — metal, artificial materials — that, through reflection on the reflective undercoat, produce loosened, fluctuating appearances (abb. 65). This is the maximum approach to light-behavior achievable with pigment. It is a transitional technique, pointing beyond itself toward pure light.
- **Rule**: Use transitional techniques to identify the direction of development, not as permanent solutions. Spray-on surface is not yet light — but it teaches the eye to desire light.
- **Source**: p.89 (abb. 65)
- **Quote (DE)**: "auf polierte flächen, metall, künstliche materialien usw. werden mit hilfe von spritzapparaten dünnste, irisierende, fließende farbschichten aufgetragen, die durch den reflektierenden spiegelnden untergrund aufgelockert, fluktuierend erscheinen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Onto polished surfaces — metal, artificial materials etc. — the thinnest, iridescent, flowing color layers are applied with spray equipment; these appear loosened and fluctuating through the reflective mirrored undercoat."
- **Category**: surface | light | material

## Die letzte Vereinfachung des Bildes: Der Projektionsschirm

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The logical endpoint of the development from material surface through Faktur through color through light is the Projektionsschirm (projection screen). The projection screen is the ideale Fläche — the ideal surface — because it receives light directly, without the mediation of pigment. It is the mathematical limit of painting.

**Evidence**: Malewitsch's white square on white ground is cited as the decisive step: "man geht kaum fehl mit der behauptung, daß die plane malewitsch'sche fläche der ideale schirm gewesen sei — darauf können werden projected light-and-shadow-effects." The fotogramm (abb. 66) realizes this: the light-sensitive layer of the photographic paper IS the projection screen — the final, most direct version of the picture surface.

**Conclusion**: The painted picture has arrived at its own dissolution. The Projektionsschirm is not a picture — it is a surface prepared to receive projected light, i.e., a spatial light-event frozen in time. The next step is not a frozen projection — it is a moving, live, time-based light projection: the reflektorische Lichtspiele.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Der Projektionsschirm als ideale Bildfläche / The projection screen as ideal picture surface
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The projection screen is the surface that receives light without resistance, without material Faktur imposing itself. It is the purest realization of what painting sought: a surface where light — not pigment — is the primary material. The fotogramm (abb. 66) is the first step toward this: it is painting with light directly on the light-sensitive surface.
- **Rule**: Understand the projection screen as the logical terminus of all surface development. Every surface is an approximation of a projection screen; every design for a surface should ask: what is the light behavior required, and how close to a pure projection screen should this surface be?
- **Source**: p.90–91
- **Quote (DE)**: "es scheint, daß — entwicklungstechnisch — das manuelle bild von den reineren 'malerischen' lichtgestaltungsmöglichkeiten der projektion überholt wird. seit der erfindung des films beschäftigen sich viele manuelle maler mit diesem problem: projektion, licht, bewegung, durchdringung. (die fotografie ist zweifellos auch eine brücke dazu.)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "It appears that — developmentally — the manual picture is being overtaken by the purer 'painterly' light-design possibilities of projection. Since the invention of film many manual painters have occupied themselves with this problem: projection, light, movement, interpenetration. (Photography is also undoubtedly a bridge toward this.)"
- **Category**: light | surface | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Fotografie als Faktur des Lichts / Photography as Faktur of light
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The photogram (abb. 66, Moholy-Nagy 1925) is the most complete realization of Faktur-durch-Licht: objects are placed directly on the light-sensitive surface, and the light's own action — its gradients, its shadows, its interpenetrations — becomes the Faktur of the image. There is no tool between the light and the surface. The Faktur IS the light's structural character.
- **Rule**: Treat the photogram as the paradigm case of direct material action: no mediation, no representation, no tool. Use this paradigm to evaluate any design technique — how much mediation stands between the governing material law and the final surface?
- **Source**: p.89 (abb. 66)
- **Quote (DE)**: "bis heute die vollkommenste umsetzung des fließenden lichtes auf einen projektionsschirm, hier: auf die lichtempfindliche schicht des fotografischen papiers."
- **Quote (EN)**: "To this day the most complete transposition of flowing light onto a projection screen — here: onto the light-sensitive layer of photographic paper."
- **Category**: light | surface | material

## Reflektorische Lichtspiele

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The reflektorischen Lichtspiele (reflective light-plays) represent the active, time-based realization of the projection screen principle. Instead of a static image frozen on a surface, the light is generated in real time by regulated light sources — colored, moving, oscillating — projected onto reflective and translucent surfaces. This is the point at which surface design becomes spatial light-design.

**Evidence**: "in der weiterführung dieser arbeit wird man zweifellos zu den beweglichen reflektorischen licht-(farben-)gestaltung kommen müssen: an stelle von farbstoff die heute existierenden optischen instrumente, die regulierbaren künstlichen (elektrischen) lichtquellen bieten eine nicht zu unterschätzende handhabe dafür, auch wenn sie vorläufig nur durch zufall oder auf umwegen — meist als lichtreklame oder teaterrequisit — in die hände des gestalters fallen."

**Conclusion**: The reflektorische Lichtspiele are the true endpoint of the Material→Surface→Light trajectory. They are also the opening onto the next level of the progression — from surface to space — because projected light in motion constructs space, not just surface. This is why Moholy-Nagy ends the chapter on Das Material here: he has arrived at the threshold of Volume and Space.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Licht als primäres Gestaltungsmaterial / Light as primary design material
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The final conclusion of the Material chapter is that light is the primary material of optical design. Pigment, surface, Faktur — all of these are approaches toward light. The designer who works with actual light — regulated light sources, reflective and translucent surfaces, projection — is working with the material itself, not its substitute.
- **Rule**: Where optical design is concerned, trace the causal chain back to light. Every surface relationship, every color, every spatial impression — these are effects of light. Design light first; derive surfaces from their required light behavior.
- **Source**: p.91
- **Quote (DE)**: "in der weiterführung dieser arbeit wird man zweifellos zu den beweglichen reflektorischen licht-(farben-)gestaltung kommen müssen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the further development of this work one will undoubtedly have to arrive at the mobile reflective light-(color-)design."
- **Category**: light | space | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-20]**: Reflektorische Lichtspiele als Übergang zum Raum / Reflective light-plays as transition to Space
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The reflektorische Lichtspiele are not merely the endpoint of surface development — they are the opening of spatial development. A moving projected light constructs a spatial event, not a surface event. The observer must move through or around it; it has temporal and spatial extent. This is why the chapter on Das Material ends here: it has arrived at the threshold of Volumen and Raumgestaltung.
- **Rule**: Treat any design with light in motion as already architectural — it organizes space, not surface. Evaluate it by the standards of spatial experience: movement through it, position within it, relation to the human body.
- **Source**: p.91
- **Category**: light | space

## Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Fakturen gleichen Pigments — drei Beispiele / Three examples of the same pigment with different Faktur
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Apply the same pigment to three identical grounds using three different methods of application (abb. 57, 58, 59). Photograph all three under identical lighting conditions. Compare the light effects produced by Faktur alone, with no change in pigment or ground.
- **Purpose**: Demonstrates that Faktur is an independent variable of the surface — changing application method while holding material constant changes the optical result entirely. Trains the student's attention to tool-Faktur as distinct from material-color. References P-11.
- **Source**: p.80 (abb. 57, 58, 59)

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Synthetisches Kunstideal — Eigengesetz des Materials erkennen / Recognizing the autonomous law of the material
- **Task** [INFERRED]: Study the development of Cubism from abb. 54 through abb. 65 as a complete sequence. For each work: identify the material used, the technique, the Faktur produced, and the degree to which the Faktur is representational or autonomous. Write a one-sentence description of each work's position on the axis: representation → autonomous Faktur.
- **Purpose**: Trains the ability to locate any artwork or design work on the Material→Faktur→Light progression. References P-09 through P-14.
- **Source**: p.74–91

## Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: abb. 54 — Pablo Picasso, 1909: Fabrik, Horta de Ebro (Ölbild)
- **Shows**: Oil painting of a factory building at Horta de Ebro. The architecture is decomposed into geometric volumes — cubes, cylinders, prisms — with multiple viewpoints shown simultaneously. Strong dark/light contrasts, no atmospheric perspective.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The first phase of Cubism — stereometric decomposition of observed form into generating volumes. References P-09.
- **Text reference**: "picasso's werk ist geeigneter als alle andern, an dieser stelle eingeschaltet zu werden, weil — besonders im anfang der kubistischen periode — die verbundenheit mit dem elementaren erlebnissen des materials, mit den tastwerten und der faktur bei ihm besonders deutlich 'greifbar' ist."
- **Source**: p.74 (abb. 54)

**FIGURE [Fig-02]**: abb. 55 — Pablo Picasso, 1910: Der Tisch (Bleistiftzeichnung)
- **Shows**: Pencil drawing of a table with objects. Geometric decomposition of tabletop objects — a compote dish, bottles, a pipe — with faceted surfaces showing multiple simultaneous viewpoints. The drawing reveals the structural geometry beneath the objects.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The analytic phase of Cubism: decomposition into Volumen before surface treatment becomes primary. References P-09.
- **Text reference**: Referenced alongside abb. 54 as examples of the stereometric first phase.
- **Source**: p.76 (abb. 55)

**FIGURE [Fig-03]**: abb. 56 — Linienfaktur / Line Faktur (diagram)
- **Shows**: A striking graphic demonstration: two diamond shapes placed vertically. The upper diamond is filled with a regular crosshatch grid (diagonal lines at 45° in both directions). The lower diamond is filled with parallel horizontal lines only. Both shapes are identical; the Faktur produced by the two line systems is entirely different — the crosshatch produces a dense, vibrating surface; the horizontal lines produce a calm, directional one.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: That Faktur is produced by the system of lines, not by the material alone. The same geometric form with different Faktur produces a different optical experience. This is the Impressionist discovery made abstract and analytical. References P-10, P-11.
- **Text reference**: "diese faktur ist im grunde nur illusionistische darstellung des begriffs 'faktur', weil bei der herstellung dieses gebildes das material — gegenüber abb. 55 — stark zurückgedrängt wurde."
- **Source**: p.79 (abb. 56)

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: abb. 57, 58, 59 — Verschiedene Fakturen desselben Ölpigments auf gleichem Grund in demselben Licht fotografiert
- **Shows**: Three photographs of circular painted surfaces, each using the same oil pigment on identical ground, photographed under identical light. Each circle shows a distinctly different surface texture: abb. 57 — long, parallel brushstrokes creating directional Factur; abb. 58 — irregular, overlapping strokes with varied pressure; abb. 59 — heavily impastoed, crumpled surface with strong three-dimensional relief.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: That Faktur (the result of tool-application) is an independent variable. Same pigment, same ground, same light — different Faktur produces entirely different optical results. The three examples are "ein deutlicher beweis dafür, daß durch die art des farb-auftrags, durch die bewegung des pinsels, verschiedene lichtwirkungen der farbe entstehen können." References P-11.
- **Text reference**: "die drei beispiele sind ein deutlicher beweis dafür, daß durch die art des farb-auftrags, durch die bewegung des pinsels, verschiedene licht·wirkungen der farbe entstehen können."
- **Source**: p.80 (abb. 57, 58, 59)

**FIGURE [Fig-05]**: abb. 60 — Pablo Picasso, 1913: Stilleben (Zeichnung mit geklebten Papierstreifen)
- **Shows**: Drawing combined with pasted paper strips. Recognizable still-life elements (newspaper fragment with "LACERBA" visible, musical instrument fragments) combined with drawn lines. The pasted paper surface has its own printed Faktur — the letterforms and printing texture are fully part of the image.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The decisive moment of papier collé — actual material Faktur introduced directly into the picture, replacing painted simulation of texture. References P-12.
- **Text reference**: "picasso besinnt sich auf die 'malerischen' ausdrucksmöglichkeiten und kehrt zur fläche zurück."
- **Source**: p.81 (abb. 60)

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: abb. 61 — Pablo Picasso, 1913: Stilleben (Relief)
- **Shows**: A relief construction — actual three-dimensional elements projecting from the picture surface. Objects of daily life (a glass, what appears to be a pipe or musical instrument) are represented through cut, folded, and assembled materials rather than painted. The surface transitions between drawing, pasted material, and sculptural relief.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The extension of papier collé into actual relief — the picture surface acquires real depth, real shadow, real Faktur. The distinction between painting and sculpture begins to dissolve. References P-12, P-13.
- **Text reference**: "aus den bemühungen zur präzisen wiedergabe des gestandsdarstellungserlebnisses wuchsen seine neoplastizistischen periode."
- **Source**: p.82 (abb. 61)

**FIGURE [Fig-07]**: abb. 62 — Pablo Picasso, 1913: Porträt (Ölbild)
- **Shows**: Large oil portrait painting in the analytic Cubist manner — a figure is decomposed into overlapping planes, multiple simultaneous viewpoints, interlocking geometric facets. Strong contrasts between light and dark planes. Striated texture patterns (Faktur) run across multiple facets of the figure, connecting areas across representational divisions.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The moment when Faktur patterns begin to override representational boundaries — the same texture continues across what would be, in naturalistic painting, separate objects. Faktur operates as an autonomous organizing principle. References P-10, P-13.
- **Text reference**: "der vorwurf des bildes beugt sich nämlich hier den gesetzen einer bildordnung, die sich aus den beziehungen der farbe, des hell-dunkels, der lagen, richtungen, proportionen von linien und flächen und der faktur kristallisiert."
- **Source**: p.84 (abb. 62)

**FIGURE [Fig-08]**: abb. 63 — Pablo Picasso, 1917: Stilleben (Ölbild, mit verschiedenen Fakturworten)
- **Shows**: Still-life oil painting showing multiple surface treatment modes (Fakturworte) within a single composition: smooth painted areas, patterned areas with regular geometric Faktur, areas suggesting material texture, and drawn linear elements — all coexisting as a vocabulary of surface modes rather than a unified atmospheric space.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Faktur fully autonomous — different surface modes operate as distinct vocabulary items within the composition. No attempt to unify them into a continuous spatial illusion. References P-13.
- **Text reference**: "irreführend sind in diesem stadium des kubismus oft die bildertitel, die den betrachter verleiten, den sinn der arbeit in ihrem ausgangspunkt zu suchen, den während des gestaltungsprozesses meist überwunden wurde."
- **Source**: p.85 (abb. 63)

**FIGURE [Fig-09]**: abb. 64 — Pablo Picasso, 1924: Harlekin mit Guitarre (Ölbild)
- **Shows**: Late Cubist oil painting of a harlequin figure with guitar. The figure retains recognizable form but the surface is organized through contrasting Faktur zones — diamond-patterned costume, smooth skin areas, geometrically subdivided background — creating a surface that is simultaneously representational and autonomous.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The final phase of Cubism cited by Moholy-Nagy: Neoplasticism, Suprematism, and Constructivism recognized this development and drew the logical conclusion — abandon representation entirely, work with pure optical elements. References P-14.
- **Text reference**: "neoplastizismus (mondrian, doesburg), suprematismus (malewitsch), konstruktivismus (tatlin, rodtschenko, lissitzky, moholy-nagy) haben dies klar erkannt und proklamiert."
- **Source**: p.87 (abb. 64)

**FIGURE [Fig-10]**: abb. 65 — Moholy-Nagy, 1926: Faktur mit Spritzapparat (kameraloses Foto)
- **Shows**: A large oval or circular form with a strongly iridescent, graduated surface — smooth transitions from bright center outward, with subtly flowing color gradations, applied by spray gun to a polished reflective ground. The surface appears to glow from within — no visible brushwork, no tool trace, the Faktur is pure light-gradient.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The maximum approach to light-behavior achievable with pigment — spray technique on reflective ground eliminates tool-Faktur, approximating the continuous gradients of light on a projection surface. Referenced as the transitional technique pointing toward actual light. References P-15, P-16.
- **Text reference**: "das war intuitiv die zielsetzung des konstruktivismus, wenn auch seine praxis höchstens bis zu einer technischen vorstufe, zu der spritztechnik gelangte."
- **Source**: p.89 (abb. 65)

**FIGURE [Fig-11]**: abb. 66 — Moholy-Nagy, 1925: Fotografische Faktur ("Fotogramm") — kameraloses Foto
- **Shows**: A photogram — objects placed directly on light-sensitive photographic paper and exposed to light, without camera. The result: white shapes (where objects blocked light), gray graduated zones (where objects partially blocked or diffused light), black zones (fully exposed areas). The surface IS the record of light's direct action — no mediation of pigment, brush, or camera.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The direct Faktur of light on a light-sensitive surface — the most complete realization of "flowing light onto a projection screen." References P-17, P-18.
- **Text reference**: "bis heute die vollkommenste umsetzung des fließenden lichtes auf einen projektionsschirm, hier: auf die lichtempfindliche schicht des fotografischen papiers."
- **Source**: p.89 (abb. 66)
