## Abbildungsverzeichnis — List of Illustrations (Complete)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The book's complete illustration index — three pages of it — is itself an argument: a building this thoroughly documented, this completely photographed and drawn, cannot be dismissed as mere architecture. It is a total work, and the index proves the totality.
**Evidence**: Three consecutive pages (pp. 219–221 in book pagination) list illustrations numbered abb. 82 through abb. 203, covering every room, every facade, every construction phase, every building type in the Dessau Bauhaus complex and the Törten housing settlement.
**Conclusion**: The exhaustiveness of documentation is pedagogical. The student and the architect who reads this book has access to every decision, every detail, every variation. There is nothing hidden.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Vollständige Dokumentation / Complete documentation as architectural argument
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: A building that cannot be fully documented — whose decisions cannot be fully shown — is a building whose logic is incomplete. The thoroughness of the illustration index signals the internal completeness of the architecture itself. Every room has a name, a number, a photograph.
- **Rule**: Document every spatial decision completely — plan, section, photograph, detail — because the act of documentation reveals whether the logic holds at every scale.
- **Source**: p.219–221 (PDF pages 220–222)
- **Quote (DE)**: [No direct verbal quote — the index is itself the text]
- **Quote (EN)**: [No direct verbal quote — the index is itself the text]
- **Category**: function | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Systematische Raumfolge / Systematic spatial sequence in documentation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The illustration index proceeds systematically through each building type (Einzelhäuser → Doppelhäuser → Siedlung Dessau-Törten → Konsumgebäude → Arbeitsamt) and within each type moves room by room, facade by facade, floor by floor, construction phase by construction phase.
- **Rule**: Document spaces in the same order the architect designed them — by program hierarchy, then by spatial sequence within each zone — so the reader reconstructs the design logic in the correct sequence.
- **Source**: p.219–221 (PDF pages 220–222)

## Publisher Catalog — Bauhausbücher Series (Both Versions)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Bauhausbücher series is a complete pedagogical program in book form. Each volume is a module in a larger argument about the future of design, architecture, and production.
**Evidence**: Two versions of the publisher's catalog appear at the back of the book (pp. 224–225): one listing 12 volumes, one listing 14 volumes — indicating that volumes 13 and 14 were announced or in production at the time of printing.
**Conclusion**: The series itself embodies the Bauhaus principle of the Gesamtwerk (total work): no single book is sufficient; the full argument requires all 14 volumes read together.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Bauhausbücher als Gesamtprogramm / The Bauhausbücher series as total program
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Both catalog pages list all Bauhausbücher with their authors, titles, page counts, and prices. The series functions as a curriculum: Gropius on international architecture (vol.1), Klee on pedagogical sketchbook (vol.2), experimental house (vol.3), theater (vol.4), Mondrian on new design (vol.5), van Doesburg on principles (vol.6), new workshop work (vol.7), Moholy-Nagy on painting/photography/film (vol.8), Kandinsky on point and line (vol.9), Oud on Dutch architecture (vol.10), Malevich on objectless world (vol.11), Gropius on Dessau buildings (vol.12).
- **Rule**: Design education cannot be delivered through a single text. The full argument requires multiple authors, multiple disciplines, multiple scales — from the pedagogical sketchbook (Klee, vol.2) to the completed building (Gropius, vol.12).
- **Source**: p.224–225 (PDF pages 224–225)
- **Quote (DE)**: "bauhausbücher / schriftleitung: walter gropius / l. moholy-nagy"
- **Quote (EN)**: "bauhaus books / edited by: walter gropius / l. moholy-nagy"
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Erweiterte Reihe / Extended series — volumes 13 and 14
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The second catalog page (p.225) lists two volumes not present in the first (p.224): volume 13 (Albert Gleizes, Kubismus — "die Geschichte des Kubismus") and volume 14 (L. Moholy-Nagy, Von Material zu Architektur — "der Weg zum Erlebnis von Material, Plastik und Architektur"). The series was still growing. Architecture from material — the title of vol.14 precisely names the Bauhaus method.
- **Rule**: The series is not closed. New disciplines are added as the argument develops. "Von Material zu Architektur" — from material to architecture — is the trajectory of the entire Bauhaus program stated in four words.
- **Source**: p.225 (PDF page 225)
- **Quote (DE)**: "von material zu architektur / der weg zum erlebnis von material, plastik und architektur"
- **Quote (EN)**: "from material to architecture / the path to the experience of material, sculpture, and architecture"
- **Category**: pedagogy | material

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Berichtigung — Fehlerkorrektur als Transparenz / Errata as transparency

## Cover Design — Front and Back

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The cover of Bauhausbauten Dessau is the book's first and last argument: an axonometric view of the entire Dessau complex, rendered in black, cream, and terracotta, communicates the total spatial organization before the reader opens a single page.
**Evidence**: The back cover (outer, p.228) presents a full isometric/axonometric illustration of the Dessau Bauhaus complex including the Meisterhäuser. The back cover inner flap (p.227) shows a fragment of a building corner detail in black line on cream. The front cover (not in this batch, but referenced by the book's overall design program) completes the pair.
**Conclusion**: The axonometric view is the optimal representation of a building whose formal language depends on three-dimensional composition — plan, elevation, and volume simultaneously legible.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Axonometrie als Darstellungsmittel / Axonometric projection as the correct representation for Funktionsgliederung
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The back cover illustration uses axonometric (isometric) projection, not perspective. In a perspective drawing, the receding parts of a building diminish and distort. In axonometric projection, all parallel lines remain parallel, all dimensions remain true. A building organized by Funktionsgliederung — distinct volumes, each with its own logic — must be shown axonometrically so the viewer can read each volume's true proportions and spatial relationships simultaneously.
- **Rule**: Use axonometric projection when the argument is about the relationship between building volumes. Use perspective only when the argument is about the sequential experience of a space from a specific viewpoint.
- **Source**: p.228 (PDF page 228)
- **Quote (DE)**: [Visual evidence only — no text on cover other than publisher imprint]
- **Quote (EN)**: [Visual evidence only — no text on cover other than publisher imprint]
- **Category**: transparency | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Farbkodierung der Bauteile / Color coding of building elements as functional differentiation
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The back cover illustration uses three colors: black (for structural elements, steel frames, window mullions, and deep shadow), cream/beige (for rendered wall surfaces and paper ground), and terracotta/orange-red (for the Meisterhäuser cubic volumes). The terracotta is not arbitrary — it distinguishes the residential Meisterhäuser from the workshop and institutional volumes, which remain in black and cream. Color performs the same function as form: identifying building type.
- **Rule**: When representing multiple building types in a single composition, use color to reinforce functional differentiation. Color is information, not decoration.
- **Source**: p.228 (PDF page 228)
- **Quote (DE)**: [Visual evidence only]
- **Quote (EN)**: [Visual evidence only]
- **Category**: function | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Meisterhäuser im Kontext des Gesamtensembles / Masters' houses within the total ensemble
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The back cover axonometric shows the Meisterhäuser and the main Bauhaus building in a single composition. The semi-detached house configuration of the Meisterhäuser is clearly legible: two cubic volumes sharing a party wall, each with identical formal vocabulary but mirror-reversed. The relationship between the small domestic scale and the large institutional scale is visible in a single image.
- **Rule**: The Meisterhäuser and the main building must always be understood as a single ensemble. They share a formal vocabulary, not merely a site. Einheit im Maßstab (unity of scale) means the same rules operate at domestic and institutional scale simultaneously.
- **Source**: p.228 (PDF page 228)
- **Quote (DE)**: [Visual evidence only — see also Einheit im Maßstab vocabulary entry]
- **Quote (EN)**: [Visual evidence only — see also Einheit im Maßstab vocabulary entry]
- **Category**: space | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Pilotenstruktur sichtbar / Pilotis structure legible in axonometric
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: In the back cover axonometric, certain volumes of the main Bauhaus building appear raised off the ground on visible columns or structural supports, with the ground plane continuous beneath them. The Pilotis principle — structure concentrated in columns, ground freed — is legible in the cover image before the reader encounters any floor plans.
- **Rule**: A building's most important structural principle must be legible from the primary exterior view. If the ground is freed by Pilotis, this must be visible, not hidden by landscaping or peripheral elements.

## Batch 12 Summary

This final batch of Bauhausbauten Dessau (pages 220–228) contains:

1. **Three pages of illustration index** (Abbildungsverzeichnis, pp.219–221): the complete listing of all illustrations from abb. 82 to abb. 203, covering the Einzelhäuser, Doppelhäuser/Meisterhäuser, Siedlung Dessau-Törten (with its six construction-year type variants), Konsumgebäude, and Arbeitsamt. The exhaustiveness of the index is itself an argument for the completeness of the building documentation program.

2. **Two blank pages** (pp.223, 226): separator pages within the book's physical structure.

3. **Two Bauhausbücher publisher catalog pages** (pp.224–225): one listing 12 volumes, one listing 14 volumes, both in full Bauhaus typographic style. The expanded 14-volume version includes Gleizes on Cubism and Moholy-Nagy's "Von Material zu Architektur." Both include the "Berichtigung" errata note. The catalog pages are themselves Bauhaus design artifacts demonstrating sachlicher Stil typography.

4. **Back cover pages** (pp.227–228): inner flap with building corner detail fragment, and outer cover with the full color axonometric of the Bauhausbauten Dessau complex — the book's summative image, combining Meisterhäuser and institutional building in a single composition that makes all core principles simultaneously legible: Funktionsgliederung, Einheit im Maßstab, Flachdach, Dachterrasse, Pilotis, structural expression.

The dominant principles of this batch: **complete documentation as argument** (P-01), **Konstruktionsschema and Zeitplan as primary drawing types** (P-03), **serial type variation** (P-04), **axonometric as correct representational mode for Funktionsgliederung** (P-10), **color as functional differentiation** (P-11), and **Einheit im Maßstab** (P-12).
