- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The Linie (line) as the primary structural force in pictorial composition. The analytical diagram isolates line from color and plane to show the skeletal compositional architecture.

---

### Argument Structure (Tab. 25 plates)

**Thesis**: A painting built primarily on Linie (line) can be analytically decomposed into its linear tensions, revealing the compositional skeleton beneath color and surface.

**Evidence**:
1. The analytical oval diagram (p.200) shows multiple line types simultaneously present in one composition: thick straight horizontals and verticals (structural axes), thin diagonal lines (dynamic tension), curved/arc lines (lyrical movement), a bold diagonal slash (dramatic force), a grid pattern (structured plane-division), a point-cluster (lower left — Punkt accumulation), a circle-cross symbol (lower right — concentric form), and arcs suggesting movement. All contained within an oval Grundfläche.
2. The linear schematic of "Kleiner Traum in Rot" (p.202) shows the same composition reduced to line alone: sweeping curves, angular intersections, scattered points, zigzag energies — the full line taxonomy in one image.
3. The color plate (p.204) shows the completed painting — warm ochre-brown ground, with all the analytical lines now carrying color, texture, and Faktur.

**Conclusion** [INFERRED]: The relationship between analytical diagram and finished painting is not illustration but proof. The diagram demonstrates that line-structure is logically prior to color. Color clothes the linear skeleton; it does not replace it. The painting can be read as a line argument dressed in color.

- **Shows**: A large oval boundary containing a dense linear composition. Identifiable elements:

  - A circle with internal cross-hatching (lower right) — circle plus contained structure, exzentrisch/konzentrisch interplay
  - A small asterisk/star point form (center, within grid zone)
  - Horizontal lines extending beyond oval boundary at left (suggesting force escaping the Grundfläche)
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The coexistence of all major Linie types within a single composition: straight (horizontal, vertical, diagonal), curved (arcs), angular (intersections), free diagonal. Also: Punkt accumulation as contrast to linear density. The oval as Grundfläche itself creates a specific compositional field — less rectangular weight distribution, more centripetal containment.

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: "Kleiner Traum in Rot" — color plate (p.204)
- **Shows**: The painting itself. Warm ochre-brown atmospheric ground dominates. Across this ground: the same linear architecture from the analytical diagram, now in color. Identifiable:
  - Black sweeping curves and straight lines matching the structural diagram
  - A large light-yellow/cream diagonal plane (angular, sharp — Dreieck tendency, yellow = acute/aggressive)
  - A deep blue-black area (lower center) — Blau (passive, cool, concentric tendency)

  - Small red circles/points
  - White-cream highlights against the warm brown ground
  - Atmospheric gradient — ground is not flat but has Faktur depth
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The color-form correspondences in action: yellow planes carry acute-angle/diagonal character; blue forms carry circular/concentric character; the warm ground (ochre/brown) serves as a neutral atmospheric Fläche. The painting demonstrates that color and form must correspond by inner necessity — each color is paired with the form whose Spannung matches its Temperatur.
- **Caption** [CERTAIN]: "KANDINSKY: Kleiner Traum in rot"
- **Source**: p.204

### Principles Derived from Register Structure

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Spannung (tension) is the master concept
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Register entry for Spannung (tension) spans pages 21 through 139 — effectively the entire theoretical body of the book. Every formal element is analyzed through its tension: the Punkt creates tension relative to the Grundfläche; the Linie is a record of applied forces (tensions); the Grundfläche has its own built-in directional tensions. Spannung is not a property of forms — it is the form of form.
- **Rule**: Analyze every formal element first as tension: what force produced it, what resistance it meets, what relationship it establishes with adjacent elements and the Grundfläche boundary.
- **Source**: p.208 (Register)
- **Category**: tension | composition | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: The line taxonomy is the most developed formal analysis in the book
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Register entry for Linie contains 12 sub-categories spanning pages 23–133 — the most extensive sub-taxonomy of any formal element. The sub-types are: Diagonale, Eckige (angular), Freie (free), Gebogene (curved), Gerade (straight), Grenzen (boundary lines), Horizontale, Kombinierte (combined), Komplexe (complex), Komplizierte (complicated), Temperatur (temperature character), Vertikale, Wellenartige (wave-like). This is the theoretical heart of the book.
- **Rule**: When analyzing any composition, begin with the line taxonomy — identify which of the 12 line types are present and in what proportion. The line structure is the compositional skeleton.
- **Source**: p.207 (Register)
- **Quote (DE)**: "Linie 23, 51-106, 123" with 12 sub-entries
- **Quote (EN)**: "Line [pages] 23, 51-106, 123" — occupying the largest page range of any sub-taxonomized element
- **Category**: line | composition | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: The Grundfläche is the culminating theoretical development
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Register entry for Fläche → Grund- spans pages 22–23, 29–30, 32, 44, 85, 109–140. The sub-range 109–140 is the final and largest sustained section of the book, confirming that the Grundfläche analysis is presented as the synthesis and culmination of all prior point and line theory. The four directional entries (Oben, Unten, Links, Rechts) each receive independent index entries, confirming that each direction has distinct compositional weight.
- **Rule**: Treat the Grundfläche not as empty support but as the final compositional element with its own directional tensions: top (light, free), bottom (heavy, dense), left (loose, lyrical), right (condensed, dramatic). Every element placed on the plane enters into tension with these directional forces.
- **Source**: p.206–208 (Register)
- **Category**: plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Klang sub-types define complexity levels in composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Register entry for Klang (resonance/sound) has five sub-types: Ein-Klang (single resonance, pp.30–31, 60), Doppel-Klang / Zwei-Klang (two-voice resonance, pp.22, 24, 26, 30–32, 109, 128), Drei-Klang (three-voice, p.65), Mehr-Klang (multi-voice, pp.30, 31, 60, 128, 133), and Ur-Klang (primordial resonance, pp.30, 31, 60, 128, 133). This is an explicit musical-structural analogy: a single element produces Ein-Klang; multiple elements in relationship produce Mehr-Klang.
- **Rule**: Assess compositional complexity by counting Klang voices: how many distinct formal tensions are sounding simultaneously? Ein-Klang = isolated element (maximal purity); Mehr-Klang = multiple elements in structured relationship (maximal complexity). Both can be compositionally valid — the test is Innere Notwendigkeit.
- **Source**: p.207 (Register)
- **Category**: composition | tension | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Übersetzung (translation/transposition) between arts is a systematic method
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Register entry for Übersetzung (translation/transposition) spans pages 36–39, 62, 85, 92, 93, 94. This is a significant page range. The concept refers to the systematic translation of formal laws between arts: how a musical tension translates into visual tension, how a dance movement translates into line character, how an architectural spatial relationship translates into compositional weight. This is not analogy — it is structural equivalence.
- **Rule**: When analyzing form, seek structural equivalents across arts (music → line character, dance → movement tension, architecture → spatial weight) not as illustrations but as cross-verification of universal formal law.
- **Source**: p.207 (Register)
- **Category**: composition | pedagogy | inner-necessity
