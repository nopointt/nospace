## III. Das Volumen (Plastik): Stadien 4–5 · Konstruktive Plastik

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The development of sculpture (Plastik) moves through five stages; the fourth stage is the fully perforated, weightless form that swings between volumes — the precondition for the fifth stage, the schwebende (hovering/suspended) Plastik, which is the highest form of volumetric art before the transition to kinetic sculpture. Alongside this developmental logic, Konstruktive Plastik — exemplified by the Russian Constructivists and the Bauhaus equilibrium exercises — establishes structure and balance as the governing law, replacing composition from aesthetic feeling with construction from mechanical and physical principle.

**Evidence**: Photographs of sculpture by Archipenko (negative/positive hollow-form, abb. 98), Brancusi (wood sculpture, abb. 99–100), Rudolf Belling (perforated block "Dreiklang," abb. 101), Archipenko's "Medrano" assemblage (glass, wood, metal, abb. 102), Rodchenko Konstruktion (abb. 104), Moholy-Nagy's own Relief 5 and Nickelplastik and multi-material Konstruktion (abb. 107, 110, 109, 111), Picasso Plastik (abb. 112), Joost Schmidt comparative spherical forms (abb. 113), Vantongerloo sphere interior (abb. 114), Russian Constructivist exhibition Moscow 1921 (abb. 115), balance constructions by Johansen, Johannes Zabel, Hermann Röseler — Bauhaus students first semester (abb. 116, 117, 118, 124), Naum Gabo konstruktion works (abb. 119, 120, 121), Picasso wire-sculpture design (abb. 122), Anni Wildberg construction metal+glass Bauhaus (abb. 123), Gerhard Spitzer and Heinz Tinzmann Bauhaus constructions (abb. 125, 126); the airship skeleton (abb. 103) as industrial precedent for the new plastic form.

**Conclusion**: Sculpture reaches its highest pre-kinetic form when: (a) material mass is dissolved by perforation and transparency; (b) balance and equilibrium replace gravitational mass as the structural law; (c) new industrial and artificial materials (metal, glass, celluloid, crystal mirror, nickel) are used for their own Factur rather than as substitutes for stone or wood; (d) the form is assembled (montiert) from components rather than carved or modeled from a block, allowing the governing law of construction to be legible in the result.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Positive-Negativ-Beziehung / Positive-Negative Relationship
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Archipenko's conscious use of hollows (Aushöhlungen) instead of projections is the first deliberate application of the positive-negative relationship in sculpture. The relief model (Gussmodell) creates an optical illusion depending on lighting: what is recessed (negativ) appears raised (positiv) depending on illumination — this deliberately leads the viewer toward an elementary understanding of positive-negative spatial relationships.
- **Rule**: Use hollows and voids as active formal elements equal in weight to projections — negative space is not absence, it is a structural partner to positive mass.
- **Source**: p.117
- **Quote (DE)**: "die erste bewußte verwendung von aushöhlungen — an stelle von erhöhungen — ist archipenkos verdienst. er ging dabei von der bekannten optischen täuschung aus, die sich bei der relief-mater zeigt: die vertieften (negativen) stellen des gussmodells erscheinen je nach beleuchtung erhöht (positiv). sein versuch führt den betrachter durch die deutliche abweichung von der gewohnten naturalistischen fassung zu den elementaren möglichkeiten der positiv-negativen beziehungen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The first deliberate use of hollows — in place of projections — is Archipenko's achievement. He proceeded from the well-known optical illusion visible in relief casting: the recessed (negative) areas of the cast model appear raised (positive) depending on illumination. His attempt leads the viewer, through the clear deviation from habitual naturalistic treatment, toward the elementary possibilities of positive-negative relationships."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Materialechtheit in der montierten Plastik / Material authenticity in assembled sculpture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Archipenko had already understood — a significant advance over the Louvre sculptors who combined marble with colored stone — that the plastic arts today must refuse surface deception: wood should not be made to look like silk, metal should not stand in for wood. Each material must be itself. The Montage-principle (assemblage) makes this law of material authenticity structurally enforced: you cannot hide what is assembled, only what is carved.
- **Rule**: In assemblage, use each material for its own Factur; never simulate one material with another.
- **Source**: p.120
- **Quote (DE)**: "archipenkos vorstoss mit der montierten plastik war eine absage an die kunstgewerblicke verwertung des materials. er wollte mit den materialien nicht oberfläche, sein holz sollte für holz, sein metall für metall usw. stehen und mit eigenem wert wirken."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Archipenko's advance with the assembled sculpture was a rejection of the craft-industrial exploitation of material. He wanted the materials not to simulate surfaces — his wood should stand for wood, his metal for metal, etc., and work with its own intrinsic value."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Technische Montage als ökonomisches Arbeitsprinzip / Technical assembly as economic working principle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The structural skeleton of a dirigible/airship (abb. 103) demonstrates that technical assembly (technische Montage) as an economic working principle has fundamentally influenced the new plastic form-making. The logic is efficiency: each strut bears only what is structurally necessary; nothing is redundant. This is not engineering-envy — it is the recognition that construction and economy of means are the same principle.
- **Rule**: Let the governing structural law be visible in the form; economize every element so that nothing is present that does not serve the construction.
- **Source**: p.121
- **Quote (DE)**: "die technische montage als ökonomisches arbeitsprinzip beeinflusste grundlegend die neue plastische gestaltung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Technical assembly as an economic working principle fundamentally influenced the new plastic form-making."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Völlig perforierte, völlig durchbrochene Plastik / Fully perforated, fully open sculpture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The fully perforated, fully open sculpture demands simultaneously a developed technical mastery and, on the other side, an abstracting spirituality: the liberation from the heaviness of material, the triumph of expressive intention over material resistance. Rudolf Belling's "Dreiklang" (abb. 101) is a transitional form: the heavy material block is dissolved into spherical extension, becomes weightless and massless swinging among volumetric relationships and interpenetrations (Durchdringungen). This is the direct precursor to Stage 5.
- **Rule**: Dissolve mass through perforation; let the relational tensions between volumes — not the volumes themselves — become the primary experience.
- **Source**: p.119, p.122
- **Quote (DE)**: "die völlig perforierte, völlig durchbrochene plastik fordert einerseits ein entwickeltes, technisches können, andererseits eine abstrahierende geistigkeit: die befreiung von der schwere des materials, das überwiegen der ausdrucksabsicht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The fully perforated, fully open sculpture demands on the one hand a developed technical mastery, on the other hand an abstracting spirituality: the liberation from the heaviness of material, the predominance of expressive intention."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Stadium 4 — Durchdringung / Stage 4 — Interpenetration
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The heavy material block — the undifferentiated spherical extension — becomes completely weightless and massless; it swings in volumetric relationships and interpenetrations (Durchdringungen). This is Stage 4. It is numbered explicitly in the text (large bold "4.") as a formal station in the developmental sequence.
- **Rule**: At Stage 4, the sculptural unit is no longer a bounded mass but a set of intersecting volumetric relationships; design by defining the interpenetrations, not the outer surface.
- **Source**: p.119
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Stadium 5 — Schwebende Plastik / Stage 5 — Hovering/Suspended Sculpture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Stage 5 is the schwebende Plastik (hovering sculpture) — referenced as see p.152 in the text. This stage is announced here as the next developmental step beyond the fully perforated form: the sculpture that is no longer grounded, that hovers in space. The text explicitly names this as the künftige stadium (future stage) of plastic development.
- **Rule**: Design for suspension and equilibrium in space — the sculptural form must read as hovering, liberated from gravitational anchoring.
- **Source**: p.119
- **Quote (DE)**: "das ist das künftige stadium der plastischen entwicklung, die kinetische plastik — ihr vorstadium: die schwebende plastik. (siehe seite 152)"
- **Quote (EN)**: "This is the future stage of plastic development, the kinetic sculpture — its precursor: the hovering sculpture. (see page 152)"
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Ein neuer Faktor — die Bemühung, vom Sockel loszukommen / A new factor — the effort to free oneself from the pedestal
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A new factor enters the series of works shown (abb. 95, 112, 117, 125, 126, 133): the effort to come free of the pedestal (die Bemühung, vom Sockel loszukommen). The pedestal is a gravitational anchor, a confession that the sculpture needs support. Moholy-Nagy's own Konstruktion (abb. 111) in opaque-, matt- and crystal-mirror glass, metal and vulcanfiber embodies this attempt. The pedestal is the last remnant of traditional sculptural hierarchy — its elimination is a structural necessity, not aesthetic preference.
- **Rule**: Eliminate the pedestal as a design goal; the sculpture must justify its position in space structurally, through balance and tension, not through a base.
- **Source**: p.128
- **Quote (DE)**: "ein neuer faktor tritt zu den bisherigen hinzu: die bemühung, vom sockel loszukommen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A new factor is added to those previously noted: the effort to come free of the pedestal."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Konstruktive Plastik — Gleichgewicht als Grundgesetz / Constructive Sculpture — Equilibrium as governing law
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In Konstruktive Plastik, the concepts of mechanics — dynamics, statics, kinetics, the problems of stability, equilibrium, balance — are worked out through plastic form; material relationships, construction, assembly (Montage) are examined. The Constructivists turned to industrial forms because a technical monomania dominated them; but this was a transitional stage (Übergangsstadium) that served to free art from the dull, distorted worldviews of "Kunst" and to give it a new scale (Maßstab) from a different side.
- **Rule**: Base sculptural structure on physical laws of balance and equilibrium, not on aesthetic composition; make the governing structural law the content of the form.
- **Source**: p.132
- **Quote (DE)**: "die schulbegriffe der mechanik, dynamik, statik, kinetik, die probleme der stabilität, des gleichgewichts wurden durch die plastische form erörtert; die materialzusammenhänge, konstruktion, montage ausgerichtet."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The fundamental concepts of mechanics — dynamics, statics, kinetics, the problems of stability and equilibrium — were discussed through plastic form; material relationships, construction, assembly were deployed."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Composition vs. Konstruktion — Der heutige Plastiker und die Ingenieursaufgaben
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The contemporary sculptor knows almost nothing from the realm of the engineer's tasks. Composition (Komposition), the golden section, and similar things are taught in academies — but nothing of statics, even though knowledge of statics is far more capable of leading toward an economic working method than knowledge of aesthetic rules. The Constructivists tried to break the chains of an exaggerated veneration of outmoded values — "die kunst ist tot, es lebe das leben!" (art is dead, long live life!).
- **Rule**: Learn statics before aesthetics; structural economy is a more reliable guide to form than compositional rules.
- **Source**: p.133
- **Quote (DE)**: "der heutige plastiker weiß kaum etwas von dem neuen aufgabenkreis des ingenieurs. komposition, goldener schnitt und ähnliches werden an akademien wohl gelehrt, aber nichts von statik, obwohl ihre kenntnis mehr als die ästetischen regeln zu einer ökonomischen arbeitsweise führen könnte."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The contemporary sculptor knows almost nothing from the new task-sphere of the engineer. Composition, the golden section and similar things are certainly taught in academies, but nothing of statics — even though knowledge of statics is more capable than aesthetic rules of leading toward an economic working method."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Gleichgewichtskonstruktion als pädagogische Übung / Balance construction as pedagogical exercise
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Bauhaus balance construction exercises (abb. 116–118, 124) demonstrate that the gestrig (yesterday's) sculptor cared little about exact weight calculation. Even a few grams or centimeters came to older sculptors not at all. At the Bauhaus, students learned to attend to every component: a balanced construction on a small flat surface — with freely suspended pendulum, with loose hanging elements — means that a small decrease in any component produces a large balancing effect (gleichbleibende Wirkung), often representing a small victory for the inventor's ingenuity. The increasing tendency toward dissolution of the block (Auflockerung) to the point of complete transparency is noted explicitly at p.140.
- **Rule**: Begin constructive plastic exercises with strict equilibrium problems; require the student to calculate weight and leverage, not to compose by eye.
- **Source**: p.133–134, p.140
- **Quote (DE)**: "der gestrige künstler kümmerte sich wenig um die exakte gewichtsberechnung seiner arbeit. auf einige kilogramm, oder gar zentner gewicht kam es bei einer älteren plastik gar nicht an. im bauhaus lernte man auch auf diese komponente achten und jeden gramm ersparnis — bei gleichbleibender wirkung — bedeutete oft einen kleinen sieg des erfinderischen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Yesterday's artist cared little about the exact weight calculation of his work. A few kilograms, or even hundredweights, made no difference to an older sculpture. At the Bauhaus, one learned to attend even to this component, and every gram saved — with unchanged effect — often represented a small inventor's victory."
- **Category**: pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Neue künstliche Materialien / New artificial materials
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The new artificial materials enter (abb. 118–120, 123): metal, glass, celluloid (Zelluloid), crystal mirror, nickel, vulcanfiber. These are announced with the caption note "die neuen künstlichen materialien treten auf." These are not substitutes for stone or wood — they have their own Factur: transparency, reflectivity, structural tension between rigidity and lightness.
- **Rule**: Select material for its own Factur — transparency, reflectivity, structural lightness — not as a replacement for traditional sculptural materials.
- **Source**: p.135
- **Quote (DE)**: "die neuen künstlichen materialien treten auf."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The new artificial materials enter."
- **Category**: material

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Das Nacheinander — Development as sequence, not progress
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: In the developmental history of sculpture (Plastik/Skulptur), the various stages follow one another (nacheinander): each closed cultural sphere (Ägyptian, Greek) shows at the beginning of its development the less or more carefully modeled block, and more careful and deep-going incisions only later. The conquest of the entire wealth of plastic expression appears to occur as a near-scientific investigation of the individual elements. This is not "progress" in the value-laden sense — it is a sequential working-through of formal possibilities.
- **Rule**: Understand each stage of the Material→Volume→Space hierarchy as a necessary step, not as inferior to later stages; the block is not primitive, it is first.
- **Source**: p.130
- **Quote (DE)**: "in der entwicklungsgeschichte der plastik (skulptur) treten die verschiedenen stadien der plastischen entwicklung nacheinander auf."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the developmental history of sculpture, the various stages of plastic development appear one after the other."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Die Eigenschaften des Materials — Zug und Druck / Properties of material — Tension and Compression
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The properties of material — its behavior under tension (Zug) and compression (Druck) — can be tested through multiple physical demands. The analysis of such works can be carried out repeatedly; the genius grip of the creator — beyond the rich intellectual, sensory-material processing — can be transformed into an actualized pedagogical stimulus.
- **Rule**: Test material properties under actual tension and compression before assigning them to a form; let the structural behavior of the material determine its use in the construction.
- **Source**: p.137
- **Quote (DE)**: "die eigenschaften des materials, sein verhalten auf zug und druck werden — vielleicht ohne deutliche absicht — durch vielfältige beanspruchung geprüft."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The properties of material — its behavior under tension and compression — are tested, perhaps without explicit intention, through manifold physical demands."
- **Category**: material

### Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Gleichgewichtskonstruktion auf kleiner Fläche / Balance construction on a small surface
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: The student constructs a freestanding balance structure mounted on a minimal base surface. Elements are attached with loose, freely hanging pendula (lockeraufgehängte Pendel) so that the equilibrium is achieved through the structural arrangement of components and their weights, not through the weight of the base. Variations: multiple components, asymmetric loading, components of differing materials (wood, metal wire, flat planes).
- **Purpose**: Trains awareness of Gleichgewicht (equilibrium) as the structural law of constructive sculpture; forces attention to mass distribution, leverage, and economy of material (→ P-10). Anticipates Stage 5 (schwebende Plastik) by training the student to think in terms of balance rather than gravitational anchoring.
- **Source**: p.133–134 (abb. 116 Johansen, abb. 117 Zabel, abb. 118 Röseler, abb. 124 Zabel)

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Gleichgewichtsstudie auf einer kleinen flachen Fläche / Equilibrium study on a small flat surface
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Johannes Zabel's Bauhaus first-semester exercise (abb. 117, 124): balance construction on a minimal flat base, with elements cantilevered at extreme angles from the base. The construction achieves equilibrium through counterweighting, not through structural symmetry. The result reads as dynamic tension frozen in a static moment.
- **Purpose**: Trains recognition that balance can be achieved through asymmetry and counterweight, not only through symmetrical arrangement (→ P-08, P-10). The increasingly pronounced dissolution tendency (Auflockerungstendenz) of the block — toward full transparency — is already visible here.
- **Source**: p.134, p.140

**EXERCISE [E-03]**: Konstruktion aus verschiedenen Materialien / Construction from different materials
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Hermann Röseler's Bauhaus first-semester exercise (abb. 118): equilibrium construction with a loosely hanging suspended pendulum element, combining materials with differing visual and physical weights. Anni Wildberg (abb. 123) and Gerhard Spitzer (abb. 125) and Heinz Tinzmann (abb. 126): Bauhaus first-semester construction exercises in metal-and-glass and wood-and-glass — multi-material constructions that use transparency to dissolve mass.
- **Purpose**: Introduces new artificial materials (Metall, Glas) into the balance construction framework; trains recognition that transparency is not mere aesthetic effect but a structural strategy for dissolving apparent mass (→ P-11, P-04).
- **Source**: p.135, p.139, p.141

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: abb. 98 — Alexander Archipenko, Terrakottaplastik (1916)

- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The positive-negative relationship as a conscious formal strategy (→ P-01). Moholy-Nagy explicitly uses this figure to introduce the concept.
- **Text reference**: "die erste bewußte verwendung von aushöhlungen — an stelle von erhöhungen — ist archipenkos verdienst." The text cites this figure as evidence of the first application.

- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The use of wood's own material character (Factur); the hole as an active form element anticipating perforation logic (→ P-04).
- **Text reference**: No direct text citation on this page; appears as part of the sequential figure series illustrating stages.

- **Shows**: A compact, arched wood sculpture — a massive curved form with an open arch that creates a negative space beneath it, standing on a plinth. The archway is structural and formal simultaneously.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: Void as structural element in dense wood; the tension between mass and opening (→ P-01, P-04).
- **Text reference**: No direct text citation; appears as comparative figure with abb. 99.
- **Source**: p.118

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: abb. 101 — Rudolf Belling, Dreiklang (1922), ein rein erhaltener, wenn auch perforierter Block
- **Shows**: A large abstract sculpture: a perforated spherical/organic form with multiple openings cut through its surface, creating an interpenetrating volumetric structure. The material reads as light, almost floating despite its mass, due to the perforations.

- **Text reference**: "der schwere materialblock, das undurchdringliche volumen, wandelt sich zu sphärischer ausdehnung, wird vollkommen massen- und gewichtslos, schwereloses schwingen von volumenbeziehungen und »durchdringungen«."

**FIGURE [Fig-05]**: abb. 102 — Alexander Archipenko, "Medrano" (1914), aus Glas, Holz und Metall montiert
- **Shows**: A standing figurative assemblage constructed from cut glass, shaped wood elements, and metal components — the figure is clearly composed of discrete assembled pieces that retain their individual material identity. The background is flat black, making the assemblage's structure legible.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Montage-principle and material authenticity (→ P-02, P-03). The text explicitly addresses Archipenko's rejection of surface simulation: wood for wood, metal for metal.
- **Text reference**: "archipenkos vorstoss mit der montierten plastik war eine absage an die kunstgewerblicke verwertung des materials."

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: abb. 103 — Das Gerippe eines Luftschiffes / The skeleton of an airship
- **Shows**: Interior construction photograph of a dirigible airship skeleton under construction in a large hangar. The structural skeleton — a lattice of ribs, longerons, and diagonal bracing in a vast curved form — is photographed from within, with artificial lighting making the construction legible as a spatial framework rather than a solid form.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Technische Montage as ökonomisches Arbeitsprinzip (→ P-03). The caption states explicitly: "die technische montage als ökonomisches arbeitsprinzip beeinflusste grundlegend die neue plastische gestaltung." The image serves as the industrial prototype for the new plastic form-principle: structure visible, nothing redundant, economy of means is the aesthetic.

- **Shows**: An open wire/rod construction forming an elliptical frame with interior diagonal and radial elements — completely open, weightless in appearance, the construction principle fully legible from every angle. The form has no solid planes; it is pure structural relationship.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The fully perforated and open construction (→ P-04); anticipates the Gleichgewichtskonstruktion exercises. The text states: "die völlig perforierte, völlig durchbrochene plastik fordert einerseits ein entwickeltes, technisches können, andererseits eine abstrahierende geistigkeit."

- **Text reference**: No direct text citation on this page; appears as part of the figure series on constructed/assembled plastik.

**FIGURE [Fig-09]**: abb. 106 — Leo Grewenig (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1924), Plastik aus Furnierholz
- **Shows**: A student work: a standing construction of thin veneer-wood strips bent and assembled into a linear, open form — essentially a three-dimensional drawing in wood. Two curved arcs rise from a minimal base; the form is completely open and reads as gesture-in-space rather than volume.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Bauhaus pedagogical application of the construction principle; the student is working toward the fully open form using a readily available, flexible material (veneer/Furnierholz). → P-04, P-03.
- **Text reference**: Caption identifies as first semester work 1924 — pedagogical context established.
- **Source**: p.123

**FIGURE [Fig-10]**: abb. 107 — L. Moholy-Nagy, 1920, Relief 5
- **Shows**: A relief construction mounted on a wall: mixed-material assemblage including a large flat black square, a cylindrical metallic rod/post element, a letter-form "S" in metal or heavy cardboard, a circular clock-like element at upper right, a sphere on a rod, and a rectangular metal bracket. The combination of elements at varying depths creates shadow patterns.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Moholy-Nagy's own exploration of depth-and-shadow effects (Höhen- und Tiefenwirkungen) using mixed materials. The text explains: "ursprünglich waren diese arbeiten aus interesse für tiefen- und höhenwirkungen in der zusammenfügung von verschiedenen materialien entstanden. die höhen- und tiefenwirkungen waren — wenn man sie mit heutigem bewusstsein sieht — mittel zur erzielung von licht- und schattenwirkungen."
- **Text reference**: "erst dann war ein solches relief in seiner art wirklich fassbar, wenn die vorspringenden (hier u. and. ein auf einem metallstabskelett montierter, mehrere zentimeter vorspringender glasstreifen) in scharfer beleuchtung vielfaltige schatten auf dem grund erzeugten."
- **Source**: p.124

**FIGURE [Fig-11]**: abb. 108 — L. Moholy-Nagy, 1921, montierte Plastik (aus maschinenbear beitetem Holz)
- **Shows**: A constructed sculpture of machined/machine-worked wood: a vertical rectangular column combined with a large curved arc element — a quarter-circle of wood — balanced against each other on a low plinth. The two elements are geometrically precise; one vertical, one curved. The construction principle is visible.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Montage as construction method; machine-processing (maschinenbear beitetem) of material as Factur appropriate to the work's Konstruktion logic. The text refers to the Renaissance woodworker's anecdote: "von dem renaissance-bildhauer erzählt man, daß er seine plastiken so klar im material — z. b. im marmorblock — eingesangen sah, daß er diese daraus mit einigen schlägen ,befreien' konnte." The modern method is opposed: the organic effect grows from the relationships of components.
- **Text reference**: "die heutige methode ist dem entgegengesetzt: materialien werden zu einem plastischen gefüge zusammenmontiert. die organische wirkung wächst aus den zusammenhängen der einzelteile."
- **Source**: p.125

**FIGURE [Fig-12]**: abb. 109 — L. Moholy-Nagy, 1921/22, zwei Konstruktionssysteme zusammengefügt (aus verschiedenen metallen: stahl, kupfer, nickel, aluminium, zink und kristallglas montiert)
- **Shows**: A complex multi-material construction: two distinct structural systems assembled together — vertical and horizontal elements in different metals (steel, copper, nickel, aluminum, zinc) with crystal glass components. The assembly creates spatial layers with varying reflectivities.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The combination of two independent structural systems into a unity through assembly; the text argues this demonstrates the principle of three-dimensionality at its most basic (→ P-08). Multiple mirroring (mehrfache Spiegelung) should mediate the illusion between architecture and plastic sculpture.
- **Text reference**: "das ganze ist — soweit es die dreidimensionalität betrifft — auf ein grundmassl gestellt. dieser versuch demonstriert gleichzeitig das ganze gebiet zwischen architektur und plastik (s. seite 200). wenn der versuch so weitergeführt wird, daß zu den vorhandenen zwei plastiken immer neue in beziehung gesetzt werden, entstehen räumliche ordnungen. (s. seite 195)"
- **Source**: p.126

**FIGURE [Fig-13]**: abb. 110 — L. Moholy-Nagy, 1921, Nickelplastik
- **Shows**: A freestanding nickel construction: a vertical rectangular post from which a large nickel spiral/coil descends in a helical form, ending at a small square base with a glass element. The nickel surface is highly reflective; the spiral form implies rotation and movement through a static object.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: New artificial material (Nickel) used for its own Factur — reflectivity and precision (→ P-11); the spiral implies Bewegung (movement) in a static form — prefiguration of kinetic plastic.
- **Text reference**: No direct text citation on this page; appears in the sequence of Moholy-Nagy's own works.
- **Source**: p.127

**FIGURE [Fig-14]**: abb. 111 — L. Moholy-Nagy, 1923, Konstruktion aus opak-, matt- und kristallspiegelglas, metall und vulkanfiber
- **Shows**: A transparent and reflective construction: flat planes of opaque, matte, and crystal-mirror glass combined with metal rods and vulcanfiber elements. The fan-like glass planes at the base catch and distribute light; the vertical metal elements rise above. The work interacts with light as a primary material — reflections and transparencies coexist.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The pedestal-liberation effort (→ P-07); the use of glass for its transparency and reflectivity as structural-visual material rather than surface decoration. The text uses this as the example for the new factor: "die bemühung, vom sockel loszukommen."
- **Text reference**: "ein neuer faktor tritt zu den bisherigen hinzu: die bemühung, vom sockel loszukommen. (siehe abb. 95, 112, 117, 125, 126, 133.)"
- **Source**: p.128

**FIGURE [Fig-15]**: abb. 112 — Pablo Picasso, 1920, Plastik (klischee: cahiers d'art)
- **Shows**: A standing mixed-material sculpture: two large circular disc forms (one dark/opaque, one lighter/transparent) mounted on a tripod base with a small rectangular face-mask element at the intersection. The work combines different surface values (matte vs. reflective) and opposes geometric forms at a human scale.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Part of the pedestal-liberation series (cited explicitly at p.128 in the list of abb. for the "Bemühung, vom Sockel loszukommen" → P-07); also demonstrates multi-material assembly at the level of fine art sculpture rather than student exercise.
- **Text reference**: Listed explicitly in the abb. sequence at p.128.
- **Source**: p.129

**FIGURE [Fig-16]**: abb. 113 — Joost Schmidt, 1928, Vergleichsstudie: positives und negatives Kegelvolumen (konvex und konkavkörper, vollkegel und hohlkegelstumpf)
- **Shows**: Four formal studies in spherical/conical volume: a convex sphere, a concave sphere (hollow), a complete cone, and a hollow truncated cone — displayed together as a comparative study of positive and negative volumetric forms. The photograph is studio-lit to maximize the reading of convexity vs. concavity.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Laboratory-style investigation of the individual elements of plastic form; the positive-negative relationship at the volumetric scale (→ P-01). The text describes this as "eine fast wissenschaftliche untersuchung der einzelelemente" (a near-scientific investigation of individual elements) following the general argument that the conquest of the full wealth of plastic expression proceeds as systematic elemental study.
- **Text reference**: "eine laboratoriumsarbeit setzt ein: die fast wissenschaftliche untersuchung der einzelelemente."
- **Source**: p.130

**FIGURE [Fig-17]**: abb. 114 — Georges Vantongerloo, 1917, Konstruktion in einer Kugel (die Kugelschale ist abgelöst)
- **Shows**: A white sculptural form: the shell of a sphere has been removed (abgelöst), revealing the internal construction — an organic, multi-lobed form with multiple perforations, showing the movement possibilities within a sphere realized as a static material form.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: A new proposition — "ein neuer vorstoss: die bewegungsmöglichkeiten innerhalb einer kugel sind im material erstarrt, in die statische form projiziert." The possibilities of movement within a sphere are frozen in material and projected into static form. This is a key bridge concept toward kinetic plastic.
- **Text reference**: "ein neuer vorstoss: die bewegungsmöglichkeiten innerhalb einer kugel sind im material erstarrt, in die statische form projiziert gezeigt."
- **Source**: p.131

**FIGURE [Fig-18]**: abb. 115 — Die Ausstellung der russischen Konstruktivisten in Moskau 1921 (foto: buch neuer künstler)
- **Shows**: Installation view of the Russian Constructivist exhibition, Moscow 1921. Multiple open wire and rod constructions of varying scales fill the room — angular, open, asymmetric structures standing freely on minimal bases. The works by various constructivists (identifiable from the context as Rodchenko, Johansen, and others) demonstrate the full range of the Konstruktive Plastik approach.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The school of Konstruktive Plastik at its historical moment (→ P-08). The text explicitly addresses the strengths and limitations of the movement: it was an Übergangsstadium that served to give art a new scale from the side of industrial forms, even if dominated by technical monomania.
- **Text reference**: "die schulbegriffe der mechanik, dynamik, statik, kinetik, die probleme der stabilität, des gleichgewichts wurden durch die plastische form erörtert."
- **Source**: p.132

**FIGURE [Fig-19]**: abb. 116 — Johansen, Moskau 1921, Gleichgewichtskonstruktion (zieht man an dem faden, gerät die plastik in eine andere, gleich wie die erste, ausbalancierte stellung)
- **Shows**: A wire/rod construction on a minimal base — the caption describes a unique mechanical property: pulling a thread causes the sculpture to move into a different position that is equally balanced as the first. The form shows crossed diagonals and angled elements in tension.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Gleichgewicht as a multi-stable structural law (→ P-08, P-10); the construction can inhabit multiple equilibrium positions — this is an early move toward kinetic plastic.
- **Text reference**: Caption directly describes the mechanical function.
- **Source**: p.133

**FIGURE [Fig-20]**: abb. 117 — Johannes Zabel (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1923), Gleichgewichtskonstruktion (auf einer kleinen Fläche ausbalanciert)
- **Shows**: A Bauhaus first-semester student equilibrium construction: long thin elements (metal or wood rods) arranged in a strong diagonal cantilever from a small base. One long horizontal element projects far to the right, counterweighted by other elements. The construction is photographed against a dark background — the shadows are part of the visual field.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The Bauhaus equilibrium exercise (→ E-01, E-02, P-10). The diagonal cantilever achieves balance through precise weight distribution; the student has learned to treat weight calculation as a design variable.
- **Text reference**: "der gestrige künstler kümmerte sich wenig um die exakte gewichtsberechnung seiner arbeit." — The Bauhaus counters this through this type of exercise.
- **Source**: p.134

**FIGURE [Fig-21]**: abb. 118 — Hermann Röseler (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1924), Gleichgewichtskonstruktion (mit einem locker aufgehängten Pendel)
- **Shows**: A Bauhaus first-semester balance construction with a loosely hanging pendulum element — a slender upright with a disk element at top, a pendulum hanging freely from a cross-arm, and the whole balanced on a very small base.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The pendulum-balance variant of the equilibrium exercise (→ E-01, P-10); the new artificial materials enter with this type of work — the caption marks: "die neuen künstlichen materialien treten auf."
- **Text reference**: Caption and the text note at p.135.
- **Source**: p.135

**FIGURE [Fig-22]**: abb. 119 — N. Gabo, 1926, Konstruktion in Holz, Metall und Zelluloid
- **Shows**: A tall vertical construction combining wood, metal, and celluloid — a structure with flat planes and curved transparent surfaces creating both opacity and transparency at different angles. The celluloid planes interact with light to create luminous surface effects.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: New artificial materials (Zelluloid) for their transparency and lightness (→ P-11); the vertical extension without mass (→ P-04).
- **Text reference**: No direct text citation; part of the "neue künstliche Materialien" visual sequence.
- **Source**: p.135

**FIGURE [Fig-23]**: abb. 120 — N. Gabo, 1924, Plastik
- **Shows**: A Gabo construction: a large elliptical ring form supported on a curved base structure, with a half-disk element inside the ring creating spatial division, and a small crystalline element below. The whole is in metal and possibly glass — precise, engineered forms.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: The fully constructed form deriving from engineering logic and material precision (→ P-08, P-11).
- **Text reference**: No direct text citation on this page.
- **Source**: p.136

**FIGURE [Fig-24]**: abb. 121 — N. Gabo, 1925, Entwurf für ein Denkmal vor einem Observatorium (Zelluloid)
- **Shows**: A complex spiraling construction in celluloid — multiple interlocking spiral planes and rectangular elements rising from a base, with the transparent celluloid planes creating layered spatial complexity. Designed as a monument/memorial for an observatory — the spiraling form implies movement and cosmic scale.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: Celluloid as a material whose transparency makes spatial layering visible (→ P-11, P-04); the monument-scale ambition of the constructed plastic form approaching architectural scale. The spiral implies the Bewegung that will become explicit in kinetic plastic.
- **Text reference**: The text at p.137 notes: "die eigenschaften des materials, sein verhalten auf zug und druck werden — vielleicht ohne deutliche absicht — durch vielfältige beanspruchung geprüft."
- **Source**: p.137

**FIGURE [Fig-25]**: abb. 122 — Pablo Picasso, 1928, Entwurf zu einer Plastik in Eisendraht
- **Shows**: A wire-sculpture design by Picasso: a tall vertical structure of bent iron wire forming a complex spatial drawing — a circular element near the top, intersecting diagonal and vertical lines, a decorative crown element, all rising from a flat base. The work is entirely linear — no planes, only edges in space.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: Wire as drawing-in-space, the extreme reduction of sculptural form to pure line and intersection (→ P-04); proximity to the fully open construction.
- **Text reference**: No direct text citation on this page.
- **Source**: p.138

**FIGURE [Fig-26]**: abb. 123 — Anni Wildberg (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1924), Konstruktion (Metall und Glas)
- **Shows**: A Bauhaus student construction in metal and glass: angular flat planes of glass and metal elements assembled in a dynamic diagonal composition — the glass elements create transparency within the construction while the metal provides structural edges. Photographed with strong directional lighting to show the glass transparencies.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Bauhaus application of new artificial materials in the equilibrium/construction exercise (→ E-03, P-11). Multi-material construction where each material contributes its own Factur.
- **Text reference**: Student work from first semester 1924 — pedagogical context established.
- **Source**: p.139

**FIGURE [Fig-27]**: abb. 124 — Johannes Zabel (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1923), Gleichgewichtsstudie
- **Shows**: A horizontal balance construction on a flat base: elements cantilevered at extreme horizontal extension, with the whole assembly achieving equilibrium through careful weight distribution. The photograph emphasizes the dramatic cantilever and the small footprint of the base.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The dissolution of the block tendency (Auflockerungstendenz) increasing toward full transparency (→ P-10, E-02). The text notes: "bei diesen und folgenden beispielen ist auffallend die immer deutlicher werdende auflösungs-tendenz des blocks, die sich bis zur völligen durchsichtigkeit steigert."
- **Text reference**: "bei diesen und folgenden beispielen ist auffallend die immer deutlicher werdende auflösungs-tendenz des blocks, die sich bis zur völligen durchsichtigkeit steigert. (s. abb. 139)"
- **Source**: p.140

**FIGURE [Fig-28]**: abb. 125 — Gerhard Spitzer (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1925), Durchdringungen (Holz, Glas)
- **Shows**: A Bauhaus student construction: two large flat rectangular planes of wood and glass intersecting (Durchdringungen) at angles, mounted on a circular turntable base. The glass plane creates transparency; the wood plane opacity — their intersection creates a spatial event at the crossing point.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Durchdringung (interpenetration) as formal operation with mixed materials (→ P-04, P-05, E-03); the Auflockerungstendenz fully present.
- **Text reference**: Part of the series cited for the pedestal-liberation effort (abb. 125 listed in P-07 reference list).
- **Source**: p.141

**FIGURE [Fig-29]**: abb. 126 — Heinz Tinzmann (Bauhaus, erstes Semester 1923), Konstruktion (Holz, Glas)
- **Shows**: A Bauhaus first-semester construction: a long horizontal element of wood crossed by a vertical glass plane, creating a cross-shaped spatial structure mounted on a low base. The clean intersection of horizontal wood and vertical glass demonstrates material contrast — opacity vs. transparency — at their crossing.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: Same principle as abb. 125 — Durchdringung and new materials (→ P-04, P-05, E-03). The simplicity of the two-element crossing emphasizes the governing principle.
- **Text reference**: Listed in the pedestal-liberation series (abb. 126 in P-07 reference list).
- **Source**: p.141
