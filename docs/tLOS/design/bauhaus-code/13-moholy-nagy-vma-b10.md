## IV. Der Raum (Architektur): Raumgestaltung · Das Biologische als Regulator · Schluss

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The highest stage of spatial design is not the organization of walls or volumes but the biological regulation of space — the creation of environments that serve the full sensory and bodily life of the human being. The dissolution of the wall through glass and structural transparency is not an aesthetic preference but an epistemological necessity: the interior and exterior must interpenetrate so that the human organism can orient itself in the total spatial continuum.

**Evidence**: Photographs of theatrical stage spaces (Gabo/Pevsner, Walter Gropius's Piscatorbühne model, Moholy-Nagy's Staatsoper Hoffmann set), exhibition architecture (AHAG Zehlendorf 1928), the Bauhaus Dessau building, industrial structures (generator, cooling towers, elevator shaft, subway tunnel, chemical plant, factory in Detroit, electrical substation), Le Corbusier's Wohnhaus, and photographic experiments showing aerial views (New York intersection, Chicago Tribune construction, rooftop sunbathing) and architectural penetration (abb.209, double-exposed "Architektur").

**Conclusion**: All training in Material, Surface, Volume, and Space culminates here: architecture is the total biological regulator of human existence. The new architecture does not enclose — it organizes flowing, interpenetrating space. Industrial construction has already achieved this unconsciously; conscious design must now achieve it deliberately.

## Das biologische als regulator schlechthin

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: Spatial design (Raumgestaltung) at its highest level is a biological regulator — it does not merely arrange walls but creates the conditions for human life at its fullest spatial, sensory, and social expression.

**Evidence**: The argument moves from theatrical space (where light, transparency, and movement are already used freely) through exhibition architecture to the full architectural project. The biological metaphor is explicit: the body needs spatial relationships (Raumverhältnisse) not merely visual ones; the new architecture must be experienced as a living organism navigates its environment.

**Conclusion**: The architect who has worked through Material → Surface → Volume arrives at Raumgestaltung not as a set of compositional options but as a biological imperative. The boundaries between inside and outside dissolve; the human being is placed in flowing, uninterrupted spatial experience.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Raumgestaltung als biologischer Regulator / Spatial design as biological regulator
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The final and highest task of architecture is to be "die letzte und höchste stufe der raumgestaltung" — a biological regulator. It does not primarily determine aesthetic pleasure but organizes the spatial experience of living biological beings. Architecture must become a direct instrument of human flourishing, not a formal exercise.
- **Rule**: Design every spatial environment as a biological system for human movement, light, air, and social interaction — never as a composition of walls.
- **Source**: p.222
- **Quote (DE)**: "die letzte und höchste stufe der raumgestaltung ist offensichtlich ihre erfassung vom biologisch möglichen her."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The final and highest stage of spatial design is evidently its apprehension from the standpoint of the biologically possible."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Raumverhältnisse als primäre Aufgabe / Spatial relationships as primary task
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The architect's concern is not the plastic form of individual elements but the spatial relationships (Raumverhältnisse) between them. The student must learn to see not the wall but the void the wall creates and bounds; not the column but the interval between columns.
- **Rule**: At every stage of design, define the spatial relationships first; the physical elements are only their instruments.
- **Source**: p.222
- **Quote (DE)**: "es kommt nicht auf eine 'plastische', bewegte außengestaltung an, sondern nur auf die raumverhältnisse, die die für einen gestaltungsnotigen erlebnissinhalte festlegen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "What matters is not a 'plastic', animated exterior design, but only the spatial relationships that fix the experiential contents necessary for design."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Bewegte Begrenzung / Flowing boundary — dissolution of the fixed wall
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The traditional wall — the opaque, fixed boundary — is the enemy of spatial continuity. The new architecture dissolves the wall into a permeable membrane: glass, lattice, transparent or translucent surfaces. The interior and exterior are no longer separated but become a single spatial continuum experienced through movement.
- **Rule**: Wherever possible, replace the opaque wall with a transparent or semi-transparent boundary so that inner and outer space interpenetrate.
- **Source**: p.222 (argument), p.221 (Bauhaus Dessau glass facade, abb.193)
- **Quote (DE)**: "aus dem kosmischen raum wird ein 'stück raum' durch ein — manchmal kompliziert erscheinendes — gewebe von begrenzungen und durchdringungen. bändern, drähten, gläsern herausgeschnitten, wie wenn der raum ein teilbares, kompaktes objekt wäre. so entsteht die neue architektur in völliger durchdringung mit dem außenraum."
- **Quote (EN)**: "From cosmic space a 'piece of space' is cut out through a — sometimes apparently complex — web of boundaries and interpenetrations: bands, wires, glass, as if space were a divisible, compact object. In this way the new architecture arises in complete interpenetration with exterior space."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Durchdringung / Interpenetration as architectural principle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Interpenetration (Durchdringung) is not a decorative effect — it is the structural principle of modern spatial organization. When interior and exterior interpenetrate, the distinction between inside and outside dissolves. The Bauhaus Dessau building (abb.193) demonstrates this: in the mirroring of the glass facade, inner and outer merge and the old massiveness of the wall — "das außen" — has dissolved.
- **Rule**: Design buildings so that interior space and exterior space visually and physically penetrate each other; the facade must be a membrane, not a barrier.
- **Source**: p.221 (abb.193), p.222
- **Quote (DE)**: "innen und außen durchdringen einander in der spiegelung der fenster. das auseinanderhalten der beiden ist nicht mehr möglich. die masse der wand, woran alles 'außen' bisher zerbrach, hat sich aufgelöst und läßt die umgebung in das gebäude fließen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Inside and outside interpenetrate each other in the reflection of the windows. Keeping the two apart is no longer possible. The mass of the wall, against which everything 'outside' formerly broke, has dissolved and allows the surroundings to flow into the building."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Flüssige Raumgliederung / Fluid spatial articulation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Spatial articulation must be fluid — walls, floors, and ceilings are no longer fixed compartments but elements that guide, channel, and direct the flow of human movement through space. The comparison to liquid is precise: space flows like water, and architecture is its vessel — not a container that imprisons but a form that shapes the flow.
- **Rule**: Articulate space through guiding surfaces and implied boundaries rather than hard enclosures; allow the visitor's movement to complete the spatial experience.
- **Source**: p.222
- **Quote (DE)**: "die grenzen werden flüssig, der raum wird im fluge gefaßt: gewaltige zahl von beziehungen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The boundaries become fluid, space is grasped in flight: an immense number of relationships."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Das Flugzeug als neues Sehorgan / The airplane as new visual organ
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The airplane is not merely transport — it is a new sense organ for spatial understanding. Aerial perspective gives the architect what the ground-level view withholds: a simultaneous grasp of the totality of spatial organization. The aerial view of a New York street intersection, a Chicago building under construction, industrial cooling towers seen from above — these reveal the spatial logic of built form that is invisible from the ground.
- **Rule**: Train spatial perception through aerial photography and bird's-eye views; the Neue Sehen of the airplane is an architectural tool, not merely a curiosity.
- **Source**: p.222, p.223 (abb.194), p.225 (abb.196), p.227 (abb.198)
- **Quote (DE)**: "das flugzeug hat in diesem zusammenhang eine besondere aufgabe: vom aeroplan aus tun sich neue sichten auf. (abb. 194–198) aber das wesentlichste für uns ist die flugzeugssicht, das vollere raumerlebnis, weil es alle gestige architekturvorstellung verändert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The airplane has a special role in this context: from the airplane new views open up. (abb. 194–198) But the most essential thing for us is the airplane view, the fuller spatial experience, because it changes all intellectual architectural conception."
- **Category**: perception

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Industrieformen als unbewußte Architektur / Industrial forms as unconscious architecture
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The engineer's structures — the elevator shaft photographed by O. Firle (abb.199), the subway tunnel (abb.200), the chemical factory containers (abb.201), the exhaust chimneys (abb.202), the Detroit car factory (abb.203), the electrical substation (abb.204) — are not architecture in the conscious sense, yet they have already solved the problem of spatial organization through function alone. They are models not for imitation but for the proof that spatial order emerges from constructive logic, not from applied style.
- **Rule**: Study industrial and engineering structures as proof of principle: functional construction generates spatial experience without aesthetic intention; the architect must do the same with intention.
- **Source**: pp.228–232 (abb.199–204)
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Die Fassade verschwindet / The facade disappears
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The concept of the "facade" — a frontal presentation surface for a building — is obsolete in the new architecture. When all sides of a building become transparent membranes, when the roof becomes a terrace and sun-terrace (abb.195), when the building is seen from above as well as below, the hierarchy of front/back/side collapses. Every surface is a spatial boundary, not a representational face.
- **Rule**: Design buildings without a privileged facade: every surface must function as a spatial boundary and every view — including from above — must be considered.
- **Source**: p.224
- **Quote (DE)**: "der begriff 'fassade' verschwindet bereits in der architektur. an dem gebäude bleibt keine stelle, die nicht als träger einer funktion ausgenützt würde. so kommt jetzt zu der frontalen ausbildung (balkone, lichtreklame) auch die ausbildung der obersichten (dachgarten, flugzeugstationen)."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The concept of 'facade' is already disappearing in architecture. No part of a building remains that is not exploited as the carrier of a function. So now alongside the frontal articulation (balconies, illuminated signs) comes the articulation of the upper views (roof gardens, airplane stations)."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Spiegelflächen und räumliche Tiefe / Mirror surfaces and spatial depth
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Mirror surfaces and the reflective properties of glass multiply space. The Neue Sehen teaches that transparent and reflective surfaces destroy the apparent solidity of architecture and reveal the spatial relationships that lie behind every material surface. Every transparency multiplies the spatial experience: though a surface is flat (Fläche), the associative activity of the brain constructs depth — vorne und hinten, hoch und tief.
- **Rule**: Use transparent and reflective surfaces not as decoration but as spatial multipliers — each transparency adds a spatial layer to the experience of the room.
- **Source**: p.220
- **Quote (DE)**: "durchsichten und durchdringungen, jede spiegelschicht steigert — obwohl sie fläche ist — durch die associative tätigkeit unseres gehirns das vorne und hinten, das hoch und tief: die räumliche wirkung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Transparencies and interpenetrations, every mirror layer intensifies — even though it is a flat surface — through the associative activity of our brain the sense of front and back, high and low: the spatial effect."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Das Biologische als oberster Regulator / The biological as supreme regulator
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: All formal and constructive principles ultimately serve one purpose: the biological wellbeing of the human being in space. The phrase "das biologische als regulator schlechthin" (the biological as regulator pure and simple) is the culminating principle of the entire book. Neither style, nor beauty, nor technical efficiency is the final criterion — it is the question of whether the spatial environment supports or damages the full biological existence of the person who inhabits it.
- **Rule**: In every spatial design decision, apply the biological criterion last and absolutely: does this space support human life at its fullest biological expression?
- **Source**: p.222
- **Quote (DE)**: "das biologische als regulator schlechthin"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The biological as regulator pure and simple"
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Koordination aller Elemente / Coordination of all elements
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The opera, theater, and stage — already liberated from purely literary dependencies — point toward the architectural synthesis. The coordination of all elements (light, volume, movement, sound, transparent surface) to a single spatial experience is the model for architecture as Gesamtwerk. Moholy-Nagy cites his own essay on "theater, zirkus, varieté" (Bauhaus-Bücher, vol.4) as the theoretical ground for this spatial synthesis.
- **Rule**: Conceive every spatial environment as a coordination of all experiential elements simultaneously — light, movement, material surface, sound — never as the sum of independently designed parts.
- **Source**: p.219
- **Quote (DE)**: "es scheint, daß aus all diesen erkenntnissen des materials, volumens und raumes von allen ausdrucksgebieten das meiste in der nächsten zukunft das teater gewinnen wird. nach einer sackgasse des rein literarischen — wie in der oper nach dem primat des akustischen — wird die koordination aller elemente einen entscheidenden ruck vorwärts geben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It appears that of all expressive domains, the theater will gain the most in the near future from all these insights into material, volume, and space. After a dead end of the purely literary — as in opera after the primacy of the acoustic — the coordination of all elements will give a decisive forward thrust."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Neue Phase der Raumbesitznahme / New phase of spatial possession
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Zeiss planetarium skeleton (abb.208) represents a new phase in spatial possession (Raumbesitznahme): a human chain climbing a swaying, transparent net — a floating staircase in the air. This is a new form of spatial experience: the body suspended in a semi-transparent structure that neither fully encloses nor fully exposes, where the spatial experience is created by the structural lattice itself rather than by any enclosing wall.
- **Rule**: Conceive spatial structures that do not enclose but suspend the human being within a spatial field — the lattice, net, and open frame are legitimate architectural elements when they define space without imprisoning it.
- **Source**: p.235
- **Quote (DE)**: "eine neue fase der besitznahme von raum: eine menschenstaffel in schwebend durchsichtigem netz, wie eine flugzeugstaffel im äter."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A new phase of the possession of space: a human relay team in a swaying transparent net, like an airplane squadron in the ether."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Glasarchitektur als Raumillusion / Glass architecture as spatial illusion
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The double-exposed photomontage "Architektur" (abb.209) — made from two superimposed negatives — creates the illusion of spatial interpenetration that the next generation of architects will perhaps actually build. The photograph is not documentation of existing architecture but a projection of possible architecture: Glasarchitektur in its fullest form, where solid and void, interior and exterior, become indistinguishable.
- **Rule**: Use photographic and graphic experiments as projective tools for architectural ideas that cannot yet be built — the image precedes and enables the construction.
- **Source**: p.236
- **Quote (DE)**: "aus zwei übereinander kopierten fotos (negativ) entsteht die illusion räumlicher durchdringung, wie die nächste generation sie erst — als glasarchitektur — in der wirklichkeit vielleicht erleben wird."
- **Quote (EN)**: "From two photographs superimposed (negative) arises the illusion of spatial interpenetration, as the next generation will perhaps first experience it in reality — as glass architecture."
- **Category**: space

### Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Erkennung industrieller Raumformen / Recognition of industrial spatial forms
- **Task** [INFERRED]: The student studies photographs of industrial and engineering structures — elevator shafts, tunnels, factory complexes, electrical installations — and identifies the spatial principles at work: interpenetration, transparency, structural lattice, aerial organization. The task is perceptual re-education: learning to see spatial organization where only "industrial necessity" is usually seen.
- **Purpose**: Trains the eye to recognize unconscious architectural solutions in engineering, establishing the principle that spatial quality derives from constructive logic (Konstruktion), not from style. Prepares the student for conscious architectural application of the same principles.
- **Source**: pp.228–232 (abb.199–204)

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Flugzeugperspektive als Raumstudium / Aerial perspective as spatial study
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: The student examines aerial photographs of city intersections, building sites, and industrial complexes and derives spatial principles invisible from the ground: overall spatial organization, the rhythm of repeated structural units, the relationship between building and void seen as a total pattern. This connects to the Neue Sehen training from earlier in the book.
- **Purpose**: Expands spatial perception beyond the pedestrian viewpoint; establishes that Raumgestaltung must account for all possible viewpoints including those made available by modern technology (airplane, camera).
- **Source**: p.222 (argument), pp.223–227 (abb.194–198)

**EXERCISE [E-03]**: Bühnenraum als Raumlaboratorium / Stage space as spatial laboratory
- **Task** [INFERRED]: The stage is used as a controlled laboratory for total spatial design: light, transparent surfaces, movement, and three-dimensional structures are combined and varied. The student observes or participates in theatrical spatial experiments (such as Gabo/Pevsner's "le chat" ballet design, Gropius's Piscatorbühne, Moholy-Nagy's own Hoffmann opera staging) to understand how all spatial elements can be coordinated.
- **Purpose**: Trains the capacity for Koordination aller Elemente — the synthesis that architecture requires. The stage is a spatial model because it allows rapid experimentation with light, transparency, and movement that would be impossible in permanent construction.
- **Source**: pp.217–219 (abb.189–191)
