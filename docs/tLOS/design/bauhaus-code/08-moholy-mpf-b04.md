## Section: Neue Sehen — The Camera's Eight Modes (continued) / Lichtgestaltung — Light as Plastic Medium

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The camera does not merely record the world — it reveals a world invisible to the unaided eye. Every image in this sequence demonstrates one of the modes of Neue Sehen: oblique angle, extreme close-up, long-exposure time-inscription, astronomical distance, electrical discharge photography, X-ray penetration, and finally the Photogramm — the purest form of Lichtgestaltung, made without a camera at all.

**Evidence**: A sequence of twenty plates spanning five categories of optical extension: (1) new angles and close-ups of familiar objects (sand, gramophone record); (2) time-based light inscription (night traffic, lightning); (3) scientific photography beyond the visible spectrum (star spectra, astronomical photography); (4) X-ray photography revealing structure invisible to the eye; (5) the Photogramm as direct light-construction. The section closes with Ludwig Hirschfeld-Mack's written score (Partitur) for the Reflektorische Farbenspiele — kinetic light-color performance — as evidence that Lichtgestaltung extends into time-based performance.

**Conclusion**: Each image proves that Produktion — images that exist only because of the medium's specific capacities — is not a special category but the norm toward which all serious photographic work tends. The X-ray and the Photogramm are the terminal cases: they make visible what no eye and no conventional camera could show. The artist's obligation is to master these capacities, not to imitate painting.

## Subsection A: Neue Sehen — Oblique Angle and Reorientation

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Umwertung des Sehens / Revaluation of Seeing
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The oblique photograph — shot from an unconventional angle or position — is not a technical error or a stylistic trick. It is an instruction to the eye. When a familiar scene is photographed from an angle that makes the image rotatable, that generates new readings with each rotation, the image becomes a device for training a new kind of attention. What was once classified as "distortion" (Verzeichnung) is now understood as an "astonishing experience" (verblüffendes Erlebnis).
- **Rule**: Shoot from angles that make the image's orientation ambiguous; a photograph that yields new compositions when rotated has achieved the revaluation of seeing.
- **Source**: p.59
- **Quote (DE)**: "Was früher als Verzeichnung galt, ist heute verblüffendes Erlebnis! Aufforderung zur Umwertung des Sehens. Dieses Bild ist drehbar. Es ergibt immer neue Sichten."
- **Quote (EN)**: "What was formerly considered distortion is today an astonishing experience! A summons to the revaluation of seeing. This image is rotatable. It always yields new views."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Gesteigerte Realität des Alltäglichen / Heightened Reality of the Everyday
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The extreme close-up of a familiar object — a gramophone record shot so that the grooves become a vast landscape, the label a distant disc — produces what I call the "heightened reality of the everyday" (gesteigerte Realität des Alltäglichen). This is not beautification. It is optical intensification: the close-up excavates from the ordinary object a visual fact that normal distance conceals. The result is simultaneously document and design — a "fertige Plakat" (finished poster) requiring no further composition.
- **Rule**: The extreme close-up of an ordinary object is not illustration — it is the discovery of a hidden composition already present in the object's surface. Photograph it until it becomes unrecognizable, then stop: that is the image.
- **Source**: p.60
- **Quote (DE)**: "Gesteigerte Realität des Alltäglichen. Ein fertiges Plakat."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Heightened reality of the everyday. A finished poster."
- **Category**: neue-sehen | production-reproduction

## Subsection B: Lichtschrift — Light Writing in Time (Long Exposure)

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Lichtschrift / Light Writing — Time-Based Inscription
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The long-exposure night photograph of moving vehicles records not the vehicles but the light they leave behind — white filaments tracing arcs across a dark urban geometry. This is Lichtschrift: the camera's shutter held open allows moving light sources to write their own trajectories. The result is an image that no eye ever saw — the accumulation of successive moments into a single spatial field. The eye sees a tram at one moment; the camera sees the entire path of the tram across time.
- **Rule**: Use long exposure to make time visible as spatial trace — the image produced is not a record of an event but a record of an event's duration.
- **Source**: p.61
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Lichtspuren der vorbeifahrenden Autos und Straßenbahnen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The light trails of the passing automobiles and streetcars."
- **Category**: neue-sehen | light | production-reproduction

## Subsection C: Wissenschaftliche Fotografie — Scientific Photography Beyond Visible Spectrum

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Fotografische Erweiterung des Sichtbaren / Photographic Extension of the Visible
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Scientific photography — star spectra, astronomical long-exposure images, electrical discharge photography — extends the visible spectrum in both directions: upward toward the cosmic (nebulae, star fields at distances no eye can resolve) and downward toward the instantaneous (lightning, electrical sparks lasting microseconds). These are not illustrations of science. They are images that exist only because of the camera's capacities: sensitivity to wavelengths beyond the eye's range, exposure times both vastly longer and vastly shorter than any human blink. Each is Produktion.
- **Rule**: Scientific photography is not a special application of the medium — it is the medium's clearest demonstration of its own nature. Include it alongside art photography; the distinction between "scientific" and "artistic" dissolves when both are understood as Neue Sehen.
- **Source**: pp.62–65

- **Quote (EN)**: [See captions below in Visual Evidence]

## Subsection D: Röntgenfotografie — X-Ray Photography as Lichtgestaltung

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Durchdringung des Körpers mit Licht / Penetration of the Body with Light
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: X-ray photography does not photograph surfaces. It photographs interiors. The camera-made image of a frog's skeleton, its bones luminous against a transparent body, reveals a structure invisible to the eye at any angle. This is the ultimate extension of Neue Sehen: not a new angle on the surface but a new dimension of penetration. The caption states it directly: "Die Durchdringung des Körpers mit Licht ist eines der größten Seherlebnisse." — the penetration of the body with light is one of the greatest visual experiences.
- **Rule**: X-ray photography is not medicine applied to art — it is the revelation that the body is a three-dimensional light-field, and the image of that field is a design problem of the first order.
- **Source**: p.67
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Durchdringung des Körpers mit Licht ist eines der größten Seherlebnisse."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The penetration of the body with light is one of the greatest visual experiences."
- **Category**: neue-sehen | light | production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: In Licht umgesetzte Materie / Matter Translated into Light
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The X-ray photograph of a Triton Tritonis shell achieves what I call "In Licht umgesetzte Materie" — matter translated into light. The shell's calcite structure, its spiral chambers, its translucent walls become a luminous architecture: white curves on black ground, form made entirely from the differential absorption of X-rays. This is Lichtgestaltung in its most literal sense. The sculptor works in stone; I work in light. The X-ray image is the record of that work.
- **Rule**: When matter is rendered as light-differential — as zones of opacity and translucency — the image is no longer a representation of the object but a light-composition of the object's internal structure.
- **Source**: p.68
- **Quote (DE)**: "In Licht umgesetzte Materie."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Matter translated into light."
- **Category**: light | production-reproduction | photogram

## Subsection E: Das Photogramm — Kameralose Aufnahme (Cameraless Photography)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Photogramm — the cameraless photograph, made by placing objects directly on light-sensitive paper and exposing to light — is the medium's Produktion in its purest form. It requires no camera, no lens, no conventional photographic apparatus. It is made entirely by the direct action of light on a sensitive surface, with objects as the tools of composition.

**Evidence**: Three of my own Photogramme (pp.69, 71–72, 74) plus two by Man Ray (pp.75–76) demonstrate the range of the technique. My spiral Photogramm shows the "Kontrastbeziehungen zwischen Schwarz-Weiß mit den feinsten Grauübergängen" — the precise gradient from full exposure to full shadow that is impossible to achieve by any other means. My geometric photogram (p.71) shows objects — a cross, rectangular forms, a mesh — overlapping in space and time: each object's shadow overlaps the others', creating a spatial composition of simultaneous exposures. Man Ray's work (pp.75–76) demonstrates that the same technique applied to organic objects (hand, egg) and mechanical objects (glass rods, crystal forms) yields entirely different visual results — proving the technique's range.

**Conclusion**: The Photogramm is not a gimmick or a curiosity. It is the camera-free demonstration that light is a plastic medium. Every Photogramm is by definition Produktion: the image could not exist in any other medium, under any other conditions. It is the Neue Sehen made absolute.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Das Photogramm als reine Lichtgestaltung / The Photogram as Pure Light Design
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Photogramm is a "Kameralose Aufnahme" — a cameraless recording. Objects are placed directly on photographic paper. Light strikes the paper where no object blocks it; the object's body casts its shadow; the edges of transparent or translucent objects create gradients. The result is an image whose tonal relationships — black, white, every gradation between — are determined entirely by the geometry of the objects and the direction of the light source. No lens distorts; no aperture limits; no shutter speed abbreviates. The image is the light itself.
- **Rule**: The Photogramm is the test of light-literacy: if you cannot compose with light alone — without camera, without lens — you have not yet understood that light is your primary material.
- **Source**: p.69
- **Quote (DE)**: "Kameralose Aufnahme. Die Kontrastbeziehungen zwischen Schwarz-Weiß mit den feinsten Grauübergängen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Cameraless recording. The contrast relationships between black and white with the finest grey gradations."
- **Category**: photogram | light | production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Photogramm und Simultanität / The Photogram and Simultaneity
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The geometric Photogramm (p.71) demonstrates Simultanität in a way that the conventional photograph cannot. Multiple objects — crosses, rectangles, mesh — are placed at different distances from the paper surface. Their shadows overlap. Each shadow modifies the others. The image contains all objects simultaneously, their spatial relationships compressed into a single tonal field. This is not montage (sequential assembly of separate images) but genuine simultaneity: all objects present at once, their optical relationships directly recorded.
- **Rule**: In Photogramm composition, place objects at multiple distances from the paper surface; the overlapping shadows will create a spatial depth-map that no single-plane composition can achieve.
- **Source**: p.71
- **Quote (DE)**: [No explicit text caption beyond "Kameralose Aufnahme. Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY"]
- **Quote (EN)**: [Cameraless recording. Photogram: MOHOLY-NAGY]
- **Category**: photogram | simultaneity | light

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Organisierte Licht- und Schattenbeziehungen / Organized Light and Shadow Relations
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Photogramm of the disc and sphere (p.72) demonstrates what I mean by "organisierte Licht- und Schattenbeziehungen" — organized light and shadow relationships. The luminous disc, the sphere's shadow, the intersecting line (a wire or rod) create a composition that is simultaneously three-dimensional (objects in space) and two-dimensional (their shadows on paper). The Photogramm collapses depth into surface while preserving the memory of depth in the gradients of the shadows.
- **Rule**: Organize the light and shadow relationships of a Photogramm as you would organize a composition in space — the paper is the record of that spatial organization, not a flat surface to be decorated.
- **Source**: p.72
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die organisierten Licht- und Schattenbeziehungen [organisierten Beleuchtung des Raums]."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The organized light and shadow relationships [organized illumination of space]."
- **Category**: photogram | light

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Das Alltägliche wird Rätselhaft / The Everyday Becomes Enigmatic
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Man Ray's Photogramm of a hand holding an egg (p.75) demonstrates the power of the technique to transform the ordinary. The hand — a familiar object — becomes through the Photogramm something unexpected: its light-silhouette isolates form from surface, context, color. The egg below the hand is simultaneously mundane and formally perfect. The caption states: "Das Alltägliche wächst hier durch die Neuverwendung des Materials ins Rätselhafte." The everyday grows into the enigmatic through the new use of the material. This is not surrealism; it is material logic.
- **Rule**: When familiar objects are used as Photogramm material, their recognition value transforms into formal tension — use this: the viewer's simultaneous recognition and estrangement is a compositional resource.
- **Source**: p.75
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das Alltägliche wächst hier durch die Neuverwendung des Materials ins Rätselhafte."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The everyday grows here through the new use of material into the enigmatic."
- **Category**: photogram | production-reproduction

## Subsection F: Malerei und Photogramm — Painting and Photogram in Dialogue

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Konstruktion als Malerei / Construction as Painting
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Page 73 presents my painting "Konstruktion K" as a direct counterpart to the surrounding Photogramme. The painting — a large transparent grey circle, a dominant vertical white/dark bar, smaller black rectangles and a thin diagonal line — is compositionally isomorphic with the Photogramme on adjacent pages. The circle, the overlapping planes, the tension between geometric solids and negative space: these are the same compositional problems I solve in light when I make a Photogramm, and in paint when I make a Konstruktion. The medium differs; the structural thinking is identical.
- **Rule**: Painting and Photogramm are two solutions to the same compositional problem — the organization of light, tone, and geometric form in a two-dimensional field. Do not treat them as opposed practices; treat them as parallel investigations.
- **Source**: p.73
- **Quote (DE)**: "MOHOLY-NAGY: Konstruktion 'K' (Malerei)"

## Subsection G: Reflektorische Farbenspiele — Kinetic Light Performance

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Reflektorische Farbenspiele (Reflective Color Plays) of the Bauhaus extend Lichtgestaltung into time and performance. If the Photogramm is light made into a static image, the Farbenspiele is light made into a temporal sequence — an "optischer Rhythmus" (optical rhythm) that unfolds in time exactly as musical rhythm unfolds in sound.

**Evidence**: H. Schwerdtfeger's Bauhaus apparatus (p.77) — photographed in motion by Höttich & Gemker, Weimar — shows "ein Moment aus der fließenden, vorwärtsdrängenden Bewegung" (a moment from the flowing, forward-pressing movement). Ludwig Hirschfeld-Mack provides a detailed written account (p.78) of three years of development of the technique, including the key insight about the relationship between optical rhythm and acoustic rhythm.

**Conclusion**: The Farbenspiele proves that light is not only a spatial but a temporal medium. The PARTITUR (score) required to notate it is not a musical score but a new notation system for optical events in time — a form of Typophoto applied to performance instruction.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Optischer Rhythmus als Zeitkomposition / Optical Rhythm as Time Composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Ludwig Hirschfeld-Mack, in his written account of the Reflektorische Farbenspiele, identifies the central discovery: that the temporal succession of colored light-forms — their "gleichmäßig geordneter Rhythmus" (uniformly ordered rhythm) — is not a decorative effect but the primary compositional dimension of the medium. Just as Kandinsky and Klee analyzed the spatial tension, rhythm, and musical relationships of forms in the static image, the Farbenspiele makes those relationships actual, not metaphorical: colors and forms move in real time, and their movement has tempo, acceleration, deceleration.
- **Rule**: In time-based light composition, rhythm is not decoration — it is structure. Compose the temporal sequence of light-events as a musician composes a score: with specific durations, transitions, and rhythmic hierarchy.
- **Source**: p.78
- **Quote (DE)**: "gleichmäßig geordneten Rhythmus — in diesem Film wie auch in modernen Filmwerken, in denen immer wieder die literarische Inhalt der Handlung die größte Rolle spielt, keineswegs geachtet."
- **Quote (EN)**: "uniformly ordered rhythm — in this film as in modern film works, in which the literary content of the narrative always plays the greatest role, by no means respected."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm | film | light

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Akustische Gliederung als Hilfsmittel für optische Zeitfolge / Acoustic Structure as Aid to Optical Time-Sequence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Hirschfeld-Mack reports a critical finding from three years of working with the Farbenspiele: the audience comprehends a temporal sequence of optical events more easily and exactly through acoustic support than through optical sequence alone. The regular ticking of a clock creates "unmittelbares und exaktes Zeitgefühl" (immediate and exact time-sense) that makes the optical appearance of an isolated light-form — otherwise floating in time — precisely locatable. "Es ist aber eine räumliche Darstellung zeitlich in eine tatsächliche Bewegung geordnet, wird das Verständnis für die zeitliche Folge durch akustische Hilfsmittel gefördert." Optical time requires acoustic scaffolding.
- **Rule**: When presenting a time-based optical sequence (film, light performance, animation), provide acoustic rhythm as a temporal scaffold — not as accompaniment but as the metric infrastructure that makes the optical sequence legible.
- **Source**: p.78
- **Quote (DE)**: "Es ist aber eine räumliche Darstellung zeitlich in eine tatsächliche Bewegung geordnet, wird das Verständnis für die zeitliche Folge durch akustische Hilfsmittel gefördert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "But when a spatial representation is organized temporally into an actual movement, comprehension of the temporal sequence is aided by acoustic means."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm | film | simultaneity

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: PARTITUR als neue Notation / Score as New Notation for Optical Events
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Reflektorische Farbenspiele requires a written score — a PARTITUR — to be performed. This score is neither a musical score nor a verbal description but a new form of notation for optical events in time: "Die Bedienung der Apparate erfolgt nach genau festgesetzter PARTITUR." The score specifies exactly which colored light-forms appear, in which sequence, with which tempo, through which transitions. This is Typophoto extended to performance: the written document is the operational instruction for a light-time composition.
- **Rule**: Design a notation system appropriate to your medium. For time-based light composition, the score must specify: form, color, duration, transition-type, and tempo — not as prose description but as a structured operational document.
- **Source**: p.78
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Bedienung der Apparate erfolgt nach genau festgesetzter PARTITUR."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The operation of the apparatus follows an exactly prescribed SCORE."
- **Category**: typophoto | optical-rhythm | film

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Farbformflächen als tatsächliche Bewegung / Color-Form-Surfaces as Actual Movement
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Hirschfeld-Mack describes the origin of the Farbenspiele in the need to produce "Farbformflächen, die im gemeinen Bilde in ihren Relationen wie Bewegung vorausschwingen, zu einer tatsächlichen, kontinuierlichen Bewegung zu steigern" — to elevate color-form-surfaces, which in the static image only suggest movement in their relations, into actual continuous movement. This is the passage from painting to film as understood from within the Bauhaus: not a technological upgrade but the logical completion of what painting was always attempting.
- **Rule**: Static composition that implies movement (through diagonal tension, rhythm, overlapping planes) is the preparatory sketch for time-based composition. Develop the implied movement into actual movement — do not stop at the static image if the work demands duration.
- **Source**: p.78
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die reflektorischen Farbenspiele haben sich unmittelbar aus dem Bedürfnis entwickelt, Farbformflächen, die im gemeinen Bilde in ihren Relationen wie Bewegung vorausschwingen, zu einer tatsächlichen, kontinuierlichen Bewegung zu steigern."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The reflective color plays developed directly from the need to elevate color-form-surfaces, which in the ordinary image suggest movement in their relations, into an actual, continuous movement."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm | film | light

The image is formally symmetrical and compositionally complete without any manipulation.
- **Type**: Produktion — the image reveals what no external observation could show: the internal skeletal architecture of a living form.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-05 (Durchdringung des Körpers mit Licht) — the X-ray as the highest mode of Neue Sehen; also demonstrates the point that penetration of matter by light is a primary visual experience, not merely a diagnostic tool.
- **Text reference**: "Die Durchdringung des Körpers mit Licht ist eines der größten Seherlebnisse." (p.67)
- **Source**: p.67 | Röntgenfoto: SCHREINER WEIMAR

**FIGURE [Fig-10]**: "Muschel. Triton Tritonis" — Shell: Triton Tritonis (X-Ray)
- **Shows**: X-ray photograph of a Triton Tritonis shell by J.B. Polak, published in "Wendingen" (Amsterdam). The shell's spiral form is rendered as luminous white curves against a dark background — the external outline a continuous flowing line, the internal chambers revealed as nested transparent zones with varying opacity. The overall form is elegant and complex, simultaneously organic and geometric.
- **Type**: Produktion — the image translates the three-dimensional opacity of the shell's calcium carbonate structure into a two-dimensional light-composition; the X-ray makes visible the internal chamber structure that is invisible to reflected light.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-06 (In Licht umgesetzte Materie) — matter literally translated into light; the shell's substance becomes a light-distribution map.
- **Text reference**: "In Licht umgesetzte Materie." (p.68)
- **Source**: p.68 | Röntgenfoto: J.B. POLAK, Aus "Wendingen," Amsterdam

**FIGURE [Fig-11]**: Photogramm: Spirale / Spiral — Kameralose Aufnahme
- **Shows**: My Photogramm of a spiral form (coiled wire or tube) placed on photographic paper and exposed to a directional light source from upper-left. The spiral casts a complex shadow: the coil itself appears as a luminous curved band with sharp edges, the interior of the coil rings creating concentric shadow zones. A directional light beam from upper-left creates a diagonal light gradient across the field, with the spiral's shadow producing the finest grey gradations between absolute black and absolute white.
- **Type**: Produktion (by definition) — a Photogramm is the medium's absolute Produktion: it requires no camera, no lens, no subject in the conventional sense. The image exists only as a direct light-action on a sensitized surface.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-07 (Das Photogramm als reine Lichtgestaltung) — the spiral Photogramm demonstrates the full tonal range from black to white achievable through direct light action; also demonstrates the formal elegance possible with a simple geometric object.
- **Text reference**: "Kameralose Aufnahme. Die Kontrastbeziehungen zwischen Schwarz-Weiß mit den feinsten Grauübergängen." (p.69)
- **Source**: p.69 | Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY

**FIGURE [Fig-12]**: "Muschel. Nautilus Pompilius" — Nautilus Shell (X-Ray)
- **Shows**: X-ray photograph of a Nautilus Pompilius shell (J.B. Polak / Wendingen, Amsterdam). The shell is shown in cross-section (the X-ray penetrates the full thickness of the shell), revealing the famous Fibonacci spiral of the internal chambers. Each chamber is rendered as a luminous zone separated from the next by a thin white wall. The outermost chamber — the living chamber — is the largest, surrounded by the dense outer shell wall. The entire image is a luminous spiral on dark ground.
- **Type**: Produktion — the internal chamber structure of the Nautilus is invisible to reflected light; only X-ray penetration reveals it. The resulting image is simultaneously a scientific record and a formal composition of extraordinary power.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-06 (In Licht umgesetzte Materie) and P-05 (Durchdringung des Körpers mit Licht) — the Nautilus X-ray is the fullest demonstration in the book of both principles simultaneously.
- **Text reference**: Caption only: "Muschel. Nautilus Pompilius" (p.70)
- **Source**: p.70 | Röntgenfoto: J.B. POLAK, Aus "Wendingen," Amsterdam

**FIGURE [Fig-13]**: Photogramm: Geometrische Formen / Geometric Objects — Kameralose Aufnahme
- **Shows**: My Photogramm of multiple geometric objects: at least one cross-shaped form (or two rectangular forms arranged as a cross), additional rectangular blocks, and a mesh or grid material visible in the upper area. Objects are placed at varying distances from the paper surface, creating overlapping shadow zones. The composition is complex and spatially layered — no single "reading" of the depth relationships is final.
- **Type**: Produktion (by definition).
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-08 (Photogramm und Simultanität) — the overlapping shadows of multiple objects create a spatially simultaneous composition; also P-07 (pure Lichtgestaltung).
- **Text reference**: Caption: "Kameralose Aufnahme. Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY" (p.71)
- **Source**: p.71 | Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY

**FIGURE [Fig-14]**: Photogramm: Scheibe und Kugel / Disc and Sphere — Kameralose Aufnahme
- **Shows**: My Photogramm (p.72) of a large disc-like form and a sphere (or hemisphere), with a thin straight line (wire or rod) crossing diagonally. The disc creates a large soft-edged luminous oval; the sphere creates a smaller circle of light with a shadow beneath it; the wire creates a sharp linear element cutting across both. The rotated caption reads: "Die organisierten Licht- und Schattenbeziehungen [organisierten Beleuchtung des Raums]."
- **Type**: Produktion (by definition).
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-09 (Organisierte Licht- und Schattenbeziehungen) — the three elements (disc, sphere, line) create an organized spatial field through their light and shadow interactions.
- **Text reference**: "Die organisierten Licht- und Schattenbeziehungen [organisierten Beleuchtung des Raums]." (p.72, rotated caption)
- **Source**: p.72 | Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY

**FIGURE [Fig-15]**: "Konstruktion 'K'" — Construction K (Painting)
- **Shows**: Reproduction of my painting "Konstruktion K" (Malerei). The composition: a large light-grey transparent circle dominating the center; a strong vertical bar (white at center, dark grey at edges) running top-to-bottom through the circle's center; two black rectangles — one at the top of the bar, one at the bottom — creating strong anchoring points; a thin diagonal line (white) in the upper-right; a small cross (+) in the lower-left area. The overall composition uses transparency, overlap, and geometric tension in a way directly analogous to the surrounding Photogramme.
- **Type**: Reproduktion in the technical sense (it is a painted object, not a camera-produced image), but Produktion in the deeper sense: the composition is constructive, not imitative. It demonstrates what painting and Photogramm share — the organization of tonal relationships in a two-dimensional field.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: P-11 (Konstruktion als Malerei) — the painting is placed in the sequence of Photogramme to demonstrate the structural continuity between light-construction and painted construction.
- **Text reference**: "MOHOLY-NAGY: Konstruktion 'K' (Malerei)" (p.73, rotated caption)
- **Source**: p.73 | Malerei: MOHOLY-NAGY

**FIGURE [Fig-16]**: Photogramm: Kugel und Lichtbündel / Sphere and Light Beam — Kameralose Aufnahme
- **Shows**: My Photogramm (p.74) featuring a sphere (glowing white circle, upper-right) and what appear to be flat transparent or translucent planes creating diagonal light-gradients across the lower two-thirds of the image. The planes overlap and create horizontal banding of alternating lighter and darker zones. The sphere sits above, casting its shadow down. A hemispherical or curved form is visible at lower-left, creating a contrasting soft-edged zone.
- **Type**: Produktion (by definition).
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-07 (Das Photogramm als reine Lichtgestaltung) — multiple transparent planes demonstrate the additive and subtractive properties of light when multiple translucent objects overlap on the paper surface.
- **Text reference**: "Kameralose Aufnahme. Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY" (p.74)
- **Source**: p.74 | Fotogramm: MOHOLY-NAGY

**FIGURE [Fig-17]**: Man Ray Photogramm: Hand und Ei / Hand and Egg — Kameralose Aufnahme
- **Shows**: Man Ray's Photogramm (Paris) of a human hand holding an egg. The hand is seen from above, fingers curled slightly; the egg is suspended below the fingertips, luminous and oval. The hand's tonal structure is a soft gradation — not the sharp-edged shadow of an opaque object but the graduated translucency of flesh. The egg below is a near-perfect oval of uniform light tone.
- **Type**: Produktion (by definition — Photogramm). Additionally, the treatment of the organic subject (hand, egg) reveals formal qualities — the hand's structural elegance, the egg's formal perfection — that conventional photography would bury in context and surface detail.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-10 (Das Alltägliche wird Rätselhaft) — the hand and egg are among the most familiar objects in existence; the Photogramm process estranges them from their familiar contexts and reveals their pure formal identities.
- **Text reference**: "Das Alltägliche wächst hier durch die Neuverwendung des Materials ins Rätselhafte." (p.75)
- **Source**: p.75 | Fotogramm: MAN RAY / PARIS

**FIGURE [Fig-18]**: Man Ray Photogramm: Mechanische Objekte / Mechanical and Glass Objects — Kameralose Aufnahme
- **Shows**: Man Ray's Photogramm (Paris) of mechanical and glass objects — what appear to be glass rods, prisms, or crystal forms, possibly combined with a mechanical object (gear or fan-like form visible at upper-center). The objects create a complex pattern of sharp refractions, multiple overlapping shadow bands, and the peculiar halo effects produced by glass objects that both transmit and refract light. The composition is angular and dense.
- **Type**: Produktion (by definition). The glass objects' refractive properties create light-forms that would be impossible to achieve with opaque objects — the Photogramm reveals the optical behavior of the material itself.
- **Demonstrates** [INFERRED]: P-07 (Das Photogramm als reine Lichtgestaltung) — glass objects extend the Photogramm's tonal range; they do not merely block or pass light but refract, concentrate, and scatter it, creating effects impossible with opaque materials.
- **Text reference**: Caption only: "Kameralose Aufnahme. Fotogramm: MAN RAY / PARIS" (p.76)
- **Source**: p.76 | Fotogramm: MAN RAY / PARIS

**FIGURE [Fig-19]**: H. Schwerdtfeger — Reflektorische Lichtspiele (Bauhaus) — Motion Still
- **Shows**: A photographic still from H. Schwerdtfeger's Bauhaus Reflektorische Lichtspiele apparatus, photographed by Höttich & Gemker (Weimar). The image captures "ein Moment aus der fließenden, vorwärtsdrängenden Bewegung" (a moment from the flowing, forward-pressing movement). Visible: several geometric forms — circular segments, rectangles — in a composition of black, white, and grey that implies motion through overlap and the blurred edges of moving elements. The image is formally similar to a Constructivist composition but captures an actual instantaneous state of a kinetic light apparatus.
- **Type**: Produktion — the image records an instantaneous state of a kinetic apparatus that is never static; no still image of this apparatus could exist without the camera.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-12 (Optischer Rhythmus als Zeitkomposition) — the still from the kinetic apparatus is evidence of the temporal flow that the written PARTITUR scores; P-15 (Farbformflächen als tatsächliche Bewegung).
- **Text reference**: "Ein Moment aus der fließenden, vorwärtsdrängenden Bewegung." (p.77)
- **Source**: p.77 | Foto: HÖTTICH & GEMKER WEIMAR | H. Schwerdtfeger, Bauhaus
