**PRINCIPLE [P-B06-01]**: Rotation als dominante Bewegung / Rotation as dominant movement
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The dominant compositional logic across this sequence of plates is rotational — arcs, spirals, and curved planes organized around implicit centers of revolution. Where earlier plates showed translation (linear recession, planar advance) as coequal with rotation, this sequence increasingly foregrounds rotation as the primary sensation-organizing operation.
- **Rule**: When rotation dominates a composition, organize curved planes concentrically or eccentrically around implied axes — never let arcs float free of a rotational logic.
- **Source**: pp.101–118 (plates sequence, inferred from visual evidence)
- **Quote (DE/FR)**: [No text on these pages]
- **Quote (EN)**: [No text on these pages]
- **Category**: form | composition | temporality

**PRINCIPLE [P-B06-02]**: Mehrere Zustände in simultanem Koexistenz / Multiple states in simultaneous coexistence
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The plates in this batch demonstrate that simultaneous coexistence of sensation-states does not require analytical fragmentation of a single object into facets. By the later works (figs. 166–182), simultaneous states are achieved through large color-zones that occupy the same surface without naturalistic spatial separation — each zone is a distinct sensation-state, not a facet of a continuous object.
- **Rule**: Simultaneous states can be articulated through color-zone boundaries as effectively as through geometric planar cuts — the Schnitt is not only a line but any boundary where one sensation-state ends and another begins.
- **Source**: pp.111–118 (color plates, figs. 166–182)

- **Quote (EN)**: [No text on these pages]

### Batch Notes

**Nature of this batch:** Pages 100–119 (PDF) are entirely a plates section — no text pages are present. This is the densest concentration of painting reproductions in the book. The plates are numbered from approximately 141 to 182, suggesting this is a late section of the plates sequence.

**Color vs. black-and-white distribution:** Of the 19 content pages (page 119 is blank), approximately 8 are color plates and 11 are black-and-white. Color plates: pp.100, 103, 104, 109, 110, 111, 112, 117, 118. Black-and-white plates: pp.101, 102, 105, 106, 107, 108, 113, 114, 115, 116.

**Multi-panel series identified:** Plates 161A/B (p.110) and 162C/D (p.111) constitute a four-panel simultaneous-states series — the most explicit structural demonstration of mehrere Zustände in the entire batch.

**Leonardo da Vinci inscription (plate 161A):** The inscription of Leonardo's name within the painting (not as a caption) is the single most theoretically significant text element in this batch. It frames Leonardo as a subject of simultaneous-viewpoint analysis — a polemical gesture against Renaissance perspective theory.

**Architectural convergence (plate 182):** The nested-arc head structure in the final full-page color plate (p.119/fig.182) represents the most architecturally legible composition in the batch and the strongest visual argument for the relevance of simultaneous-movement theory to spatial design.

**Figure number sequence gaps:** Some figure numbers visible in the batch appear non-sequential (e.g., 141, then 144, 145, 147, 151, 152, 154…). This suggests interleaved text pages (with figures numbered in the main text section) account for the gap numbers — the plates section is a separate physical gathering but shares the figure numbering sequence with the inline text illustrations.
