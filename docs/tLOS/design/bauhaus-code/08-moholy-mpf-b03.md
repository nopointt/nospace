## Das Simultane oder Polykino (The Simultaneous or Poly-Cinema)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The rectangular flat projection screen is a technical convention, not an aesthetic law. The cinema must be rebuilt — architecturally and optically — to accommodate simultaneous, multi-surface, spherical projection, so that film can achieve true Simultanität: multiple independent narrative streams presented at once to an audience whose perceptual apparatus has been trained by modern urban life.

**Evidence**: Moholy-Nagy proposes two concrete technical modifications. First: replacing the flat screen with a spherical (Kugelsegmentförmige) concave surface of large radius, set at 45° to the viewer, onto which several films are projected simultaneously from moving projectors scanning left-right, right-left, top-bottom, and bottom-top. Second: using a rotating prism in front of the projection lens to eliminate the costly frame-by-frame animation work currently required for absolute Lichtgestaltung. He draws an analogy to the acoustic and optical simultaneity already experienced at the Potsdamer Platz — the horn of the automobile, the tram bell, the underground roar, the newspaper vendor's cry, the loudspeaker — all arriving at once. The analogy with the provincial newcomer overwhelmed by the Potsdamer Platz confirms that Simultanität is the natural perceptual condition of modernity, not an experimental provocation.

**Conclusion**: Cinema must evolve from a single-channel narrative medium into a multi-channel space-time composition instrument. The great synthetic film is not a sequence of pictures projected onto one wall; it is a constructed perceptual environment commanding optical tempo, spatial simultaneity, and the full four-dimensional capacity of light in motion.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Simultanität / Polykino — Simultaneous multi-surface projection
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The conventional rectangular projection surface is historically contingent. A spherical Projektionsfläche of large radius, on which multiple independent films run simultaneously — crossing, overlapping, replacing one another — converts cinema from a single narrative channel into a synthetic perceptual field.
- **Rule**: Design projection environments as multi-channel spatial compositions; never treat the screen as a neutral neutral container for a single temporal stream.
- **Source**: p.39
- **Quote (DE)**: "Man müßte ein Kino bauen, das für verschiedene Versuchszwecke hinsichtlich Apparatur und Projektionsfläche eingerichtet ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "One would have to build a cinema equipped for various experimental purposes with regard to apparatus and projection surface."
- **Category**: simultaneity

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Kugelförmige Projektionsfläche / optische Verzerrungskorrektur — Spherical surface and distortion mastery
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The spherical concave projection surface, set at approximately 45° to the audience, has a sufficiently large radius and small depth that it corrects the optical distortion (Projektionsverzerrung) produced by projecting onto a flat surface at an angle. Mastering distortion is not a correction problem — it is a compositional resource.
- **Rule**: Treat projection distortion as a plastic variable; design surfaces that either eliminate it or exploit it, but never accept it as an unexamined default.
- **Source**: p.39
- **Quote (DE)**: "Diese Projektionsfläche muß einen sehr großen Radius haben, also sehr geringe Tiefe haben, und für den Zuschauer in einem Sehwinkel von ca. 45° angebracht werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This projection surface must have a very large radius, thus very little depth, and must be positioned for the viewer at a viewing angle of approximately 45°."
- **Category**: neue-sehen

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Mehrere unabhängige Filme gleichzeitig / Deckung und Schnitt — Layered simultaneous films cutting to new synthesis
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Two films — say, the life of a man (A) and the life of a woman (B), both told from birth to death — can run simultaneously on the same spherical surface, crossing or running in parallel, their intersections (Ehe, Liebe) emerging as new compositional events. A third film (C) can run simultaneously with A and B, arriving from a different direction and cutting them sinngemäß — meaningfully, not arbitrarily.
- **Rule**: In simultaneous composition, arrange independent channels so that their intersections generate new semantic events; the crossing itself must be designed, not incidental.
- **Source**: p.40
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die beiden Filme können dann entweder sich kreuzend, in durchschneidenden Geschehnisfolgen oder parallel nebeneinander weiterlaufen; oder es kann ein neuer gemeinsamer Film der beiden Personen an die Stelle der beiden ersten treten."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The two films can then either run crossing, in intersecting narrative sequences, or parallel alongside one another; or a new common film of the two persons can take the place of the first two."
- **Category**: simultaneity

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Simultanität als Bewegungsdarstellung in der zweiten Dimension — Simultaneity as motion representation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The large spherical Projektionsfläche has the advantage over the conventional flat surface in representing movement — for example, a car travelling from one end to the other — with greater illusion of the second dimension, because on flat surfaces a fixed image point must always be held.
- **Rule**: When representing motion, choose projection geometry that sustains the illusion of continuous spatial trajectory rather than a fixed frame imprisoning a moving subject.
- **Source**: p.40 (bottom)
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die große Projektionsfläche hat auch den Vorzug, einen Bewegungsvorgang, sagen wir den eines Autos, von einem Ende zum anderen mit größerer Illusion darzustellen (Bewegung in der zweiten Dimension) als die jetzigen Projektionsflächen, auf welchen immer ein Bild fixiert werden muß."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The large projection surface also has the advantage of representing a movement process — say that of a car from one end to the other — with greater illusion (movement in the second dimension) than present projection surfaces, on which one image point must always remain fixed."
- **Category**: simultaneity

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Drehendes Prisma / kontinuierlich laufender Apparat — Rotating prism for continuous projection
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The technical solution to continuous multi-surface projection is simple and inexpensive: place a rotating prism in front of the film projection lens. This eliminates the need for costly frame-by-frame Tricktisch-Zeichnungen (animation desk drawings) or mechanically complex light-shadow plays, enabling absolute Lichtgestaltung through a continuously running mechanical apparatus.
- **Rule**: Seek the mechanical simplicity that unlocks compositional complexity; the prism that costs nothing in production can unlock the full field of Lichtgestaltung.
- **Source**: p.41
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die technische Lösung solcher Projektionen wie die des Autos oder der erwähnten Skizze ist sehr einfach und gar nicht kostspielig. Es muß nur ein drehendes Prisma vor die Linse der Filmprojektionsapparate eingeschaltet werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The technical solution to such projections as that of the car or the mentioned sketch is very simple and not at all costly. One only needs to insert a rotating prism in front of the lens of the film projection apparatus."
- **Category**: film

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Simultane Wiederholung / stufenweise Überholen — Simultaneous repetition and overtaking
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The large Projektionsfläche also permits the simultaneous repetition of a running film strip by projecting multiple copies side-by-side from separate apparatuses. One can begin a new copy at the start of a movement while the original is still advancing — so the same motion is simultaneously shown at multiple stages — creating new visual effects through the layering of temporal phases of the same event.
- **Rule**: Exploit the capacity to show multiple temporal phases of a single motion simultaneously; repetition with temporal offset is a compositional resource, not redundancy.
- **Source**: p.41
- **Quote (DE)**: "Man kann so den Anfang einer Bewegung während ihres Weiterschreitens — stufenweise überholt — immer wieder zeigen und damit neue Wirkungen erzielen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "One can thus repeatedly show the beginning of a movement while it is still advancing — overtaken step by step — and thereby achieve new effects."
- **Category**: optical-rhythm

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Akustisch-optische Simultanität / Großstadt als Schulung des Sinnesorgans — The city as sensory training
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The technical development and the metropolis have expanded our perceptual organs to the capacity for simultaneous acoustic and optical experience. At the Potsdamer Platz, Berliners already experience this: car horns, tram bells, the underground roar, the newspaper vendor's cry, a loudspeaker — all simultaneously. The provincial newcomer is overwhelmed; the Berliner hears them simultaneously. This is the model for the cinematic audience of the future: trained to receive multiple simultaneous optical streams.
- **Rule**: Design for an audience whose perceptual capacity has been trained by urban modernity; do not design for the provincial eye expecting single-channel sequential delivery.
- **Source**: p.41
- **Quote (DE)**: "Sie unterhalten sich, sie hören gleichzeitig: die Hupen der Autos, das Klingeln der Straßenbahn, das Tuten der Omnibusse, das Hallo des Kutschers, das Sausen der Untergrundbahn, das Schreien des Zeitungsverkäufers, die Töne eines Lautsprechers usw."
- **Quote (EN)**: "They converse, they hear simultaneously: the horns of the cars, the ringing of the tram, the hooting of the buses, the coachman's shout, the roaring of the underground, the cry of the newspaper vendor, the sounds of a loudspeaker, etc."
- **Category**: simultaneity

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Moderne Optik und Akustik als künstlerische Gestaltungsmittel — Modern optics and acoustics as artistic forming media
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Just as the simultaneous optical and acoustic phenomena of the metropolis can be received by the open modern person and enrich them, the same simultaneity must be adopted as an artistic forming medium (Gestaltungsmittel). The analogy of optics and acoustics is not decorative — it is a structural argument: what the ear has already learned in the city, the eye must learn in the cinema.
- **Rule**: Treat simultaneity as a structural principle of composition, not as an effect; the simultaneous is the natural state of modern experience made visible.
- **Source**: p.41
- **Quote (DE)**: "Ebenso analog, daß moderne Optik und Akustik, als Mittel künstlerischer Gestaltung verwendet, das nur von einem für die Gegenwart offenen Menschen aufgenommen werden und ihn bereichern können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Equally analogous, that modern optics and acoustics, used as means of artistic forming, can only be received and enriched by a person open to the present."
- **Category**: simultaneity

## Von technischen Möglichkeiten und Forderungen (On Technical Possibilities and Demands)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The practical precondition for absolute Filmgestaltung is excellent apparatus and the most highly developed machinery. Every technical obstacle to absolute Lichtgestaltung is a removable obstacle, not a fundamental limit. The artist's obligation is to analyze the opto-phonetic relationships so radically that entirely new forms emerge.

**Evidence**: Moholy-Nagy identifies the historical difficulty as the reliance on costly Tricktisch-Zeichnungen (animation desk drawings) or mechanically complex light-shadow plays for producing Lichterscheinungen. He proposes that mechanically movable light sources, combined with moving stencils (Schablonen mit Schlitzformen), can replace this labor. He notes that the same principle applies to both representational (Objekt-Aufnahmen) and absolute Lichtgestaltung. The key argument is that the analysis of opto-phonetic relationships must be so radical that entirely new forms — veränderter neuer Formen — emerge, not merely variations on the old.

**Conclusion**: The work of traditional optical form-making (with pigment) is historically over. The new work — with light — has barely begun. The medium is not yet the message because artists have not yet possessed the medium. Opened eyes and opened ears belong to every creative work; photography is one of the most important factors in the new life.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Mechanisch bewegliche Lichtquellen / Schablonen mit Schlitzformen — Mechanically movable light sources as compositional tools
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The variety of light phenomena in film can be increased by mechanically movable light sources. Between the light source and the light-sensitive strip, small movable stencils (Schablonen) with slit-forms (Schlitzformen — patterns, etc.) can be placed and, through their continuous illumination of the strip, used both for representational (Objekt-) and absolute Lichtgestaltung.
- **Rule**: Use mechanical motion of light sources and interposed stencils as compositional variables; the moving aperture is an instrument of Lichtgestaltung, not merely a technical accessory.
- **Source**: p.42
- **Quote (DE)**: "So kann man zwischen die Lichtquelle und den lichtempfindlichen Streifen kleine bewegliche Schablonen mit Schlitzformen (Mustern usw.) einschalten und dadurch eine wechselnde Belichtung des Streifens fortlaufend ermöglichen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Thus one can place small movable stencils with slit-forms (patterns, etc.) between the light source and the light-sensitive strip, and thereby continuously enable a changing exposure of the strip."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Radikale Analyse der optofonetischen Beziehungen — Radical analysis of opto-phonetic relationships
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The analysis of opto-phonetic relationships must be so radical that entirely new, transformed forms emerge. All experiments, speculations, and attempts amount to nothing if they do not arise from the inclination and concentration that have been the foundation of every creative work in photography and film.
- **Rule**: Analyze the relationship between optical and acoustic elements not as an illustration problem but as a compositional problem requiring entirely new forms — veränderter neuer Formen.
- **Source**: p.42
- **Quote (DE)**: "Aber alle Prüfungen, Versuche, Spekulationen sind nichts, wenn sie nicht aus Neigung und Konzentration, die die Grundlage einer jeden schöpferischen Arbeit, auch der Fotografie und des Films gewesen sind, entstehen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "But all examinations, attempts, speculations amount to nothing if they do not arise from inclination and concentration, which have been the foundation of every creative work, including photography and film."
- **Category**: production-reproduction

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Das neue Licht als Arbeitsmaterial / Ende der Pigmentarbeit — Light as the new working material; end of pigment work
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The work with pigment (the traditional painted image) is historically superseded. The new work is with light. The traditional image is historical, superseded. Opened eyes and ears — Geöffnete Augen und Ohren — belong to every creative work; photography has become one of the most important factors in the new life. Many more forward-moving years lie ahead before the general recognition of the photographic techniques reaches the point where it can enrich general knowledge.
- **Rule**: Work with light as the primary plastic medium; treat the pigment-based image as a historical form, not a model.
- **Source**: p.43
- **Quote (DE)**: "Das traditionelle Bild ist historisch geworden und vorbei. Geöffnete Augen und Ohren werden in jedem Augenblick mit dem Reichtum des optischen und fonetischen Wunders erfüllt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The traditional image has become historical and is past. Opened eyes and ears are in every moment filled with the richness of the optical and phonetic wonder."
- **Category**: light

## Visual Evidence: The Neue Sehen Gallery (pp. 46–58)

The following thirteen pages constitute a gallery section demonstrating specific modes of Neue Sehen through selected photographs with short captions. Each image is chosen to demonstrate a specific perceptual mode enabled by the camera — modes unavailable to the uneducated eye. Moholy-Nagy's captions are the analytical keys.

---

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: "Zeppelin III fliegt über den Ozean" (Zeppelin III flies over the ocean)
- **Shows**: Dramatic photograph of an airship (Zeppelin III) crossing a stormy ocean sky; heavy cloudscape, turbulent waves below, the airship silhouetted at upper right. Photographer: Gross. Source: Zeitbilder.
- **Type**: Reproduktion — records an existing external reality; the airship exists independently of the camera.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The "romantic" landscape mode — but Moholy-Nagy's caption immediately diagnoses this as a historical error: this is the photo imitating painting. The caption reads: "Das ist die 'romantische' Landschaft. Nach der glänzenden — allerdings nicht wiederholbaren — Periode der Daguerreotypie hat der Fotograf alle Richtungen, Stile, Erscheinungsformen der Malerei nachzuahmen versucht. Es dauerte ca. 100 Jahre, bis er zur richtigen Verwendung seiner eigenen Mittel kam." The image functions as a negative example — Reproduktion in its most regressive form: imitation of painting.
- **Text reference**: Caption explicitly diagnoses the image as painting-imitation; the 100-year delay is the historical argument for why Neue Sehen was delayed.
- **Source**: p.46

---

**FIGURE [Fig-02]**: "Paris" — Foto: A. Stieglitz (New York)
- **Shows**: Stieglitz's photograph of Paris, rotated 90° in the book layout (image printed sideways — the viewer must turn the book). Urban street scene, impressionistic blur, figures moving, tonal gradients. Caption printed vertically.
- **Type**: Reproduktion — records existing urban reality; but the caption diagnoses a transition.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The Sieg (victory) of Impressionism — but a specifically photographic Impressionism. Caption: "Der Sieg des Impressionismus erber die mitverstandene Fotografie. Der Fotograf ist Maler geworden, anstatt seinen Apparat fotografisch zu benutzen." Stieglitz is presented as the artist who crossed into painting-territory using photographic means — and thus as the case study in what must be overcome. The rotated layout of the image is itself a formal argument: the diagonal orientation enacts the oblique view (Schrägansicht) that Neue Sehen will embrace, but here deployed accidentally rather than constructively.
- **Text reference**: Caption is the full analytical statement.
- **Source**: p.47

**FIGURE [Fig-03]**: "Fliegendes Kranichsheer" (Flying flock of cranes)
- **Shows**: Dense flock of cranes in flight, filling the entire frame; strong high-contrast tonal distribution, the Hell-Dunkel (light-dark contrast) of multiple bird silhouettes against lighter sky — wings spread, bodies overlapping, motion implied through positional variety.
- **Type**: Produktion [INFERRED] — the image could not have been composed or achieved without the camera's capacity to freeze a moment of mass motion invisible to the normal eye.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: The schöne Verteilung des Hell-Dunkels (beautiful distribution of light and dark) as a photographic value independent of the subject. Caption: "Die schöne Verteilung des Hell-Dunkels, die unabhängig von dem Bildmotiv allein schon wirksam ist." The chiaroscuro here is not Rembrandt decoration — it is the structural composition of the image, independent of the depicted subject.
- **Text reference**: Caption explicitly states the principle: tonal distribution (Hell-Dunkel) is an independent compositional value.
- **Source**: p.48
