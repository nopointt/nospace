## Der Punkt (continued) — Geometrischer Punkt, Schrift, Schweigen

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The geometric point is an invisible, immaterial entity — conceptually equal to zero; yet within that zero, specifically human properties are hidden, above all the highest brevity of silence and speech combined.
**Evidence**: Etymology and usage of the period-symbol in written language; the point as interruption in flowing speech (Schrift); shock (Stoß) as the mechanism by which dead conventional habits can be broken open.
**Conclusion**: The point exists in two worlds simultaneously — the external, practical-purposive world and an internal, self-standing, innerlich-zweckmäßig world. The second world is the world of painting.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Geometrischer Punkt / Geometric Point
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The geometric point is an invisible entity. It must be defined as an immaterial entity. Materially considered, the point equals zero. Within this zero, however, various properties are hidden that are "human": the geometric point is, in our conception, the highest brevity, the highest and most singular connection between Schweigen und Sprechen (silence and speech).
- **Rule**: Treat the point as the most concentrated form of silence — a form that holds maximum tension in minimum extension.
- **Source**: p.19
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der geometrische Punkt ist ein unsichtbares Wesen. Er muß also als ein unmaterialles Wesen definiert werden. Materiell gedacht gleicht der Punkt einer Null."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The geometric point is an invisible entity. It must therefore be defined as an immaterial entity. Materially considered, the point equals zero."
- **Category**: point

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Punkt als Schweigen / Point as Silence
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Klang (resonance/sound) associated with silence as habitually bound to the point is so loud that it completely overwhelms all other properties. The point belongs to the narrow circle of conventional phenomena with their traditional Klang — the point is mute.
- **Rule**: The conventional point suppresses its inner Klang through habit; liberation from the practical-purposive context is required before the point can speak.
- **Source**: p.19
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der Klang des mit dem Punkt gewohnheitsmäßig verbundenen Schweigens ist so laut, daß er die anderen Eigenschaften vollkommen übertönt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The resonance of the silence conventionally bound with the point is so loud that it completely drowns out all other properties."
- **Category**: point | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Stoß (Shock) als Befreiung / Shock as Liberation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A shock is capable of tearing us out of a dead state into a living sensation. Shocks coming from outside (illness, misfortune, war, revolution) tear with force — for shorter or longer durations — from the circle of traditional habits. Shocks coming from inside (Von Innen) are of a different kind — they are caused by humans from within; they find suitable ground within the person who is able to observe through the "glass screen" (Glasscheibe), hard but easily shattered. The open eye and the open ear lead even the smallest shocks to greatest experiences.
- **Rule**: The Punkt can only become an expressive element when the viewer has been released — by inner or outer Stoß — from the deadening of habitual practical-purposive reading.
- **Source**: p.19–20
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die von i n n e n kommenden Erschütterungen sind anderer Art — sie werden vom Menschen selbst verursacht und haben also in ihm selbst einen geeigneten Boden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Shocks coming from within are of a different kind — they are caused by humans themselves and therefore find suitable ground within themselves."
- **Category**: point | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Herausreißen / Tearing Out
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Through the gradual tearing of the point from the narrow circle of its conventional practical Wirkens (workings), it gains its own increasingly silent inner properties — an ever more growing Klang.
- **Rule**: Increasing the free space around a point and increasing the point's size simultaneously reduces the Klang of the writing and strengthens the Klang of the point.
- **Source**: p.20
- **Quote (DE)**: "Durch das allmähliche Herausreißen des Punktes aus dem engen Kreis seines gewohnten Wirkens bekommen seine bis jetzt schweigenden inneren Eigenschaften einen immer mehr wachsenden Klang."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Through the gradual tearing of the point from the narrow circle of its conventional workings, its hitherto silent inner properties gain an ever-growing resonance."
- **Category**: point

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Erster Fall — Punkt als Unterstreichung / First Case — Point as Emphasis
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The point transferred from practical-purposive context into an illogical one creates a two-tone sound (Zweiklang) — Schrift-Punkt — outside practical context. It is a balancing of two worlds that can never arrive at equilibrium. This is a doubly purposeless revolutionary state. The outer Klang of the symbol is veiled and the inner Klang blinks out for a moment and is immediately extinguished.
- **Rule**: In typographic composition, a point placed for rhetorical emphasis (Posaunenklang) is still within the practical-purposive world; its inner Klang is suppressed.
- **Source**: p.21
- **Category**: point | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Zweiter Fall — Punkt isoliert / Second Case — Point Isolated
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The point transferred from its practical-purposive context, placed outside the chain of running sentences, needs a larger free space around itself to receive a Resonanz (resonance). Nevertheless its Klang remains gentle, modest, and is overwhelmed by the Klang of the surrounding space.
- **Rule**: To gain strength and clarity, the isolated point must be both enlarged (greater size) and given greater free space; then its Klang gains clarity and force.
- **Source**: p.21
- **Quote (DE)**: "Bei Vergrößerung der freien Umgebung und der Größe des Punktes selbst vermindert sich der Klang der Schrift und der Klang des Punktes gewinnt an Deutlichkeit und Kraft (Fig. 1)."
- **Quote (EN)**: "With enlargement of the free environment and the size of the point itself, the Klang of the writing diminishes and the Klang of the point gains in clarity and force."
- **Category**: point | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Selbständiges Wesen / Self-Standing Entity
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The point has nevertheless been torn from its practical-purposive context and takes the leap from one world into another — from the world of Unterordnung (subordination), from the practical-purposive, to a world where it frees itself as a selbständiges Wesen (self-standing entity) and begins to live its innerlich-zweckmäßige (internally purposive) life. The first contact of the Tool with the material Fläche is already this leap.
- **Rule**: The moment the tool first touches the surface is the instant the point becomes autonomous — an act of inner necessity, not of external purpose.
- **Source**: p.22
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der Punkt ist trotzdem aus seinem Gewohnheitszustand herausgerissen worden, und so nimmt er den Anlauf zum Sprung aus einer Welt in eine andere, wo er sich von der Unterordnung, vom Praktisch-Zweckmäßigen befreit, wo er als ein s e l b s t ä n d i g e s W e s e n anfängt und wo seine Unterordnung sich zu einer innerlich-zweckmäßigen verwandelt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The point is nevertheless torn from its habitual state, and so takes the run-up for the leap from one world into another, where it frees itself from subordination, from the practically-purposive, where it begins as a self-standing entity, and where its subordination transforms into an internally-purposive one."
- **Category**: point | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Durch Zusammenstoß — Erster Kontakt / By Collision — First Contact
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The point is the result of the first collision of the Tool with the material Fläche — Papier, Holz, Leinwand, Stück, Metall, usw. (paper, wood, canvas, piece, metal, etc.). These material Grundflächen are fertilized by this first collision.
- **Rule**: The first mark — the first Punkt — is the originating act of a composition. The Grundfläche is fertilized, not merely marked, by this encounter.
- **Source**: p.22
- **Category**: point | plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Begriff — Äußerer Begriff des Punktes / Concept — External Concept of the Point
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The outer concept of the point in painting is imprecise. The materialized, invisible geometric point must take on a certain measurable size, one that claims a certain area of the Grundfläche. Furthermore it must have certain limits — Umrisse (outlines) — that separate it from its environment. Two conditions determine the concept of the point: (1) the relationship of the point to the Grundfläche with regard to size, and (2) the size-relationship to the other forms on this Fläche.
- **Rule**: Never define a point absolutely — always define it relationally: relative to the Grundfläche and relative to other forms present.
- **Source**: p.22–23
- **Quote (DE)**: "Der äußere Begriff des Punktes in der Malerei ist unpräzis. Der materialisierte unsichtbare geometrische Punkt muß eine gewisse Größe bekommen, die eine gewisse Fläche der Grundfläche in Anspruch nimmt."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The external concept of the point in painting is imprecise. The materialized invisible geometric point must take on a certain size, one that claims a certain area of the Grundfläche."
- **Category**: point | plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Größe — Relative Größe des Punktes / Size — Relative Size of the Point
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A point can grow and unnoticed cover the entire Grundfläche. Where is then the boundary between point and Fläche? Two conditions apply: (1) the relationship of the point to the Grundfläche regarding size, and (2) the size-relationship to the other forms on the Fläche. What can always function as a point on an otherwise empty Grundfläche must be called a Fläche when even a very thin line is added.
- **Rule**: The point-to-plane identity is determined by context, not by absolute measurement. A form is a point only when it reads as punctual relative to everything else present.
- **Source**: p.23–24
- **Quote (DE)**: "Was noch immer als Punkt auf der sonst leeren Grundfläche gelten kann, das muß als Fläche bezeichnet werden, wenn z. B. eine sehr dünne Linie auf die Grundfläche hinzukommt (Fig. 2)."
- **Quote (EN)**: "What can still count as a point on an otherwise empty Grundfläche must be called a Fläche when, for example, a very thin line is added to the Grundfläche."
- **Category**: point | plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: An der Grenze — Punkt als äußere Grenze / At the Boundary
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Today we are capable, merely by feeling, of determining also the approaching size of the point — the reaching of this moment, to which the point as such begins to disappear and in its place a Fläche lives. This is a means toward an end. In this case the dis-solution of absolute Klang, the Auflösungsbetonung (dissolution emphasis), the Unpräzisitätsklang (imprecision resonance) in Form, the instability, the positive (and also negative) movement, the flimmering, the Spannung, the unnaturalness of inner transgression (the inner Klänge of the point and the Fläche collide together, overshoot each other and rebound), the Doppelklang through one Form.
- **Rule**: A point at the edge of its own definition (nearly becoming a Fläche) produces a specific compound resonance: instability + Spannung + Doppelklang. Use this deliberately when complexity of expression through a single form is required.
- **Source**: p.24
- **Category**: point | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Abstrakte Form / Abstract Form
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: This multiplicity and complicatedness in the expression of the "smallest" form — achieved even through small changes of its size — offers a plausible example of the expressive power and depth of the abstract expressive means. With further development of the receiver's capacity, more precise terms would be determined with greater precision by means of size measurements. The numerical expression is here unavoidable.
- **Rule**: Abstract formal analysis requires quantitative precision. The inability to provide exact numerical expressions is the embryonic state of today's art theory; future theory will achieve this.
- **Source**: p.24–25
- **Category**: point | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Zahlenausdruck und Formel / Numerical Expression and Formula
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: There is only the danger that the numerical expression will remain behind the feeling-sensation and will thereby become an obstacle. It is related to the "Fliegentod" (flytrapper). The formula is also an easy club with which humans surround their warm arms. But on the other side, striving to free oneself from the clamps, preparing for further leaps, for new values and finally for new forms — formulas die and are replaced by newly born ones.
- **Rule**: Formulas and numerical expressions are tools toward inner necessity, not substitutes for it. When a formula becomes a habit, it must be broken like any other conventional habit.
- **Source**: p.24
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Form des Punktes — Kreisform / Form of the Point — Circle Form
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The outer boundary — Umrisse — of the point is what determines its äußere Form. Abstractly thought or in conception, the point is ideally small, ideally round — it is essentially a little ideal circle. Just as its size is relative, so also are its limits relative. In real form the point can take on infinitely many shapes — its circle form can get small pointed teeth, it can incline toward other geometric forms, can be pointed, can tend to the square. Through a tendency toward absolute immobility, it goes to the square. With jagged edges, the teeth can be small or large, fine or coarse in relation to each other, standing in various relationships to one another. There are no fixed boundaries here, and the realm of point-forms is unlimited (Fig. 3).
- **Rule**: The conceptual point is a circle. All real points deviate from this ideal. The degree and direction of deviation is a compositional variable with its own Klang.
- **Source**: p.25–26
- **Quote (DE)**: "Abstrakt gedacht oder in der Vorstellung ist der Punkt ideellklein, ideellrund. Er ist eigentlich ein ideellkleiner Kreis."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Abstractly conceived or in conception, the point is ideally small, ideally round. It is essentially an ideal small circle."
- **Category**: point

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Grundklang / Basic Resonance
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: So size and form are correspondingly variable according to the basic Klang of the point. This variability should not be understood otherwise than as a relative inner coloring of its inner ground nature, which always resonates with it.
- **Rule**: Every point has a Grundklang — its irreducible essential resonance — which persists regardless of size or form variation. Size and form are modulations on this Grundklang, not replacements for it.
- **Source**: p.25
- **Category**: point | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Absolutes / Absolutes
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: It must always be emphasized that completely mono-tone, so-to-speak monochromatically radiating elements in reality do not exist — not even the elements with the designation "basic" or "primal elements" ("Grundelemente") are of a more primitive nature, but on the contrary of a more complex nature. All concepts that sound like "primitivity" are also only relative concepts, and our so-called "scientific" language is merely relative. Absolutes we do not know.
- **Rule**: Never claim to work with absolute primal forms — all formal categories are relative. The discipline is the systematic analysis of relations, not the discovery of absolutes.
- **Source**: p.25
- **Quote (DE)**: "Es muß aber immer betont werden, daß vollkommen reinklingende, sozusagen einfarbig ausstrahlende Elemente in der Realität nicht existieren... Absolutes kennen wir nicht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "It must always be emphasized that completely pure-sounding, so-to-speak monochromatically radiating elements in reality do not exist... Absolutes we do not know."
- **Category**: inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-17]**: Innerer Begriff — Punkt als konzentrische Behauptung / Inner Concept — Point as Concentric Claim
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Inwardly understood, the Punkt as such sets up a certain Behauptung (claim) that is organically connected with the highest Zurückhaltung (reserve). The Punkt ist die innerlich knappste Form (the point is the inwardly most concise form). The point is introverted. This property does not fully disappear — even in cases of its outwardly most pointed form. The point is a small world — separated from all sides more or less uniformly from the surrounding and appears in cases of highest roundness as not present. Only the concentric Spannung reveals its inner nature more — all other properties point more to the square.
- **Rule**: The point's primary tensional quality is concentric (inward-pulling toward its own center). This differentiates it from all linear forms, which are eccentric.
- **Source**: p.26
- **Quote (DE)**: "D e r P u n k t i s t d i e i n n e r l i c h k n a p p s t e F o r m."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The point is the inwardly most concise form."
- **Category**: point | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-18]**: Spannung des Punktes / Tension of the Point
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The point's Spannung is ultimately always concentric — even in cases of eccentric tendencies, where a Doppelklang of the concentric and eccentric enters. The point is a small world — uniformly separated from all sides from the surrounding environment. Its fusion with the surrounding is minimal and it appears in cases of highest roundness as not present. It claims its place firmly and shows not the slightest inclination to move in any direction, neither horizontal nor vertical. The forward- and backward-movement is also not present. Only the concentric Spannung reveals its inner nature more — all other properties point more to the square.
- **Rule**: Design with the point means designing with inward-directed tension. To introduce movement, the point must be supplemented or replaced by a line.
- **Source**: p.26
- **Category**: point | tension

**PRINCIPLE [P-19]**: Fläche — Punkt als Fläche / Plane — Point as Plane
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The point asserts itself strongly in the Grundfläche and claims its place as the knappste ständige Behauptung (most concise constant claim), which arises briefly, firmly, and quickly. Therefore the point is in the outer and inner sense the Ur-element der Malerei und speziell der "Graphik" (primal element of painting and especially of "Graphic").
- **Rule**: The point is the irreducible primal element of all visual composition — in painting and especially in graphic work. Every composition traces back to this origin.
- **Source**: p.26
- **Quote (DE)**: "Deshalb ist der Punkt in äußerem und in innerem Sinne das U r - e l e m e n t d e r M a l e r e i und speziell der 'Graphik'."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Therefore the point is in outer and inner sense the primal element of painting and especially of 'Graphic'."
- **Category**: point | plane

**PRINCIPLE [P-20]**: "Element" und Element / "Element" and Element
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The concept of Element can be understood in two different ways — as outer and as inner concept. Outwardly every individual drawn or painted form is an Element. Inwardly it is not this form itself but the inner Spannung living within it. And in fact it is not the outer forms of the living work that constitute its content, but the living Kräfte (forces) = Spannungen living in these forms. If the Spannungen should suddenly disappear as if by magic, the living work would die immediately.
- **Rule**: The element is not the visible form — the element is the Spannung within the form. Work with Spannungen, not with shapes.
- **Source**: p.27
- **Quote (DE)**: "Innerlich ist nicht diese Form selbst, sondern die in ihr lebende innere Spannung ein Element."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Inwardly it is not this form itself but the inner tension living within it that is an element."
- **Category**: tension | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-21]**: Komposition — Begriff der Komposition / Composition — Concept of Composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: My definition of the concept "Komposition" is: Die Komposition ist die innerlich-zweckmäßige Unterordnung (1) der Einzelelemente and (2) des Aufbaues (Konstruktion) unter das konkrete malerische Ziel (Composition is the internally-purposive subordination of (1) individual elements and (2) the construction under the concrete pictorial goal). Therefore: when a Einklang (unison) is the given pictorial goal that is exhaustively embodied, then the Einklang in this case equals a Komposition.
- **Rule**: Composition is not assembly of parts — it is the subordination of all elements to a single internally-determined goal. A single point in a Grundfläche, if it fully embodies the goal, is already a Komposition.
- **Source**: p.31
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Komposition ist die innerlich-zweckmäßige Unterordnung 1. der Einzelelemente und 2. des Aufbaues (Konstruktion) unter das konkrete malerische Ziel."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Composition is the internally-purposive subordination of (1) individual elements and (2) the construction under the concrete pictorial goal."
- **Category**: composition | inner-necessity

**PRINCIPLE [P-22]**: Basis der Komposition / Basis of Composition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Outwardly grasped, differences in compositions are merely quantitative, where in the case of the "Urbild des malerischen Ausdrucks" the self-sufficient element is completely absent. Even when the estimation of the work takes an exclusively qualitative Basis, the composition is at minimum a Einklang. This minimum is two-fold — with clear emphasis on the difference between outer and inner Maßen und Mitteln. That purely single Zweiklänge at exact observation do not actually occur can here only be stated as a claim; it will be proven elsewhere. In any case a composition on qualitative Basis arises only through the use of Mehrklängen (poly-tones).
- **Rule**: The qualitative basis of composition always requires at least a Einklang; quantitative compositions (merely counting differences) are never sufficient on their own.
- **Source**: p.31
- **Category**: composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-23]**: Azentraler Aufbau / Acentric Construction
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In the moment of the displacement of the point from the center of the Grundfläche — acentric construction — the Doppelklang becomes audible: (1) absolute Klang of the point, (2) Klang of the given position of the Grundfläche. The second Klang, that by the central construction was silenced to silence, becomes clearly audible again and transforms the absolute Klang of the point into a relative one.
- **Rule**: Moving a point from the center of the Grundfläche activates both the point's own Klang and the Klang of its position within the Grundfläche. Central placement suppresses positional Klang; acentric placement makes it audible.
- **Source**: p.31
- **Quote (DE)**: "In dem Augenblick der Verschiebung des Punktes aus dem Zentrum der Grundfläche — azentraler Aufbau — wird der Doppelklang hörbar: 1. absoluter Klang des Punktes, 2. Klang der gegebenen Stelle der Grundfläche."
- **Quote (EN)**: "In the moment of the displacement of the point from the center of the Grundfläche — acentric construction — the double-tone becomes audible: 1. absolute resonance of the point, 2. resonance of the given position of the Grundfläche."
- **Category**: point | plane | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-24]**: Quantitative Vermehrung — Mehrere Punkte / Quantitative Multiplication — Multiple Points
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: A companion point on the Grundfläche gives rise to a far more complicated result. The repetition is a mighty means of intensifying the inner Spannung, and is at the same time a means for the production of primitive rhythm, which is in turn a means for the production of primitive harmony in every work of art. Apart from the two tones we have to do with: 1. inner Klang of the first point, 2. repetition of this Klang, 3. Doppelklang of the first point, 4. Doppelklang of the second point, 5. Klang of the sum of all these Klänge.
- **Rule**: Each added point multiplies the number of relational Klänge exponentially. With two points: 5 distinct Klänge must be heard simultaneously. Composition is the management of this multiplicity.
- **Source**: p.32
- **Quote (DE)**: "Die Wiederholung ist ein mächtiges Mittel zur Steigerung der inneren Spannung, und ist gleichzeitig ein Mittel zum primitiven Rhythmus, welcher wieder ein Mittel zur Erzielung der primitiven Harmonie in jeder Kunst ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Repetition is a mighty means for intensifying inner tension, and is at the same time a means for primitive rhythm, which is in turn a means for achieving primitive harmony in every art."
- **Category**: point | tension | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-25]**: Natur — Punkt in der Natur / Nature — Point in Nature
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: In another non-pictorial realm — in Nature — the accumulation of points occurs purposively and organically-necessarily. These natural forms are in reality small spatial bodies and stand in the same relationship to the abstract (geometric) point as the painterly one. Looking at seeds, we see when some open like a beautiful, smooth poppy-globe (ultimately is a larger sphere point), we discover in this warm globe a compositionally-planned built-up heap of cold blue-grey points, which carry in themselves the latent resting propagation force, exactly like the painterly point. Also in Nature the point is an inwardly turned entity full of possibilities (Figs. 5 u. 6).
- **Rule**: Nature uses the point as a compositional unit in the same structural way as painting. The astronomer's star cluster and the botanist's seed structure are analytical evidence for point-composition laws.
- **Source**: p.32–33 / p.36
- **Category**: point | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-26]**: Punkt in anderen Künsten / Point in Other Arts
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Points are to be found in all arts, and their inner force will more and more rise to the consciousness of the artist. Their significance may not be overlooked. In Plastik und Architektur the point is the result of the intersection of several Flächen — it is the conclusion of a room-corner and on the other side the origin of the emergence of these Flächen. In Gothic buildings the points are emphasized through sharp spikings — in Chinese buildings, the point leading curve is given short, precise reports, clear as the transition to the dissolution of the Luftraum (air space) that surrounds the building. A deliberate application of the point can be assumed in buildings of this type — it occurs in planned, compositionally striving to the highest point masses. Spitze = Punkt (Point = Peak). In dance (Tanz), especially in the old ballet form, the term "Pointen" derives from "point" — the quick running on tips of toes leaves the point on the floor. Modern Tanz: in some cases the fivefold tip forms a five-cornered Fläche — head, two hands, two feet-tips, where the ten fingers form ten smaller points (Fig. 9, Fig. 10). Also the rigid, short immobilities can be grasped as points — active and passive Punktierung.
- **Rule**: Any sudden cessation of movement (in dance, architecture, music) functions compositionally as a Punkt. The formal law of the point extends across all art forms.
- **Source**: p.34 / p.36
- **Category**: point | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-27]**: Punkt in der Malerei — Zeit / Point in Painting — Time
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The absence of movement-drive on and away from the Fläche reduces the perception-time of the point to minimum, and the time element in composition in special cases makes the point unavoidable. It equals short drum strokes or triangle beats in music, or short pecks of the woodpecker's beak in Nature.
- **Rule**: The point is the minimum time-unit of visual composition. Use it when maximum temporal compression is required — it is the visual equivalent of the percussion beat.
- **Source**: p.28
- **Category**: point | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-28]**: Zeit in der Malerei / Time in Painting
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The question of the time element in painting stands for itself and is not simple. A few years ago a wall was introduced here that separated painting and music as two art territories from each other. The seemingly clear and justified division: Painting — Raum (Fläche) / Musik — Zeit was introduced.
- **Rule**: The Malerei-Raum / Musik-Zeit division is a surface-level classification only. Both arts share formal time-elements; the theoretical boundary between them must be questioned by any serious Kunstwissenschaft.
- **Source**: p.28–29
- **Category**: composition | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-29]**: Urbild des malerischen Ausdrucks / Primordial Image of Pictorial Expression
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The simplest and most concise is the case of the centrally-lying point — the point in the center of the Grundfläche, which is a square (Fig. 4). The driving-back of the Grundflächen-Wirkung reaches its maximum force here and presents a singular case. The two-tone — Punkt, Fläche — takes on the character of a Einklanges: the Fläche-Klang can relatively not be counted. On the path of simplification this is the last in the series of successive resolutions of Einklänge and Doppelklänge, through the switching-off of all more complicated elements — reduction of the composition to the single primal element. This case therefore represents the Urbild des malerischen Ausdrucks (primordial image of pictorial expression).
- **Rule**: A single point at the exact center of a square Grundfläche is the limiting case of composition — the Urbild. All other compositions are elaborations of this origin-state.
- **Source**: p.30
- **Quote (DE)**: "So stellt dieser Fall das U r b i l d d e s m a l e r i s c h e n A u s d r u c k s dar."
- **Quote (EN)**: "This case therefore represents the primordial image of pictorial expression."
- **Category**: point | plane | composition

**PRINCIPLE [P-30]**: Anzahl der Elemente im Werke / Number of Elements in the Work
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Theoretically the point can, if it is at the same time (1) a complex (size + form) and (2) a sharply circumscribed unity, in certain cases of its interaction with the Grundfläche be a sufficient expressive means. Purely schematically considered, a work can consist of a single point as its last end. This claim should not be held for idle. The question of how these elements are used is as important as the question of the necessary number of them for even a schematically conceived work.
- **Rule**: The number of elements in a work is not a question of abundance but of necessity. A single point can be sufficient. The question is always: what does inner necessity require?
- **Source**: p.29–30
- **Category**: composition | inner-necessity

## Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Befreiung des Punktes durch Kontext-Versetzung / Liberation of the Point through Context Displacement
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: The student places a point first within a running sentence (Erster Fall), then isolated on the same page as a sentence (Zweiter Fall), then fully removed from typographic context with increasing free space and increased size. The student observes at each stage: which Klang is dominant — the Klang of the surrounding writing or the Klang of the point itself?
- **Purpose**: Training perception of how context determines Klang. Trains the ability to hear inner tensions rather than see external forms.
- **Source**: p.21–22

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Variation der Punktformen / Variation of Point Forms
- **Task** [INFERRED]: The student produces a series of point forms (as in Fig. 3) — circle, square, triangle-tending, jagged, asymmetric — and analyzes the Grundklang of each. How does the deviation from the ideal circle (ideellrund) affect the concentric vs. eccentric tension?
- **Purpose**: Trains the understanding that point form is a variable — the ideal is the circle; all real points deviate. Understanding the Klang of that deviation.
- **Source**: p.25–26

**EXERCISE [E-03]**: Zentraler vs. azentraler Aufbau / Central vs. Acentric Construction
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: A single point is placed first at the exact center of a square Grundfläche (as in Fig. 4). The student then displaces it to various positions and listens for the Doppelklang — both the absolute Klang of the point and the Klang of its position within the Grundfläche. At center: Einklang. Off-center: Doppelklang.
- **Purpose**: Trains awareness of the Grundfläche as an active compositional force — specifically that the Grundfläche's spatial zones have different Klänge that interact with whatever is placed in them.
- **Source**: p.31

**EXERCISE [E-04]**: Quantitative Vermehrung — Analyse der Klänge / Quantitative Multiplication — Analysis of Tones
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Two points are placed on a Grundfläche. The student enumerates all 5 relational Klänge (inner Klang of point 1; repetition of that Klang; Doppelklang of point 1; Doppelklang of point 2; Klang of the sum) and assigns a quality to each. Then a third point is added and the multiplication is tracked again.
- **Purpose**: Trains compositional hearing — the capacity to perceive simultaneous multiple Klänge and to understand why composition (innerlich-zweckmäßige Unterordnung) is necessary to organize them.
- **Source**: p.32

## Visual Evidence

**FIGURE [Fig-01]**: Fig. 1 — Punkt mit freier Umgebung (implied, referenced in text)
- **Shows**: A point placed outside the context of running text with expanding free space around it; the point and the free environment.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-06 — isolation and free space cause the Klang of the surrounding writing to diminish and the Klang of the point to increase in clarity and force.
- **Text reference**: "Bei Vergrößerung der freien Umgebung und der Größe des Punktes selbst vermindert sich der Klang der Schrift und der Klang des Punktes gewinnt an Deutlichkeit und Kraft (Fig. 1)."
- **Source**: p.21

**FIGURE [Fig-02]**: Fig. 2 — Punkt und dünne Linie auf Grundfläche
- **Shows**: A large filled black circle (point) with a thin curving line on the same Grundfläche; the line is lighter and more sinuous than the point.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-10 — the same form that was a "point" on an empty Grundfläche becomes a Fläche the moment a thin line is introduced. Relative size defines identity.
- **Text reference**: "Was noch immer als Punkt auf der sonst leeren Grundfläche gelten kann, das muß als Fläche bezeichnet werden, wenn z. B. eine sehr dünne Linie auf die Grundfläche hinzukommt (Fig. 2)."
- **Source**: p.23

**FIGURE [Fig-03]**: Fig. 3 — Beispiele der Punktformen / Examples of Point Forms
- **Shows**: Two rows of 16 point-forms: circle, square, diamond, five-pointed star (solid), triangle (pointing up), triangle (pointing down), irregular spiky star, cross-star, four-pointed star variant, heart-like form, snowflake-like, irregular spiky form — showing the full range from smooth-geometric to jagged-organic.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-14 — the realm of point-forms is unlimited. The ideal point is the circle; deviations toward angularity, toward organic irregularity, toward square, toward triangle constitute the infinite range of actual point-forms, each with its own Klang.
- **Text reference**: "Hier sind keine Grenzen festzustellen, und das Reich der Punkte ist unbegrenzt (Fig. 3)."
- **Source**: p.26

**FIGURE [Fig-04]**: Fig. 4 — Zentralpunkt in quadratischer Grundfläche / Central Point in Square Grundfläche
- **Shows**: A black square outline (the Grundfläche) with a single small filled black circle positioned at the geometric center. Absolute symmetry in all directions.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-29 — the Urbild des malerischen Ausdrucks. The central point in the square Grundfläche is the ultimate simplification of composition: the Grundflächen-Wirkung is maximally suppressed, the two-tone becomes a Einklang, all complex elements are eliminated.
- **Text reference**: "Der einfachste und knappste ist der Fall des zentralliegenden Punktes — des Punktes im Zentrum der Grundfläche, die ein Quadrat ist (Fig. 4)."
- **Source**: p.30

**FIGURE [Fig-05]**: Fig. 5 — Sternhaufen im Herkules
- **Shows**: A full-page black-and-white astronomical photograph of a globular star cluster (Hercules cluster). Dense white points at center, gradually dispersing outward. Source: Newcomb-Engelmans Popul. Astronomie, Leipzig 1921, S. 294.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-25 — Nature produces point-compositions by necessity and purposiveness. The star cluster is a natural analog of the point-composition; the stars are compositional points at astronomical scale, following the same formal laws as the painterly point.
- **Text reference**: "Auch in der Natur ist der Punkt ein in sich gekehrtes Wesen voller Möglichkeiten (Fig. 5 u. 6)."
- **Source**: p.33 (full-page plate)

**FIGURE [Fig-06]**: Fig. 6 — Nitritbilder, 1000-fach vergrößert
- **Shows**: Microscopic photograph of nitrite crystals at 1000x magnification, showing irregular organic-shaped dark points distributed within a lighter circular field. Source: Kultur d. Gegenwart, T. III, Abtlg. IV, 3, S. 71.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-25 — at microscopic scale, organic chemistry produces point-formations structurally parallel to those in painting and astronomy. Points in nature accumulate in "heaps" (Komplexe geometrischer Punkte) that behave like painterly compositions.
- **Text reference**: Reference same as Fig. 5 — "auch in der Natur ist der Punkt ein in sich gekehrtes Wesen voller Möglichkeiten."
- **Source**: p.33

**FIGURE [Fig-07]**: Fig. 7 — Ling-ying-zi-Außentor / Chinese Outer Gate
- **Shows**: Black ink illustration of a Chinese architectural gate structure — the Ling-ying-zi outer gate — showing curved roof elements converging to upward-pointing tips. Source: "China" v. Bernd Melchers, 2 Bd., Folkwang Vlg., Hagen i. W., 1922.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-26 — in Chinese architecture the point is deliberately emphasized: the curve leading to a point achieves the transition to the dissolution of the Luftraum. Short, precise reports, clear as point — compositional accent.
- **Text reference**: "In chinesischen Bauten durch eine zum Punkt führende Kurve ebenso klar erreicht wird — es werden kurze, präzise Anschläge hörbar, als Übergang zur Auflösung des Luftraums, die in dem den Bau umgebenden Luftraum verklingt."
- **Source**: p.34

**FIGURE [Fig-08]**: Fig. 8 — Pagode der Drachenstuhlheit in Schanghai (erbaut 1411)
- **Shows**: Full-page line drawing of a Chinese pagoda — seven-tiered, each tier with upward-curving eave tips — rising to a point at apex. The entire structure is a composition of successively smaller tiers converging to the highest point.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-26 — the pagoda is the architectural embodiment of the compositional principle "Spitze = Punkt" (peak = point). The entire mass of the building is organized as a trajectory toward the point at top; the point is the compositional goal of the whole construction.
- **Text reference**: "Gerade an Bauten von dieser Art läßt sich eine bewußte Anwendung des Punktes vermuten, da er hier in planmäßig aufgeteilten und kompositionsmäßig zur höchsten Spitze strebenden Massen vorkommt. Spitze = Punkt."
- **Source**: p.35 (full-page plate)

**FIGURE [Fig-10]**: Fig. 10 — Graphisches Schema des Sprunges / Graphic Schema of the Leap
- **Shows**: Diagram of a modern dance leap by Tänzerin Palucca — two overlapping circles (body trajectory) with small point clusters at extremities (head, hands, feet) and two isolated points (left and right margin). The crossing point of the circles is emphasized.
- **Demonstrates** [CERTAIN]: P-26 — in the modern Tanz, the body forms five tip-points (head, 2 hands, 2 feet-tips), and the ten fingers form ten smaller points. The Tanzfläche is punctuated by both active points (moving) and passive points (still moments). Schema maps the spatial trajectory and point-moments of the leap.
- **Text reference**: "Auch die starren, kurzen Unbeweglichkeiten können als Punkte aufgefaßt werden. Also aktive und passive Punktierung, was in Zusammenhang mit der musikalischen Form des Punktes steht."
- **Source**: p.36
