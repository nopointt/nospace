## Tischlerei — Fortsetzung (Carpentry Workshop — Continued)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The furniture workshop produces objects whose form is determined entirely by use-requirements: folding, stacking, combining, transforming. Decoration is absent because it would contradict the logic of the object.
**Evidence**: Brendel's folding tea table, Buscher's modular room-furniture system, Buscher's children's building blocks and toys, Schrammen's child's scooter, Nosselt's chess table, Hartwig's Bauhaus chess set — each demonstrates a different aspect of functional form-finding.
**Conclusion**: The Tischlerei output proves that Reduktion is not poverty of means but precision of means: every element present is necessary, nothing present is ornamental.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Klappbarkeit als Gestaltungsprinzip / Foldability as a design principle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Brendel's tea table (p.35) uses folding side panels (herunterklappbare Flügel) to transform a compact storage form into an extended service surface. The cross-plan of the extended table is not decorative — it is the direct geometric consequence of four fold-out panels meeting at a square centre.
- **Rule**: Design the transformation mechanism first; let the resting and extended forms both follow from that mechanism's geometry.
- **Source**: p.35
- **Quote (DE)**: "Teetisch mit herunterklappbaren Flügeln."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Tea table with fold-down wings."
- **Category**: function | form-economy

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Baukastenprinzip — Möbel als kombinierbare Einheiten / Building-block principle — furniture as combinable units
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Buscher's modular room furniture (pp.36–38) treats individual storage units, tables, and rolling elements as a combinable system. The photograph on p.36 shows the full room arrangement with units deployed as desk, storage wall, and rolling cart — all from the same type-vocabulary. The system is usable als Tisch, Stuhl und Wagen (as table, chair, and cart).
- **Rule**: Design furniture as a type-system, not as individual pieces; each unit must be combinable with every other unit without adaptation.
- **Source**: pp.36–37
- **Quote (DE)**: "Benutzbar als Tisch, Stuhl und Wagen verwendbar"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Usable as table, chair, and cart"
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Lackierung als Materialwahrheit / Lacquer as material honesty
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Buscher's modular furniture (p.37) is described as "farbig lackiert, Kästen oben und teilweise unten mit Linoleumbelag" — coloured lacquer on wood, with linoleum surface facing. The lacquer is not disguising the wood as another material; it is protecting and differentiating functional zones. The linoleum facing on work surfaces is an honest declaration of wear-resistance function.
- **Rule**: Surface treatment must declare function — protective coating and wear-surface are legitimate; imitation of another material is not.
- **Source**: p.37
- **Quote (DE)**: "farbig lackiert, Kästen oben und teilweise unten mit Linoleumbelag"
- **Quote (EN)**: "coloured lacquer, boxes with linoleum facing top and partially below"
- **Category**: material-honesty

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Spielzeug als Gestaltungsaufgabe / Toy as design problem
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: The children's toys on pp.39–41 (Buscher building blocks/ships, Buscher/Herschel play figures from coloured wood, Buscher play set with multiple configurations) are not minor workshop output — they are application of the same Gestaltungsgesetz as furniture. Simple geometric solids in lacquered hardwood. The play-forms emerge from combining a limited vocabulary of types (sphere, disc, cylinder, cube, cone).
- **Rule**: A toy is a type-set; design the minimal geometric vocabulary from which maximum combinatorial play emerges.
- **Source**: pp.39–41
- **Category**: form-economy | standardization

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Funktion als Form des Schachfigur / Function as the form of the chess piece
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Hartwig's Bauhausschach (pp.44–45) is explicitly described as "Ein Spiel mit NEUEN BRETTSTEINEN, die entsprechend ihrer Funktion gestaltet sind" — the pieces are designed according to their function, meaning their movement-rule. The text fully describes the formal logic: BAUER und TURM (Pawn and Rook) move at right angles, expressed by the cube; SPRINGER (Knight) moves in L-shape across 4 squares, expressed by a right-angle hook form; LÄUFER (Bishop) moves diagonally to the board-edge, expressed by a diagonal cross cut from a cube; KÖNIG (King) moves at right angles and diagonally — a small cube on a larger cube; DAME (Queen), the most mobile piece, is a cylinder and sphere — in strong contrast to the cubic King, expressing her superior range.
- **Rule**: Let the operational rule of each component directly generate its form — the user should be able to read the function from the shape without instruction.
- **Source**: pp.44–45
- **Quote (DE)**: "Ein Spiel mit NEUEN BRETTSTEINEN, die entsprechend ihrer Funktion gestaltet sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "A game with NEW BOARD PIECES, designed according to their function."
- **Category**: function | form-economy

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Dame als beweglichste Form / Queen as the most mobile form
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The text for the Bauhaus chess set explicitly states the Dame (Queen) "ist die beweglichste Figur, besteht aus Zylinder und Kugel. Sie steht in starkem Kontrast zu König, Turm und Bauer, deren Form der Würfel ist zur Darstellung des Schweren und Massigen." The Queen's rotational symmetry (cylinder + sphere) signals freedom of movement in all directions; the cubic forms signal directional restriction and mass.
- **Rule**: Use rotational symmetry to signal omnidirectional freedom; use rectilinear mass to signal constrained, axial movement.
- **Source**: p.45
- **Quote (DE)**: "die beweglichste Figur, besteht aus Zylinder und Kugel. Sie steht in starkem Kontrast zu König, Turm und Bauer, deren Form der Würfel ist zur Darstellung des Schweren und Massigen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "the most mobile piece, consists of cylinder and sphere. It stands in strong contrast to King, Rook, and Pawn, whose form is the cube — for the representation of the heavy and massive."
- **Category**: function | form-economy

## Metallwerkstatt (Metal Workshop)

### Argument Structure
**Thesis**: The Metallwerkstatt, under Formmeister L. Moholy-Nagy and Technischer Meister Chr. Dell, produces vessels and objects whose form is the direct result of metalworking logic: the sphere and hemisphere as the natural form of spun metal, the cylinder as the natural form of drawn tube, ebonite (Ebenholz) as the thermally honest handle material.
**Evidence**: Brandt's tea infuser, tea service, and silver service; Rittweger's extract pot; Krajewski's tea glass holder; Wagenfeld's saucière; Albers's glass-and-silver fruit bowls — all demonstrate material specificity: metal is formed by rotation and pressure, not by carving or casting decoration.
**Conclusion**: The Metallwerkstatt output demonstrates that Industrieform in metal means exploiting the rotational geometries natural to lathe and spinning-wheel production, combined with material contrast (metal body / ebonite handle) that honestly declares thermal function.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Drehform als Materialwahrheit des Metalls / Rotational form as material honesty in metal
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Every vessel in the Metallwerkstatt section (Brandt tea service pp.48–49, silver service p.53; Rittweger extract pot p.47; Wagenfeld saucière p.54) is built from spheres, hemispheres, cylinders, and discs — the geometries produced by rotating metal on a lathe or spinning-wheel. No faceted or carved forms appear. The reflective polished surface is not decorative finish but the natural result of metalworking and the proof of material quality.
- **Rule**: In metal, design exclusively with forms producible by rotation and pressure; polished reflectivity is the honest surface of worked metal, not an added decoration.
- **Source**: pp.46–54
- **Category**: material-honesty | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Materialkombination nach Funktion / Material combination by function
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Across all Metallwerkstatt pieces, handles and knobs are consistently Ebenholz (ebonite/ebony), while bodies are Neusilber (German silver/alpaca), Messing (brass), Bronze, or Tombak. The ebonite handle is not a decorative contrast — it is thermally non-conductive, solving the heat-transfer problem that a metal handle cannot solve. The combination is dictated by functional differentiation, not aesthetic preference.
- **Rule**: Combine materials only where each material uniquely solves a sub-function that the other cannot; never mix materials for decorative contrast alone.
- **Source**: pp.46–54
- **Quote (DE)**: "Neusilber und Bronze mit Ebenholzgriffen; innen versilbert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "German silver and bronze with ebonite handles; silver-plated interior."
- **Category**: material-honesty | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Versilberung als funktionale Oberfläche / Silver plating as functional surface
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Several pieces (Brandt tea infuser p.46, Brandt tea service p.49) are noted as "innen versilbert" (silver-plated interior). This is not decorative — silver is chemically inert and does not affect taste, making it the functionally correct interior surface for tea vessels. The exterior remains unplated Neusilber or bronze, maintaining material contrast between functional zones.
- **Rule**: Apply surface treatment only where function requires it; differentiate treated from untreated surfaces as a declaration of functional zones.
- **Source**: pp.46, 49
- **Category**: material-honesty | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Glas-Metall-Kombination / Glass-metal combination
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Albers's Fruchtschalen (fruit bowls, p.52) combine Glas (glass), Neusilber (German silver), and Holzkugeln (wooden balls). The glass bowl is held by a flat silver ring-frame with wooden ball spacers at three points. The combination is structurally honest: glass provides the transparent containing form; silver provides the structural ring that holds and lifts; wood provides the non-slip, non-scratching contact point. Each material solves a distinct sub-problem.
- **Rule**: In mixed-material objects, each material must solve a problem the others cannot — structural ring, transparent container, non-slip contact.
- **Source**: p.52
- **Quote (DE)**: "Fruchtschalen. Glas, Neusilber mit Holzkugeln."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Fruit bowls. Glass, German silver with wooden balls."
- **Category**: material-honesty | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Sieb als konstruktives Element / Strainer as constructive element
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Both the Brandt tea infuser (p.46, "Kännnchen für Tee-Extrakt mit tiefem Sieb") and the Rittweger extract pot (p.47, "Tee-Extraktkanne, Messing mit Neusilbersieb und Ebenholzknopf") integrate the strainer as a structural sub-component, not as an add-on accessory. The strainer is visible, accessible, and defines the formal relationship between vessel and lid. In Krajewski's tea glass holder (p.50, "mit federndem Ring zum Festhalten des Glases"), the spring-loaded retention ring is equally a structural-functional element that becomes visible form.
- **Rule**: Make functional sub-components structurally visible — the strainer, the spring ring, the hinge are not to be hidden; they are the object's honest anatomy.
- **Source**: pp.46–47, 50
- **Category**: function | material-honesty
