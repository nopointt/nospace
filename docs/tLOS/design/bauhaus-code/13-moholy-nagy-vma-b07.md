## III. Das Volumen (Plastik): Übergang zur kinetischen Plastik · Geschichte des kinetischen · Das Licht

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The static sculptural form — however refined — remains incomplete. The Plastik must transcend equilibrium and enter movement; only through movement (Bewegung) does volume begin to generate virtual spatial relationships that cannot be achieved by any static arrangement. This progression leads inevitably to light as the final, highest medium for constructing virtual volume and space.

**Evidence**: Student Gleichgewichtsstudien (balance studies) from multiple Bauhaus semesters (1924–1928); Rodchenko's 1921 Konstruktion; biotechnical construction elements including the spiral; Bauhaus student kinetic sculptures and suspended works; Naum Gabo's kinetic pendulum (1922); Joost Schmidt's paraboloid and hyperboloid works (1928); Fritz Kuhr's "vierdimensionale" Plastik (1924); Franz Ehrlich's mechanically moving Schaufenster designs; toy and fireworks photographs demonstrating virtual volume through motion; and the historical manifesto evidence from Kemény and Moholy-Nagy (1922) and Gabo/Pevsner (1920).

**Conclusion**: Plastik = Material + Massenbeziehungen. The kinetic sculpture reveals a formula: Plastik = Material + Volumenbeziehungen. This elevates the discipline beyond static form into temporal and spatial event. Light, finally introduced at the close of this batch, is identified as the most powerful instrument yet discovered for generating virtual volume — spatial construction through immaterial means.

## Über die plastische „Form"

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The concept of "plastische Form" must be redefined. It is not derived from stereometric or biotechnical construction elements alone, but from the analysis of the developmental stages (Entwicklungsstadien) of Plastik itself.

**Evidence**: The author introduces the distinction between what is understood today as "plastische Form" and what actually governs it: not the use of stereometric (cube, sphere, cylinder, pyramid) or biotechnical construction elements, but the formal governing law derived from the analysis of plastic development.

**Conclusion**: Understanding "plastische Form" requires tracing the developmental logic, not cataloguing geometric units.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Plastische Form / Plastic Form
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The term "plastische Form" as commonly understood — derived from stereometric shapes or biotechnical construction elements — must be replaced by a definition grounded in the Entwicklungsstadien of sculpture itself. Form is not a catalogue of shapes; it is the outcome of a governing law applied to material.
- **Rule**: Define form through its developmental logic, not its geometric inventory.
- **Source**: p.148
- **Quote (DE)**: "der wirkliche Formbegriff der plastik ergibt sich aus der analyse der plastischen entwicklungsstadien — nicht aber aus der verwendung von stereometrischen oder biotechnischen konstruktionselementen (figuren), was man heute noch im allgemeinen unter »plastische form« versteht."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The true concept of form in sculpture arises from the analysis of the developmental stages of sculpture — not from the use of stereometric or biotechnical construction elements (figures), which is what is still today generally understood by 'plastic form'."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Stereometrische Konstruktionselemente / Stereometric Construction Elements
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Until recently, stereometric elements — sphere, cylinder, cube, prism, pyramid — were taken as the foundation of sculptural design. These are now to be supplemented by biotechnical construction elements (Figuren): crystal, sphere, cone, plane (Fläche), band, rod, spiral (Schraube). The biologist Raoul Francé identifies seven such biotechnical elements as the fundamental constructive elements of the entire world.
- **Rule**: Extend the sculptural vocabulary beyond classical stereometry to include biotechnical forms — especially the spiral, which proves the most versatile.
- **Source**: p.148
- **Quote (DE)**: "bis vor kurzem wurden überwiegend die stereometrischen elemente wie kugel, kegel, zylinder, kubus, prisma, pyramide als grundlage der plastik angenommen (abb. 134). heute wäre als neue gruppe die verwendung biotechnischer elemente hinzuzufügen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Until recently the stereometric elements — sphere, cone, cylinder, cube, prism, pyramid — were predominantly taken as the foundation of sculpture (fig. 134). Today a new group would need to be added: the use of biotechnical elements."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Die Spirale als biotechnisches Konstruktionselement / The Spiral as Biotechnical Construction Element
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Among biotechnical construction elements, the spiral (Schraube) is the most versatile. Its constructive use led to astonishing solutions compared with earlier (Baroque) aesthetics. Through conscious deployment of the full inventory of technical-biotechnical construction elements, a new sense of beauty emerged.
- **Rule**: The spiral is the paradigmatic biotechnical form: deploy it structurally, not decoratively — it carries the entire construction.
- **Source**: p.149
- **Quote (DE)**: "die konstruktive verwendung dieser elemente, ganz besonders der spirale (schraube) — führte zu überraschenden lösungen gegenüber den früheren (barocken) ästetischen. (abb. 135, 136) so gelangte man zu der bewußten auswertung des ganzen vorrats technisch-biotechnischer konstruktionselemente. das ergab zugleich einen neuen schönheitsbegriff."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The constructive use of these elements, especially the spiral — led to astonishing solutions compared with earlier (Baroque) aesthetics. (fig. 135, 136) Thus one arrived at the conscious exploitation of the full inventory of technical-biotechnical construction elements. This also yielded a new concept of beauty."
- **Category**: volume

### Exercises & Pedagogy

**EXERCISE [E-01]**: Gleichgewichtsstudie (Balance Study)
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Students construct free-standing sculptures in which equilibrium is achieved through the formal relationships of elements, not through a stabilizing base. Multiple materials are combined (glass, metal, wood). The base (Sockel) is to be abolished — the sculpture must balance on a minimal contact point or stand without a traditional plinth.
- **Purpose**: Trains understanding of physical force relationships (Gleichgewichts- und Durchdringungsprobleme) as a prerequisite to constructive Plastik. References PRINCIPLE [P-01] — form arises from governing law, not from geometric inventory.
- **Source**: pp.142–147 (abb. 127–133)

**EXERCISE [E-02]**: Gleichgewichtsstudie auf spezifischen Gewichten verschiedener Holzarten / Balance Study on Specific Weights of Different Woods
- **Task** [CERTAIN]: Construct a balance study in which the right half is made of heavy wood to counterbalance the extended left part made of light wood. The sculpture must stand fully balanced on a single point.
- **Purpose**: Trains direct material knowledge (Factur, specific weight) as a constructive parameter — the material's intrinsic character determines form. References PRINCIPLE [P-02].
- **Source**: p.147 (abb. 133, Toma Grote, Bauhaus erstes Semester 1924)

## Der Übergang zur kinetischen Plastik

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The five stadiums of Plastik culminate not in the static suspended form but in Bewegung itself. The transition from the static to the kinetic is not a rupture — it is the logical completion of the trajectory from material to spatial event.

**Evidence**: Three stadiums are identified in the approach to kinetische Plastik: (1) illusionistische kinetik — forms that appear to move but do not; (2) schwebende Plastik — forms liberated from gravity; (3) actual kinetic constructions. Through Bilderbeispiele (abb. 127–141) these three stadia are shown to be developmentally necessary.

**Conclusion**: "Plastik = Material + Massenbeziehungen" must be upgraded to "Plastik = Material + Volumenbeziehungen." The sculptural event is the relationship between volumes — which can only be fully expressed when those volumes move through space or generate virtual volumes through movement.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Plastik = Material + Volumenbeziehungen / Sculpture = Material + Volume-Relations
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The final formulation of sculpture's essence: it is not simply material plus mass relationships (Massenbeziehungen), but material plus Volumenbeziehungen — the relationships between volumes in space, including virtual volumes generated through movement.
- **Rule**: Design sculpture as a system of volume-relations, not as a mass-arrangement.
- **Source**: p.155
- **Quote (DE)**: "damit wechselt das ursprüngliche phänomen: von der plastik = material + massenbeziehungen zur aufgabe der aufgelockerten objekten »plastik = material + volumenbeziehungen.«"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Thus the original phenomenon shifts: from sculpture = material + mass-relations to the task of the loosened objects: 'sculpture = material + volume-relations.'"
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Virtuelles Volumen / Virtual Volume
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Movement generates volume. A static form occupies real volume; a moving form generates virtual volume — a spatial envelope traced by the moving element that is perceptually present but materially absent. This virtual volume is as real in spatial experience as any solid form.
- **Rule**: Treat the virtual volume traced by a moving element as a designed spatial event, not a by-product of motion.
- **Source**: pp.155–156
- **Quote (DE)**: "die schwebende und zugleich kinetische plastik; d. h. sie entstehen durch bewegung von meist linienhaften materialumfangen, ringen, stäben und anderen objekten (abb. 140, 141, 149, 152). damit wechselt das ursprüngliche phänomen: von der plastik = material + massenbeziehungen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The suspended and simultaneously kinetic sculpture; that is, they arise through the movement of mostly linear material extents, rings, rods, and other objects (fig. 140, 141, 149, 152). Thus the original phenomenon shifts."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Die schwebende Plastik — Aufhebung der Basis / Suspended Sculpture — Abolition of the Base
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The schwebende Plastik is the fourth stadium of sculptural development. Its defining characteristic is the abolition of the Sockel (plinth/base) — the sculpture no longer sits on a stabilizing mass but floats, its support made immaterial or invisible. This requires calculating all material values: Biegsamkeitsgrenze, Elastizität, Materialwerte.
- **Rule**: Eliminate the base as a stabilizing mass; design equilibrium into the structure itself so it can stand or float without a plinth.
- **Source**: p.153
- **Quote (DE)**: "die materialwerte, biegsamkeitsgrenze, elastizität usw. werden bei diesen arbeiten hineinkalkuliert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The material values, bending threshold, elasticity, etc., are calculated into these works."
- **Category**: volume

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Auflösung des festen Materials durch Bewegung / Dissolution of Solid Material through Movement
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Movement dissolves solid material into virtual form. When a solid spins or oscillates, its Faktur, Textur, and structure become negligible — what matters is the virtual spatial envelope generated. This is proven by the spinning top: at rest it shows material character; in motion it becomes pure virtual volume.
- **Rule**: When designing for movement, the material's intrinsic character (Faktur) becomes irrelevant — design the virtual volume, not the object.
- **Source**: p.164
- **Quote (DE)**: "das untere bild zeigt deutlich, daß die beschaffenheit des materials, seine struktur, textur und faktur bei der bildung des virtuellen volumens vernachlässigbare größen sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The lower image shows clearly that the constitution of the material — its structure, Textur, and Faktur — are negligible quantities in the formation of the virtual volume."
- **Category**: volume

## Die schwebende Plastik (Stadium 4 — explizit)

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The fourth stadium of sculptural development demands that the sculpture free itself entirely from gravity — not by resting on a minimal base but by appearing to float. The "illusionistic" label applied to early student attempts is accurate: they do not truly float, but they create the perceptual condition of floating through minimal, dematerialized supports.

**Evidence**: Student works abb. 137–139: Korona Krause (spiral spring support), Irmgard Sörensen-Popitz (diagonal glass plate with maximum load), Hinrich Bredendieck (glass tubes in spring-wire system). These three solutions show independent parallel resolution of the same problem — as with the Gleichgewichtsstudien.

**Conclusion**: The illusionistic kinetic and the schwebende Plastik together prepare the student for the real kinetic problem — which can only be solved definitively through magnetic or electrical forces, not yet available in the training context.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Schweben als höchste Stufe des Gleichgewichtsproblems / Floating as the Highest Stage of the Equilibrium Problem
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The suspended sculpture represents the most complex stage of the balance problem — it is simultaneously theoretical (about material, volume), spatial (about relationships), and temporal (anticipating movement). The floating condition is the prerequisite for entering kinetic design.
- **Rule**: Design floating before designing moving — master the static suspension of volume before introducing actual motion.
- **Source**: p.152
- **Quote (DE)**: "während des statischen, auf einer unterlage ruhenden plastiken in bezug auf ihre umgebung eine bestimmte lage einnehmen und innerhalb dieser lage richtungsbeziehungen zur erde: horizontal, vertikal, schräg — enthält die schwebende plastik, zunächst theoretisch, die material- bzw. volumenbeziehungen und alle zugehörigen attribute nur in bezug auf sich selbst."
- **Quote (EN)**: "While static sculptures resting on a support take up a determined position with respect to their surroundings and within that position maintain directional relationships to the ground: horizontal, vertical, diagonal — the suspended sculpture contains, initially theoretically, the material and volume-relations and all associated attributes in reference only to itself."
- **Category**: volume

## Die kinetische (bewegliche) Plastik

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The kinetic sculpture represents a step beyond the suspended form toward actual Bewegung. It is the highest currently attainable stage of sculptural development, and it demonstrates that Plastik = Material + Volumenbeziehungen in its most complete form.

**Evidence**: From the loosening of the massive form one step further would be the kinetic — the schwebende being simultaneously kinetic. Virtual volume relationships are formed through movement of largely linear material extents, rings, rods, and other objects (abb. 140, 141, 149, 152). The cosmic connections — whether kinetic and schwebende Plastik can have connections with celestial body rotation — require further biological research.

**Conclusion**: Plastik advances from material through form through equilibrium through suspension into kinetic event. The next step — true magnetic/gravitational kinetics — remains to be realized.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Biotechnik als Konstruktionsprinzip / Biotechnik as Construction Principle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The "Randplastik" (edge/boundary sculpture) — a sculpture that describes a movement path through its volume-mass-material relationships — is a fully valid sculptural form. It contains within itself the entire dynamic of the biotechnical Konstruktion. Fritz Kuhr's "vierdimensionale" Plastik is cited as an example of this.
- **Rule**: Design sculpture as a path-description (Bewegungsbahn), not as a static object — the form encodes its own movement.
- **Source**: p.160
- **Quote (DE)**: "auf einsto beschreibt obige plastik eine bewegungsbahn, wobei ihre volumenbeziehungen, material- und maßverhältnisse am deutlichsten zur wirkung kommen. man kann sagen, daß eine solche plastik eine vollwertige »randplastik« ist."
- **Quote (EN)**: "At once, the above sculpture describes a movement path, whereby its volume-relations, material- and size-ratios come to effect most powerfully. One can say that such a sculpture is a fully valid 'boundary sculpture'."
- **Category**: volume

## Zur Geschichte der kinetischen Plastik

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The kinetic plastic has a history and a theory. Two foundational manifestos establish the intellectual grounding: the realist manifesto of Gabo and Pevsner (Moscow 1920) and the dynamisch-konstruktives Kraftsystem manifesto of Kemény and Moholy-Nagy (Berlin 1922, published in Sturm, Heft 12).

**Evidence**: Direct quotations from both manifestos establishing the governing principles of kinetische Plastik. Then: the argument that today's sculptors working in illusionistic kinetics are not yet solving the full problem — they must move toward a dynamisch-konstruktives Kraftsystem.

**Conclusion**: The kinetisch-konstruktive Kraftsystem is identified as the proper goal: "die vitale Konstruktivität ist die Erscheinungsform des Lebens und das Prinzip aller menschlichen und kosmischen Entfaltungen." Light, magnetism, electricity will be the future materials.

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Dynamisch-konstruktives Kraftsystem / Dynamic-Constructive Force System
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The goal of kinetische Plastik is not the representation of movement but the construction of a dynamic force system in which the viewer is an active participant — not a passive observer. This is the "kinetisch-konstruktives Kraftsystem" formulated in the 1922 Kemény/Moholy-Nagy manifesto.
- **Rule**: Design kinetic works as force-field systems that activate the viewer's space, not as demonstrations of mechanical motion.
- **Source**: p.162
- **Quote (DE)**: "die vitale konstruktivität ist die erscheinungsform des lebens und das prinzip aller menschlichen und kosmischen entfaltungen. in der kunst umgesetzt bedeutet sie heute die aktivmachung des raumes mittels dynamisch-konstruktiver kraftsysteme, d. h. die ineinander-konstruierung der in dem fysischen raume sich real gegeneinander spannenden kräfte und ihre hinein-konstruierung in den gleichfalls als kraft (spannung) wirkenden raum."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Vital constructivity is the manifestation-form of life and the principle of all human and cosmic unfolding. Translated into art it means today the activation of space through dynamically-constructive force systems — that is, the mutual construction of the forces really tensioning against each other in physical space and their construction into the space that likewise acts as force (tension)."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Konstruktivität als Organisationsprinzip / Constructivity as Organizational Principle
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Konstruktivität as an organizational principle of human endeavors (from the Kemény/Moholy-Nagy manifesto) means: instead of the static Gestaltungsform of classical art, the dynamic single-construction of dynamic, material, force, and space-relationships must be organized. Classical art works with material-construction (material and form-relationships); dynamic construction must organize material-technical, force-relationships, and space (Raumgestaltung) as force.
- **Rule**: Organize art not as static form but as a dynamic construction of force-relationships in space.
- **Source**: p.162
- **Quote (DE)**: "die konstruktivität als organisationsprinzip der menschlichen bestrebungen führte in der gestaltungsform, welche entweder zu einem technischen naturalismus ausartete, oder zu solchen formvereinfachungen, die in der beschränkung auf die horizontale, vertikale und diagonale stecken geblieben sind."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Constructivity as organizational principle of human endeavors led in the design-form, which either degenerated into a technical naturalism, or to such simplifications of form that stayed stuck in the restriction to the horizontal, vertical, and diagonal."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Gabo/Pevsner Grundgesetz der kinetischen Plastik / Gabo/Pevsner Fundamental Law of Kinetic Sculpture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: From the Realist Manifesto (Gabo/Pevsner, Moscow 1920): "Die Grundgesetze der Kunst müssen auf den soliden Grundlagen aufgebaut sein." The foundational laws cited: "die Grundsteine des gesamten Weltprozesses, sie genügen sämtlichen Vorgängen des Weltprozesses, um sie zu ihrem Optimum zu bringen" — specifically referencing the seven biotechnical elements.
- **Rule**: Build on the fundamental laws of the world-process (biotechnical elements) — do not derive from taste or style.
- **Source**: p.162
- **Quote (DE)**: "aus dem realistischen manifest von gabo und pevsner: »nun sind raum und zeit die beiden ausschließlichen formen der lebensfüllung. also hat auch die kunst sich nach diesen beiden grundformen zu richten, wenn sie das wahre leben erfassen will.«"
- **Quote (EN)**: "From the realist manifesto of Gabo and Pevsner: 'Now space and time are the two exclusive forms of life-fullness. Therefore art also must orient itself toward these two basic forms, if it wishes to grasp true life.'"
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Plastik und der Begriff der Masse / Sculpture and the Concept of Mass
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: From Kemény/Moholy-Nagy manifesto: "Wir verneinen das Volumen als räumlichen Ausdrucksform; sowenig mit einem Volumen gemessen werden wie eine Flüssigkeit mit dem Metermass." The concept of mass as the primary sculptural material is rejected. Space, not mass, is the true medium.
- **Rule**: Reject mass-volume as the measure of sculpture; design the enclosed space, the force-field, the spatial event.
- **Source**: p.162
- **Quote (DE)**: "wir verneinen das volumen als räumlichen ausdrucksform; sowenig mit einem volumen gemessen werden wie eine flüssigkeit mit dem metermaß, so könnte der raum dem umfang einer undurchdringlichen masse abgelesen werden."
- **Quote (EN)**: "We deny volume as a spatial form of expression; just as little as a liquid can be measured with a meter-rule, so could space be read off from the surface of an impenetrable mass."
- **Category**: space

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Spielzeug als zeitgemäße Plastik / Toys as Contemporary Sculpture
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Toys, fountains, fireworks — these are early kinetic sculptures. They contain intelligent translations of technical ideas that communicate more about the essence of technical processes than learned lectures. They are precursors to the kinetic art of the future.
- **Rule**: Study toys, fountains, and fireworks as proto-kinetic design — they demonstrate virtual volume, force systems, and temporal spatial events in accessible form.
- **Source**: p.164
- **Quote (DE)**: "spielzeuge sind in vielen fällen die zeitgemäßen plastiken. sie enthalten oft geistreiche übersetzungen technischer ideen, die meist mehr von dem wesen technischer vorgänge vermitteln als gelehrte vorträge."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Toys are in many cases the contemporary sculptures. They often contain intelligent translations of technical ideas that communicate more about the essence of technical processes than learned lectures."
- **Category**: volume

## Das Licht

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: Light is not illumination — it is a spatial projection medium, a primary tool for creating virtual volume. The transition from kinetische Plastik to light marks the entry into a new phase of spatial design.

**Evidence**: Since the use of high-quality artificial light sources — reflectors, rotating mechanisms, neon tubes, the lettering of advertising signs — light has become an elementary Gestaltungsfaktor. Yet it is not yet legitimate (noch nicht legitim). Examples: light in the nocturnal city, aircraft beacons, reflectors of advertising, the rotating letters of company signs, neon sources.

**Conclusion**: Light will be the next primary material after kinetics. The reflectors of light sources, neon tubes, the rotating letters of signage — all are already, unconsciously, practicing light-space design. The designed light-space event is the logical next step after the kinetic. References: abb. 150–157 (fireworks, urban light), and abb. 157–165 (to follow in b08).

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-15]**: Licht als Gestaltungsfaktor / Light as Design Factor
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Light has become an elementary Gestaltungsfaktor — a primary shaping element — for the creation of virtual volume. Since the deployment of high-quality artificial light sources, light can create spatial events that cannot be achieved by any static material form. However, it is "noch nicht legitim" — not yet recognized as a legitimate design material.
- **Rule**: Treat light as material for spatial construction, not as illumination of existing form.
- **Source**: p.166
- **Quote (DE)**: "das licht ist in diesem zusammenhang — als räumliche projektion — ein hervorragendes mittel zur erzeugung von virtuellen volumen. (abb. 150—157) seit der verwendung hochwertiger intensiver künstlicher lichtquellen ist das licht ein elementarer gestaltungsfaktor geworden. allerdings noch nicht legitim."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Light in this context — as spatial projection — is an outstanding means for the generation of virtual volume. (fig. 150–157) Since the use of high-quality, intensive artificial light sources, light has become an elementary design factor. However, not yet legitimate."
- **Category**: light

**PRINCIPLE [P-16]**: Vorberechnete Lichtwirkung / Pre-calculated Light Effect
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Fireworks prove that pre-calculated light effects (direction, movement, color) achieve a substantially higher result than undesigned light phenomena. The lesson of fireworks for spatial design: light must be calculated in advance, not left to chance.
- **Rule**: Pre-calculate the direction, movement, and color of light as precisely as any structural calculation.
- **Source**: p.166
- **Quote (DE)**: "feuerwerk ist spiel und wissenschaft zugleich. es erbringt auch den beweis dafür, daß vorberechnete wirkungen (richtung, bewegung, farbe usw.) eine wesentliche steigerung erzielen können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Fireworks are play and science simultaneously. They also provide the proof that pre-calculated effects (direction, movement, color, etc.) can achieve a substantial amplification."
- **Category**: light
