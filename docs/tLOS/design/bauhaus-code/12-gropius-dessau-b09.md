## Siedlung Dessau-Törten — Aerial, Site Plan, Construction, and Dwelling Theory

This batch covers pages 159–178 of "Bauhausbauten Dessau" (1930), PDF pages 160–179 (0-indexed). The subject throughout is the **Siedlung Dessau-Törten**: the housing estate Gropius designed and built in three construction campaigns (1926, 1927, 1928) adjacent to the Bauhaus. These pages present the estate as the logical extension of Bauhaus principles into mass housing: the same arguments about Funktionsgliederung, Typisierung, and sachlicher Stil that govern the school building are here applied to the Wohnungsfrage — the housing question. Construction rationalization, the Großbaustelle, standardized types, and the Baukastenverfahren (kit-of-parts system) are the dominant themes.

---

## Section 1 — Aerial View and Site Plan: Siedlung as Urban Argument

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Siedlung Dessau-Törten is not a collection of houses but a planned urban organism — its layout, orientation, and sequential construction phases are as designed as the school building itself. The aerial photograph and master plan demonstrate that urbanism at scale follows the same logic as a single building: zoning, orientation, circulation, and growth are all resolved.

**Evidence**: Aerial photograph (abb. 142) from the Junkers Luftbild, Dessau, showing the first construction phase (1926) from the air; master plan (abb. 143) showing all three construction campaigns — 60 units (1926), 100 units (1927), 156 units (1928) — with street layout, orientation, and geplante Sport- und Spielplätze (planned sports and play areas) indicated.

**Conclusion**: The estate demonstrates that rational, economic housing can simultaneously be urban planning. The serial repetition of the type does not produce monotony when the overall plan is correctly designed: the rows are oriented to sun and wind, the streets follow the natural curve of the land, the density is controlled, and communal facilities are provided.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-01]**: Vogelschau-Planung / Urban Planning from the Air
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The aerial view is the planning view. Only from above can the full argument of the Siedlung be read: the orientation of the rows (north–south for optimal sun access to each unit), the curved street system following the terrain, the logical separation of phases, and the provision for communal facilities. What appears repetitive from the ground is revealed from above as a coherent spatial strategy.
- **Rule**: Always design a settlement from the aerial plan first — sun orientation, street pattern, density, and communal space must be resolved at the urban scale before the individual unit is designed.
- **Source**: p.159–160
- **Quote (DE)**: "siedlung dessau-törten / erster bauabschnitt 1926 aus der vogelschau"
- **Quote (EN)**: "Siedlung Dessau-Törten / first construction phase 1926 from the bird's-eye view"
- **Category**: urbanism

**PRINCIPLE [P-02]**: Gesamtlageplan / Phased Master Plan as Frozen Framework
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Gesamtlageplan (master plan) for the Baujahre 1926, 1927, and 1928 shows that growth was pre-planned in sequential phases of 60, 100, and 156 units respectively. The street system and the location of communal facilities (sport and play areas) were fixed before the first unit was built. The plan is not a residue of completed buildings — it is the instrument that governs them.
- **Rule**: Fix the master plan — streets, orientation, communal facilities, phasing — before designing any individual unit. Growth must be governed by a pre-established spatial framework.
- **Source**: p.160
- **Quote (DE)**: "gesamtlageplan für die baujahre 1926 (60 einheiten), 1927 (100 einheiten), 1928 (156 einheiten)"

## Section 2 — Construction System 1926: Bauhausbauten Typ

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Typ des Baujahres 1926 demonstrates the constructive logic of Bauhaus housing: bearing walls are of Schlackenbeton (slag concrete hollow blocks), floor slabs span freely between them, and non-bearing front walls are thin, isolating, and independent of the structure. The model and section make the structural argument visible.

**Evidence**: Konstruktionsmodell (abb. 144, construction model) of the 1926 type; construction text on p.161 describing the system; floor plans of Ober- and Erdgeschoss (abb. 146, 147) for the 1926 type; construction section (abb. 149) with Rapiddeckenbalken detail; Keller- und Fundamentgrundriss (abb. 150) for the 1927 type.

**Conclusion**: The structural system is transparent: load paths are concentrated in the Schlackenbetonmauerwerk, floors span between party walls, and non-structural front walls are assembled from insulating thin-shell units. This is the same principle as the curtain wall on the Bauhaus school building, now applied to mass housing.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-03]**: Schlackenbetonmauerwerk / Slag Concrete Construction as Thermal and Structural Economy
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The bearing walls (Brandgiebel — party/fire walls) are built of Schlackenbeton-Hohlkörpern (slag concrete hollow-block units). These are simultaneously structural and thermally efficient. The floor beams span freely from party wall to party wall. The front façade walls are non-bearing, constructed from thin isolating blocks — they carry no load and are designed purely for thermal separation.
- **Rule**: Separate the structural role (party walls carry loads) from the enclosure role (front and rear walls insulate only). Each element performs its single function optimally.
- **Source**: p.161
- **Quote (DE)**: "tragende brandwände aus schlackenbeton-hohlkörpern, decken frei gespannt von brandgiebel zu brandgiebel aus betonquelbalken, die — balken neben balken — trocken verlegt werden. die frontwände werden durch isolierende, nicht tragende füllwände aus schlackenbeton-hohlsteinen gebildet, die auf armierten, freigespannten betonbalken mit direkter lastübermittlung auf die brandgiebel ruhen."
- **Quote (EN)**: "Bearing party walls of slag concrete hollow blocks, floor slabs freely spanned from party wall to party wall using concrete expansion beams, laid dry beam by beam. The front walls are formed by insulating, non-bearing infill walls of slag concrete hollow blocks, which rest on reinforced, freely spanning concrete beams with direct load transfer to the party walls."
- **Category**: material | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-04]**: Trockenverlegung / Dry-Laid Construction for Speed and Reversibility
- **Statement** [INFERRED]: Floor beams ("balken neben balken — trocken verlegt") are laid dry — without mortar between beams. This speeds construction significantly and is consistent with the larger argument for industrialized, rationalised building: wet trades are reduced, components are prefabricated, and assembly is a logistical rather than a craft operation.
- **Rule**: Reduce wet trades to the minimum. Wherever possible, replace wet construction (mortar, plaster) with dry assembly of prefabricated components.
- **Source**: p.161
- **Category**: standardization | workshop-integration

## Section 3 — Dwelling Types 1927 and 1928: Typus and Wohnungsminimum

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The housing question ("Wohnungsfrage") is a question of the minimum: what is the minimum dwelling — the Standardwohnung — that satisfies the biological, hygienic, and social needs of each adult individual? This is not a cultural question but a scientific one. The Typus is not a constraint on individuality but its precondition: only through typification and standardization can sufficient good housing be produced at affordable cost. The 1927 and 1928 types demonstrate successive refinements of the minimum plan.

**Evidence**: Dense theoretical text (pp.162–163) on the Wohnungsminimum with footnote reference to Dr. Paul Vogler, Berlin; photograph of the Siedlung from the south (p.163); further theoretical text (pp.163–164) on Typus, Norm, and cultural development; floor plans for 1927 type (abb. 151 ground floor, abb. 152 upper floor) and 1928 type (abb. 153 combined ground/basement, abb. 154 combined ground/upper).

**Conclusion**: The Typus is not architectural poverty — it is the highest form of cultural organisation. The Standardwohnung, designed around biological and social minimums rather than arbitrary convention, represents the most advanced expression of collective intelligence. The variation in plan between the 1926, 1927, and 1928 types is evidence not of inconsistency but of systematic refinement.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-05]**: Wohnungsminimum / The Minimum Dwelling as Scientific Standard
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The question of the minimum dwelling is "nach dem wohnungsminimum an raum, luft, licht, wärme, die der mensch braucht, um bei der vollentwicklung seiner lebensfunktionen durch die behausung keine hemmungen zu erfahren" — the minimum of space, air, light, and warmth that a person requires so that their vital functions are not constrained by their dwelling. This minimum is not arbitrary or cultural but biological: it can be established scientifically. The large apartment is not better than the small one unless it is rationally organised; a well-organised small room has higher living value than a larger irrational one.
- **Rule**: Establish the spatial, thermal, and hygienic minimum for every room type through biological and social analysis, not tradition. Design to the minimum — not less, not more — and then standardize it.
- **Source**: p.162
- **Quote (DE)**: "die frage nach dem wohnungsminimum ist die nach dem elementaren minimum an raum, luft, licht, wärme, die der mensch braucht, um bei der vollentwicklung seiner lebensfunktionen durch die behausung keine hemmungen zu erfahren, also ein 'minimum vivendi' an stelle eines modus 'non moriendi'."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The question of the minimum dwelling is that of the elementary minimum of space, air, light, and warmth that a person needs so that, in the full development of their vital functions, they experience no hindrance from their dwelling — thus a 'minimum vivendi' rather than a mere mode of 'non moriendi' [not dying]."
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-06]**: Raumvergrößerung vs. Raumqualität / Size is Not Quality
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: When light, air, sun, and warmth are culturally and biologically more important than sheer volume, and are equally economical at normal living prices, then the prescription follows: increase the quality of space rather than its quantity. Enlarging the room serves nutrition as warmth once served — it is a primitive substitute for genuine spatial quality. The correct response to housing poverty is not larger rooms but better rooms.
- **Rule**: Prioritize light, air, solar orientation, and thermal comfort over room area. A smaller, well-oriented room with good light and air is superior to a larger room lacking these qualities.
- **Source**: p.162
- **Quote (DE)**: "wenn die zuführung von licht, sonne, luft und wärme aber kulturell wichtiger und bei normalen bodenpreisen auch ökonomischer ist als die vermehrung an raum, so lautet das gebot: vergrößert die räume, verkleinert die räume, spart eher an nahrung als an wärme."
- **Quote (EN)**: "When the provision of light, sun, air, and warmth is culturally more important and at normal land prices also more economical than the multiplication of space, then the commandment is: increase the rooms [in quality], reduce the rooms [in area], save rather on nutrition than on warmth."
- **Category**: space | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-07]**: Typus und Norm / The Type as Cultural Achievement, Not Constraint
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Typus is not an invention of the present age — it was always the sign of a formed social order. The automobile buyer today does not think of having a vehicle built to individual measure — it is apparent that only serial production raises quality and lowers price. The Typus is not the enemy of individual expression but its precondition at the cultural level: "die norm ist immer ein letztes, reifstes ergebnis aus der übereinstimmung sachlicher lösungen verschiedener individuen" — the norm is always the final, most mature result of the convergence of objective solutions by different individuals.
- **Rule**: Use the type as the basis — not as the endpoint. The type concentrates the collective intelligence of many solutions into a single mature form. Individualize above the type, never against it.
- **Source**: p.163
- **Quote (DE)**: "der typus ist nicht ein hemmnis kultureller entwicklung, sondern geradezu eine ihrer voraussetzungen. er birgt die auslese des besten in sich und scheidet das elementare, überindividuelle vom subjektiven ab. das märchen von der vergewaltigung des individuums durch typung und normung schwindet bei einem rückblick auf die geschichte."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The type is not an obstacle to cultural development but rather one of its preconditions. It harbours the selection of the best and separates the elementary, supra-individual from the subjective. The tale of the violation of the individual through typification and standardization vanishes upon looking back at history."
- **Category**: standardization | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-08]**: Standardwohnung / The Standard Dwelling as Grundforderung
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Standardwohnung is the scientifically grounded minimal dwelling that represents a justified, objective minimum from the point of view of purpose and meaning. It would be the fundamental requirement: "jede erwachsene mensch sein eigenes, auch kleines zimmer" — every adult person their own room, however small. From this basic right the standard dwelling derives: it is not a lesser form of housing but the baseline of social justice in built form.
- **Rule**: Define the Standardwohnung as the minimum that guarantees every adult their own space, adequate light, warmth, and air. This is the social floor, not the architectural ceiling.
- **Source**: p.162–163
- **Quote (DE)**: "die standardwohnung würde die selbstverständliche grundforderung darstellen: jeder erwachsene mensch sein eigenes, auch kleines zimmer!"
- **Quote (EN)**: "The standard dwelling would represent the self-evident fundamental requirement: every adult person their own room, however small!"
- **Category**: function | space

**PRINCIPLE [P-09]**: Wiederholung und Ordnung / Repetition of the Type as Social Clarity
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The Typus, through its repetition, produces clarity in the cityscape ("bei der wiederholung typischer hausgebilde steigt die sachliche qualität"). Natural competition among different types placed alongside each other demonstrates the best solution, provides healthy variation, and the repetition of typical house forms raises the objective quality of the urban image. It is always the final, most mature result of the agreement of objective solutions of different individuals. It always reflects the character of the nation and the individual, and the recurrence of the equal parts acts in an ordering and calming manner.
- **Rule**: Accept that the Typus, repeated at urban scale, produces order rather than monotony. The recurrence of equal parts is ordering and calming — this is social architecture, not institutional uniformity.
- **Source**: p.163–164
- **Quote (DE)**: "die einheitlichkeit dieser zeile innerhalb des ganzen stadtbildes muß äußerlich zum ausdruck kommen, die abweichung der größen gibt uns trotzdem die nötige variation. die besten stadtbilder der vergangenheit in unserm oder in andern ländern geben den bündigen beweis, daß sich schönheit und klarheit eines stadtbildes mit der durchführung des typus, mit der wiederholung typischer hausgebilde steigert."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The unity of this row within the overall urban image must find outward expression; the variation in sizes nevertheless gives us the necessary variation. The best city images of the past in our or other countries provide conclusive proof that the beauty and clarity of an urban image increases with the deployment of the type, with the repetition of typical building forms."
- **Category**: urbanism | standardization

## Section 4 — Rationalisierung der Baustelle: The Construction Site as Factory

### Argument Structure

**Thesis**: The Großbaustelle (large construction site) is the primary precondition for construction rationalization. Only at scale can specialised management, machinery, and logistics be economically deployed. The construction site must be organised like a factory: standardised components arrive by rail or truck, are stored in sequence, and are placed by crane according to a predetermined programme. The primitive economic fact is that our need for shelter can be met with diminishing expenditure of money and material through ever-increasing organisational rationalization.

**Evidence**: Construction site organization plan (abb. 155, 1926); aerial photograph of the organized Baustelle 1926 (abb. 156); cost savings chart (abb. 157, Zeltplan für die rohbauten des Baujahres 1926); tower crane photographs in film-strip format (abb. 158, 159, 160, 161) showing the Turmdrehkran placing Rapiddeckenbalken, armierte Betonbalken, and armierte Betonentlastungsbalken; exterior photographs of the structural frame before Füllwände (abb. 162 Straßenseite, abb. 163 Gartenseite); key construction text on pp.171–179.

**Conclusion**: The Turmdrehkran, the standardised precast beam, and the organised Großbaustelle are not merely technical conveniences — they are the physical expression of the argument that building must be industrialised if the housing question is to be solved. The construction site IS the argument.

---

### Principles

**PRINCIPLE [P-10]**: Großbaustelle als Rationalisierungsvoraussetzung / The Large Construction Site as Precondition
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The large construction site is the primary precondition for rationalization. With its scale grows the possibility of deploying specially trained personnel and of distributing the overhead costs of management, supervision, and machinery more favourably than on small construction sites.
- **Rule**: Aggregate construction programmes into large, coordinated sites wherever possible. Small, scattered sites cannot justify the investment in machinery, organisation, and specialist management that rationalization requires.
- **Source**: p.171
- **Quote (DE)**: "die großbaustelle ist hauptvoraussetzung der rationalisierung. mit ihrem umfang wächst die möglichkeit zum einsatz speziell geschulter organe und verteilen sich die generalunkosten für leitung, aufsicht, maschinen günstiger als bei kleinen bauvorhaben."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The large construction site is the primary precondition for rationalization. With its scale grows the possibility of deploying specially trained personnel, and the general overhead costs for management, supervision, and machinery are distributed more favourably than on small building projects."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-11]**: Volkswirtschaftliche Grundtatsache / The Primitive Economic Fact of Housing
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The primitive economic fact is that our need for shelter can — like all other needs of the human individual — be satisfied with diminishing expenditure of money and material through ever-increasing organisational rationalization. This truth led to the machine, to mass production, to rationalization. Rationalization is not hostile to the building trades — it is unavoidable for the economy: "die volkswirtschaft nicht fortzudenken sind und die für das bauen die gleiche bedeutung besitzen wie für alle anderen zweige menschlicher betätigung."
- **Rule**: Treat housing construction as an economic sector subject to the same rationalisation as manufacturing. The resistance to industrialised building is not a defence of craft culture — it is economic waste.
- **Source**: p.170
- **Quote (DE)**: "die primitive volkswirtschaftliche tatsache ist, unsere bedürfnisse an raum, luft, licht, wärme, die der mensch braucht, durch immer sich steigernde organisation zu befriedigen, dieser trieb führte zur maschine, zur arbeitseilung, zur rationalisierung, begriffe, die aus unserer volkswirtschaft nicht fortzudenken sind und die für das bauen die gleiche bedeutung besitzen wie für alle anderen zweige menschlicher betätigung."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The primitive economic fact is that our needs for space, air, light, and warmth — what a person requires — [can be satisfied] through ever-increasing organisation; this drive led to the machine, to the division of labour, to rationalization — concepts that cannot be thought away from our economy and that for building have the same significance as for all other branches of human activity."
- **Category**: standardization | function

**PRINCIPLE [P-12]**: Mechanisierung als Befreiung / Mechanization as Liberation, Not Degradation
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Mechanization in its ultimate effect can only serve the human individual — to relieve them of material work so as to free spirit and hand for higher achievement. If mechanization became an end in itself, the most important thing — the living fullness of human nature — would wither. The roots of the struggle between the old handicraft culture and the new machine culture lie here. It is unavoidable that the new era develops from craft and machine work a new, organic unity.
- **Rule**: Use mechanization to eliminate repetitive physical labour from the construction process, thereby freeing workers for tasks requiring skill and judgment. Mechanization is emancipatory when correctly directed; it is degrading only when it becomes an end in itself.
- **Source**: p.173
- **Quote (DE)**: "alle mechanisierung kann in ihrer letzten auswirkung nur den einen sinn haben, das menschliche individuum von materieller arbeit zur befriedigung seiner lebensbedürfnisse zu entlasten, damit geist und hand für die höhere leistung frei werden. wäre die mechanisierung selbstzweck, so müßte das wichtigste, die lebendige volle menschennatur verkümmern."
- **Quote (EN)**: "All mechanization can in its ultimate effect have only one meaning: to relieve the human individual of material work in satisfying their vital needs, so that spirit and hand are freed for higher achievement. Were mechanization an end in itself, the most important thing — the living, full human nature — would have to wither."
- **Category**: standardization | pedagogy

**PRINCIPLE [P-13]**: Neue Bautechnik / The New Building Technique: Skeleton vs. Infill
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: The essential characteristic of the new building technique is that the building's room-enclosing walls are no longer conceived as one integral whole to be built up — that is, no longer as in the brick building are all the walls together the load-bearing elements for the entire building; instead, the load of the entire building is transferred to a structural skeleton of steel or concrete. The walls between the columns are constructed only to be as resistant as possible to weather — warmth and cold — as well as to sound, with the goal of the greatest possible weight saving and saving of transport material; these non-bearing room-enclosing parts are therefore manufactured from thinner construction units of higher-quality material, e.g. from thin-shell concrete units.
- **Rule**: Separate the structural skeleton (steel or concrete frame) from the room-enclosing skin (insulating infill panels). The skeleton carries all loads; the skin provides thermal and acoustic separation only. Design each for its specific role.
- **Source**: p.177
- **Quote (DE)**: "das wesentlichste merkmal der neuen bautechnik beruht darin, die funktionen der gebäudeabschließenden wand zu zerlegen; d.h. nicht mehr wie bisher beim ziegelhaus die gesamten wände als tragende teile des hauses zu errichten, sondern die last des ganzen gebäudes lediglich auf ein stützenskelett aus stahl oder beton zu verlegen und durch verwendung hochwertigerer materialien, wie eben stahl und beton, an tragender masse zu sparen, die wände zwischen den stützen dagegen nur so zu konstruieren, daß sie gegen witterungseinflüsse — wärme und kälte — sowie gegen schallstörungen sichern."
- **Quote (EN)**: "The most essential characteristic of the new building technique consists in breaking down the functions of the room-enclosing wall; that is, no longer constructing all walls as load-bearing parts of the building as in the brick building, but instead concentrating the load of the entire building solely on a structural skeleton of steel or concrete, and through the use of higher-quality materials — steel and concrete — saving on load-bearing mass, while the walls between the supports are constructed only to provide protection against weather — warmth and cold — as well as against sound disturbance."
- **Category**: material | function | transparency

**PRINCIPLE [P-14]**: Baukastenverfahren / The Kit-of-Parts: Industrial Prefabrication and Dry Assembly
- **Statement** [CERTAIN]: Just as modern industrial building materials have come into existence and approach the precision of industrial properties, so too will the methods of building increasingly adopt the economical procedures of industry — that is, one will proceed to disassemble the building body into parts that are no longer manufactured at the construction site but rather in stationary workshop factories produced serially by machine, so that these parts can now in variable composition — the building-kit in the large — be assembled on site in the dry-assembly method, independent of season and weather.
- **Rule**: Decompose the building into standardized, prefabricated components manufactured in the workshop, then assembled on site. The construction site becomes an assembly operation, not a manufacturing operation. Dry assembly replaces wet construction wherever possible.
- **Source**: p.178–179
- **Quote (DE)**: "man wird dem ziele entgegengehen, die baukörper in teile zu zerlegen, die nicht mehr an der baustelle, sondern in stationären werkstättenbetrieben serienmäßig maschinell hergestellt werden, so daß diese teile nunmehr in variabler komposition — der baukasten im großen — im trockenmontageverfahren an der baustelle, unabhängig von saison und witterung, montiert werden können."
- **Quote (EN)**: "One will pursue the goal of disassembling the building body into parts that are no longer manufactured at the construction site but rather in stationary workshop factories produced serially by machine, so that these parts can now in variable composition — the building-kit in the large — be assembled at the construction site in the dry-assembly method, independent of season and weather."
- **Category**: standardization | workshop-integration
