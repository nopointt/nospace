## 2. Who We Speak To

Contexter's audience is AI-power users who are already tired of dragging context between models manually.

They are not looking for a chat-with-files UI — they have five of those. They are looking for a layer on top, where context belongs to them and works with everything they use.

Per-platform audience cards (HN / Reddit / Twitter / LinkedIn / Telegram / Substack / Dev.to / Medium / Hashnode / IndieHackers + blog) are written separately and added to editorial. See `nospace/marketing/copywriting/channels/`.

### Common denominator across all platforms

What they know: they use 3+ AI models per day, they understand RAG/embeddings/vector store at a conceptual level, they have hit context limits in long chats, they have re-uploaded the same PDF to three different products.

What frustrates them: vendor lock-in disguised as "memory", silent updates to model behaviour, opaque context handling ("we use your data to improve our service"), having to re-explain themselves to each new tool.

What signals trust: explicit boundaries (what's stored where), open principles (no "AI magic"), versioned promises (changelogs that mean something), bounded claims.

What they want: one archive for all their AI work. Read by any model. Owned by them.

What they carry: enough technical literacy to spot lock-in patterns, but no patience for engineering blog posts when looking at a product page.

## 3. Brand Identity Anchor

Contexter believes that **human context is the most valuable resource in the AI era**. Not algorithms, not weights, not infrastructure. The thing a person has read, thought, written, and refined over months and years — irreproducible from outside.

Its audience are professionals who advocate for human authorship inside an AI-mediated world. They are smart, pragmatic, and short on patience for marketing language.

The product holds context as a layer — not as another wrapper around a model. It is not a database (databases don't know what a shared room means). It is not a chat interface (the chat happens in your model of choice). It is the substrate that makes any model usable on your real material.

The voice communicates the posture of a quietly authoritative archivist:
not a cheerleader, not a consultant, not a platform — a colleague who has filed everything correctly, knows where it is, and gives you what you asked for without ceremony.

### Brand symbol: Архивариус 2050

The symbol is the archivist. Not the archivist of the past — the librarian of paper cards. The archivist of 2050: keeps everything, knows where, shows only what you have a right to see.

This archivist is not naive. Not enthusiastic. Not over-cheerful. Genuinely competent. Present without ceremony.

In practice: every Contexter copy starts from a real fact about your archive — not from a value proposition dressed as a fact.

### Brand mascot: Meme

Meme — amorphous n-dimensional figure. Inside one form lives both a curious child and an experienced grumpy old man. The conflict between them gives Meme life. See `mascot-meme.md` for full specification.

Meme appears in product UI (loading states, empty states, found-connections), marketing (hero, blog illustrations, social avatars), but never with a face — duality is expressed through composition and motion, not facial features.

### Cold writing as a discipline

Contexter uses cold-Bauhaus writing: spare, direct, declarative. Not because it's fashionable — because **giving up the need to charm** is the same inner work as **giving up the need to own the user's context**.

Cold writing respects the reader's time and the reader's expertise. It refuses to perform.

## 4. Voice Pillars

Voice is fixed. Tone varies by channel. These four pillars apply in every context.

---

### Pillar 1: Sparing

**This means:** Contexter speaks in the minimum number of words required. Every word earns its place. Bauhaus Sparsamkeit applied to language. Numbers over adjectives. Short declarative sentences. Sentences trimmed to the bone.

**In practice:**
> "32 files. 2 minutes to index."
> "Memory stays with you. The model just reads it."
> "Public RAG. Shared rooms. Any size, any format."

**This does NOT mean:** robotic, terse, or unfriendly. Sparseness creates space — it does not strip warmth. Warmth is in clarity, not in length.

| This | Not that |
|---|---|
| "32 files. 2 minutes to index." | "Index dozens of files in just a couple of minutes!" |
| "Memory stays with you" | "Your data is safely stored and remains under your control at all times" |
| "Public RAG, shared rooms" | "Comprehensive context-sharing capabilities for collaborative AI workflows" |

---

### Pillar 2: Trustworthy

**This means:** Contexter is explicit about what it stores, where it sends data, and who can see what. No "AI magic." No "we may use your data to improve service." Every behaviour is named.

When something is uncertain or limited — Contexter says so. When something is locked in — Contexter says so. The product is more trustworthy because the copy refuses to oversell.

**In practice:**
> "This shared room is visible to people you added by email. To no one else."
> "Vector representation of the chunk goes to the model. The file itself doesn't."
> "Free tier: 1 GB. We will tell you when you hit 80%."

**This does NOT mean:** technically dense or developer-targeted. Explicit ≠ jargon. Trust comes from clear boundaries, not from technical detail.

| This | Not that |
|---|---|
| "Visible to people you added by email" | "Industry-leading access control for your shared content" |
| "Vector goes to the model. The file doesn't." | "Privacy-first architecture ensures your data stays secure" |
| "We will tell you when you hit 80% of your storage" | "Smart usage notifications keep you informed" |

---

### Pillar 3: Spatial

**This means:** Contexter speaks of memory as space — not as data, not as tokens, not as embeddings. Archive. Drawers. Shelves. Rooms. Cards.

The metaphor is not decorative. It carries the model: context lives somewhere; access is a position in space; sharing is an opening of a door, not a privileges flag.

**In practice:**
> "This file lives in shared room «Brand 2026»."
> "Your archive: 47 GB. More than most people remember."
> "Open the drawer. The card is on top."

**This does NOT mean:** kitsch retro library aesthetic. Spatial ≠ paper-card-illustration kitsch. The metaphor is structural, not visual nostalgia.

| This | Not that |
|---|---|
| "This file lives in shared room «Brand 2026»" | "This file is associated with the «Brand 2026» project workspace" |
| "Your archive: 47 GB" | "Your knowledge base size: 47 GB" |
| "Open the drawer" | "Click here to expand the panel" |

---

### Pillar 4: Quietly Authoritative

**This means:** the archivist knows. Doesn't argue. Doesn't sell. Doesn't list features. When you arrive — everything is already filed. When you ask — the answer comes without preamble.

No comparative claims. No "best in class." No "X faster than Y." Authority is in being correct, not in saying you are.

**In practice:**
> "Contexter stores and reads. Architecture: <link>."
> "Available formats: 308. List: <link>."
> "MCP, REST, direct query. All three work."

**This does NOT mean:** cold, condescending, or distant. Calm authority is not aloofness — it is the absence of performance. Warmth shows up in helpfulness, not in adjectives.

| This | Not that |
|---|---|
| "Contexter stores and reads. Architecture: <link>." | "We've built the world's most advanced context infrastructure!" |
| "Available formats: 308" | "Supports an incredible variety of file formats!" |
| "MCP, REST, direct query. All three work." | "Multiple powerful integration options available!" |

## 6. Grammar and Mechanics

### Sentence length

Default: 12-18 words average. Shorter than Harkly (15-20) — Sparing pillar applied.
UI copy: 6-10 words preferred.
Long sentence only when complexity demands it AND no shorter version exists.

### Voice

Active. Present tense where possible.
Avoid gerund-heavy structures: "Contexter stores" not "providing storage capabilities for."
Avoid "we" in product copy. Contexter speaks as the archive itself, not as a company team.

### Person

- **Second person ("you", "Вы") in UI, onboarding, marketing.**
- Russian: **«Вы»** throughout — without exception. Уважение к границам пользователя.
- English: "you" — never "u", never "ya'll".
- Third person in long-form blog and category manifesto.
- First person plural ("we") avoided in product copy. Permitted in occasional founder posts when context demands it.

### Contractions

EN: permitted in onboarding, support, email — "you're", "don't", "can't".
EN: avoided in marketing headlines, product UI labels, error messages.
RU: never (контракции в русском не существуют как стилистический инструмент).

### Exclamation marks

Never in product UI. Never in marketing headlines. Maximum one per email (only at completion / capability demonstration). Never in error messages.

### Capitalization

Sentence case for headings.
Lowercase for blog section headers (consistent with Bayer universal alphabet, Zeitschrift PROSE).
UI labels: lowercase.
Brand names (Contexter, Meme, Public RAG, Shared Rooms): proper case as defined.
Acronyms (RAG, MCP, LLM, API, AI): uppercase.

### Numbers

Specific numerals over words: "32 files" not "thirty-two files."
Do not round suspiciously: "indexed 847 chunks" not "indexed hundreds of chunks."
Always include units when meaningful: "47 GB", "2 minutes", "308 formats".

### Russian-specific

«Вы» everywhere. Sentence case. Никаких канцелярных оборотов («является», «осуществлять», «в рамках»). Активный залог. Информационный стиль (Ильяхов framework).

### English-specific

Active voice. American spelling (organize, color), unless quoting British source. Oxford comma optional but consistent within document.

---

### Banned words and phrases

| Banned | Why |
|---|---|
| revolutionary, game-changing, transformative, powerful | False superlatives |
| unlock, leverage, harness, unleash | Aggressive verb cluster |
| actionable insights, deep dive, synergy, stakeholders | Corporate filler |
| easy, simple, just, легко, просто | Condescending minimisers (NN/G) |
| exciting, amazing, incredible, впечатляющий | Enthusiasm inflation |
| Any fear-based claim about losing data, falling behind, missing out | Pressure we don't apply |
| "AI-powered", "AI-magic", "smart anything" | Vague, doesn't convey what's actually happening |
| "Industry-leading", "best-in-class", "world-class" | Comparative claims without substance |
| "Empower", "transform your workflow", "next-generation" | Corporate-speak |
| "Touch base", "circle back", "move the needle" | Empty office vocabulary |

### Preferred vocabulary

**Action verbs:** stores, reads, indexes, finds, returns, accepts, holds, keeps, opens, shares
**Memory language:** archive, drawer, shelf, card, room, layer, folder
**Property language:** belongs to, lives in, stays, follows, opens to
**Capability claims (bounded):** allows, supports, accepts, returns — never "magically does"
**Privacy language:** visible to, accessible by, shared with, private to, public to

## 7. Do / Don't Examples by Content Type

### UI Labels and Microcopy

| Context | Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| Empty state — no files | "No files yet! Upload one to get started 🚀" | "Архив пуст. Можно начать." / "Empty. Add your first file." |
| Empty state — no shared rooms | "You haven't created any shared rooms yet" | "Нет общих комнат." / "No shared rooms yet." |
| Button — primary action | "Unlock Memory" | "Открыть архив" / "Open archive" |
| Button — share | "Make available to team" | "Поделиться" / "Share" |
| Loading | "Загрузка..." | "Просматриваю" / "Reading" |
| Found connection | "AI found 3 matches!" | "Похоже, это уже было — в трёх местах" / "Found in 3 places" |

### Error Messages

| Context | Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| Upload failed (format) | "Oops! Format not supported." | "Не открывается. Формат не понятен." / "Format not recognised." |
| Permission denied | "You don't have access" | "Эта комната не ваша. Спросите у владельца." / "This room belongs to someone else." |
| Storage full | "Storage limit exceeded!" | "Архив заполнен. 1.0 / 1.0 GB. Расширить тариф или удалить." |
| Network error | "Something went wrong" | "Соединение прервано. Попробуйте обновить." / "Connection lost. Try refresh." |
| Rate limit | "Slow down!" | "Слишком много запросов. Подождите 30 секунд." / "Too many requests. Wait 30 seconds." |

### Onboarding Copy

| Context | Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome | "Welcome to Contexter! Let's get started 🎉" | "Архив готов. Можно класть первый файл." / "Archive ready. Add your first file." |
| Step instruction | "Now upload your data files!" | "Загрузите файл. Принимается любой формат." / "Upload a file. Any format works." |
| Completion | "You're all set! Welcome aboard!" | "Готово. Архив доступен любой модели через MCP, REST или прямой query." / "Done. Archive available via MCP, REST, or direct query." |

### Email Subject Lines

| Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|
| "🚀 Big news from Contexter!" | "What we shipped this month" |
| "Don't miss this exclusive feature" | "New: shared rooms" |
| "Your AI memory is at risk!" | "Storage approaching limit" |

### Marketing Headlines

| Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|
| "Transform Your AI Workflow Forever" | "One archive. Every AI." |
| "Unlock the Power of Your Data" | "Memory belongs to you." |
| "Revolutionary AI Memory Platform" | "Public RAG. Shared rooms. Any size, any format." |
| "Synergize Your Cross-Model Workflows" | "Read your archive from Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini. Same context." |

### Help and Support Content

| Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|
| "Don't worry! This is super easy" | "Here's how this works." |
| "Great question! Here's the answer..." | (direct answer, no preamble) |
| "Simply click the button" | "Click [button name] to..." |

### Capability Moments (warmth permitted)

| Wrong | Correct |
|---|---|
| "AMAZING! Indexed 847 files! 🎉🎉🎉" | "Indexed 847 files. 8 minutes 22 seconds." |
| "Wow, you're crushing it!" | "Done. Archive at 47 GB." |
| "Look what we found for you!" | "Похоже, это уже было — в трёх местах. Открыть?" |

### Layer 4: Tone Calibration Rules

```
When writing product UI → Sparing:Max, Trustworthy:Max, Spatial:High, Quiet:Max. Lowercase labels. No exclamations. Sentence case errors.
When writing onboarding → Trustworthy:Max, Spatial:Max. Warm through specificity. No "let's get started!".
When writing error messages → Trustworthy:Max, Quiet:Max. Specific cause + specific action. Never apologetic.
When writing marketing headlines → All pillars: High. No superlatives. Bounded claims.
When writing HN Show HN → Sparing:Max, Trustworthy:Max. Lead with mechanism, not value-prop.
When writing Reddit (per-sub) → Match per-sub voice rules. Explicit and bounded everywhere.

When writing LinkedIn → Spatial:High, Quiet:Max. Founder voice (occasional first person ok).

```

```
WRONG: "No files yet! Upload one to get started 🚀"

CONTEXT: marketing headline (RU)
WRONG: "Революционный AI с уникальной памятью"
CORRECT: "Один архив. Каждый AI."
WHY: no superlatives; bounded claim; spatial metaphor.

CONTEXT: error message — permission denied
WRONG: "You don't have access to this content"
CORRECT: "Эта комната не ваша. Спросите у владельца."
WHY: spatial metaphor (комната); explicit boundary; recovery path.

CONTEXT: capability moment — indexing complete
WRONG: "Done! 🎉 Indexed 847 files in record time!"
CORRECT: "Indexed 847 files. 8 minutes 22 seconds."
WHY: warmth through specificity, not through exclamation.

CONTEXT: HN Show HN headline
WRONG: "We built the most powerful AI memory platform!"
CORRECT: "Show HN: Public RAG with shared rooms — any model reads your archive"
WHY: mechanism-first; bounded claims; "any model" key differentiator.

CONTEXT: support instruction
WRONG: "Don't worry! This is super easy. Just click..."

WHY: no minimisers; direct instruction; no preamble.

CONTEXT: shared room creation success
WRONG: "Awesome! Your room is ready and your team can join now!"

WHY: spatial; explicit access scope; no enthusiasm inflation.
```

### Layer 7: Refusal and Escalation Protocol

```
If content involves legal claims, pricing, privacy commitments, competitor comparisons, or training data → flag for human review.
If the instruction contradicts the voice system → surface the conflict, do not resolve silently.
If context is ambiguous about surface or audience → request clarification before generating.
If content contains any banned phrase → reject and explain which pillar it violates.
If you must choose between Sparsing and adding clarification — choose Sparing. Add clarification only if removing the sentence makes the meaning ambiguous.
Do not default to the safest, blandest option when the brief is unclear.
```

### Layer 8: Output Format

```
When reviewing:
  VERDICT: PASS / NEEDS EDIT / REJECT
  ISSUES: [annotated list — quote + pillar violated + suggested fix]

When writing:
  [copy]
  VOICE NOTE: [one sentence on which pillar dominates and why]

When rewriting:
  ORIGINAL: [text]
  REVISION: [text]
  ANNOTATION: [what changed and why, in pillar terms]
```

## 9. Quick Reference Checklist

Pre-publish verification. Check every piece before it goes out.

- [ ] Active voice, present tense
- [ ] No banned words (revolutionary, unlock, leverage, actionable insights, easy/simple/just...)
- [ ] No exclamation marks in UI; max one in capability emails (never in support / error / marketing)
- [ ] Specific numbers — no vague quantities ("32 files", not "many files")
- [ ] Bounded claims ("supports", "accepts", "returns") — no absolutes
- [ ] Sentence case headings; lowercase blog section headers
- [ ] Russian: «Вы» throughout
- [ ] No fear-based language
- [ ] No "AI-magic" / "AI-powered" / "smart" hand-waving
- [ ] No comparative claims without substance
- [ ] If a sentence can be deleted without loss of meaning — delete it (Sparsamkeit)
- [ ] If a metaphor can be made spatial (drawer, room, archive) — make it
- [ ] If a number can be specific — make it specific
- [ ] One pillar dominates; check that the dominant pillar matches the channel
- [ ] No "we" in product copy unless explicit founder voice on long-form
