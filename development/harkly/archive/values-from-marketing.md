## The Core Idea

**Harkly is about listening.**

We help ordinary people be heard.
We help businesses be genuinely friendly to their audience — not performatively.
We help businesses value, listen to, and understand the people they serve.

This is not a feature. This is the reason the product exists.

---

## What Harkly Is

A research tool. But not a cold one.

Harkly believes that the most important thing in any business is understanding people.
Not user segments. Not cohorts. People. With stories, frustrations, and moments of joy.

We are researchers. We value scientific rigor — evidence over opinion, pattern over anecdote.
We are also human. We value human happiness, dignity, and the right to be understood.

These two things are not in tension. They are the same thing.

## Core Values

### 1. Every voice matters
Ordinary people carry the most important information about a product.
Not because they're experts — because they're the ones experiencing it.
Harkly exists to make that voice legible.

### 2. Listening is a discipline
Real listening requires rigor. Structure. Tools.
Casual listening produces casual insight.
We take listening seriously — methodologically, ethically, structurally.

### 3. Science serves people
We use research methods because they produce truth, not because they signal expertise.
The goal is understanding, not the appearance of understanding.
Numbers and frameworks are means, not ends.

### 4. Human happiness is the metric
Behind every data point is a person with a life.
The best product insight is one that helps you make that person's experience better.
We measure what matters: human outcomes, not vanity metrics.

### 5. No performance
We do not perform expertise.
We do not perform empathy.
We do not perform seriousness.
We simply do the work, and we show what we found.

## Positioning

### Market Position: Methodological Leader

Harkly is not a tool that competes on features.
Harkly introduces a category, owns its method, and sets the standard.

**Category:** **Customer Signals Research**
Harkly defines this term and uses it consistently.
Customer Signals Research = the structured practice of listening to what customers
already say, do, and feel — before they're asked a leading question.

**Why methodological leader:**
Any analytics tool can show numbers. Any survey platform can collect answers.
What Harkly brings is a framework — a method for turning noise into signal
before you've spent a single ruble on user interviews.
We don't just give you data. We give you the discipline of listening.

## The 3-Layer Methodology

This is Harkly's research framework. It is the product promise.

### Layer 1 — OSINT: Organic Signals
What people say when no one is watching.
Reviews, forums, social threads, comments — unguarded, unfiltered, unprompted.
This is the rawest signal. Also the most honest.

Harkly aggregates this layer automatically.
No survey design. No recruitment. No leading questions.

### Layer 2 — Silicon Sampling
LLM as a synthetic focus group.
Before spending time and money on human interviews, use AI to map the territory:
What themes might matter? What hypotheses are worth testing? What did we miss?

Silicon Sampling is not a replacement for human research.
It is a navigation tool — so that when you do talk to people, you ask better questions.

### Layer 3 — Human Depth
Real conversations. Real people.
But now you arrive informed, not fishing.
You know what to probe. You know what to challenge.
Layer 1 + 2 made Layer 3 ten times more productive.

## TOV Direction (for v2 rewrite)

Voice should feel like:
> A thoughtful senior researcher who genuinely cares about the people behind the data.
> Who has read widely, thinks carefully, and speaks plainly.
> Who would never say "leverage synergies" or "touch base" or "game-changer."
> Who uses precise language because imprecision is disrespectful to the reader.

Aesthetic reference points:
- **Tone:** The Economist (clarity + intelligence) meets Moleskine (warmth + craft)
- **Not:** TechCrunch (hype), McKinsey (jargon), Mailchimp (forced quirky)
- **Scientific warmth:** like a paper by Richard Feynman — rigorous but human
