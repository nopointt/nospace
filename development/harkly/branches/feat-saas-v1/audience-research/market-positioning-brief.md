## 1. Best-Fit Customer

**Primary target:** Senior/Staff UX or CX researcher at B2B SaaS or enterprise company, working without adequate ResearchOps support, expected to deliver insights faster than the research cycle allows.

**Profile signal from data:**
- Senior/Staff ICs now >50% of UXR market (State of UXR 2025, +9.8 pts)
- 65% of orgs have no dedicated ReOps — researcher does everything alone
- 41% of ReOps orgs have exactly 1 specialist (bandwidth = zero slack)
- Primarily working in contexts where PRIMARY research is constrained: NDA, no user access, B2B stakeholder gatekeeping, security clearance

**Secondary target:** PM or UXD doing their own research (democratization segment) — no methodology background, needs guardrails and fast output.

**Who hurts most (beachhead):**
Solo researcher or team of 2-3. B2B or regulated industry. No users to talk to directly. Deadline set by product decision, not research cycle. Expected to synthesize 100s of documents into stakeholder-ready deliverable in 1-2 weeks. Using Google + Notion + ChatGPT stitched together and trusting nothing.

---

## 2. Market Alternative (What They Use Today)

Desk research has no dedicated tool. The current stack:

| Step | Tool used | The problem |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Finding sources | Google Search, ChatGPT | Results generic, no quality filter, hallucinated citations |
| Collecting / storing | Notion, Google Docs | Manual paste, no structure, breaks at scale |
| Synthesizing | Manual reading, ChatGPT | Time sink, no traceability, AI summaries flatten nuance |
| Artifact creation | PowerPoint, Miro | Manual, hours of formatting |
| Stakeholder delivery | Slides, email | No link back to sources, no reuse |

**Why existing tools fail:**
- **Dovetail** — built for primary research (interviews, usability). AI synthesis not trustworthy: "teams end up hunting through transcripts to verify claims anyway, which defeats the purpose." (Great Question, 2026)
- **Notion/Confluence** — "works for <10 studies/quarter, 1-2 researchers. Maintenance overhead of a manual system starts eating into research time as team grows." (Renaissance Rachel, 2024)
- **ChatGPT/Claude (generic)** — hallucinated citations, no traceability, misses study context, "AI systems mis-cite their sources and ignore study goals" (UXtweak, 2024)
- **Rayyan/Covidence** — academic-only (journal articles), unusable for reports/reviews/OSINT/social

**The gap:** No tool handles the full desk research pipeline: question framing → source planning → corpus ingestion → semantic extraction → traceable synthesis → shareable artifact.

## 3. Unique Attributes (What Only Harkly Does)

| Attribute | What it means in practice |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Multi-frame question formulation** | One research question → PICO, issue tree, HCD, McKinsey framing simultaneously. Researcher doesn't have to know methodology schools. |
| **Source planner** | Recommends which databases, OSINT sources, report types match the question — not generic Google search. |
| **Semantic corpus triage** | 1000+ documents → ranked by relevance to hypothesis, not just keyword match. No more reading everything. |
| **Cross-format extraction** | Pulls facts, metrics, quotes, themes, signals from PDFs, articles, reports, reviews — any source type. |
| **Traceable synthesis** | Every insight links to source. No hallucination problem. Stakeholder can verify any claim in 1 click. |
| **Artifact auto-generation** | PRISMA flow, evidence map, empathy map, fact pack — ready in minutes, not days. |

**The combination that doesn't exist elsewhere:** desk research + multi-methodology + traceable AI synthesis in one workflow.

## 5. Market Category

**Category: AI Desk Research Platform**

Not "research repository" (Dovetail). Not "user interview tool" (Maze). Not "generic AI" (ChatGPT).

Harkly is the first tool purpose-built for **secondary research** — the research type that:
- Requires no user access (works in B2B, regulated, constrained environments)
- Is the fastest growing mode (democratization forcing everyone to do their own)
- Is the biggest AI opportunity: "Desk research is the task AI does best" (User Interviews, 2025)

**How to explain the category on the landing page:**
> "Harkly is where desk research actually gets done — from question to artifact, in one place."

## 6. Relevant Trends (Why Now)

**Three forces converging right now:**

**Force 1: Primary research is getting harder**
- B2B and enterprise = NDA, no user access, security restrictions (Vitaly Friedman, 221R)
- Layoffs cut research teams: 21% of orgs laid off UXRs (State of UXR 2025)
- Research demand up 55%, team size flat (State of UXR 2024)

**Force 2: Democratization broke quality**
- PMs and UXDs doing their own research: "Research is an activity, not a job" (Zelmanow, 172R)
- 400+ research tools, but "most teams still face same 3 problems: finding users takes forever, getting answers takes weeks, process costs too much" (Maze 2025)
- More research happening at lower quality without methodology guardrails

**Force 3: AI adoption without trust**
- 80% of researchers using AI (+24 pts from 2024)
- 91% worried about accuracy/hallucinations
- Core problem: "Core problem with AI synthesis: trust — teams hunt through transcripts to verify claims anyway" (Great Question 2026)
- Desk research = best AI fit, but no purpose-built tool exists for it

**The window:** Researchers adopted AI habits but no tool delivered trustworthy, structured, traceable desk research. That's the gap Harkly closes. Now.
