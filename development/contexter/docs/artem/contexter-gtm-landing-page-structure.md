### Core Section Order (proven high-converting structure)

Based on synthesis of 2025–2026 sources. Data-driven ordering:

```
1. Navigation (minimal, sticky, persistent CTA)
2. Hero (headline + subheadline + CTA + visual proof)
3. Trust bar (integration logos OR customer logos — see notes)
4. Pain framing ("Do you recognize this?")
5. Solution reveal / How it works (3-step flow)
6. Feature highlights (3–4 key capabilities)
7. Demo / Product proof (show the thing working)
8. Social proof / Testimonials (role-specific)
9. Use case segmentation (by persona/role)
10. Integration ecosystem (full logo grid)
11. Pricing (if self-serve and simple)
12. Final CTA (repeat primary offer)
13. Footer
```

### Key 2025–2026 Data Points

**Reading level drives conversion more than polish**
Arclen 2026 Playbook: pages written at 5th–7th grade reading level convert at 12.9% — 6x higher than professional-level copy at 2.1%. For non-technical audiences: plain language is not "dumbing down," it is the highest-converting approach.

**Outcome-first messaging is now table stakes**
2024–2025 shift: pages leading with transformation outperform feature-list pages. Non-technical users need to see "what changes for me" before "how does this work."

**One CTA rule**
Single primary CTA = highest conversion. Every additional competing CTA reduces overall conversion. Exception: hero can have "primary CTA" + "secondary/lower-commitment CTA" (e.g., "Watch demo" as secondary).

**Social proof must be distributed, not clustered**
Arclen: layering proof near every CTA outperforms having one testimonials section. 92% of consumers hesitate to buy with no reviews present.

**Video testimonials lift 80% over text-only**
Arclen 2026 Playbook. For AI products: a 30-second screen-recording testimonial outperforms even the best-written quote.

**Form friction: 3–5 fields max**
HubSpot: 11 fields → 4 fields = +120% conversions. Optimal: 3–5 fields. 67.8% abandonment at 7+ fields.

**Free trial model nuance for non-tech**
Arclen: credit card required trials convert at 48.8% vs 18.2% no-card — but only when product delivers clear value in the first session. For non-tech users with longer activation curves: no-card free tier + rapid onboarding is preferred. AI-driven onboarding cuts time-to-productivity by 40%.

**Mobile-first is table stakes**
60–82.9% of landing page traffic is mobile. Non-tech audiences especially skew mobile. Section order must work as vertical scroll on 390px width.

**Benchmark metrics (2025–2026)**
- SaaS median conversion rate: 3.8% (Unbounce)
- Top performers: 8–12%
- Personalized CTAs: 202% better than generic (Genesys Growth)
- AI personalization conversion lift: +36–40%
- Page speed: 1s load = 3x higher conversion vs 5s; each extra second = -4.42% conversion

### Video hero vs static hero: synthesis

**There is no universal winner. Context determines best choice.**

Evidence summary:
- 88% of B2B buyers watched product videos in past 3 months (B2W.tv 2025)
- Wyzowl 2025: 84% say a brand's video has convinced them to buy
- Unbounce (35,000 landing pages): video impact varies significantly by industry and implementation
- Studio1 Design: video backgrounds can hurt performance — page speed is a conversion variable
- ALF Design Group: looping demo video or GIF animation works well when kept lightweight

**For non-technical AI SaaS (Contexter's segment):**

The winning pattern in 2025–2026 for this specific category is NOT a full-screen video background. It is one of:

**Option A: Product UI screenshot with subtle animation**
- Static screenshot of the actual product interface
- One or two animated elements (typing indicator appearing, response generating)
- Load time: fast (image-based)
- Conversion signal: "this is what you'll actually see"
- Best for: audiences who need to visualize the product before committing

**Option B: Short looping demo GIF (10–15s)**
- Shows the core action: user asks question → AI responds with context-sourced answer
- No click required
- Lightweight (optimized GIF or video/webm < 2MB)
- Best for: when the "magic moment" can be shown in under 15 seconds
- Reference: Otter.ai (video background), Grammarly (animated demo showing 3 use cases)

**Option C: Output-first static (Gamma model)**
- Show the final result/output rather than the process
- For Contexter: show an AI chat where the response includes "from your Notion doc, from yesterday's Slack"
- No animation needed — the content of the output is the demo
- Best for: when output is visually distinctive and self-explanatory

**Recommendation for Contexter:** Option C or B. Show the AI response that includes context citations. The "sourced from your [tool]" attribution is Contexter's visceral moment — equivalent to Gamma's finished slide deck.

### Auto-play silent loop vs click-to-play

**Auto-play silent loop wins for above-fold hero. Click-to-play wins for mid-page deep demo.**

Rationale:
- Auto-play removes decision friction ("should I engage with this?")
- Silent loop communicates product capability with zero user effort
- ALF Design Group: "looped animations or autoplay previews" recommended, keep lightweight
- Click-to-play with sound: reserve for mid-page demo section where high-intent visitors self-select
- Best practice: include subtle visual affordance that content is playing (fade-in, progress indicator)

Pattern from analyzed pages:
- Otter.ai: silent autoplay video background (hero)
- Grammarly: animated inline demo (no click, no sound)
- Loom: product demo visible in hero without click
- Gamma: static product output (no animation needed)

### Above the Fold Checklist (mandatory elements)

Based on synthesis of 2025–2026 best practices + direct analysis of Otter, Grammarly, Loom:

```
MANDATORY (must be visible without scrolling):
[ ] 1. Headline — 5–10 words, outcome-first, 5th-grade reading level
[ ] 2. Subheadline — expands headline, max 20 words, mentions mechanism
[ ] 3. Primary CTA — single button, action verb, low friction ("Try free", "Start free")
[ ] 4. CTA subtext — removes friction ("Free plan · No credit card · Takes 2 min")
[ ] 5. Visual proof — product UI, demo video/GIF, or output example
[ ] 6. Trust micro-signal — 1–2 elements: user count, rating, "works with X"

STRONG RECOMMENDATION (include if space allows):
[ ] 7. Secondary CTA — lower commitment ("Watch demo" / "See how it works")
[ ] 8. Nav CTA — sticky "Try free" or "Log in" in header
[ ] 9. Logo bar or integration logos — visible without scrolling OR immediately below hero

MUST NOT BE ABOVE FOLD:
[ ] Feature list (below-fold only)
[ ] Long explanation paragraphs (3+ sentences)
[ ] Multiple competing primary CTAs
[ ] Abstract taglines without benefit ("The future of AI memory" = fails)
[ ] Technical jargon ("MCP protocol", "vector embeddings", "context infrastructure")
```

**Hero headline principles (ALF Design Group + Arclen 2026):**
- Must answer in 3 seconds: what is this + why does it matter
- Structure: "[outcome] for [audience]" OR "[problem solved]" OR "[transformation]"
- Reading level: 5th–7th grade. Jargon = 2.1% conversion. Plain language = 12.9% conversion
- Max 8 words is guideline — clarity beats brevity
- Avoid: "platform", "solution", "next-generation", "powerful", "seamless"

## 4. Recommended Structure for Contexter

### Context
- Product: Contexter — "One memory. Every AI."
- Audience: non-technical (analysts, PMs, marketers, founders)
- Core value: connect all your tools → AI remembers your context → every AI gets smarter
- Primary pain: context fragmentation across ChatGPT, Claude, Notion, Slack, email — re-entering context manually every time
- Distribution: MCP-first, self-serve, free tier entry
- Stage: pre-PMF, building audience
- Competitive gap: Supermemory targets developers. Contexter owns the non-tech angle.

### Section-by-Section Structure

---

#### Section 0: Navigation (sticky)

**Purpose:** Persistent orientation and re-entry point for returning visitors

**Content:**
- Logo (left)
- 3 nav links max: "How it works" / "Integrations" / "Pricing"
- Right side: "Log in" (text link) + "Try free" (button, accent color)

**Design principles:**
- Sticky on scroll: yes — keeps CTA perpetually accessible
- Minimal — non-tech users are not looking for extensive nav
- CTA button uses brand accent, high contrast

**Reference:** Otter.ai (minimal nav + 2-level CTA), Grammarly (sticky "Get Grammarly - it's free")

---

#### Section 1: Hero

**Purpose:** Answer "what is this and why should I care?" in under 3 seconds (ALF Design Group)

**Content structure:**
- Headline (5–8 words, outcome-first, 5th–7th grade level)
  - Example frame: "Your AI finally knows your work"
  - Alternative frame: "One memory for every AI you use"
  - Must avoid: "context infrastructure", "memory layer", "MCP protocol"
- Subheadline (15–20 words, mechanism hint)
  - Example frame: "Connect your tools once. ChatGPT, Claude, and every AI you use gets the full picture."
- Primary CTA button: "Connect my tools — free" or "Try Contexter free"
- CTA subtext: "Free plan · No credit card · 2-minute setup"
- Visual: animated product UI showing AI chat response with source attribution ("Based on your Notion page from yesterday" or "Found in your last week's Slack message") — Option C or B per Section 3 analysis
- Trust anchor (1–2 of): tool count ("Works with 30+ tools"), early user count, or App Store/PH rating

**Reference:** Grammarly (social proof number + animated demo in hero), Otter.ai (outcome headline + video bg + CTA subtext), Gamma (output as hero visual)

**Anti-patterns (Supermemory analysis):**
- Do NOT use "context infrastructure" or "AI agents" framing
- Do NOT use technical visualization (memory graph, vector nodes) as hero visual
- Do NOT lead with two competing primary CTAs ("Start Building" vs "Get personal supermemory")

---

#### Section 2: Trust Bar — "Works With" Integration Logos

**Purpose:** Instant credibility reset and objection pre-emption ("Does this work with my tools?")

**Content:**
- Label: "Works with your tools" or "Connects to everything you use"
- Logo grid: ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, Gemini, Notion, Slack, Google Docs, Gmail, Obsidian, Linear, Jira, GitHub, and more
- Subtle animation: logos fade in or slow-scroll
- Small "and 30+ more →" link to integrations page

**Why integration logos, not customer logos:**
At pre-PMF stage, customer logos are likely unknown brands. Integration logos (ChatGPT, Notion, Slack) carry recognition weight that no early customer can provide. More importantly: for Contexter's value proposition, the answer to "does this work with MY tools?" is the #1 pre-click question from non-tech users.

**Reference:** Otter.ai (logo bar immediately after hero), Loom (24-logo grid visible above fold), Grammarly (rotating logos in hero)

---

#### Section 3: Pain Framing

**Purpose:** Create emotional recognition — visitor says "yes, that's me"

**Content format:** Card grid (3–4 cards) or visual list
- Each card: icon + 1-line pain scenario (second person "you")
- Example cards:
  - "You paste the same background into every AI chat"
  - "ChatGPT doesn't know what Claude knows"
  - "Your AI answers are generic — it doesn't know your work"
  - "You switch tools constantly, starting from scratch each time"

**Design notes:**
- Cards should feel like a self-check ("is this you?"), not a lecture
- Each card subtly hints at the fix — avoids negativity spiral
- Reading level: casual, direct, 5th grade
- Section header: "Sound familiar?" or "If you use AI, you know this feeling"

**Reference:** No single best reference — this is a 2025 pattern (problem-before-solution). Grammarly hints at pain in headline ("take care of the details").

---

#### Section 4: Solution Reveal / How It Works

**Purpose:** Show the mechanism — "here's what Contexter actually does, in 3 steps"

**Content:** 3-step visual flow (horizontal on desktop, vertical on mobile)

- Step 1: Connect your tools
  - Visual: logos of connected tools + simple checkbox UI
  - Text: "Link your apps once — no configuration required"

- Step 2: Contexter builds your memory
  - Visual: simplified flow animation (docs → memory → AI)
  - Text: "Contexter reads, indexes, and remembers everything across your tools"

- Step 3: Every AI knows your context
  - Visual: split-screen AI chat — without Contexter (generic answer) vs with Contexter (specific, cited answer)
  - Text: "ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor — all of them now know your work"

**The Step 3 visual is the highest-priority creative asset:** showing AI response WITH context vs WITHOUT is the "aha moment" equivalent to NotebookLM's podcast demo and Gamma's slide output.

**Reference:** Otter.ai ("Like having your own executive assistant" framing), NotebookLM (upload → understand → generate), Loom ("How It Works" as section 3)

---

#### Section 5: Feature Highlights

**Purpose:** Address "but what exactly does it do?" for detail-oriented visitors who scroll past the how-it-works

**Content:** 3–4 feature cards in icon grid

| Feature | Icon | Benefit Label | 1-sentence description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Universal sync | connect icon | "All your tools, one memory" | Connects your apps and keeps context current automatically |
| Works with every AI | brain/AI icon | "Every AI gets smarter" | Works with ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, and any MCP-compatible tool |
| Private by default | lock icon | "Your data, your control" | Nothing leaves your account without your permission |
| Zero workflow change | flow icon | "No new habits needed" | Works in the background — no app to open, no commands to learn |

**Notes:**
- "Private by default" is critical for non-tech users — they fear "what happens to my data when I connect all my tools"
- "Zero workflow change" pre-empts the "sounds like a lot of setup" objection
- Avoid: "MCP protocol", "vector embeddings" — translate to outcomes

**Reference:** Otter.ai (6 feature cards with icons), Grammarly (alternating text/video feature demos)

---

#### Section 6: Demo / Product Proof

**Purpose:** Eliminate "I still don't get how this actually works" — show the product in action

**Content:**
- One prominent, realistic product screenshot or short GIF
- Shows: user types a question → AI responds with context-aware answer that cites a specific source ("Based on your Notion page 'Q2 Strategy' from last Tuesday, here's what I found: ...")
- Optional: side-by-side "Before Contexter / After Contexter" comparison
- Section header: "See it in action" or "This is what your AI looks like with Contexter"
- Embedded secondary CTA: "Try it yourself →" or "Watch the 2-min demo"

**Why this section exists separately from the hero:**
Hero visual shows the end state. Demo section shows the process + source attribution. Source attribution is Contexter's differentiator — "your AI tells you WHERE the answer came from."

**Reference:** Loom (interactive video player in page), NotebookLM (Audio Overview demo as conversion driver), Gamma (output as demo)

---

#### Section 7: Testimonials / Social Proof

**Purpose:** Peer validation — "people in my role use this and it worked"

**Content:**
- 3–6 testimonial cards in grid
- FORMAT REQUIREMENT: role-specific (PM, analyst, marketer, founder — no generic "user")
- Card structure: quote (2 sentences max) + name + role/title + company or "works at [company type]"
- Optional: Product Hunt or G2 rating badge if available

**Why role-specific is critical:**
Arclen: 92% of consumers hesitate with no reviews. But for non-tech users, generic testimonials ("it's so useful!") carry zero weight. Seeing "Sarah, Product Manager at a 50-person startup" says exactly: "someone like me found this valuable."

**At launch / pre-reviews:**
Use beta user quotes. Even "I've been testing this for 2 weeks" is credible if the person's role is named. Avoid: generic stock-photo testimonials with no role context.

## 5. Key Insights Summary

### The 8 non-negotiable principles for Contexter's landing page

**1. Outcome-first, always — no exceptions**
Non-tech users don't care about "MCP protocol" or "vector embeddings." They care that "AI finally remembers my work." Translate every feature to a user outcome. 5th–7th grade reading level = 12.9% conversion vs 2.1% for professional language (Arclen 2026).

**2. Show, don't tell — and show it above the fold**
The #1 conversion driver for AI products in 2025–2026 is showing the product working. Silent autoplay or output-as-hero. The "AI responds with context citation" moment must be visible either in the hero visual or directly below (Section 4 Step 3).

**3. Trust bar uses integration logos, not customer logos (at early stage)**
"Works with ChatGPT, Claude, Notion, Slack" is more compelling than 5 unknown company logos. This is Contexter-specific insight: integration logos carry recognition weight that unknown early customers cannot.

**4. Privacy objection is the #1 conversion killer for this value prop**
Non-tech users connecting "all their tools" will have a visceral fear: "where does my data go?" A feature card + footer security link + "private by default" phrasing must appear. Grammarly's "This is responsible AI" section before pricing is the template.

**5. One CTA, many placements — consistent throughout**
Consistent primary action (free trial / connect tools) at every level: Nav, Hero, mid-page, Pricing, Final CTA. No competing calls. The only acceptable secondary CTA: "Watch demo" (lower commitment, not competing conversion action).

**6. Mobile-first section order is the actual page order**
Non-tech audiences are especially mobile-heavy. The vertical scroll experience IS the page: Hook → Proof → How it works → Features → Social proof → CTA. Every section must make full sense on 390px with no horizontal layout.

**7. Role-specific social proof converts; generic social proof does not**
A PM testimonial converts PMs. An analyst testimonial converts analysts. "I love this tool!" from someone with no role attribution converts no one. This is especially critical for non-tech users who need to see themselves in the user base.

**8. The "before/after" demo is the highest-leverage creative investment**
Contexter's visceral moment: same question, asked to an AI without Contexter vs with Contexter. Without: generic answer. With: specific answer citing "from your Notion doc 'Q2 Strategy', from last Tuesday's Slack message." This single visual asset, done well, will outperform every other element on the page in conversion impact.

### Competitive structural gap (from Supermemory analysis)

Supermemory.ai is optimizing its landing page for developers and enterprises:
- "Context infrastructure for your AI agents" — developer vocabulary
- 5-layer technical architecture visualization above fold
- SDK implementations section
- 5 competing CTAs
- Benchmarks vs Mem0/Zep (competitor comparison targeting technical evaluators)

This leaves the entire non-technical segment unaddressed by the primary competitor. Contexter should explicitly own this gap in every structural decision: headline language, visual choices, testimonial selection, CTA copy. The structural competitive moat is: "the only AI memory product that non-technical users actually understand."
