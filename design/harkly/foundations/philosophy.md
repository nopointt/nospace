# Harkly Design Philosophy

## Origin

Harkly is a research tool for people who listen. Its design must embody the same quality: attentive, precise, warm, calm.

The design system inherits structural theory from Bauhaus (1925–1931) through the tLOS design system. But where tLOS is a sovereign spatial OS with Malevich black and Mondrian austerity, Harkly is a research SaaS with the warmth of a Moleskine notebook and the clarity of The Economist.

The Bauhaus skeleton remains. The flesh is Harkly's own.

## Three Foundations

### 1. Bauhaus Structure (from tLOS)

Five principles govern every visual decision:
- **Gestaltung** — form from structure, not imitation
- **Gleichgewicht** — dynamic equilibrium through asymmetry
- **Materialwahrheit** — honest to the screen medium
- **Sparsamkeit** — minimum elements, maximum clarity
- **Raumgestaltung** — space is active, not leftover

These are universal. They work for dark and light, cold and warm, dense and breathing.

### 2. Brand Character (Harkly's own)

The brand symbol is the **healed inner child** — curious without performance, attentive without agenda. This translates directly to design:

| Brand quality | Design expression |
|---|---|
| **Attentive** | Space to breathe. Nothing competes for attention. One dominant per viewport. |
| **Precise** | Systematic scale. No arbitrary values. Every element earns its place. |
| **Humanist** | Warm palette. Soft corners. People, not data points. |
| **Calm** | Resolved motion. No urgency. No flashing. Quiet confidence. |

### 3. Spatial Paradigm (from tLOS)

Harkly is not a web app. It is a spatial interface:
- **Infinite canvas** — not pages, not routes
- **Frames** — spatial objects with position, size, z-layer
- **Omnibar** — primary input for all actions
- **Floors** — Z-axis layers, each an infinite canvas
- **Branches** — parallel infinite canvases on the same floor

Full spatial rules: `harkly-spatial-interface-rules.md`

## What Harkly Is Not

| Anti-pattern | Why |
|---|---|
| A dashboard | Dashboards show numbers. Harkly reveals meaning on a spatial canvas. |
| A dark theme app | Darkness is tLOS (Malevich). Harkly is warm light (Kandinsky Gelb-polus). |
| A dense professional tool | Density is tLOS. Harkly breathes (looseness, not stiffness). |
| A web app with pages | Pages are DOM. Harkly is spatial — frames on infinite canvas. |
| A skeuomorphic design | Materialwahrheit. Screen = light + geometry + typography. No fakes. |

## The Warm Bauhaus

Bauhaus is often read as cold, industrial, minimal. But:

- **Oud (Book 10, P-06):** "I see no reason why the new architecture should renounce round forms."
- **Kandinsky (Book 9):** Free curves carry "lyrical warmth and individual expressivity."
- **Moholy-Nagy (Book 13):** Space is a "biological regulator" — designed for human comfort.
- **Klee (Book 2):** Compositions resolve into "a self-standing, calmly-moving or motion-calm whole."

Harkly lives in this warm Bauhaus tradition. Not the cold factory. The warm workshop.
