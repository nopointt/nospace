## Material logic (the soul of v4)

Two material registers in one body, encoding the child/old-archivist duality:

- **BRONZE parts** = the old archivist. Solid metal, museum-aged, warm honey-amber where light catches. NOT glowing. Time-tested, accumulated, silent.
- **VERDIGRIS PATINA patches (green-blue)** = the curious child. Bioluminescent, subtle inner cyan-teal glow. Alive presence. Casts faint cool reflections on environment.

The duality: bronze holds knowledge, patina lights up with awareness. Together — one Meme.

## Master prompt (v4 canon)

```
Cinematic 16:9 illustration. Subject: a small floating amorphous creature whose body is a single thick wire-line of solid aged bronze metal with green-blue verdigris patina that subtly glows from within with soft cyan-teal bioluminescent light. The body is an open spiral curl combined with a partial arc — abstract, NO arms, NO legs, NO human silhouette, NOT walking. The creature floats in mid-air, suspended like a deep-sea bioluminescent organism made of metal.

The material has two distinct registers:
— BRONZE parts (warm honey-brown highlights where light catches the curve): solid, non-glowing, sculptural metal, museum-aged, NOT shiny

The patina light gently illuminates the surrounding bronze and casts faint cool-light reflections on the floor and nearby drawers.

Inside the upper portion of the spiral curl — a minimal face: two small simple bronze dots for eyes (one slightly larger and rounder = curious child, the other slightly smaller and squinted = wise old archivist) and one short curved line for a mouth (gentle downward curve at center with corners pulled in just slightly — simultaneous grump + curiosity). Asymmetric face. The eye dots are bronze (non-glowing). The patina around the face glows softly, as if life emanates from the curve itself, not from the eyes.

The duality is material-coded: bronze = age, accumulated knowledge, the old archivist; bioluminescent patina = aliveness, presence, the curious child. They share one body.

Setting: vast dark archive interior of 2050. Endless rows of metal card-catalog drawers receding into atmospheric depth toward right side of frame. Style: museum bronze sculpture meets deep-sea bioluminescent creature meets Bauhaus minimalism. Marianne Brandt metalwork heritage, Avatar Pandora living-glow material treatment. Patina suggests age AND life simultaneously — old metal that remembers, alive patina that breathes.
```

## What worked (visual analysis of approved render)

- **Open spiral + partial arc form** — Klee P-24 transitional symbol read perfectly. Always one opening — top-left of the curl.
- **Single line, no human silhouette** — completely abstract body, no biped/limb interpretation.
- **Patina glow is subtle, not neon** — emits enough light to feel alive, not so much it becomes a logo or neon sign. Right register.
- **Asymmetric face** — minimal 2 dots + 1 line. Reads expressive without being cartoony. Slight downturn mouth + asymmetric eye sizes carry the dual character.
- **Composition** — fixed asymmetric Gleichgewicht. Figure on left third, archive depth recedes right, open drawer anchors the action without competing with the creature.
- **Light/shadow split** — Moholy-Nagy P-09 light-as-composition principle. Warm directional light from upper-left meets cool inner glow of patina. Light side of frame public, shadow side private — semantic.
- **Drawer with cards** — implies retrieval/filing without literal interaction. Just the act of drawer being open is enough.
- **Bronze warm-cool register** — bronze parts reflect warm ambient, patina emits cool. Material itself carries the duality without explicit labelling.

## What to preserve in future regenerations

When generating other Meme states (curious / knowing / processing / error / bridging) or other surfaces (avatar, favicon, marketing hero, light theme variant):

1. **Material formula stays:** bronze (non-glowing, warm) + verdigris patina (glowing, cool cyan-teal). NEVER swap: don't make bronze glow, don't make patina dead.
2. **Form stays open:** spiral + arc with one opening. Never close fully.
3. **Face stays minimal:** 2 dots + 1 line. Asymmetric. Bronze (non-glowing). Inside upper portion of curl.
4. **Body stays amorphous:** no limbs, no head separated from body, no walking, no standing.
5. **Composition uses asymmetric Gleichgewicht:** never centered.

## Variant-specific adjustments (for future renderings)

- **Curious state:** spiral curl opens slightly wider, face dot eyes a touch larger and rounder. Patina glow brightens 10-15%.
- **Knowing state:** spiral pulls inward by a quarter turn, face eyes squint slightly. Patina glow steady, even cooler tone.
- **Bridging state (found connection):** a second arc-extension reaches out from the curl toward another point in space, suggesting reach.
- **Processing state:** body in slow rotation, patina glow pulses very slowly (1 cycle per 8 seconds). Motion blur acceptable.
- **Error state:** spiral closes its opening (only state where form fully closes). Patina glow shifts from cyan-teal to dim red-orange. Face mouth flat.
- **Light theme variant:** bronze stays bronze, patina glow shifts from cyan-teal to soft warm gold (matches `#1E3EA0` blue accent compositionally — uses cool patina against warm light setting; in light theme the creature is OUTSIDE on a sunlit archive porch, not inside the dark archive).
- **Avatar / favicon:** silhouette of just the spiral form, no archive setting. 1:1 aspect. Bronze + patina material preserved at small scales.
- **Marketing hero:** wider 21:9 cinematic. More archive depth. Same composition principle.
